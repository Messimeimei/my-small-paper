This paper investigates gender bias in autoregressive large language models (LLMs) by examining bias transfer—the correlation between models’ intrinsic bias (as measured by next-token prediction) and their bias in zero- or few-shot prompting—across a range of experimental settings and open-source models, with a particular focus on the effectiveness of prompt engineering techniques for mitigating such bias. However, the novelty of the work is limited, as there is already a substantial body of literature addressing similar questions regarding zero- and few-shot evaluation of causal LLM bias (see, for example, Ganguli et al., 2023; Bai et al., 2024; Lin et al., 2024; Huang et al., 2023; and the comprehensive survey by Ranjan et al., 2024). The paper does not cite this closely related prior work and is written as if previous studies of LLM bias were restricted to masked language models or fine-tuned autoregressive models, which artificially inflates its contribution. While the specific focus on bias transfer as a metric and the detailed experimental findings may offer some incremental novelty—particularly since prior work such as Ladhak et al. (2023) has only considered bias transfer in the context of summarization tasks and fine-tuning—the overall research area is already crowded. Thus, the main contribution appears to be the application of the bias transfer metric to a broader set of tasks and models, but this advance is modest in light of the extensive existing literature. The paper would benefit from a more thorough engagement with prior work and a clearer articulation of how its findings differ from or build upon established research in the field.
