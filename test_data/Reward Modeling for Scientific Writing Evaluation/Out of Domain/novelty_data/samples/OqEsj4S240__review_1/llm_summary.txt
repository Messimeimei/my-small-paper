This submission introduces a trajectory-based topological analysis of large language model (LLM) representations using zigzag persistence and a new "persistence similarity" metric, applying these tools to both interpretability and layer pruning. The work is a technically meaningful extension of prior topological data analysis (TDA) methods, moving from static (e.g., Betti numbers) to dynamic, across-layer analysis, and proposes persistence similarity as a novel pruning criterion. While the technical adaptation of zigzag persistence to LLMs is non-trivial, the conceptual advance is incremental, representing a logical progression within the TDA-for-neural-networks literature. The empirical results support the effectiveness of the proposed metric for pruning, but the submission omits direct comparison to some relevant prior TDA-based similarity and pruning methods, and somewhat overstates its novelty relative to existing work. Overall, the paper offers a substantive technical contribution, but reviewers should note the incremental nature of the advance and the need for a more comprehensive related work discussion.
