This paper introduces Spark Transformer, a drop-in replacement structure for transformers aimed at accelerating large language model (LLM) inference by exploiting sparsity. Unlike previous works that primarily focus on estimating sparsity through dedicated predictors, Spark Transformer proposes an end-to-end approach that leverages statistical Top-K selection within both the feed-forward and attention components of the model. While this structure appears to offer a new perspective on sparsity-based LLM inference, the novelty is limited, as statistical Top-K methods—particularly those for gradient and activation compression—have already been extensively studied in prior literature ([1], [2], [3]). For example, [1] introduced a comprehensive statistical Top-K selection framework using threshold estimation for various statistical distributions, and [2] explored Gaussian-based threshold estimation, with [1] further generalizing this approach. Additionally, [6] presents a similar method for using partial weights to approximate token scores, which overlaps with aspects of Spark Transformer’s approach. To more clearly establish its contribution, the paper should explicitly differentiate its statistical Top-K mechanism from these existing methods and articulate the unique challenges or innovations involved in integrating Top-K selection into transformer-based architectures. Without a clearer distinction from prior work, the main advance appears incremental, and the paper’s novelty would be strengthened by a more thorough comparison and justification of its approach relative to established techniques.
