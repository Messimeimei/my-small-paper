YOLO-MARL presents an incremental advance in LLM-augmented multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) by reducing LLM involvement to a single pre-training interaction per environment, addressing efficiency concerns present in prior works that use continual or episodic LLM guidance. While the use of LLMs for high-level planning and strategy generation in MARL is well established (e.g., SMART-LLM, KnowAgent, LLM-Augmented DRL), YOLO-MARL’s main contribution is minimizing LLM calls, which offers practical computational and financial benefits but is not a fundamentally new integration paradigm. Claims of being "among the first" to integrate LLMs for planning in MARL are overstated, as the field is already active with several similar approaches; the true novelty lies in the frequency and timing of LLM use rather than the conceptual framework. The reported empirical gains across standard benchmarks are notable, though it is unclear how much these improvements stem from the one-time LLM interaction versus other implementation details. Reviewers should recognize YOLO-MARL as a valuable efficiency-oriented refinement within a rapidly evolving field, but not as a radical departure from existing LLM-MARL methodologies.
