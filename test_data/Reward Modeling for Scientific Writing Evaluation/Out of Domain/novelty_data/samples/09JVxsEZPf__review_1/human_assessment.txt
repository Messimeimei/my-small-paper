This paper introduces SAFEPATCHING, a post-safety alignment (PSA) method designed to address safety, over-safety, and utility challenges in large language models (LLMs) by applying a two-stage framework that leverages distinct safety patches based on harmful data. While the approach is positioned as a novel solution to the limitations of existing PSA methods, its novelty appears limited, as the core methodology relies on the SNIP score proposed by Lee et al. and model merging techniques previously introduced by Yu et al. and Hui et al. The paper does not provide a thorough explanation of the unique contributions or advancements that distinguish SAFEPATCHING from these established methods. As a result, the specific innovations and the rigor of the proposed tool remain unclear. To better establish its contribution, the paper would benefit from more clearly articulating how its approach advances beyond prior work and what distinctive elements, if any, SAFEPATCHING introduces to the field of post-safety alignment for LLMs.
