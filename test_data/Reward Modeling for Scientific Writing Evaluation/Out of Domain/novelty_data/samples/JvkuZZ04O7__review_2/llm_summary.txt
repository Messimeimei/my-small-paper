This assessment finds that the submission presents a practical, incremental advance in knowledge-graph-based retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) by empirically demonstrating that a simple, efficient MLP-based subgraph retriever—adaptively sized to LLM context—can match or outperform more complex GNN- or LLM-based retrieval systems. The main contribution is the explicit, tunable subgraph size adaptation and the streamlined, modular pipeline, but these are positioned as engineering refinements rather than conceptual breakthroughs. The authors' claims of novelty are somewhat overstated, as several prior works already support modular, training-free LLMs and flexible subgraph retrieval, and the differences are often a matter of degree or terminology. The work is best viewed as a valuable empirical validation of the "simple is effective" hypothesis in this domain, rather than a fundamental shift in retrieval or reasoning methodology. Reviewers should recognize the submission's practical merits while noting its incremental nature and the need for more nuanced comparisons to prior art.
