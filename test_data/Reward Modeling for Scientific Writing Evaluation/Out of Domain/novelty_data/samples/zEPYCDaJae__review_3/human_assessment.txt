This paper presents DataSEA, a fully automated framework that leverages large language models (LLMs) and search engines to enable dataset discovery, evaluation, and custom analysis, aiming to provide an integrated solution for web data management. While the system creatively combines existing tools and engineering results to automate aspects of data engineering, its novelty is limited by a heavy reliance on established technologies such as LLMs and web search engines. The approach primarily uses LLMs and Google Search in a manner akin to a web crawler, supplemented by some HTML processing techniques, rather than introducing fundamentally new algorithms or methodologies. The framework does not sufficiently discuss or evaluate the reliability of integrating these existing tools, nor does it demonstrate significant innovation beyond their combination. As such, the contribution is more characteristic of a technical engineering application than a research advance. To strengthen its claim to novelty, the paper would benefit from showcasing innovations that go beyond the straightforward integration of current tools and from more clearly articulating its unique contributions at the outset. Overall, while the system may serve as a useful engineering solution, its incremental nature and dependence on prior technologies limit its impact in terms of research novelty.
