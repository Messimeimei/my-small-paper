This paper investigates whether an Evaluation-Oriented Problem-Solving (EOP) framework can enhance the problem-solving capabilities of small language models, proposing a method that incorporates multiple trials (breadth-first) and a reasoning evaluator to improve reasoning and synthesis. While the authors highlight four main contributions—understanding failure modes, emphasizing the role of evaluators, introducing a novel evaluation-based prompting method, and addressing varying levels of evaluator expertise—these contributions largely reflect established consensus in current research. The novelty of the work is therefore limited, as similar evaluation-oriented approaches have already been explored in various learning tasks, and the effectiveness of such methods for improving reasoning in large models is supported by prior empirical evidence. The paper’s validation of EOP for small LLMs aligns with intuitive expectations and does not clearly establish a significant advance over existing studies. Furthermore, the lack of analysis regarding the specific characteristics of problem-solving tasks and how they are addressed within the EOP framework makes it difficult to discern the unique contribution of this work. Overall, the novelty of the approach is unclear, and the paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison with prior work and a clearer articulation of how its methods specifically advance the field.
