The submission "From Tokens to Words: On the Inner Lexicon of LLMs" situates itself at the intersection of probing internal representations, tokenization, and mechanistic interpretability, focusing on how LLMs internally compose subword tokens into word-level representations and proposing a practical, finetuning-free method for vocabulary expansion. While the work builds on and extends recent probing and mechanistic studies (notably Feucht et al., 2024; Geva et al., 2022), its main conceptual claims about intrinsic detokenization and an "inner lexicon" are incremental, as prior work has addressed similar questions using different methodologies. The submission's most substantive and novel contribution is its practical intervention: expanding the model's effective vocabulary at inference time by fusing multi-token word representations, which is not present in prior literature. Its systematic, layer-wise analysis of where and how detokenization occurs, and its comprehensive robustness evaluation to tokenization artifacts, represent meaningful methodological extensions. However, the claim of being the "first" to provide evidence for intrinsic detokenization is somewhat overstated, and reviewers should note that the conceptual advances are evolutionary rather than revolutionary, with the practical vocabulary expansion method being the clearest point of differentiation.
