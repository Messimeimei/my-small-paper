This paper investigates the vulnerability of machine unlearning methods to weight quantization, revealing that 4-bit quantization can restore "forgotten" information in models unlearned via techniques such as gradient ascent or negative preference optimization. To address this, the authors propose SURE, a weight saliency-based unlearning framework designed to maintain unlearning efficacy even under aggressive quantization. While the empirical observation that quantization can reverse unlearning is novel and provides valuable insight for the field, the methodological contribution of the SURE framework itself exhibits weak novelty. Similar saliency-based unlearning approaches, utilizing the same type of saliency map, have already been explored in prior work, notably by Fan et al. (ICLR 2024). As such, the main innovation lies in the identification of quantization as a threat to unlearning, rather than in the proposed SURE method, which does not substantially advance the methodological state of the art. The paper would benefit from more clearly distinguishing SURE from existing saliency-based unlearning techniques to better establish its unique contribution.
