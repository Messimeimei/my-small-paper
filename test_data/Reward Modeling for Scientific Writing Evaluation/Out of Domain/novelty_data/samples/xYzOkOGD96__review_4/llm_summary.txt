This submission addresses the task of grounded video caption generation, aiming to produce video captions where each mentioned object/entity is explicitly and temporally grounded with bounding boxes. Its main contributions are the explicit unification of captioning and temporally consistent object grounding, the introduction of a new manually annotated test set (GROC), and a large-scale automatic annotation pipeline (HowToGround). While the work claims to be the first to tackle this task, several recent efforts (e.g., ActivityNet-Entities, PG-Video-LLaVA, Grounded-VideoLLM) have addressed closely related problems, sometimes with finer granularity or broader scope. The architectural and methodological advances are largely incremental, adapting existing components for the specific task, and the novelty primarily lies in task formalization and dataset scale rather than conceptual innovation. The submission overstates its novelty and omits direct comparison to the most relevant recent works, so reviewers should carefully consider the true delta relative to the rapidly evolving field.
