This paper introduces RLSF, a reinforcement learning framework that integrates symbolic feedback into the post-training of large language models (LLMs). The core contribution lies in developing a system that generates polynomial-sized symbolic feedback—such as compiler outputs—and translates this feedback into per-token rewards, enabling more fine-grained learning signals during RL post-training. While the general idea that fine-grained feedback improves learning is well-established in the reinforcement learning community, with extensive prior work on reward shaping and process-based rewards, this insight is less common in the NLP domain, where RLHF typically relies on binary or scalar feedback. As such, simply demonstrating that fine-grained signals outperform coarse ones is not particularly novel. However, the paper distinguishes itself by proposing a method to extract and translate token-level feedback from symbolic sources, a direction that, to the reviewer's knowledge, has not been previously explored. This approach offers an important message for researchers working on RL post-training: it highlights the potential of leveraging rewards from symbolic systems, which is a notable strength. Nonetheless, the novelty is primarily in the mechanism for converting symbolic feedback into actionable, token-level rewards, rather than in the broader concept of using fine-grained feedback itself.
