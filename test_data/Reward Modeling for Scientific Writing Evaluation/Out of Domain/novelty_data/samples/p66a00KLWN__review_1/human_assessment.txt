This paper introduces NEXT-Mol, a model for 3D molecule generation that sequentially combines a pre-trained 1D language model (based on SELFIES) with a 3D diffusion model to generate chemically valid molecular structures and predict their 3D conformations. While the approach leverages transfer learning between 1D and 3D representations and incorporates enhancements in model scaling and architecture, the novelty from a machine learning perspective is limited. The core methodology primarily involves integrating existing components—specifically, LLaMA-based language models and established diffusion models—without introducing fundamentally new techniques. The process of transferring 1D information to 3D, which is highlighted as a key aspect of the approach, offers minimal innovation and limited added value for the conformer generation task, as the necessary molecular information is already present in the graph representation. Overall, the contribution is largely an application and combination of known methods rather than a significant advancement in machine learning methodology, and the paper would benefit from more clearly articulating how its integration of 1D and 3D models provides unique advantages over prior work.
