NExT-Mol introduces a novel approach by explicitly transferring knowledge from a large-scale 1D molecular language model (SELFIES-based MoLlama) to a 3D diffusion model for conformer generation, using a cross-modal projector—a combination not previously demonstrated in the literature. While prior works have explored 1D language modeling and 3D diffusion separately or in joint 2D/3D settings, NExT-Mol is the first to leverage large-scale 1D pretraining for 3D molecular generation, resulting in incremental but real improvements on standard 3D benchmarks (FCD, COV-R). The claimed architectural innovations (e.g., relational multi-head self-attention) may represent refinements rather than conceptual leaps, and some terminology (e.g., "cross-modal projector") repackages established techniques. The authors' claims of 100% validity and transformative novelty are somewhat overstated, as 100% validity is a property of SELFIES and prior models have also scaled up or improved validity. Overall, the main contribution is a substantive integration of established paradigms (large-scale 1D LMs and 3D diffusion) via transfer learning, with advances that are meaningful but largely incremental in the context of a rapidly evolving field.
