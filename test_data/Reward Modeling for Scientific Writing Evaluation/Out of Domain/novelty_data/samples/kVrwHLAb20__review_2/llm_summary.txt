The submission, *Ward*, addresses the emerging problem of RAG Dataset Inference (RAG-DI)—detecting unauthorized use of proprietary data in Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems—by adapting LLM watermarking techniques and providing formal statistical guarantees. Its main contributions are the formalization of RAG-DI as a distinct problem, the introduction of the Frad dataset for realistic evaluation, and empirical evidence that *Ward* outperforms membership inference attacks and simple baselines in black-box and adversarial settings. While the work is positioned as the first in this space, prior research on dataset usage detection (e.g., Domain Watermark, EnTruth) is highly relevant and could, in principle, be adapted to RAG, making the "first" claim somewhat overstated. The technical novelty primarily lies in the RAG-specific formalization and evaluation, rather than in the underlying watermarking mechanisms, which are extensions of existing methods. The submission would benefit from a more thorough comparison to related dataset watermarking approaches in the RAG context and from addressing recent work on black-box watermark attacks, but overall represents a timely and relevant extension in the field.
