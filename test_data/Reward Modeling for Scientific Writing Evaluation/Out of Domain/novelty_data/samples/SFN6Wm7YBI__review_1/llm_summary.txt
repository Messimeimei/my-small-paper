LegoScale is presented as a unified, PyTorch-native, modular system for distributed LLM pre-training, aiming to integrate 3D/4D parallelism, elastic scaling, and production-grade features in a single package. Its main contribution lies in the breadth and integration of features—combining parallelism strategies, workflow tools, and hardware-aware optimizations—rather than introducing fundamentally new algorithms or parallelism techniques. While the authors claim significant novelty and "firsts," these are somewhat overstated, as recent frameworks like Merak and FSDP also offer modular, PyTorch-native, and composable distributed training with similar goals. The empirical improvements in speed and scalability are real but may stem from implementation and integration quality rather than conceptual advances. Overall, the submission's primary value is in system-level integration and user experience, and reviewers should calibrate expectations accordingly, recognizing that the technical advances are incremental within a rapidly evolving field.
