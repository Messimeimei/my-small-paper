This submission presents a comprehensive empirical benchmarking study of large language model (LLM) deployment on edge devices, systematically varying model size, fine-tuning method, data amount, compression, and fine-tuning time. Its main contribution is the integration and synthesis of multiple axes—parameter-efficient fine-tuning, model compression, and on-device customization—into a single, broad set of practical guidelines for practitioners. While the authors claim to be the first to address the "full range" of constraints, several recent works (e.g., Pu et al., SLoRA, MobileLLM) have conducted similar systematic studies and provided guidelines, though often with a narrower or different focus. The novelty here is incremental and lies in the breadth and integration of empirical benchmarking, rather than in new methods or fundamentally new empirical approaches. Reviewers should recognize the practical value of the comprehensive synthesis, but calibrate expectations regarding conceptual novelty, as the work represents a routine extension in a rapidly evolving, empirically driven field.
