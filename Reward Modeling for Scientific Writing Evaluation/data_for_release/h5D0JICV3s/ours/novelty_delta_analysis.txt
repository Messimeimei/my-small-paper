# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating PIT-QMM in the Research Landscape:**

- **Problem Focus:** PIT-QMM addresses *no-reference point cloud quality assessment* (NR-PCQA), specifically without access to pristine references, and aims to overcome data scarcity and generalizability issues.
- **Methodological Cluster:** PIT-QMM is most closely aligned with the *multimodal and large multimodal models* cluster, which includes MM-PCQA, LMM-PCQA, and MMF-PCQM. These works combine 3D point cloud data with 2D projections and, in some cases, text.
- **Key Related Works:**
  - **LMM-PCQA (Zhang et al., 2024):** Uses large multimodal models (LMMs) with 2D projections and text, supplementing with point cloud statistics.
  - **MM-PCQA (Zhang et al., 2022c):** Fuses 3D and 2D features for NR-PCQA.
  - **PQA-Net (Liu et al., 2021):** Uses multi-view 2D projections for feature extraction.
  - **CoPA (Shan et al., 2024):** Employs contrastive learning with multi-view projections.
- **Distinctive Positioning:** PIT-QMM claims to be the first *end-to-end* point-image-text LMM for PCQA, with a novel dataset construction strategy integrating all three modalities.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The field has seen a progression from 2D/3D fusion (MM-PCQA) to leveraging large pre-trained multimodal models (LMM-PCQA), with increasing sophistication in fusion and representation.
- PIT-QMM’s main novelty is the *end-to-end* integration of point, image, and text modalities, whereas prior works often use sequential or modular fusion (e.g., LMM-PCQA supplements 2D LMMs with point cloud statistics, not full 3D features).
- The submission’s dataset construction strategy (combining text, 2D, and 3D) is an incremental but logical extension of prior multimodal approaches.

**Evaluation Approaches:**
- PIT-QMM uses standard PCQA benchmarks (WPC, LS-PCQA, SJTU-PCQA) and metrics (MOS, PSNR, SSIM), consistent with the field.
- The use of ablation studies for dataset construction is in line with best practices.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**

- **LMM-PCQA:** Described as supplementing 2D LMM predictions with point cloud statistics, and as building on Q-Align by fine-tuning on projections and adding a regressor for structural features.
  - *Independent check:* LMM-PCQA does not perform end-to-end multimodal fusion; it uses 2D LMMs and adds point cloud features post-hoc.
- **PQA-Net:** Characterized as extracting multi-view features from cubic projections for joint quality/distortion prediction.
  - *Accurate:* PQA-Net is a multi-view projection-based method, not multimodal in the sense of integrating text or 3D features directly.
- **CoPA:** Cited as a contrastive learning approach using image projections and patch mixing.
  - *Accurate:* CoPA is a contrastive, projection-based method, not a full multimodal model.
- **3DTA:** Not compared due to differing evaluation protocols.
  - *Accurate but incomplete:* 3DTA is a transformer-based 3D model; the lack of comparison is justified by protocol, but the technical relationship is not discussed.
- **GPA-Net, IT-PCQA, ResSCNN, MM-PCQA:** Listed as NR-PCQA baselines, with no detailed technical comparison.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The authors consistently position PIT-QMM as the *first* end-to-end point-image-text LMM, emphasizing the lack of prior work integrating all three modalities in a single model.
- There is a tendency to understate the sophistication of LMM-PCQA and MM-PCQA by focusing on their lack of end-to-end fusion, rather than acknowledging their advanced fusion strategies or use of large pre-trained models.
- The characterization of prior work is generally accurate but sometimes omits nuanced technical similarities (e.g., LMM-PCQA’s use of text and 2D, albeit not end-to-end).

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contribution 1: First end-to-end point-image-text LMM for PCQA**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** LMM-PCQA (Zhang et al., 2024), MM-PCQA (Zhang et al., 2022c)
- **Claimed Difference:** PIT-QMM is *end-to-end* and fuses all three modalities (point, image, text) in a single model, whereas LMM-PCQA uses 2D LMMs with post-hoc point cloud statistics.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - LMM-PCQA does not perform joint end-to-end training across all modalities; it fine-tunes a 2D LMM and adds point cloud features via a regressor.
  - MM-PCQA fuses 3D and 2D, but not text.
  - PIT-QMM’s integration of text, 2D, and 3D in a single end-to-end model is a substantive technical difference, though it builds directly on the multimodal fusion paradigm.
  - The “first” claim is accurate in the sense of *joint* end-to-end fusion, but the conceptual leap is incremental rather than radical.

**Claimed Contribution 2: Novel multimodal dataset construction strategy**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** MM-PCQA, LMM-PCQA, PQA-Net (for multi-view projections)
- **Claimed Difference:** PIT-QMM combines text, 2D projections, and 3D views in dataset construction.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - MM-PCQA and PQA-Net use 2D projections and 3D data, but not text.
  - LMM-PCQA uses text and 2D projections, but not 3D features in dataset construction.
  - PIT-QMM’s dataset construction is a logical extension, adding text to the 2D+3D paradigm.
  - The novelty is incremental; the main technical challenge is in aligning and fusing the modalities, which is not deeply detailed in the provided content.

**Claimed Contribution 3: Superior performance with fewer training iterations**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** LMM-PCQA, MM-PCQA, CoPA, GPA-Net
- **Claimed Difference:** PIT-QMM outperforms SOTA with less training.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - Performance gains may be due to model scale, data augmentation, or training tricks, not necessarily conceptual advances.
  - The claim is empirical and should be interpreted in light of implementation details and fair comparison (e.g., same data, compute, and evaluation protocols).

**Claimed Contribution 4: Fully differentiable model usable as a training loss**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** MPED, JND-based metrics, differentiable IQA models
- **Claimed Difference:** PIT-QMM can be used as a loss in quality-sensitive applications.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - Differentiability is a desirable property, but not unique; several recent PCQA models and metrics are differentiable.
  - The claim is accurate but not a major conceptual advance.

- **Recent Surveys:** See “A Survey on Point Cloud Quality Assessment” (2023), “Deep Learning for 3D Point Cloud Quality Assessment: A Review” (2023).
- **Trends:** Shift from handcrafted to deep/multimodal models, increasing use of large pre-trained models, and focus on perceptual alignment.
- **Incremental Advances:** Many recent papers extend prior work by adding modalities, improving fusion, or leveraging larger models/datasets.
- **Reviewer Calibration:** Novelty is often incremental; “first” claims are common but usually represent logical next steps rather than paradigm shifts.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Overstated Novelty:** The “first end-to-end point-image-text LMM” claim is technically accurate but builds directly on LMM-PCQA and MM-PCQA; the conceptual advance is incremental.
- **Empirical Improvements:** Performance gains may be due to model scale, data, or training, not just architectural innovation.
- **Terminology Differences:** “End-to-end” and “multimodal” are used variably; prior works may have similar fusion but different training regimes.
- **Routine Extensions:** The dataset construction strategy (adding text to 2D+3D) is a routine extension of existing multimodal approaches.
- **Comparisons:** Lack of direct comparison with some recent works (e.g., “Zoom to Perceive Better,” “Plain-PCQA”) may overstate the delta.
