DB-GPT-Hub proposes an open-source benchmark suite and evaluation pipeline for fine-tuning large language models (LLMs) on text-to-SQL tasks, aiming to address gaps in reproducibility, extensibility, and transparency. While the submission emphasizes its focus on fine-tuning (as opposed to prompt-based evaluation), several recent frameworks—such as CodeS, BIRD, and TrustSQL—already support fine-tuning and offer similar modular, extensible, and open-source benchmarking capabilities. The claimed novelty of DB-GPT-Hub appears to be incremental, potentially residing in implementation details or documentation quality, but this is not substantiated by direct technical comparisons with prior work. The authors overstate the lack of fine-tuning benchmarks and omit discussion of several highly relevant recent contributions, which weakens the case for uniqueness. Reviewers should consider the submission as a potentially useful engineering advance, but should request explicit side-by-side comparisons with existing frameworks to clarify its true contribution.
