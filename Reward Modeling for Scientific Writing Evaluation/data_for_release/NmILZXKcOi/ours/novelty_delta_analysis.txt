# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Landscape:**

- **Submission Focus:**  
  DB-GPT-Hub introduces an open benchmark suite and evaluation pipeline for LLM-empowered text-to-SQL, with a particular emphasis on *fine-tuning* medium to large open LLMs, as opposed to the more commonly explored prompt-based (in-context learning, ICL) approaches.

- **Closest Prior Works:**  
  - **Benchmarking and Evaluation Frameworks:**  
    - *Text-to-SQL Empowered by Large Language Models: A Benchmark Evaluation* (Gao et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024)
    - *Benchmarking the Text-to-SQL Capability of Large Language Models: A Comprehensive Evaluation*
    - *SQL-PaLM: Improved Large Language Model Adaptation for Text-to-SQL* (Li et al., 2023a; Sun et al., 2023)
    - *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL Capabilities of Large Language Models: A Study on Prompt Design Strategies* (Nan et al., 2023)
    - *A comprehensive evaluation of ChatGPT's zero-shot Text-to-SQL capability* (Liu et al., 2023; Trummer, 2022)
    - *CodeS: Towards Building Open-source Language Models for Text-to-SQL* (not cited)
    - *FinSQL: Model-Agnostic LLMs-based Text-to-SQL Framework for Financial Analysis* (not cited)
    - *TrustSQL: Benchmarking Text-to-SQL Reliability with Penalty-Based Scoring* (not cited)
    - *Benchmarking and Improving Text-to-SQL Generation under Ambiguity* (not cited)
    - *BIRD* (large-scale, realistic benchmark, not cited)

- **Methodological Clusters:**  
  DB-GPT-Hub is most closely aligned with *Cluster A: Benchmarking and Evaluation Frameworks* and *Cluster B: LLM Model Development and Adaptation* (see landscape). It is differentiated by its explicit focus on *fine-tuning* open LLMs for text-to-SQL, whereas many prior benchmarks and evaluation suites have focused on prompt-based or zero/few-shot settings.

- **Problem Space:**  
  The submission addresses the lack of open, standardized, and extensible benchmarks for *fine-tuning* LLMs on text-to-SQL, as well as the need for reproducible, transparent, and comprehensive evaluation pipelines. This is a recognized gap, as most prior work has focused on prompt-based evaluation or closed-source models.

- **Evaluation Approaches:**  
  The use of Spider as a core dataset is standard; the introduction of a new DB-GPT-Hub dataset is a potential extension, but the degree of novelty depends on its scale, realism, and adoption compared to BIRD, BULL, and other recent datasets.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  While the submission claims to be the "first" open benchmark suite for fine-tuning LLMs on text-to-SQL, several recent works (e.g., CodeS, SQL-PaLM, BIRD, TrustSQL) have also released open-source frameworks, datasets, or pipelines for benchmarking LLMs (including fine-tuned models). The main delta appears to be the *explicit modularity, extensibility, and focus on fine-tuning* in the open-source context, but the degree of differentiation from these prior works requires careful scrutiny.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**

- **Prompt-Based vs. Fine-Tuning:**  
  Authors consistently characterize prior work (e.g., Gao et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024) as "mainly highlight[ing] the ICL abilities of LLMs and their accuracy in generating SQL queries," implying a gap in fine-tuning-focused benchmarking.

- **Positioning of Own Contribution:**  
  The submission positions DB-GPT-Hub as filling the gap for *fine-tuning* evaluation, suggesting that prior benchmarks are insufficient for this purpose.

- **Citation Contexts:**
  - *Text-to-SQL Empowered by Large Language Models: A Benchmark Evaluation* is cited as focusing on ICL, not fine-tuning.
  - *SQL-PaLM* is cited as an example of fine-tuning, but not as a benchmark suite.
  - *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL Capabilities...* is cited for prompt design, not benchmarking.
  - *A comprehensive evaluation of ChatGPT's zero-shot Text-to-SQL capability* is cited as an example of prompt-based evaluation.

**Accuracy and Balance:**

- The characterization of Gao et al. (2023) and Zhang et al. (2024) as focusing on ICL is generally accurate, but these works also include some fine-tuning baselines and discuss the challenges of benchmarking fine-tuned models.
- The submission does not cite or discuss several recent open-source benchmarking efforts (e.g., CodeS, TrustSQL, BIRD), which also address fine-tuning and open evaluation.
- The authors' rhetoric tends to overstate the lack of fine-tuning benchmarks, underplaying the incremental progress made by recent works.

**Discrepancies:**

- The claim that prior work "mainly" focuses on ICL is somewhat overstated; while ICL is dominant, fine-tuning is increasingly explored, and some recent benchmarks do support it.
- The submission does not acknowledge the modularity and extensibility of other recent frameworks (e.g., BIRD, TrustSQL, CodeS), which may offer similar capabilities.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contribution 1:**  
*DB-GPT-Hub is the first open benchmark suite focusing on large-scale fine-tuning of LLMs for text-to-SQL tasks.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *CodeS* (not cited): Open-source LLMs and benchmarking for text-to-SQL, including fine-tuning.
  - *BIRD* (not cited): Large-scale, realistic benchmark with support for fine-tuning and prompt-based evaluation.
  - *SQL-PaLM*: Focuses on fine-tuning LLMs for text-to-SQL, but not as a general benchmark suite.
  - *TrustSQL*: Open-source benchmarking with reliability metrics, supports fine-tuning.

- **Critical Examination:**  
  - The "first" claim is not fully substantiated, as CodeS, BIRD, and TrustSQL have released open-source benchmarks and pipelines supporting fine-tuning.
  - The main difference may be in the *degree* of modularity, extensibility, and documentation, but this is not clearly established in the submission.
  - The submission does not provide a direct, side-by-side comparison with these frameworks.

- **Substantive vs. Superficial Differences:**  
  - If DB-GPT-Hub offers unique features (e.g., plug-and-play modules, broader LLM/model support, easier extensibility), these should be explicitly detailed and compared.

*Standardized, comprehensive, and extensible evaluation pipeline and codebase for benchmarking LLMs on text-to-SQL.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**

- **Critical Examination:**  
  - Several recent frameworks also claim extensibility and comprehensive evaluation.
  - The submission does not provide a technical comparison (e.g., supported models, datasets, evaluation metrics, extensibility mechanisms) to substantiate superiority or uniqueness.

**Claimed Contribution 3:**  
*Enables transparent, reproducible, and consistent comparison of LLM models after fine-tuning for text-to-SQL.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *TrustSQL* (focus on reliability and reproducibility), *BIRD*, *CodeS*.

  - Transparency and reproducibility are common goals in recent benchmarks.

**Claimed Contribution 4:**  
*Supports easy extension to new datasets, models, modules, and evaluation programs, as well as compatibility with prompting techniques.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *BIRD*, *TrustSQL*, *CodeS*.

- **Critical Examination:**  
  - Extensibility is a standard feature in modern open-source benchmarks.
  - The submission does not provide evidence of unique extensibility (e.g., plugin architecture, API design) compared to prior work.

**General Observations:**

- Many claimed differences may be *implementation-level* (e.g., code modularity, documentation) rather than *conceptual*.
- The submission does not address or compare with several highly relevant recent works, which may offer similar or overlapping features.
