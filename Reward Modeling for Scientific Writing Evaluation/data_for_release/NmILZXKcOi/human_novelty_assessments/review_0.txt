This paper presents a unified training and evaluation pipeline for assessing the fine-tuning capabilities of partial open-source language models on text-to-SQL tasks, specifically across the SPIDER and BIRD benchmarks. The authors claim that there is a lack of benchmarks for evaluating fine-tuning in this domain and position their work as addressing this gap by curating a codebase and summarizing experimental insights. However, the novelty of the contribution is limited, as both SPIDER and BIRD already provide training sets and have been widely used for fine-tuning and benchmarking in prior work, including studies on training-based approaches (e.g., CodeS, granite, SuperSQL) and in-context learning methods. The paper does not introduce new datasets, metrics, or evaluation methodologies, making it difficult to identify a clear advance over existing resources. Instead, the main contribution appears to be the consolidation of existing benchmarks into a single codebase on GitHub, without substantive innovation in benchmarking design or experimental analysis. As such, the significance of this work is questionable in the context of established benchmarks, and the authors need to more clearly articulate what specific gap their research fills and how it advances the field beyond current capabilities. To establish a stronger academic contribution, the paper would benefit from more in-depth analysis, user studies, or new taxonomies that go beyond merely reproducing and aggregating existing resources.
