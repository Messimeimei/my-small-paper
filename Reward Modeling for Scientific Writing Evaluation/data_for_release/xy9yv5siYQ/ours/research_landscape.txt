# 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, several dominant methodological paradigms emerge for 3D scene and dynamic object reconstruction from monocular video:

- **3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) and Variants:**  
  - Used for explicit, efficient, and real-time scene representation and rendering (Submission, MoSca, Shape of Motion, Deformable 3D Gaussians, COLMAP-Free 3DGS, InstantSplat, 3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Rendering).
- **Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) and Dynamic Extensions:**  
  - Implicit scene representation, often extended for dynamic scenes and joint pose optimization (GNeRF, RoDynRF, Non-Rigid NeRF, TiNeuVox, TR-NeRF).
- **Hybrid/Layered Representations:**  
  - Combining explicit and implicit models, or layered neural fields for multi-object or panoptic reconstruction (SelfRecon, MultiPly, PanoRecon).
- **Deformation Fields and Motion Bases:**  
  - Modeling dynamic scene changes via deformation fields, motion scaffolds, or SE(3) motion bases (Submission, MoSca, Shape of Motion, Deformable 3D Gaussians, Non-Rigid NeRF, HeadRecon).
- **Camera Pose Estimation without External Tools:**  
  - Joint or self-supervised optimization of camera poses, often without reliance on SfM/COLMAP (Submission, MoSca, COLMAP-Free 3DGS, GNeRF, RoDynRF, InstantSplat).
- **Feature Volume Fusion and Voxel-based Methods:**  
  - Sparse/dense feature volume fusion, often with attention or recurrent modules for real-time and memory-efficient reconstruction (NeuralRecon, Incremental Dense Reconstruction, MuSR, Flora).
- **Physically-Inspired Regularization:**  
  - ARAP, temporal smoothness, and other regularizers to improve geometric and temporal consistency (MoSca, Deformable 3D Gaussians, Submission).

### Method Clusters

- **Cluster 1: Explicit 3D Gaussian Splatting Approaches**
  - Submission, MoSca, Shape of Motion, Deformable 3D Gaussians, COLMAP-Free 3DGS, InstantSplat, 3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Rendering
  - Focus: Explicit, efficient, and often real-time scene representation; dynamic scene modeling via deformation fields or motion scaffolds; often joint pose optimization.
- **Cluster 2: Implicit Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) and Dynamic Extensions**
  - GNeRF, RoDynRF, Non-Rigid NeRF, TiNeuVox, TR-NeRF
  - Focus: Implicit scene representation, dynamic scene modeling, joint pose and radiance field optimization, often with regularization for robustness.
- **Cluster 3: Hybrid/Layered and Panoptic Representations**
  - SelfRecon, MultiPly, PanoRecon
  - Focus: Combining explicit and implicit representations, layered neural fields for multi-object or panoptic scene understanding, instance segmentation.
- **Cluster 4: Feature Volume Fusion and Voxel-based Methods**
  - NeuralRecon, Incremental Dense Reconstruction, MuSR, Flora
  - Focus: Real-time, memory-efficient, and high-resolution reconstruction using feature volume fusion, attention, and recurrent modules.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Unified Representations:**  
  - Increasing trend toward unified models that handle both static and dynamic regions (Submission, MoSca, RoDynRF).
- **Pose-Free and Self-Supervised Approaches:**  
  - Growing focus on eliminating reliance on external pose estimation (COLMAP), using joint or self-supervised optimization (Submission, MoSca, COLMAP-Free 3DGS, GNeRF, RoDynRF, InstantSplat).
- **Deformation Modeling:**  
  - Deformation fields, motion scaffolds, and motion bases are central to dynamic scene modeling, with various regularization strategies for temporal and geometric consistency.
- **Efficiency and Real-Time Performance:**  
  - Explicit representations (3DGS, voxel-based) and efficient optimization (e.g., TiNeuVox, Flora) are prioritized for real-time applications.
- **Hybridization:**  
  - Combining explicit and implicit methods to leverage strengths of both (SelfRecon, MultiPly).

# 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Dynamic 3D Scene Reconstruction from Monocular Video:**  
  - Central to most papers, with focus on both static and dynamic elements, often under unknown camera poses (Submission, MoSca, Shape of Motion, Deformable 3D Gaussians, RoDynRF, Non-Rigid NeRF).
- **Camera Pose Estimation without External Tools:**  
  - Joint or self-supervised estimation of camera poses, especially in dynamic or challenging scenes (Submission, MoSca, COLMAP-Free 3DGS, GNeRF, RoDynRF, InstantSplat).
- **Novel View Synthesis (NVS):**  
  - High-quality, often real-time, novel view synthesis for both static and dynamic scenes (Submission, MoSca, Shape of Motion, Deformable 3D Gaussians, 3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Rendering, InstantSplat).
- **Panoptic and Multi-Object Reconstruction:**  
  - Holistic scene understanding, including semantic and instance segmentation (PanoRecon, MultiPly).
- **Human/Avatar Reconstruction:**  
  - High-fidelity, temporally coherent human or head reconstruction from monocular video (SelfRecon, Neural Reconstruction of Relightable Human Model, HeadRecon, TR-NeRF).
- **Efficiency and Scalability:**  
  - Real-time performance, memory efficiency, and scalability to large or complex scenes (Flora, MuSR, TiNeuVox, NeuralRecon).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Dynamic Scene Reconstruction:**  
  - Submission, MoSca, Shape of Motion, Deformable 3D Gaussians, RoDynRF, Non-Rigid NeRF all address dynamic scene reconstruction, but differ in representation (explicit 3DGS vs. implicit NeRF), pose estimation strategy, and regularization.
- **Pose-Free Reconstruction:**  
  - Submission, MoSca, COLMAP-Free 3DGS, GNeRF, RoDynRF, InstantSplat all tackle pose-free reconstruction, but with different optimization frameworks (bundle adjustment, adversarial training, sequential frame processing, etc.).
- **Human Reconstruction:**  
  - SelfRecon, Neural Reconstruction of Relightable Human Model, HeadRecon, TR-NeRF focus on human/face modeling, using hybrid, implicit, or deformation-based methods, often with canonical space mapping and pose-driven deformation.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **Unified Modeling:**  
  - Increasingly, methods aim to jointly model static/dynamic regions, camera motion, and object deformation in a single framework (Submission, RoDynRF).
- **Robustness to Challenging Scenarios:**  
  - Emphasis on robustness to dynamic objects, motion blur, poor textures, and occlusions (Submission, MoSca, RoDynRF, GNeRF, MultiPly).
- **Temporal and Geometric Consistency:**  
  - Regularization and architectural choices to ensure temporal smoothness and geometric consistency (Deformable 3D Gaussians, Submission, MoSca).

# 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **Dynamic Scene Benchmarks:**  
  - DyCheck, NVIDIA DynamicNeRF, MPI Sintel, Tanks and Temples, CO3D, ScanNet, 7-Scenes, Replica, Mip-NeRF360, Google Scanned Objects.
- **Human/Avatar Datasets:**  
  - Self-captured monocular videos, public multi-person datasets, synthetic and real dynamic human scenes.
- **Real-World and Synthetic Videos:**  
  - Many methods test on both synthetic and real-world monocular videos, often with challenging dynamics or occlusions.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Rendering Quality:**  
  - PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS are standard for novel view synthesis and rendering quality.
- **Camera Pose Estimation Accuracy:**  
  - Used in pose-free and joint optimization methods.
- **Tracking Accuracy:**  
  - 2D/3D tracking accuracy for dynamic scene and human reconstruction.
- **Segmentation and Panoptic Metrics:**  
  - For panoptic and multi-object reconstruction (PanoRecon, MultiPly).
- **Efficiency Metrics:**  
  - Training/rendering time, memory/storage cost, real-time performance (fps).
- **Completeness and Resolution:**  
  - For dense reconstruction methods (MuSR, Incremental Dense Reconstruction).

### Patterns and Comparisons

- **Standardization:**  
  - PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS are nearly universal for image-based evaluation.
- **Emphasis on Real-Time and Efficiency:**  
  - Many recent works report fps, training time, and memory usage as key metrics.
- **Pose and Tracking Metrics:**  
  - Increasingly important for dynamic and pose-free methods.
- **Qualitative and Quantitative:**  
  - Both types of results are often reported, especially for new or challenging tasks.

- **Papers:** SelfRecon, MultiPly, PanoRecon
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Hybrid explicit-implicit or layered neural representations.

- **Papers:** NeuralRecon, Incremental Dense Reconstruction, MuSR, Flora

- **Papers:** SelfRecon, Neural Reconstruction of Relightable Human Model, HeadRecon, TR-NeRF, MultiPly
- **Characteristics:**

- **Characteristics:**

- **Clusters 1, 2 & 3:**

- **Clusters 4 & 1/2:**  
  - Feature volume fusion and voxel-based methods often complement explicit/implicit representations for efficiency and scalability.

# 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Implicit to Explicit Representations:**  
  - Early works focused on implicit NeRF-based methods; recent trends favor explicit 3D Gaussian Splatting for efficiency and real-time performance.
- **Pose-Free and Self-Supervised Learning:**  
  - Shift from reliance on external pose estimation (SfM/COLMAP) to joint/self-supervised pose and scene optimization.
- **Unified and Hybrid Models:**  
  - Evolution toward unified representations that jointly model static/dynamic regions, camera motion, and object deformation.
- **Deformation Modeling:**  
  - Increasing sophistication in modeling dynamic scenes, from simple deformation fields to motion scaffolds and SE(3) motion bases.
- **Efficiency and Scalability:**  
  - Emphasis on real-time, memory-efficient, and scalable methods, with explicit representations and feature volume fusion.
- **Panoptic and Multi-Object Understanding:**  
  - Expansion from single-object to panoptic and multi-object scene understanding, with layered and instance-aware representations.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **3D Gaussian Splatting:**  
  - Extended from static to dynamic scenes, with deformation fields, motion scaffolds, and unified static/dynamic modeling.
- **NeRF:**  
  - Extended to dynamic scenes, pose-free operation, and hybrid adversarial optimization (GNeRF).
- **Feature Volume Fusion:**  
  - Enhanced with attention, recurrent modules, and sparsity for efficiency and high resolution.
- **Hybrid Representations:**  
  - Combining explicit and implicit models for improved fidelity and coherence (SelfRecon, MultiPly).

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Explicit vs. Implicit Representations:**  
  - Explicit (3DGS) methods offer efficiency and real-time performance; implicit (NeRF) methods offer flexibility and high fidelity.
- **Pose-Free Optimization:**  
  - Competing strategies for pose estimation: bundle adjustment, adversarial training, sequential frame processing, etc.
- **Regularization Strategies:**  
  - Competing regularization methods for temporal/geometric consistency (ARAP, AST, multi-dimensional loss).
- **Hybridization:**  
  - Complementary approaches that combine strengths of explicit and implicit methods.

# SUMMARY

The research landscape is rapidly evolving toward unified, efficient, and robust dynamic 3D scene reconstruction from monocular video, with a strong trend toward explicit 3D Gaussian Splatting and hybrid representations. Pose-free and self-supervised optimization, sophisticated deformation modeling, and real-time performance are key drivers. The field is also expanding into panoptic, multi-object, and human-centric reconstruction, with increasing emphasis on scalability, efficiency, and robustness to challenging real-world scenarios.
