The submission presents an incremental advance in dynamic, pose-free 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) for monocular video-based scene reconstruction, introducing a Hexplane encoder and SE(3)-based pose initialization as its main technical novelties. While the authors claim to be the first to achieve unified, high-quality dynamic 3DGS reconstruction without known camera poses, similar capabilities have already been demonstrated in recent works such as MoSca and Shape of Motion, with differences primarily in architectural and optimization details. The claimed contributions—unified static/dynamic modeling, pose-free operation, and geometric regularization—are routine in the field, with the main delta being the specific implementation of the Hexplane encoder and pose estimation module. The authors’ positioning of prior work sometimes exaggerates their own novelty and underrepresents the unification and capabilities present in related methods. Overall, the work is a solid, well-executed technical improvement within a rapidly evolving area, but reviewers should recognize that its advances are incremental rather than fundamentally new.
