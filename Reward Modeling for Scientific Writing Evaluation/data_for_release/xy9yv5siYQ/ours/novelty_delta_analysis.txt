# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission, "Mono-DynGS," is positioned within the *explicit 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS)* cluster, specifically targeting *dynamic 3D scene reconstruction from monocular videos without known camera poses*.
- The most closely related prior works are:
  - **MoSca (Lei et al., 2024):** Dynamic Gaussian fusion from casual videos, pose-free, uses 3DGS, and models deformation fields.
  - **Shape of Motion (Wang et al., 2024):** 4D reconstruction from a single video, dynamic Gaussians, Fourier-based motion modeling.
  - **COLMAP-Free 3DGS (Fu et al., 2024):** 3DGS with self-supervised camera pose estimation, but focused on static scenes.
  - **RoDynRF (Liu et al., 2023):** Dynamic NeRF-based, pose-free, with separate static/dynamic modeling.
  - **3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Rendering (Kerbl et al., 2023):** Foundational 3DGS method for static scenes.
- The submission’s *problem space* is at the intersection of:
  - Dynamic scene reconstruction (with both static and dynamic regions)
  - Monocular video input
  - No known camera poses (pose-free)
  - Unified representation for static/dynamic regions
  - Efficient, high-quality novel view synthesis

**Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission is most closely aligned with *Cluster 1: Explicit 3D Gaussian Splatting for Dynamic Scenes*, but also draws from *Cluster 2: Implicit NeRF-based dynamic scene methods* in its use of deformation fields and regularization.
- The *Hexplane-based encoder* for unified static/dynamic modeling is a distinguishing architectural choice, but similar goals (unified modeling) are present in RoDynRF and MoSca.
- The *camera initialization module* for pose estimation is a variant of recent pose-free 3DGS approaches (e.g., COLMAP-Free 3DGS, MoSca).

**Evaluation Approaches:**
- The submission uses standard dynamic scene benchmarks (DyCheck, NVIDIA DynamicNeRF, MPI Sintel) and metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, pose accuracy), consistent with recent work.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is a *direct extension* of recent pose-free, dynamic 3DGS methods, with incremental architectural and optimization innovations (Hexplane encoder, specific pose initialization, regularization).
- The *unified static/dynamic modeling* and *pose-free operation* are not unique to this work, but the specific combination and implementation details (Hexplane, SE(3) pose estimation with 3DGS) may be novel in aggregate.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors consistently group prior work into "static 3DGS," "dynamic 3DGS," and "pose-free dynamic NeRF/3DGS" categories.
- They claim prior dynamic 3DGS methods (MoSca, Shape of Motion) either use less unified deformation fields or less efficient pose estimation.
- They characterize RoDynRF as lacking unified static/dynamic modeling and as using less effective deformation fields.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- **MoSca (Lei et al., 2024):**  
  - Authors state MoSca uses "DQB interpolation on some key points as its deformable field" and a "cluster-based deformable field" for dynamic foregrounds and pose optimization.
  - *Independent check:* MoSca does use a cluster-based deformable field and pose-free optimization, but also supports unified modeling to some extent. The distinction is in the *specific* deformation field and optimization, not the overall capability.
- **Shape of Motion (Wang et al., 2024):**  
  - Authors say it "imposes Fourier transformation to fit the trajectory of dynamic Gaussians."
  - *Accurate*, but the difference is in the motion modeling (Fourier vs. Hexplane/SE(3)), not in the overall ability to model dynamic scenes.
- **COLMAP-Free 3DGS (Fu et al., 2024):**  
  - Cited as showing "how to incorporate 3DGS with camera pose estimation," but focused on static scenes.
  - *Accurate*, but the submission extends this to dynamic scenes.
- **RoDynRF (Liu et al., 2023):**  
  - Authors claim RoDynRF "fails to generate fine details" and "employs two distinct TensorRFs...with no information sharing."
  - *Accurate* in that RoDynRF uses separate models, but the lack of information sharing is a design choice, not a fundamental limitation.
- **3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Rendering (Kerbl et al., 2023):**  
  - Cited as the base 3DGS method; authors claim their work extends it to dynamic, pose-free settings.
  - *Accurate*.

**Discrepancies:**
- Authors sometimes *overstate* the lack of unified modeling in prior work (e.g., MoSca, RoDynRF), when in fact these works do address unified modeling, albeit with different architectures.
- The *novelty* of the Hexplane encoder and SE(3)-based pose initialization is presented as a major advance, but similar ideas (multi-plane encoders, SE(3) motion bases) exist in the literature (e.g., Deformable 3D Gaussians, Shape of Motion).

**Rhetoric:**
- The language sometimes *overemphasizes* the limitations of prior work to highlight the submission’s contributions, especially regarding "first" claims and "unified" modeling.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

### Claimed Contribution 1: "First to achieve high-quality dynamic 3D scene reconstruction from monocular videos without known camera poses using a unified 3D Gaussians representation."
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** MoSca (Lei et al., 2024), Shape of Motion (Wang et al., 2024), RoDynRF (Liu et al., 2023)
- **Actual Delta:**  
  - MoSca and Shape of Motion both perform dynamic 3DGS-based reconstruction from monocular, pose-free videos.
  - MoSca uses a cluster-based deformable field and joint pose optimization; Shape of Motion uses Fourier-based motion modeling.
  - The submission’s *Hexplane encoder* and *SE(3) pose initialization* are new in detail, but the *overall capability* (pose-free, unified dynamic 3DGS) is not unique.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The *combination* of Hexplane and SE(3) pose initialization is new, but the "first" claim is overstated; prior works already achieve similar goals with different architectures.

### Claimed Contribution 2: "Unified representation for static and dynamic regions, enabling information sharing and improved reconstruction quality."
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** MoSca, RoDynRF
- **Actual Delta:**  
  - RoDynRF uses separate models for static/dynamic, but MoSca and others (e.g., Deformable 3D Gaussians) also pursue unified modeling.
  - The *Hexplane encoder* is a specific architectural choice for unification, but the *concept* is not new.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The Hexplane is a technical variation; the unification goal is shared by prior work.

### Claimed Contribution 3: "Efficient camera initialization module based on relative pose estimation between image pairs using 3D Gaussians."
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** COLMAP-Free 3DGS, MoSca, InstantSplat
- **Actual Delta:**  
  - COLMAP-Free 3DGS and MoSca both perform pose-free optimization, but the submission’s *SE(3) relative pose estimation* using 3DGS is a new implementation detail.
  - The *concept* of pose-free, 3DGS-based initialization is not new; the *specific method* (SE(3) with Hexplane) is.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The delta is in the *implementation* and possibly efficiency/robustness, not in the conceptual advance.

### Claimed Contribution 4: "Employs a Hexplane-based encoder to disentangle and jointly optimize static background, camera motion, and dynamic object deformation."
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Shape of Motion, Deformable 3D Gaussians, MoSca
- **Actual Delta:**  
  - Prior works use different encoders (Fourier, cluster-based, motion bases) for similar goals.
  - The Hexplane is a new encoder design, but the *joint optimization* of static/dynamic/camera is a shared goal.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The Hexplane is a technical variant; the disentanglement/joint optimization is not unique.

### Claimed Contribution 5: "Incorporates depth and optical flow regularization to enhance geometric consistency."
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** MoSca, Deformable 3D Gaussians, RoDynRF
- **Actual Delta:**  
  - Regularization for geometric/temporal consistency is common; the specific use of depth and optical flow is a detail.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - This is a routine adaptation, not a conceptual novelty.

### Claimed Contribution 6: "Demonstrates superior performance over prior methods (with and without known camera poses) on multiple dynamic scene datasets."
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** All above
- **Actual Delta:**  
  - Empirical improvements are shown, but may be due to implementation, regularization, or dataset choices rather than conceptual advances.
