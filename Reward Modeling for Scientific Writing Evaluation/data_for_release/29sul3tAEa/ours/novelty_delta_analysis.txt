# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating HyperAdapter in the Landscape:**

- **Methodological Cluster:** HyperAdapter sits at the intersection of the *adapter-based* and *hypernetwork-based* continual learning clusters. It leverages a hypernetwork to generate adapter parameters for each task, using a frozen pre-trained backbone to prevent catastrophic forgetting.
- **Closest Prior Works:**
  - **EASE (Zhou et al., 2024):** The most directly comparable prior adapter-based continual learning method, which inserts per-task adapters into a frozen backbone.
  - **HyperMask, Partial Hypernetworks, Dependency Preserving Hypernetworks:** Hypernetwork-based continual learning methods, but these typically generate parameters for the entire network, not just adapters.
  - **Prompt-based Methods (L2P, CODA-Prompt, DAP):** Competing parameter-efficient, rehearsal-free approaches that use prompts instead of adapters.
  - **Prototype-based and MoE Adapter Methods (SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA):** Related in their focus on modularity, scalability, and dynamic adaptation.
- **Problem Space:** HyperAdapter addresses catastrophic forgetting in rehearsal-free, pre-trained model-based continual learning, with a focus on scalability (parameter growth), task-agnostic inference, and positive transfer.
- **Evaluation:** Uses standard benchmarks (CL-100, ImageNet-R, DomainNet) and average final accuracy, aligning with field norms.

**Independent Assessment:**
- HyperAdapter is not the first to use hypernetworks in continual learning, but appears to be the first to use a hypernetwork specifically to generate *adapters* for pre-trained model-based continual learning.
- The method is most closely related to EASE (adapter-based, but with per-task adapters and linear parameter growth) and to hypernetwork-based methods (which typically generate full network weights, not adapters).
- The use of task embeddings and input features for task-agnostic inference aligns with trends in both prompt-based and some adapter-based methods (e.g., DAP, L2P).
- The field is active, with several recent works exploring similar directions (see "Papers from related work not cited" for additional context).

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**

- **EASE (Zhou et al., 2024):**
  - Cited as "the only adapter-based continual learning method prior to our work for a fair comparison."
  - Characterized as introducing adapters into continual learning, but with scalability issues (linear parameter growth).
  - Authors claim HyperAdapter is more scalable due to hypernetwork-generated adapters.
  - *Independent check:* EASE does use per-task adapters, leading to linear parameter growth. However, other adapter-based methods (e.g., SEMA, MoE-Adapters) also address scalability, though not always in the same way.

- **Prompt-based Methods (L2P, CODA-Prompt, DAP):**
  - Cited as strong rehearsal-free baselines, with prompt-based methods sometimes outperforming rehearsal-based ones.
  - Authors position adapter-based methods (including HyperAdapter) as outperforming prompt-based ones.
  - *Independent check:* Prompt-based methods are indeed strong baselines; DAP and CODA-Prompt are state-of-the-art in some settings. The claim that adapter-based methods outperform prompt-based ones is context-dependent and may not hold universally.

- **Hypernetwork-based Methods (HyperMask, Partial Hypernetworks, Dependency Preserving Hypernetworks):**
  - Cited as generating parameters for the entire network, not leveraging pre-trained backbones as effectively.
  - Authors claim these methods do not fully exploit pre-trained models.
  - *Independent check:* These methods do use hypernetworks for full or partial weight generation, often without leveraging frozen pre-trained backbones. The distinction is accurate, though some recent works (e.g., PHA) use hypernetworks to generate adapters.

- **Rehearsal-based Methods:**
  - Cited as impractical due to privacy and memory constraints.
  - *Independent check:* This is a standard argument in the field and is generally accepted.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The authors sometimes overstate the uniqueness of their approach (e.g., "the only adapter-based method prior to our work" for EASE, omitting SEMA, MoE-Adapters, and others).
- The claim that prompt-based methods are outperformed by adapter-based methods is not universally true and depends on the benchmark and implementation.
- The distinction between generating full network weights (prior hypernetwork methods) and generating adapters (HyperAdapter) is valid, but the field is rapidly evolving, and some recent works (e.g., PHA) are not cited.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claim 1: First to use hypernetworks to generate adapters for pre-trained model-based continual learning**
- **Closest Prior Work:** EASE (adapters, but not hypernetworks), HyperMask/Partial Hypernetworks (hypernetworks, but not for adapters), PHA (hypernetwork-generated adapters, but not cited).
- **Actual Delta:** HyperAdapter appears to be the first to combine hypernetworks and adapters *in the context of pre-trained, rehearsal-free continual learning* for vision tasks, with a focus on scalability and task-agnostic inference.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim is mostly accurate, but omits very recent works (e.g., PHA, "Prototype-based HyperAdapter for Sample-Efficient Multi-task Tuning") that use hypernetworks to generate adapters, albeit with different focuses (e.g., sample efficiency, prototypes).
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The use of a hypernetwork to generate adapters is a substantive architectural difference from EASE and from prior hypernetwork methods that generate full weights.

**Claim 2: Scalable, rehearsal-free continual learning with fewer additional parameters as tasks increase**
- **Closest Prior Work:** EASE (linear parameter growth), SEMA/MoE-Adapters (modular/mixture-of-experts for scalability), PHA (hypernetwork-generated adapters).
- **Actual Delta:** HyperAdapter's parameter growth is sub-linear due to the hypernetwork, which is a meaningful improvement over EASE. However, SEMA and MoE-Adapters also address scalability, and PHA uses a similar approach.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The scalability claim is valid relative to EASE, but not unique in the broader context (SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA).
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The scalability improvement is substantive over EASE, but similar ideas exist in other recent works.

**Claim 3: Eliminates need for task identity during inference via input features and task embeddings**
- **Closest Prior Work:** DAP, L2P, HyperMask, PHA (all support task-agnostic inference).
- **Actual Delta:** HyperAdapter's use of input features to select task embeddings is similar to DAP and L2P's prompt selection, and to PHA's prototype-based routing.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim is accurate, but not unique; task-agnostic inference is a common feature in recent methods.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The mechanism is similar in spirit to prior work, though the specific implementation (hypernetwork-generated adapters) is new.

**Claim 4: Promotes positive knowledge transfer across tasks, unlike methods that partition knowledge into independent subspaces**
- **Closest Prior Work:** EASE (partitioned subspaces), SEMA/MoE-Adapters (mixture of experts), PHA (prototype-based transfer).
- **Actual Delta:** HyperAdapter's shared hypernetwork may facilitate transfer, but mixture-of-experts and prototype-based methods also promote transfer.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim is somewhat overstated; other methods (SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA) also promote transfer.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The positive transfer claim is plausible, but not unique.

**Claim 5: Establishes new SOTA for rehearsal-free continual learning, even surpassing multi-task upper bounds**
- **Closest Prior Work:** DAP, CODA-Prompt, EASE, SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA.
- **Actual Delta:** Reported results show improvements over DAP, EASE, and CODA-Prompt on specific benchmarks. However, not all recent methods are compared (e.g., SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA).
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The SOTA claim is only as strong as the baselines included; omission of some recent methods weakens the claim.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The empirical gains are substantive over the compared baselines, but the comparison set is incomplete.

# 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Field Maturity:** The field is highly active, with rapid innovation in parameter-efficient, scalable, and task-agnostic continual learning. Many new methods appear each year.
- **Recent Surveys:** "A Continual Learning Survey: Defying Forgetting in Classification Tasks" (De Lange et al., 2021) and "Analyzing and Reducing Catastrophic Forgetting in Parameter Efficient Tuning" (2023) provide overviews.
- **Trends:** Shift from full fine-tuning to adapters/prompts/LoRA; from task-aware to task-agnostic inference; from static to dynamic/expandable architectures; from rehearsal to rehearsal-free methods.
- **Incremental Advances:** Many recent papers propose new combinations of adapters, hypernetworks, prompts, and prototypes, often with incremental architectural or routing innovations.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Overstated Novelty:** The claim of being the first to use hypernetworks to generate adapters is accurate relative to EASE and older hypernetwork methods, but omits very recent works (e.g., PHA) with similar ideas.
- **Empirical Improvements:** Gains over DAP, EASE, and CODA-Prompt are reported, but may be influenced by implementation details, hyperparameter tuning, or differences in backbone initialization.
- **Terminology Differences:** "Adapter" vs. "prompt" vs. "expert" vs. "prototype"—differences in terminology sometimes mask conceptual similarities (e.g., modular routing, dynamic parameter generation).
- **Routine Extensions:** The use of task embeddings and input features for task-agnostic inference is now common; the novelty lies in the specific combination with hypernetwork-generated adapters.
- **Incomplete Baselines:** Not all relevant recent methods (SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA, etc.) are included in comparisons, which may overstate the empirical and conceptual delta.

# 6. RELATED WORK CONSIDERATIONS

- **Omitted Relevant Work:**
  - "Prototype-based HyperAdapter for Sample-Efficient Multi-task Tuning" (PHA): Uses hypernetworks to generate adapters, combining prototypes and hypernetworks.
  - SEMA, MoE-Adapters: Address scalability and modularity in adapter-based continual learning.
  - "Self-Expansion of Pre-trained Models with Mixture of Adapters for Continual Learning": Mixture-of-experts approach for scalable adaptation.
  - "Read Between the Layers": Uses intra-layer representations for rehearsal-free continual learning.
- **Additional Comparisons Needed:** Including SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA, and other recent adapter/hypernetwork methods would provide a more complete picture.
- **Potentially Misleading Characterizations:** EASE is not the only adapter-based method; prompt-based methods are not always outperformed by adapters; hypernetwork-based methods are evolving to include adapter generation.
- **Exaggerated Limitations:** The claim that prior hypernetwork methods "do not fully exploit pre-trained models" is somewhat overstated, as some recent works do leverage pre-trained backbones.

# 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Differences:**
  - HyperAdapter is among the first to use a hypernetwork to generate adapters for pre-trained, rehearsal-free continual learning, with sub-linear parameter growth and task-agnostic inference.
  - The combination of hypernetworks, adapters, and input-conditioned task embeddings is a novel architectural integration, though similar ideas are emerging in very recent work (e.g., PHA).
- **Main Relationships to Existing Research:**
  - Closest to EASE (adapter-based, but with linear parameter growth) and to recent hypernetwork-based methods (HyperMask, PHA).
  - Shares goals with prompt-based (DAP, L2P) and mixture-of-experts (SEMA, MoE-Adapters) methods.
- **Strongest Differentiation:**
  - Over EASE: Sub-linear parameter growth via hypernetwork-generated adapters.
  - Over prompt-based methods: Adapter-based adaptation may offer more flexible intermediate representation control.
- **Weakest Differentiation:**
  - Compared to very recent works (PHA, SEMA, MoE-Adapters), the conceptual delta is smaller; differences may be in implementation details or specific architectural choices.
- **Discrepancies:**
  - The claim of being the only adapter-based continual learning method prior to this work (re: EASE) is inaccurate; other methods exist.
  - The SOTA claim is weakened by omission of some recent, relevant baselines.
  - The field is rapidly evolving, and the novelty delta is narrowing as similar ideas are concurrently explored.

---

**Overall, HyperAdapter represents a meaningful architectural advance in the context of pre-trained, rehearsal-free continual learning, but its novelty is somewhat overstated due to omission of very recent, closely related works. The empirical gains are promising, but should be interpreted in light of incomplete baseline comparisons. The field is highly active, and reviewers should calibrate expectations accordingly.**
