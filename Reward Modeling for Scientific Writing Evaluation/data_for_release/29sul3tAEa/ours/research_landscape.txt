## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the following methodological paradigms dominate:

- **Adapter-based Methods**: Inserting lightweight, trainable modules (adapters) into frozen pre-trained backbones for task-specific adaptation (e.g., HyperAdapter, EASE, C-ADA, SEMA, MoE-Adapters).
- **Hypernetwork-based Methods**: Using hypernetworks to generate weights or masks for target networks, often conditioned on task or input features (e.g., HyperAdapter, HyperMask, Partial Hypernetworks, Dependency Preserving Hypernetworks, PHA).
- **Prompt-based Methods**: Employing learnable prompts or prompt pools to steer frozen pre-trained models for new tasks (e.g., L2P, CODA-Prompt, DAP).
- **Prototype-based Methods**: Leveraging class or task prototypes (first- and second-order statistics) for classification and knowledge retention (e.g., LayUP, RanPAC, PHA).
- **Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) and Modular Routing**: Dynamically selecting or mixing among multiple adapters or experts for each input/task (e.g., SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA).
- **Experience Replay and Rehearsal**: Using real or synthetic data to replay past experiences and mitigate forgetting (e.g., SSR, CLS-ER, CLEAR, I-LoRA).
- **Attention and Masking**: Hard or soft attention mechanisms to control parameter updates or select relevant subspaces (e.g., HAT, HyperMask, CODA-Prompt).
- **Parameter-efficient Tuning (PETL/PEFT)**: Fine-tuning only a small subset of parameters (e.g., LoRA, adapters, prompts) for scalability and efficiency (e.g., I-LoRA, SEMA, MoE-Adapters).

### Method Clusters

- **Adapter/Hypernetwork Cluster**: HyperAdapter, EASE, SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA, HyperMask, Partial Hypernetworks, Dependency Preserving Hypernetworks.
- **Prompt-based Cluster**: L2P, CODA-Prompt, DAP, C-ADA.
- **Prototype/Feature-based Cluster**: LayUP, RanPAC, PHA.
- **Replay/Rehearsal Cluster**: SSR, CLS-ER, CLEAR, I-LoRA.
- **Attention/Masking Cluster**: HAT, HyperMask, CODA-Prompt.
- **Survey/Analysis/Taxonomy**: CL Survey, Forgetting Analysis.

### Methodological Trends and Patterns

- **Frozen Pre-trained Backbones**: Most methods freeze the backbone to prevent catastrophic forgetting and only adapt small modules (adapters, prompts, etc.).
- **Parameter Efficiency and Scalability**: There is a strong trend toward methods that scale sub-linearly with the number of tasks (e.g., HyperAdapter, SEMA, MoE-Adapters).
- **Task-agnostic Inference**: Increasing focus on methods that do not require task identity at inference (e.g., HyperAdapter, L2P, DAP, HyperMask).
- **Dynamic/On-demand Expansion**: Several methods expand model capacity only when needed, based on distribution shifts or task novelty (e.g., SEMA, MoE-Adapters, CAL in C-ADA).
- **Mixture and Routing**: Weighted mixtures of adapters/experts and dynamic routing are replacing hard selection (e.g., SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA).
- **Prototype and Feature Statistics**: Use of first- and second-order statistics from multiple layers for robust, memory-efficient continual learning (e.g., LayUP, RanPAC).

### Relationships Between Approaches

- **Adapters vs. Prompts**: Both aim for parameter-efficient adaptation, but adapters modify intermediate representations, while prompts steer the model via input conditioning.
- **Hypernetworks as Adapter Generators**: HyperAdapter and PHA use hypernetworks to generate adapter parameters, blending hypernetwork and adapter paradigms.
- **Prototype-based and Adapter/Prompt Methods**: Some methods (PHA, LayUP) combine prototype-based task representations with adapter or hypernetwork-based adaptation.
- **Replay vs. Rehearsal-free**: Some methods rely on replay (SSR, CLS-ER, CLEAR), while others are strictly rehearsal-free (HyperAdapter, C-ADA, CODA-Prompt, LayUP).

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Catastrophic Forgetting**: The central challenge—loss of previously acquired knowledge when learning new tasks sequentially.
- **Rehearsal-free Continual Learning**: Learning without storing or replaying past data, often due to privacy or memory constraints.
- **Scalability and Parameter Efficiency**: Avoiding linear growth in parameters or memory as the number of tasks increases.
- **Task-agnostic Inference**: Eliminating the need for task identity at test time.
- **Stability-Plasticity Dilemma**: Balancing retention of old knowledge (stability) with acquisition of new knowledge (plasticity).
- **Domain and Distribution Shift**: Adapting to new domains or tasks with significant distributional differences from pre-training.
- **Efficient Knowledge Transfer and Reuse**: Promoting positive transfer and avoiding negative transfer or interference.
- **Generalization and Robustness**: Maintaining performance under domain shift, low-data regimes, or noisy task streams.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Catastrophic Forgetting**: Tackled via frozen backbones (HyperAdapter, L2P), adapters/prompts (EASE, C-ADA, CODA-Prompt), hypernetworks (HyperAdapter, HyperMask), and replay (SSR, CLS-ER, CLEAR).
- **Rehearsal-free CL**: Achieved by architectural innovations (adapters, prompts, hypernetworks) and prototype-based methods (LayUP, RanPAC), often with no access to prior data.
- **Scalability**: HyperAdapter and SEMA use hypernetworks and modular expansion to keep parameter growth sub-linear; MoE-Adapters and SEMA use dynamic expert selection.
- **Task-agnostic Inference**: HyperAdapter, L2P, DAP, HyperMask, and some prompt-based methods use input features or learned embeddings to select adaptation modules without explicit task IDs.
- **Stability-Plasticity**: SEMA, I-LoRA, and CLS-ER use dual-memory or expansion strategies; C-ADA and EASE freeze old parameters and expand for new tasks.
- **Domain Shift**: C-ADA introduces scaling/shifting modules; DAP and LayUP use instance-level adaptation and multi-layer statistics for robustness.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Task-incremental to Realistic/Unstructured CL**: RealCL and related works move beyond idealized, structured task sequences to more realistic, random, or unstructured task orders.
- **From Full-model to Parameter-efficient Tuning**: Shift from full fine-tuning to adapters, prompts, and LoRA for efficiency and scalability.
- **From Hard Task Boundaries to Task-agnostic/Online CL**: Many methods now operate without explicit task boundaries or task IDs, reflecting real-world deployment scenarios.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **Vision**: CIFAR-100, ImageNet-R, DomainNet, OfficeHome, Stanford Cars-196, TinyImagenet, ImageNet-A, ImageNet-Sketch, ImageNetV2.
- **Language**: GLUE, SuperNI, AlpacaEval, MMLU, NLI, sentiment analysis datasets.
- **Vision-Language**: CLIP-based datasets (not always named).
- **Reinforcement Learning**: Atari, DMLab.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Accuracy**: Most common, often average final accuracy across tasks.
- **Error Rate**: Used in some vision benchmarks.
- **Forgetting**: Quantitative measure of performance drop on previous tasks.
- **Parameter/Memory/Compute Efficiency**: Model size, memory usage, compute cost.
- **Sample/Data Efficiency**: Performance in low-data or few-shot settings.
- **Novelty-specific Metrics**: Generality Destruction (GD), Syntactic/Semantic Forgetting (SynF/SemF), performance gain over SOTA, parameter training burden reduction.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Average Final Accuracy**: Standard for rehearsal-free and class-incremental settings.
- **Forgetting and Retention**: Explicitly measured in many works, especially those focusing on stability-plasticity.
- **Parameter/Memory Efficiency**: Increasingly reported as methods focus on scalability.
- **Sample Efficiency**: Highlighted in few-shot and low-resource adaptation works (e.g., PHA).
- **Plug-in and Ablation Studies**: Some works (LayUP) report gains as plug-ins to existing methods.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Vision vs. Language**: Vision CL uses more diverse datasets and benchmarks; language CL often focuses on generalization and knowledge retention.
- **Rehearsal vs. Rehearsal-free**: Rehearsal-based methods compare buffer sizes and replay strategies; rehearsal-free methods emphasize architectural or algorithmic innovation.
- **Task-agnostic vs. Task-aware**: Some methods report both settings, showing robustness to unknown task identity.

## 4. RESEARCH CLUSTERS

### Cluster 1: Adapter/Hypernetwork-based Continual Learning
- **Papers**: HyperAdapter (submission), EASE, SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA, HyperMask, Partial Hypernetworks, Dependency Preserving Hypernetworks.
- **Characteristics**: Use of adapters or hypernetworks for parameter-efficient, scalable, and often task-agnostic continual learning. Emphasis on modularity, dynamic expansion, and knowledge reuse.
- **Relationships**: HyperAdapter and PHA both use hypernetworks to generate adapters; SEMA and MoE-Adapters use modular expansion and mixture-of-experts; EASE and C-ADA focus on adapter extensibility and freezing.

### Cluster 2: Prompt-based Continual Learning
- **Papers**: L2P, CODA-Prompt, DAP, C-ADA.
- **Characteristics**: Use of learnable prompts or prompt pools to adapt frozen pre-trained models. Focus on task-agnostic inference, input-conditioned adaptation, and overcoming prompt selection inefficiencies.
- **Relationships**: CODA-Prompt and L2P are foundational; DAP advances with instance-level, pool-free prompts; C-ADA moves beyond prompt learning to adapter-based methods.

### Cluster 3: Prototype/Feature-based and Memory-efficient CL
- **Papers**: LayUP, RanPAC, PHA.
- **Characteristics**: Leverage class/task prototypes and feature statistics (including multi-layer and second-order) for robust, memory-efficient continual learning. Often compatible as plug-ins to other methods.
- **Relationships**: LayUP and RanPAC focus on prototype-based classification; PHA combines prototypes with hypernetwork-generated adapters.

### Cluster 4: Replay/Rehearsal and Dual-memory Methods
- **Papers**: SSR, CLS-ER, CLEAR, I-LoRA.
- **Characteristics**: Use of real or synthetic replay buffers, dual-memory systems, or parameter interpolation to balance stability and plasticity.
- **Relationships**: SSR uses self-synthesized data; CLS-ER and CLEAR use experience replay; I-LoRA uses LoRA parameter interpolation in a dual-memory framework.

### Cluster 5: Attention/Masking and Subspace Methods
- **Papers**: HAT, HyperMask, EASE.
- **Characteristics**: Use of attention masks or subspace partitioning to isolate task-specific knowledge and prevent interference.
- **Relationships**: HAT and HyperMask use attention/masks; EASE uses subspace ensembles with adapters.

### Cluster 6: Analysis, Taxonomy, and Benchmarking
- **Papers**: CL Survey, Forgetting Analysis, RealCL.
- **Characteristics**: Provide taxonomies, new evaluation metrics, and realistic benchmarks for continual learning.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From Full Fine-tuning to Parameter-efficient Tuning**: Early CL methods fine-tuned all parameters, but the field has shifted to adapters, prompts, and LoRA for efficiency and scalability.
- **From Task-specific to Task-agnostic Inference**: Methods now increasingly avoid requiring task identity at inference, using input features or learned embeddings for adaptation.
- **From Static to Dynamic/Expandable Architectures**: SEMA, MoE-Adapters, and C-ADA introduce dynamic expansion, only growing the model when necessary.
- **From Hard Selection to Mixture/Routing**: Weighted mixtures of adapters/experts (SEMA, MoE-Adapters, PHA) replace hard selection, improving flexibility and transfer.
- **From Single-layer to Multi-layer Feature Utilization**: LayUP and RanPAC show the value of leveraging intermediate representations and higher-order statistics.
- **From Real Data Replay to Synthetic/No-rehearsal**: SSR and other methods synthesize rehearsal data, while many new methods are strictly rehearsal-free.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Adapters**: Evolved from static, per-task modules (EASE) to hypernetwork-generated (HyperAdapter, PHA) and mixture-of-experts (SEMA, MoE-Adapters).
- **Hypernetworks**: Progressed from full weight generation (HyperMask, Dependency Preserving HN) to partial (Partial Hypernetworks) and adapter-specific (HyperAdapter, PHA).
- **Prompts**: From prompt pools (L2P) to decomposed attention-based (CODA-Prompt) and instance-level, pool-free (DAP).
- **Replay**: From experience replay (CLEAR) to self-synthesized (SSR) and dual-memory (CLS-ER, I-LoRA).

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Adapters vs. Prompts**: Compete as parameter-efficient adaptation strategies; some works (C-ADA) argue for adapters over prompts in certain settings.
- **Hypernetworks vs. Modular Expansion**: Hypernetworks generate parameters on demand, while modular expansion adds new modules; some methods combine both (HyperAdapter, PHA).
- **Replay vs. Rehearsal-free**: Replay methods are effective but may be impractical due to privacy/memory; rehearsal-free methods are increasingly competitive.
- **Prototype-based vs. Adapter/Prompt-based**: Prototype methods are memory-efficient and robust to domain shift; adapters/prompts offer more flexible adaptation.

# Conclusion

The research landscape in continual learning with pre-trained models is rapidly evolving toward parameter-efficient, scalable, and task-agnostic solutions. The **submission paper, HyperAdapter**, exemplifies this trend by combining hypernetworks and adapters for scalable, rehearsal-free continual learning, eliminating the need for task identity, and promoting positive transfer. It builds upon and extends prior work in adapters, hypernetworks, and task-agnostic adaptation, and is situated at the intersection of several active research clusters. The field is marked by a convergence of ideas from modularity, dynamic expansion, prototype-based learning, and efficient replay, with increasing emphasis on realistic, unstructured continual learning scenarios and robust, generalizable evaluation.
