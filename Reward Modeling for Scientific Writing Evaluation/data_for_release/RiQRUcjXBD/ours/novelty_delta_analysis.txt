# Novelty Delta Analysis for Reviewer Support: SCIPIP (ICLR 2025 Submission)

---

## 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**a. Position within the Research Landscape**

- **Problem Space:** SCIPIP addresses the challenge of information overload in scientific literature, aiming to support the generation of novel, feasible research ideas by combining advanced literature retrieval with LLM-based idea generation.
- **Methodological Clusters:**  
  - **Retrieval-Augmented LLMs:** SCIPIP’s SEC-based retrieval (Semantics, Entities, Citation) aligns with recent trends in retrieval-augmented LLMs (e.g., RefAI, BioReader, GRAPHENE, COVID-19 KG).
  - **Automated Idea Generation:** The dual-path idea proposal (literature-inspired + brainstorming) situates SCIPIP alongside ResearchAgent and AI Scientist, which also use LLMs for iterative idea generation.
  - **Dual-Path Architectures:** While dual-path is a common neural design (e.g., DRCE, DMAGNet), SCIPIP applies this concept at the workflow level (idea generation), not at the neural architecture level.

**b. Closest Prior Works**

- **Retrieval-Augmented LLMs:** RefAI, BioReader, GRAPHENE, COVID-19 KG.
- **LLM-based Idea Generation:** ResearchAgent, AI Scientist, Data-Driven Concept Network.
- **Dual-Path/Hybrid Approaches:** ResearchAgent (iterative, multi-agent), dual-path neural architectures (in other domains).

**c. Relationship to Methodological Clusters**

- SCIPIP is most closely related to the intersection of retrieval-augmented LLMs and automated idea generation. Its SEC-based retrieval is a variant of multi-dimensional retrieval, and its dual-path idea generation is a workflow-level innovation rather than a new neural architecture.
- The use of a large, multi-dimensional literature database is consistent with trends in knowledge graph and graph-augmented retrieval (GRAPHENE, COVID-19 KG).

**d. Place in Problem Space and Evaluation**

- SCIPIP’s focus on both retrieval and idea generation, with evaluation via citation/idea overlap and LLM-based assessment, reflects the field’s move toward multi-faceted, context-aware systems.
- The evaluation approach (retrospective overlap, LLM-based scoring) is similar to that in AI Scientist and ResearchAgent.

**e. Independent Assessment**

- SCIPIP is not the first to combine retrieval-augmented LLMs with idea generation, but its specific combination of SEC-based retrieval and dual-path idea proposal is a novel workflow configuration within this rapidly evolving space.

## 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**a. How Authors Characterize Related Work**

- The only explicit citation context provided is to Swanson (1986), referenced as the origin of Literature-Based Discovery (LBD):  
  > "Around 60 years ago, scientists began exploring scientific discoveries based on literature retrieval, known as Literature-Based Discovery (LBD) (Swanson, 1986)."

- No direct citations or comparative statements are provided for recent, closely related systems (e.g., RefAI, BioReader, ResearchAgent, AI Scientist, GRAPHENE).

**b. Patterns in Positioning**

- The authors position their work as a next step in LBD, emphasizing the integration of advanced retrieval and idea generation.
- There is an implicit claim that prior literature retrieval methods are limited to "simple keyword matching" and do not leverage user background or literature context.

**c. Accuracy and Balance**

- The characterization of prior retrieval methods as "simple keyword matching" is not fully accurate for recent systems (e.g., RefAI, BioReader, GRAPHENE), which use neural, semantic, and graph-based retrieval.
- The lack of direct comparison to recent retrieval-augmented LLMs and idea generation systems (e.g., ResearchAgent, AI Scientist) omits important context.

**d. Discrepancies**

- The authors understate the sophistication of recent retrieval systems and do not acknowledge the existence of LLM-based idea generation frameworks.
- The novelty of the dual-path idea proposal is not contextualized against iterative or multi-agent idea generation approaches (e.g., ResearchAgent).

**e. Rhetoric**

- The language ("ineffective literature retrieval methods," "do not fully leverage user background or literature context") overstates the limitations of prior work, given the existence of recent multi-dimensional and context-aware retrieval systems.

## 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

### Contribution 1: **Dual-Path Idea Proposal Strategy**

- **Claim:** Combines literature-inspired and brainstorming-based idea generation to balance feasibility and originality.
- **Closest Prior Work:**  
  - **ResearchAgent:** Uses iterative, entity-centric LLM-based idea generation with ReviewingAgents for feedback.
  - **AI Scientist:** Automates the research pipeline, including idea generation, with chain-of-thought prompting and self-reflection.
- **Claimed Difference:** Explicit dual-path (literature-based + brainstorming) as opposed to iterative or agent-based generation.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The dual-path approach is a workflow-level distinction; prior systems (ResearchAgent, AI Scientist) use iterative, feedback-driven, or multi-agent strategies, which may implicitly cover both literature-inspired and original idea generation.
  - The explicit combination and filtering to "balance feasibility and originality" is a specific design choice, but not a fundamentally new paradigm.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The difference is more in workflow structuring than in underlying capability; prior systems could be configured to achieve similar results.
  - The novelty is incremental, representing a new configuration rather than a conceptual leap.

### Contribution 2: **SEC-Based Literature Retrieval Method**

- **Claim:** Introduces a retrieval method using Semantics, Entities, and Citation co-occurrence for more comprehensive and accurate retrieval.
- **Closest Prior Work:**  
  - **RefAI:** Retrieval-augmented LLM with multivariable ranking and GPT-4 summarization.
  - **BioReader:** Retrieval-enhanced T5-based transformer with neural retriever and cross-attention.
  - **GRAPHENE:** Graph-augmented document representation, learning-to-rank, external knowledge.
  - **COVID-19 KG:** Knowledge graph with semantic and graph embeddings.
- **Claimed Difference:** Integration of semantics, entities, and citation co-occurrence in a unified retrieval method.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Prior systems already use multi-dimensional retrieval (semantic, entity, graph/citation-based). The specific combination and operationalization of SEC is a variant, not a fundamentally new approach.
  - The novelty lies in the particular integration and possibly in implementation details, not in the conceptual components.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The SEC method is an incremental extension of existing multi-dimensional retrieval strategies.
  - Improvements may be due to dataset scale, feature engineering, or implementation, rather than a conceptual advance.

### Contribution 3: **Large-Scale, Multi-Dimensional Literature Database**

- **Claim:** Constructs a large, multi-dimensional database for fast, rich access to relevant papers.
- **Closest Prior Work:**  
  - **GRAPHENE, COVID-19 KG, BioReader:** All construct large, multi-dimensional or graph-based literature databases.
- **Claimed Difference:** Scale and multi-dimensionality.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The construction of such databases is standard in recent retrieval-augmented LLM systems.
  - The scale (48,895 NLP papers) is substantial but not unprecedented.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - This is a routine adaptation; the novelty is limited unless the database enables qualitatively new capabilities.

### Contribution 4: **Demonstrated Superior Retrieval and Idea Generation**

- **Claim:** Outperforms existing methods in citation/idea overlap and LLM-based innovation evaluation.
- **Closest Prior Work:**  
  - **RefAI, BioReader, ResearchAgent, AI Scientist:** All report similar evaluation metrics (retrieval overlap, idea overlap, LLM/human evaluation).
- **Claimed Difference:** Superior performance.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Empirical improvements may result from implementation, dataset, or evaluation choices rather than conceptual innovation.
  - The use of LLM-based evaluation is now common; the combination of metrics is not unique.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - Performance gains are important but do not, by themselves, establish conceptual novelty.

## 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Field Maturity:**  
  - The field of retrieval-augmented LLMs and automated idea generation is highly active and rapidly evolving, with many new systems and frameworks emerging in the last 2–3 years.
- **Recent Surveys:**  
  - "Bias and Unfairness in Information Retrieval Systems: New Challenges in the LLM Era" (2023) provides a systematic review of LLM-IR systems.
  - "Recent Advance in Content-based Image Retrieval: A Literature Survey" (2023) covers retrieval methods, including multi-modal and graph-based approaches.
- **Trends:**  
  - Movement from keyword-based to multi-dimensional, context-aware retrieval.
  - Increasing integration of retrieval, generation, and evaluation in end-to-end research agents.
  - Growing attention to bias, fairness, and human factors.
- **Incremental Advances:**  
  - Many recent papers report incremental improvements via new combinations of retrieval features, workflow structuring, or evaluation metrics.

- **Routine Adaptations:**

- **Empirical vs. Conceptual Advances:**  
  - Performance gains are important but do not, by themselves, establish conceptual novelty.

## 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Differences:**  
  - SCIPIP’s explicit dual-path (literature-inspired + brainstorming) idea proposal is a specific workflow structuring not previously formalized in this way, though similar capabilities exist in iterative/agent-based systems.
  - The SEC-based retrieval method is a particular integration of semantic, entity, and citation features, but similar multi-dimensional retrieval exists in prior work.
- **Main Relationships to Existing Research:**  
  - SCIPIP is an incremental advance at the intersection of retrieval-augmented LLMs and automated idea generation, reflecting current trends in the field.
- **Strongest Differentiation:**  
  - The explicit dual-path workflow and the operationalization of SEC-based retrieval, though these are variants of existing approaches.
- **Weakest Differentiation:**  
  - Claims about the novelty of the literature database and the superiority of retrieval/idea generation are not strongly differentiated from prior work.
- **Discrepancies:**  
  - Authors understate the sophistication of recent retrieval and idea generation systems.
  - The field is more advanced than the submission’s related work section suggests; omitted comparisons weaken the novelty claim.

---

**In summary:**  
SCIPIP presents a well-integrated system that combines advanced retrieval and idea generation, but its main contributions are incremental and reflect current trends in the field. The explicit dual-path workflow and SEC-based retrieval are specific design choices, but similar capabilities and components exist in recent literature. The authors’ characterizations of prior work are incomplete and, at times, misleading, overstating the novelty of their approach. Direct comparison to recent, closely related systems is necessary for a full assessment of contribution delta and novelty.
