## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the following methodological themes emerge:

- **Counting/Summing-based MIL**: Simple aggregation of instance features, sometimes with learned weights, to predict bag labels (Submission, Related 1).
- **Attention-based MIL**: Use of attention mechanisms to weight instances within a bag, often with neural network parameterization (Related 2, 3, 5, 18, 20).
- **Graph-based MIL**: Modeling relationships between instances or bags using graph neural networks or Markov networks (Related 1, 9, 10).
- **Teacher-Student/Iterative Coupling**: Iterative or coupled training of instance and bag-level models, often with knowledge distillation (Related 4, 6).
- **Causal/Interventional MIL**: Use of causal inference or backdoor adjustment to mitigate spurious correlations (Related 7).
- **Bag-level Data Augmentation**: Creating new bags via mixing or pseudo-labeling to improve generalization (Related 5, 6, 12).
- **Prompt/Linguistic Prior Integration**: Leveraging language models and prompt learning for feature aggregation and interpretability (Related 11).
- **Classical Feature Engineering + Deep Learning**: Combining traditional feature selection (e.g., BoVW, k-mers) with deep models (Related 13, 21).
- **Counting in Non-MIL Contexts**: Counting objects in images using deep learning and attention (Related 15, 16, 17, 19, 22).

### Method Clusters

- **Cluster A: Counting/Summing and Simple Aggregation**
  - **Papers**: Submission, Related 1, 21
  - **Description**: These methods focus on counting or summing informative features or motifs, sometimes with learned weights, arguing that simple aggregation can outperform complex models in certain MIL settings.
  - **Relationship**: The submission builds directly on Related 1, extending counting with learned weights and projection, and is conceptually similar to motif-based approaches (Related 21).

- **Cluster B: Attention-based Deep MIL**
  - **Papers**: Related 2, 3, 5, 18, 20
  - **Description**: These works use attention mechanisms to learn instance importance within bags, often parameterized by deep neural networks. Some extend attention with interpretability (Related 5), adaptive sampling (Related 3), or global channel-spatial interactions (Related 20).
  - **Relationship**: The submission critiques the complexity of this cluster, proposing that counting can be as effective.

- **Cluster C: Graph-based and Structured MIL**
  - **Papers**: Related 1 (gTCR), 9, 10
  - **Description**: These methods model relationships between instances or bags using graphs, Markov networks, or GNNs, allowing for more expressive modeling of dependencies and ambiguity.
  - **Relationship**: Graph-based approaches are sometimes combined with attention or counting (Related 1).

- **Cluster D: Iterative/Teacher-Student Coupling**
  - **Papers**: Related 4, 6
  - **Description**: These works iteratively couple instance and bag-level models, using teacher-student frameworks for knowledge transfer and joint optimization.

- **Cluster E: Causal/Interventional MIL**
  - **Papers**: Related 7
  - **Description**: Introduces causal inference (backdoor adjustment) to address confounding and spurious correlations in MIL.

- **Cluster F: Bag-level Data Augmentation**
  - **Papers**: Related 5, 6, 12
  - **Description**: Methods that augment the training set by generating new bags, either via pseudo-labeling, Shapley value-guided splitting, or mixing.

- **Cluster G: Prompt/Linguistic Prior Integration**
  - **Papers**: Related 11
  - **Description**: Uses language models (e.g., GPT-4, CLIP) and prompt learning to guide feature aggregation and improve few-shot MIL.

- **Cluster H: Classical Feature Engineering + Deep Learning**
  - **Papers**: Related 13, 21
  - **Description**: Combines traditional feature selection (BoVW, k-mers) with deep learning for robust performance, especially on small datasets.

- **Cluster I: Counting in Non-MIL Contexts**
  - **Papers**: Related 15, 16, 17, 19, 22
  - **Description**: Focus on object/crowd counting using deep learning, attention, and feature fusion, often outside the MIL paradigm.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Simplicity vs. Complexity**: There is a visible tension between simple aggregation/counting (Submission, Related 1) and complex, deep attention-based or graph-based models (Related 2, 3, 9, 10, 18, 20).
- **Interpretability**: Several attention-based methods (Related 2, 5) and Shapley value approaches (Related 5) emphasize interpretability, a trend also echoed in the submission’s focus on transparent feature counting.
- **Efficiency**: Adaptive sampling (Related 3), counting (Submission, Related 1), and motif-based methods (Related 21) highlight computational efficiency, especially important for large-scale or high-dimensional data.
- **Integration of External Knowledge**: Prompt learning and language models (Related 11) represent a new direction, integrating external knowledge for improved performance in few-shot settings.
- **Causal Inference**: The use of causal models (Related 7) is an emerging trend to address dataset bias and confounding.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) Classification**: Predicting bag-level labels from sets of instances, often with weak supervision (Submission, Related 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 18).
- **Instance Importance/Interpretability**: Identifying which instances within a bag are most responsible for the bag label (Related 2, 5, 18).
- **Efficiency and Scalability**: Reducing computational cost in MIL, especially for large bags or high-dimensional data (Submission, Related 3, 6, 12).
- **Domain Shift and Generalization**: Addressing domain shift between training and test data, especially in WSI and cross-domain settings (Related 4, 6, 7, 16, 17).
- **Counting in Images**: Counting objects (cells, grains, people) in images, sometimes in a MIL-like setting (Related 15, 16, 17, 19, 22).
- **Learning from Label Proportions**: LLP and bag-level label proportion learning (Related 12).
- **Active Learning in MIL**: Efficiently selecting bags/instances to label in weakly supervised settings (Related 8).
- **Few-shot and Weakly Supervised Learning**: Learning from few labeled bags or instances (Related 11, 22).
- **Handling Tumor Heterogeneity**: Improving MIL for WSI by addressing heterogeneity (Related 14).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **MIL Classification**: Approached via counting (Submission, Related 1), attention (Related 2, 3, 5, 18), graph models (Related 1, 9, 10), and prompt learning (Related 11).
- **Instance Importance**: Attention weights (Related 2, 5, 18), Shapley values (Related 5), and motif-based features (Related 21).
- **Efficiency**: Counting/summing (Submission, Related 1), adaptive sampling (Related 3), and motif-based methods (Related 21) focus on reducing complexity.
- **Domain Shift**: Iterative coupling and teacher-student frameworks (Related 4, 6), causal adjustment (Related 7), and cross-domain adaptation (Related 16).
- **Counting in Images**: Specialized deep architectures for segmentation and counting (Related 15, 16, 17, 19, 22), sometimes with attention or feature fusion.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **MIL as Weak Supervision**: Most works treat MIL as a weakly supervised learning problem, with only bag-level labels available.
- **Instance-to-Bag Aggregation**: The core challenge is how to aggregate instance information to make bag-level predictions, with various methods (counting, attention, graph, prompt) proposed.
- **Interpretability and Efficiency**: There is a growing emphasis on making MIL models interpretable and efficient, especially for large-scale or high-dimensional data.
- **Generalization and Robustness**: Many works address robustness to domain shift, label noise, or heterogeneity, especially in medical imaging.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **Medical Imaging (WSI, Histopathology)**: CAMELYON16, TCGA (Lung, Breast, Kidney, NSCLC, RCC), BRACS, BACH-2018, MNIST-based MIL, real-life histopathology datasets.
- **Immune Repertoire**: Large CMV binding dataset, simulated and real-world immune repertoire data.
- **Crowd/Object Counting**: ShanghaiTech Part A/B, UCF_CC_50, UCF-QNRF, FSC-147, CARPK, wheat grain images.
- **General MIL Benchmarks**: Image/text categorization, object detection/tracking, sound classification, distribution regression.
- **Few-shot/Few-label**: Breast, lung, cervical cancer WSI datasets (few-shot settings).

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Classification Accuracy**: Most common for MIL and patch/bag-level tasks.
- **AUC, F1-score**: Used in medical imaging and immune repertoire classification.
- **MAE, MSE**: Standard for counting tasks (crowd, object, grain).
- **Interpretability**: Qualitative or quantitative assessment of instance importance (attention, Shapley values).
- **Efficiency**: Computational cost, number of queries (active learning), FLOPs.
- **Instance-level Metrics**: For tasks where instance labels are available or inferred.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **MIL Tasks**: Focus on bag-level accuracy, sometimes with instance-level interpretability.
- **Counting Tasks**: MAE/MSE dominate, with some works reporting R^2, MAE, MRE for regression/counting.
- **Medical Imaging**: AUC and accuracy are standard, with some works reporting slide-level and instance-level metrics.
- **Efficiency**: Increasing attention to computational cost, especially for large-scale or high-dimensional data.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Direct Comparisons to SOTA**: Most works benchmark against state-of-the-art methods on standard datasets.
- **Emphasis on Interpretability**: Some attention-based and Shapley value methods explicitly evaluate interpretability.
- **Few-shot and LLP**: Special evaluation protocols for few-shot and label proportion settings.
- **Active Learning**: Number of queries to reach target performance is a key metric.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Simple to Complex and Back**: Early MIL methods used simple aggregation (counting, mean/max pooling). The field then moved to deep attention-based and graph-based models for greater flexibility and performance. Recent works (Submission, Related 1, 21) revisit simplicity, showing that counting/summing with learned weights can outperform or match complex models, especially in high-dimensional, low-sample settings.
- **Attention Mechanisms**: Evolved from basic weighted averages to sophisticated neural attention (Related 2, 3, 5, 18, 20), with increasing focus on interpretability and efficiency.
- **Graph and Structured Models**: Markov networks (Related 9) and GNNs (Related 10) introduced to model dependencies and ambiguity, especially in settings where bag/instance relationships are complex.
- **Iterative and Coupled Training**: Emerged to address domain shift and lack of end-to-end optimization in MIL pipelines (Related 4, 6).
- **Causal Inference**: Recent introduction of causal adjustment (Related 7) to address confounding and spurious correlations, especially in medical imaging.
- **Data Augmentation and Sampling**: Bag-level augmentation (Related 5, 6, 12) and adaptive sampling (Related 3) developed to improve generalization and efficiency.
- **Integration of External Knowledge**: Prompt learning and language models (Related 11) represent a new frontier, leveraging external knowledge for few-shot and weakly supervised MIL.
- **Hybrid Approaches**: Combining classical feature engineering with deep learning (Related 13, 21) for robust performance on small or complex datasets.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Counting/Summing**: Extended with learned weights and projection (Submission), motif-based features (Related 21), and integration with attention (Related 1).
- **Attention**: Enhanced with interpretability (Related 5), adaptive sampling (Related 3), and global channel-spatial interactions (Related 20).
- **Graph Models**: Extended from Markov networks (Related 9) to Bayesian GNNs (Related 10).
- **Teacher-Student**: Iterative coupling and knowledge distillation (Related 4, 6) build on standard MIL pipelines.
- **Causal Models**: Orthogonal to other methods, can be combined for further gains (Related 7).
- **Prompt Learning**: Integrates with MIL for few-shot settings (Related 11).

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Counting vs. Attention**: Submission and Related 1 argue for the efficacy of counting/summing over complex attention models, especially in certain data regimes.
- **Graph-based vs. Independent Bag Models**: Graph-based methods (Related 9, 10) compete with independent bag models, offering more expressive power at higher complexity.
- **Causal vs. Standard MIL**: Causal adjustment (Related 7) is complementary, can be layered on top of existing MIL methods.
- **Prompt Learning**: Represents a complementary direction, especially for few-shot and weakly supervised settings.

# Conclusion

The research landscape represented by these papers is rich and diverse, with a central focus on Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) and its applications in medical imaging, immune repertoire analysis, and counting tasks. The field has evolved from simple aggregation methods to complex attention, graph, and causal models, with recent work revisiting the power of simplicity and efficiency. Interpretability, efficiency, and robustness are recurring themes, with new directions emerging in prompt learning and causal inference. The submission paper is well-positioned within this landscape, offering a principled and efficient alternative to complex MIL models, and its claims are substantiated by both methodological trends and empirical results across the literature.
