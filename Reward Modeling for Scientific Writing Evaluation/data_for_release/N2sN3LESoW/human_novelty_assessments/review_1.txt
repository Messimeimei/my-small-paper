This paper identifies that the binary format of RLHF data labels fails to reflect the actual pairwise difference of human preference and proposes to weight the pairwise samples with respect to the semantic gap in order to provide supervision signals beyond binary labels. The proposed method is simple and has a clear connection to related works. The data-dependent margin and beyond-binary motivation is a good direction for preference optimization, however what the paper presents doesn't fully exploit the potential, as there can be a much wider spectrum of margins that more faithfully match the motivation, such as length difference or response-level LM embedding distance. A more comprehensive study over these options may bring in further contribution in this direction and potentially address the non-improvement for Arena-Hard and MT-Bench.
