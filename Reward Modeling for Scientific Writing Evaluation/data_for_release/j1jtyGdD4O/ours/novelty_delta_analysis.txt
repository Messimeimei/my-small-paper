# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission, "CONDITIONAL DIFFUSION MODEL FOR WEATHER PREDICTION WITH UNCERTAINTY QUANTIFICATION" (CoDiCast), is positioned at the intersection of two major trends in machine learning for weather prediction:
- The use of **diffusion models** for generative, probabilistic forecasting and uncertainty quantification (UQ).
- The application of **transformer/cross-attention mechanisms** for integrating spatiotemporal context.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **GenCast (Price et al., 2024):** Diffusion-based ensemble forecasting for medium-range, global weather prediction.
- **DiffCast, GED, LDCast:** Diffusion models for precipitation nowcasting (short-term, local).
- **FuXi, FourCastNet, GraphCast, FengWu:** Transformer-based, high-resolution, and/or ensemble ML weather models.
- **Uncertainty quantification for data-driven weather models (Bülte et al., 2024):** Systematic analysis of UQ in ML weather models.

**Methodological Cluster Placement:**
- **Primary Cluster:** Diffusion-based generative and probabilistic models for weather (with GenCast as the closest peer for global, medium-range forecasting).
- **Secondary Cluster:** Transformer-based models with attention/cross-attention for spatiotemporal integration (FengWu, GraphCast, FourCastNet).

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- **Problem:** Accurate, global, medium-range weather prediction with integrated UQ and computational efficiency.
- **Evaluation:** Uses ERA5, standard accuracy metrics, inference speed, and UQ metrics—consistent with recent work in this area.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is not the first to use diffusion models for weather, but is among the first to apply them to global, medium-range forecasting with explicit UQ (alongside GenCast).
- The use of cross-attention for conditioning on past observations is a technical detail that may or may not be unique, depending on GenCast and other diffusion-based models’ architectures (see below).
- The field is rapidly evolving, with several recent works (GenCast, SwinRDM, LDCast) exploring similar directions.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors consistently position prior diffusion work (DiffCast, GED, LDCast) as limited to "precipitation nowcasting" and "localized predictions," implying their work is the first to scale diffusion to global, medium-range forecasting.
- GenCast is acknowledged as a "recently proposed close-sourced conditional diffusion-based ensemble forecasting for medium-range weather prediction," but the submission claims novelty in being open, and in specific technical aspects (e.g., cross-attention).
- Transformer-based models (FourCastNet, GraphCast, FengWu, etc.) are cited as deterministic and lacking integrated UQ.
- Uncertainty quantification in prior MLWP models is described as insufficient, with most models "falling short in modeling the uncertainty" (citing Bülte et al., 2024; Jaseena & Kovoor, 2022).

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The characterization of nowcasting diffusion models as "localized" is accurate for GED, DiffCast, LDCast.
- The description of GenCast as "close-sourced" is factually correct, but the technical differences are not deeply discussed.
- The claim that most transformer-based models are deterministic is accurate, but some (e.g., FourCastNet) have ensemble or UQ extensions.
- The assertion that prior MLWP models "fall short" in UQ is somewhat overstated; recent works (GenCast, LDCast, Bülte et al., 2024) have made significant progress.

**Discrepancies:**
- The submission does not fully acknowledge the technical overlap with GenCast, especially regarding the use of conditional diffusion for global forecasting and UQ.
- The distinction between CoDiCast and GenCast is not clearly articulated beyond openness and possible architectural details.
- The limitations of prior work may be somewhat exaggerated to emphasize novelty.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contribution 1: First to apply conditional diffusion models for global weather prediction with UQ.**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** GenCast (Price et al., 2024).
- **Independent Assessment:** GenCast is also a conditional diffusion model for global, medium-range weather forecasting with ensemble/UQ capabilities. The main difference appears to be that GenCast is not open-sourced, while CoDiCast is (if true). The technical novelty (e.g., cross-attention mechanism) is not clearly contrasted.
- **Delta:** The "first" claim is not fully substantiated; GenCast predates or is concurrent. The main difference may be implementation openness and possibly architectural details (cross-attention), but this is not deeply analyzed in the submission.

**Claimed Contribution 2: CoDiCast achieves a trade-off between accuracy, UQ, and computational efficiency.**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** GenCast, SwinRDM, FourCastNet.
- **Independent Assessment:** Many recent models (GenCast, SwinRDM) report similar trade-offs. The submission does not provide direct, side-by-side comparisons with GenCast or SwinRDM, making it difficult to verify the claimed superiority.
- **Delta:** The trade-off is a common goal; the specific advantage of CoDiCast is not clearly isolated from implementation or hardware differences.

**Claimed Contribution 3: Integration of cross-attention to condition on past observations in the diffusion process.**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** GenCast (if it uses similar conditioning), SwinRDM (which integrates recurrent and attention mechanisms).
- **Independent Assessment:** The use of cross-attention for conditioning is a technical detail. It is not clear from the submission or GenCast’s public information whether this is a substantive architectural advance or a routine adaptation. Other models (e.g., SwinRDM, PriSTI) also use attention/cross-attention in diffusion or spatiotemporal models.
- **Delta:** The novelty may be incremental; cross-attention is a standard mechanism in transformer-based models.

**Claimed Contribution 4: Demonstrates that diffusion models can generate diverse, realistic weather scenarios, capturing uncertainty.**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** GenCast, LDCast, SwinRDM, DiffCast.
- **Independent Assessment:** This is a property of diffusion models in general, and has been demonstrated in prior work (GenCast for global, LDCast/DiffCast for nowcasting). The submission’s results may reinforce this, but do not constitute a conceptual advance.
- **Delta:** The demonstration is confirmatory rather than novel.

# 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Field Maturity:** The field is highly active and rapidly evolving, with major advances in the last 2–3 years. Diffusion models for weather are a very recent trend (2023–2024).
- **Recent Surveys:** "Uncertainty quantification for data-driven weather models" (Bülte et al., 2024) provides a systematic review of UQ in MLWP.
- **Trends:** Movement from deterministic to probabilistic/generative models; from nowcasting to global, medium-range forecasting; from post-hoc to integrated UQ.
- **Incremental Advances:** Many recent papers introduce architectural tweaks (e.g., attention, hybridization) as "novelty," but the field is characterized by rapid, sometimes parallel, incremental progress.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Overstated Novelty:** The "first" claim is not fully supported given GenCast’s existence; the main difference may be openness or minor architectural details.
- **Characterization Alignment:** Authors’ claims about prior work’s limitations (especially UQ) are somewhat overstated; recent works have addressed these issues.
- **Empirical Improvements:** Reported speed/accuracy gains may be due to hardware, implementation, or dataset differences rather than conceptual advances.
- **Terminology Differences:** "Cross-attention" and "conditional diffusion" are standard terms; differences may be superficial if prior models use similar mechanisms under different names.
- **Routine Extensions:** The use of cross-attention for conditioning is a routine adaptation in transformer/diffusion models.

- **Omitted Relevant Work:** SwinRDM, PriSTI, and several recent transformer/diffusion models for global weather are not cited or compared.

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Differences:** CoDiCast is among the first open implementations of conditional diffusion for global, medium-range weather forecasting with UQ. The use of cross-attention for conditioning is a technical detail, but not clearly a conceptual advance.
- **Main Relationships:** The submission is most closely related to GenCast (diffusion for global weather/UQ), with secondary relationships to SwinRDM, LDCast, and transformer-based models (FengWu, GraphCast).
- **Strongest Differentiation:** Openness of implementation (if true); possible minor architectural differences (cross-attention).

- **Reviewer Guidance:** The main delta is incremental and may be in implementation openness or minor architectural details. The field is moving rapidly, and many advances are parallel and incremental.
