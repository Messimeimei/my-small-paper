This paper introduces a framework called Self-controller for length control in large language models (LLMs), which leverages a state reflector module and a multi-round dialogue process to enable the LLM to maintain awareness of its generated response length and iteratively adjust outputs. The approach incorporates a binary search optimization based on the linearity and monotonicity of textual length, and provides theoretical guarantees on time complexity. While the methodology demonstrates some novel aspects—particularly the integration of a state reflector to inform the LLM of precise length statistics during generation—the overall contribution may not be substantial enough for acceptance at a top-tier venue like ICLR. The main point of novelty appears to be the state reflector module, but it remains unclear how this component and the overall framework compare to existing length control baselines. The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion clarifying the specific differences and advances over prior work, as well as a stronger justification of the novelty and significance of the proposed approach.
