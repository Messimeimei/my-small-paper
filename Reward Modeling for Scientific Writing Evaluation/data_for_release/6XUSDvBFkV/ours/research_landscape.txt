## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the dominant methodologies are **quantization** (especially ultra-low and sub-1-bit quantization), **sparsity/pruning**, and **hybrid compression** (combining quantization, pruning, and/or knowledge distillation). The focus is on compressing large language models (LLMs) for efficient inference and deployment, particularly on resource-constrained hardware.

#### **Cluster 1: Ultra-Low-Bit and Sub-1-Bit Quantization**
- **Papers:** Submission (STBLLM), BiLLM, OneBit, DB-LLM, PB-LLM, Sub-bit Neural Networks, QuIP
- **Description:** These works push the limits of quantization, aiming for 1-bit or even sub-1-bit per weight. They introduce structural binarization (STBLLM), dual-binarization (DB-LLM), sign-value decomposition (OneBit), and sub-bit kernel selection (Sub-bit NN). Techniques include adaptive binarization, residual approximation, and knowledge distillation to mitigate performance loss.
- **Trends:** Increasing sophistication in identifying and preserving salient weights (e.g., via Hessian or activation analysis), adaptive or layerwise quantization, and hardware-aware implementations (e.g., custom CUDA kernels).

#### **Cluster 2: Low-Bit Quantization (2-4 bits)**
- **Papers:** OmniQuant, ABQ-LLM, AWQ, QQQ, BitDistiller, SpQR, OWQ, GPTQ
- **Description:** These methods target 2-4 bit quantization, balancing aggressive compression with minimal accuracy loss. Innovations include learnable quantization parameters (OmniQuant), activation-aware scaling (AWQ), block-wise error compensation (ABQ-LLM), and hybrid sparse-quantized formats (SpQR).
- **Trends:** Emphasis on post-training quantization (PTQ) for scalability, outlier detection and handling, and specialized inference kernels for speed.

#### **Cluster 3: Pruning and Sparsity**
- **Papers:** OWL, SparseGPT, Wanda, FLAP, SliceGPT
- **Description:** These works focus on pruning (removing weights or neurons) to induce sparsity, often in a structured or adaptive manner. Methods include outlier-weighted layerwise sparsity (OWL), one-shot pruning (SparseGPT), activation-weighted pruning (Wanda), and structured pruning via row/column deletion (SliceGPT).
- **Trends:** Move from unstructured to structured and adaptive sparsity, with increasing attention to hardware efficiency and minimal retraining.

#### **Cluster 4: Hybrid and Joint Compression**
- **Papers:** CLIP-Q, Deep Compression, SpQR (hybrid), PB-LLM (partial binarization)
- **Description:** These combine quantization and pruning (sometimes with Huffman coding or knowledge distillation) in a unified framework, often with fine-tuning or retraining for optimal performance.

#### **Cluster 5: Knowledge Distillation and Training-Aware Compression**
- **Papers:** BitDistiller, OneBit (QKD), DB-LLM (DAD)
- **Description:** These methods integrate knowledge distillation or quantization-aware training to transfer knowledge from full-precision models or mitigate quantization-induced bias.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Salient Weight Identification:** Increasingly sophisticated metrics (Hessian, activation outliers, standardized importance) are used to identify which weights require higher precision or special treatment.
- **Layerwise and Adaptive Strategies:** Many methods adapt quantization or pruning ratios per layer, recognizing non-uniform sensitivity across the model.
- **Hardware-Aware Design:** Custom CUDA kernels, TensorCore utilization, and inference engines (e.g., TinyChat, vLLM) are developed to realize practical speedups.
- **Hybridization:** There is a trend toward combining sparsity and quantization, or integrating knowledge distillation, to push compression further without catastrophic accuracy loss.
- **Post-Training vs. Training-Aware:** PTQ dominates for scalability, but QAT and distillation are increasingly used for ultra-low-bit regimes.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

1. **Memory and Computational Inefficiency of LLMs**
   - All papers address the challenge of deploying LLMs on resource-constrained devices by reducing model size and inference cost.

2. **Accuracy Degradation at Low Bitwidths**
   - Many works (STBLLM, BiLLM, OneBit, DB-LLM, BitDistiller) focus on preventing the collapse in performance when quantizing to 1-2 bits.

3. **Efficient Post-Training Compression**
   - PTQ methods (OmniQuant, GPTQ, AWQ, OWQ, SpQR) aim to compress models without retraining, crucial for very large models.

4. **Hardware-Efficient Inference**
   - Several papers (STBLLM, ABQ-LLM, AWQ, QQQ, SpQR) explicitly target acceleration on GPUs or edge devices, often with custom kernels.

5. **Sparsity and Pruning for LLMs**
   - Pruning methods (OWL, SparseGPT, Wanda, FLAP, SliceGPT) address the need for structured sparsity that aligns with hardware and preserves accuracy.

6. **Hybrid Compression and Joint Optimization**
   - Some works (CLIP-Q, Deep Compression, SpQR) tackle the challenge of combining multiple compression techniques for maximal gain.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Sub-1-Bit Quantization:** STBLLM introduces structured binarization and adaptive grouping, while BiLLM uses Hessian-guided selection and residuals, and OneBit uses sign-value decomposition. All aim to preserve accuracy at extreme compression.
- **Salient Weight Handling:** AWQ, OWQ, PB-LLM, and BiLLM all propose methods to identify and protect or reconstruct the most important weights.
- **Pruning:** OWL and Wanda use activation-based metrics, while SparseGPT and FLAP use second-order or fluctuation-based metrics for more effective pruning.
- **Hybrid Methods:** SpQR and CLIP-Q combine quantization and sparsity, with SpQR focusing on outlier isolation and CLIP-Q on joint optimization.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Uniform to Adaptive:** Early works often used uniform quantization/pruning; recent methods adapt strategies per layer or per group.
- **From Unstructured to Structured:** There is a shift toward structured compression (N:M sparsity, block/group quantization) for hardware compatibility.
- **From Weight-Only to Weight+Activation:** Some methods (AWQ, QQQ) extend quantization to activations for further gains.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **Language Modeling:** WikiText2 is the most common for perplexity evaluation.
- **LLM Benchmarks:** LLaMA (1/2/3), OPT, Mistral, Falcon, and instruction-tuned models (Vicuna, OpenFlamingo) are widely used.
- **Downstream Tasks:** BoolQ, code reasoning, and zero-shot benchmarks are used for accuracy.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Perplexity:** The primary metric for language modeling performance.
- **Accuracy:** Used for downstream tasks (e.g., BoolQ, code, QA).
- **Speedup:** Inference speedup over FP16 or other baselines (e.g., x17.85 in STBLLM).
- **Memory Compression:** Reduction in memory footprint or bits per weight.
- **Compression Ratio:** For pruning and hybrid methods.
- **Resource Efficiency:** Quantization/pruning time, data requirements.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Perplexity at Various Bitwidths:** Direct comparison of perplexity at 1, 2, 3, 4 bits per weight.
- **Relative Accuracy Loss:** Some report percentage of original performance retained.
- **Speedup and Memory:** Hardware-aware methods report real-world speedup and memory savings.
- **Scalability:** Ability to quantize/prune very large models (up to 175B parameters) is highlighted.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Direct Baseline Comparison:** Most papers compare against prior SOTA at the same bitwidth or sparsity.
- **Ablation Studies:** Some (e.g., STBLLM, BiLLM) provide ablations for each component.
- **Hardware Benchmarks:** Increasingly common, especially for methods claiming practical deployment.

- **Papers:** OmniQuant, ABQ-LLM, AWQ, QQQ, BitDistiller, SpQR, OWQ, GPTQ

- **Papers:** CLIP-Q, Deep Compression, SpQR, PB-LLM

### Cluster 5: **Knowledge Distillation and Training-Aware Compression**
- **Papers:** BitDistiller, OneBit, DB-LLM
- **Characteristics:** Use of knowledge distillation or QAT to mitigate quantization-induced performance loss, especially at ultra-low bitwidths.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From Classic Compression to LLM-Specific Methods:** Early works (Deep Compression, CLIP-Q) established the value of joint pruning and quantization. As LLMs emerged, methods adapted to their scale and unique properties (e.g., outlier weights, activation distributions).
- **From Uniform to Adaptive/Structured Compression:** There is a clear evolution from uniform quantization/pruning to adaptive, layerwise, and structured approaches (N:M sparsity, block/group quantization).
- **From Weight-Only to Weight+Activation and Outlier-Aware:** Methods increasingly consider activation statistics and outlier analysis for both quantization and pruning.
- **From PTQ to QAT and Distillation:** While PTQ remains dominant for scalability, QAT and knowledge distillation are adopted for ultra-low-bit regimes to recover lost accuracy.
- **From Unstructured to Hardware-Friendly Compression:** Structured sparsity and quantization, as well as custom inference kernels, are developed to realize practical speedups on modern hardware.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Salient Weight Identification:** Evolved from simple magnitude-based to Hessian/activation-based and standardized importance metrics.
- **Residual and Partial Binarization:** BiLLM and PB-LLM introduce residual and partial binarization, extended by STBLLM with structured binarization and adaptive grouping.
- **Hybrid Formats:** SpQR and CLIP-Q combine sparsity and quantization, with SpQR introducing outlier isolation for weights.
- **Hardware-Aware Implementations:** STBLLM, ABQ-LLM, AWQ, and QQQ develop custom kernels and inference engines for real-world deployment.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing:** Sub-1-bit quantization (STBLLM, BiLLM, OneBit) vs. low-bit quantization (OmniQuant, AWQ, GPTQ) vs. pruning (OWL, SparseGPT).
- **Complementary:** Hybrid methods (SpQR, CLIP-Q) combine strengths of quantization and pruning; knowledge distillation (BitDistiller, DB-LLM) can be layered atop quantization/pruning.
