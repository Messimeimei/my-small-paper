## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, several methodological themes emerge, especially around **exploration in reinforcement learning (RL) and bandit problems**, with a strong focus on **Thompson Sampling (TS)**, **Langevin Monte Carlo (LMC)**, **uncertainty estimation**, and **policy optimization**. The methods can be grouped as follows:

#### **Cluster 1: Thompson Sampling and Posterior Sampling Methods**
- **Direct Posterior Sampling with LMC:**  
  - Submission Paper (PETS), Related Papers 1, 3, 9: Use LMC (and variants like underdamped LMC) to sample from the posterior over value functions or model parameters, enabling more accurate and diverse exploration than Gaussian approximations.
- **Neural Network-based TS:**  
  - Related Papers 2, 4, 10, 15: Use deep neural networks (NeuralTS, ENNs, dropout/bootstrapping, FGTS.CDB) to represent uncertainty and sample from the posterior for TS in complex, high-dimensional settings.
- **Epsilon-Greedy and Discounted TS Variants:**  
  - Submission Paper (PETS, via ϵ-exploring TS), Related Papers 5, 8, 12: Combine TS with epsilon-greedy or discounting strategies to balance exploration and exploitation, or to adapt to non-stationary environments.

#### **Cluster 2: Exploration via Uncertainty and Intrinsic Motivation**
- **Ensemble and Disagreement-based Exploration:**  
  - Related Papers 4, 18, 19, 21: Use ensembles (ENNs, EDE, model disagreement) to estimate epistemic uncertainty or intrinsic rewards, driving exploration in RL.
- **Count-based and Pseudocount Exploration:**  
  - Related Papers 16, 17: Generalize count-based exploration to high-dimensional spaces using hash functions or neural networks (CFN) to estimate state visitation counts or pseudocounts.

#### **Cluster 3: Policy Optimization and Actor-Critic Methods**
- **Maximum Entropy and Stochastic Policy Optimization:**  
  - Related Papers 6, 23: Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) uses entropy regularization for robust, stable exploration in continuous control.
- **Proximal Policy Optimization and Variants:**  
  - Related Papers 7, 13: PPO and OPPO (optimistic PPO) use clipped objectives and exploration bonuses for stable, efficient policy updates.
- **Model-based Planning with Policy Networks:**  
  - Related Paper 11: POPLIN integrates policy networks with online planning for efficient model-based RL.

#### **Cluster 4: Exploration in Model-based RL**
- **Model-based RL with Exploration Guarantees:**  
  - Related Papers 1, 11, 22: Use LMC for exploration in model-based RL, combine planning with policy networks, or introduce constrained model updates for monotonic improvement.

#### **Cluster 5: Exploration with Demonstrations and Auxiliary Objectives**
- **Demonstration-guided Exploration:**  
  - Related Paper 20: Incorporates demonstrations and auxiliary objectives to guide exploration in sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Langevin Monte Carlo (LMC) as a Unifying Tool:** LMC is increasingly used for posterior sampling in both bandit and RL settings, bridging the gap between theoretical guarantees and practical deep RL (Submission, Related 1, 3, 9).
- **Deep Uncertainty Estimation:** There is a shift from simple Gaussian approximations to richer, neural network-based uncertainty models (NeuralTS, ENNs, bootstrapping, ensembles).
- **Hybridization of Exploration Strategies:** Methods increasingly combine TS with epsilon-greedy, count-based, or intrinsic motivation approaches for more robust exploration.
- **Integration with Policy Optimization:** Exploration strategies are being embedded into policy optimization frameworks (PPO, SAC, OPPO), often with theoretical regret or sample complexity guarantees.
- **Model-based and Model-free Synergy:** Some methods (e.g., PETS, POPLIN) show that exploration techniques can be plugged into both model-based and model-free RL algorithms.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Building Blocks

- **From Gaussian to LMC-based Posterior Sampling:**  
  - Early TS methods used Gaussian approximations; recent works (Submission, Related 1, 3, 9) use LMC for more accurate, scalable posterior sampling, enabling theoretical and practical advances in exploration.
- **From Tabular to Deep and Continuous Spaces:**  
  - Methods have evolved from tabular and linear settings to deep neural networks and continuous control (Related 2, 4, 10, 15, Submission).
- **From Simple to Hybrid Exploration Strategies:**  
  - Epsilon-greedy and count-based methods are now hybridized with TS, intrinsic motivation, and ensemble-based approaches for robustness and efficiency (Submission, Related 5, 8, 12, 16, 17, 18, 19, 21).
- **Integration with Policy Optimization:**  
  - Exploration strategies are increasingly integrated into policy optimization frameworks (SAC, PPO, OPPO), with theoretical guarantees and empirical validation (Related 6, 7, 13, 23).
- **Model-based RL with Exploration Guarantees:**  
  - Recent works (Related 11, 22) focus on combining exploration with model-based planning and ensuring monotonic improvement.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing:**  
  - LMC-based TS vs. neural/ensemble-based TS: Both aim for efficient exploration, but differ in how uncertainty is represented and sampled.
  - Count-based/pseudocount vs. intrinsic motivation: Both provide exploration bonuses, but differ in how novelty is measured.
- **Complementary:**  
  - Exploration strategies (TS, count-based, intrinsic) can be plugged into policy optimization or model-based RL frameworks for improved performance.
  - Demonstration-guided exploration (Related 20) can be combined with intrinsic or TS-based methods for challenging tasks.
