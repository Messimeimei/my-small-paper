# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Research Landscape:**

- **Problem Focus:** The submission addresses *efficient exploration in reinforcement learning (RL) with high-dimensional continuous controls*, specifically via *practical Thompson Sampling (TS)* using *Langevin Monte Carlo (LMC)* and *parallel Markov chains* for posterior sampling.
- **Closest Prior Work:**
  - **Ishfaq et al. (2024):** Demonstrates LMC-TS for RL, but only in *discrete* action spaces (e.g., Atari). Provides theoretical regret analysis for TS in discrete settings.
  - **Xu et al. (2022):** Shows Laplace approximations are inefficient in high dimensions, motivating LMC-based approaches.
  - **Chapelle & Li (2011):** Early work on TS in bandits.
  - **Recent neural/ensemble TS works (not cited):** NeuralTS, ENNs, and others extend TS to deep/high-dimensional settings, often for bandits or contextual RL.
- **Methodological Cluster:** The submission is most closely aligned with the *LMC-based posterior sampling for exploration* cluster, extending it from discrete to continuous control RL.
- **Problem Space:** The submission targets the *intractability of posterior sampling* and *action selection* in continuous action spaces, a recognized challenge in the field.
- **Evaluation Approach:** Theoretical regret analysis (matching best-known discrete bounds) and empirical integration with standard RL algorithms (POMP, MBPO, SAC) on continuous control benchmarks.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is a direct extension of LMC-TS (Ishfaq et al., 2024) from discrete to continuous controls, with additional practical considerations (parallel chains, integration with off-the-shelf RL).
- The use of parallel Markov chains for improved exploration diversity is a practical adaptation, not a fundamentally new theoretical concept.
- The field has seen a trend toward integrating TS with deep RL and continuous controls, with several neural/ensemble-based TS methods (some not cited) addressing similar challenges.

**Potential Discrepancies:**

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contribution 1:** *First practical TS-based exploration for RL with continuous controls using LMC and parallel Markov chains.*

- **Closest Prior Work:** Ishfaq et al. (2024) (LMC-TS for RL, discrete actions); neural/ensemble TS (NeuralTS, ENNs, etc.).
- **Actual Delta:** The main difference is the adaptation of LMC-TS to continuous action spaces, with practical implementation via parallel Markov chains to improve sample diversity.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The use of LMC for continuous controls is a non-trivial extension, as action selection in continuous spaces is more challenging. However, the core idea (LMC-TS) is not new, and parallel chains are a practical, not conceptual, innovation.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim of being "first" is somewhat overstated, as neural/ensemble-based TS methods have addressed continuous/high-dimensional settings, though not always with LMC. The use of parallel chains is a practical engineering choice, not a theoretical breakthrough.

**Claimed Contribution 2:** *Theoretical regret analysis for TS in RL with continuous controls, matching best-known discrete setting bounds.*

- **Closest Prior Work:** Ishfaq et al. (2024), Zhang et al. (2021), Wang et al. (2020).
- **Actual Delta:** The submission adapts the regret analysis from discrete to continuous controls, showing that the same order of regret bound holds under certain regularity conditions.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The extension is technically non-trivial but closely follows the structure of prior proofs. The main novelty is in handling approximate action selection in continuous spaces.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim is accurate in terms of technical achievement, but the novelty is incremental, building directly on Ishfaq et al.

**Claimed Contribution 3:** *Demonstration that PETS can be integrated into existing RL algorithms (POMP, MBPO, SAC) without modifying their core algorithms or hyperparameters.*

- **Closest Prior Work:** Integration of exploration strategies into off-the-shelf RL (e.g., count-based, intrinsic motivation, ensemble methods).
- **Actual Delta:** The submission shows empirical plug-and-play integration, but similar claims have been made for other exploration strategies.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** This is a practical demonstration rather than a conceptual advance.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim is accurate but not unique; many exploration methods are designed for such integration.

**Claimed Contribution 4:** *Empirical evidence that maintaining n parallel Markov chains for posterior sampling improves exploration diversity and performance.*

- **Closest Prior Work:** Ensemble/disagreement-based exploration (Pathak et al., ENNs, bootstrapping).
- **Actual Delta:** Using parallel LMC chains is a specific instantiation of the general idea of promoting diversity in posterior samples.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The approach is practical and may improve performance, but the underlying idea is well-established.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The novelty is in the specific implementation, not the conceptual approach.
