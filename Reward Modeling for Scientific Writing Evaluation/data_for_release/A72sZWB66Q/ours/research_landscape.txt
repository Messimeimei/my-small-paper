## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the surveyed papers, the following methodological themes emerge:

- **Feature Fusion and Multi-Branch Architectures:**  
  - *Global and Local Feature Fusion:* Several works (GLFF, Fusing Global and Local Features, Gram-Net) propose architectures that combine global (semantic, texture) and local (patch, artifact) features, often using attention mechanisms or patch selection modules.
  - *Attention Mechanisms:* Attention-based modules (e.g., AMSFF, AFFM, multi-head attention) are used to enhance feature fusion and focus on informative regions.

- **Parameter-Efficient and Modular Adaptation:**  
  - *LoRA and Hypernetworks:* The submission (HyperDet) and Mixture of Low-rank Experts both leverage LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) for efficient fine-tuning of large vision models (e.g., CLIP-ViT). HyperDet introduces a hypernetwork to generate and merge multiple LoRA weights, forming an ensemble of expert detectors.
  - *Mixture-of-Experts (MoE):* Used in MoLE and HyperDet to combine specialized detectors for improved generalization.

- **Frequency and Spectrum Analysis:**  
  - *Frequency-Domain Features:* Several works (Leveraging Frequency Analysis, Detecting and Simulating Artifacts, A Closer Look at Fourier Spectrum Discrepancies, Think Twice Before Detecting GAN-generated Fake Images) analyze and exploit frequency artifacts (e.g., upsampling artifacts, spectral decay) for detection.
  - *Spectrum-Based Classifiers:* Classifiers trained on frequency representations rather than pixel values.

- **Pretrained Vision-Language Models and Feature Space Probing:**  
  - *CLIP-ViT Feature Space:* Universal Fake Image Detectors and Mixture of Low-rank Experts use CLIP-ViT’s feature space for detection, employing nearest neighbor or linear probing rather than end-to-end training.

- **Reconstruction-Based and Representation Learning:**  
  - *Diffusion Reconstruction Error (DIRE):* Uses reconstruction error from a pretrained diffusion model as a detection signal.
  - *Multi-view Completion:* Multi-view completion and cross-view classification (Multi-view Completion Representation) to reduce overfitting to frequency artifacts.

- **Handcrafted and Statistical Feature Extraction:**  
  - *Co-occurrence Matrices, SRM Filters, Edge Graphs:* Used for capturing pixel-level or structural artifacts (e.g., HyperDet’s grouped SRM filters, co-occurrence matrices, edge graph regularization).

- **Hierarchical and Fine-Grained Detection:**  
  - *Hierarchical Multi-label Learning:* HiFi-Net models forgery attributes at multiple levels for detection and localization.

- **GAN Fingerprinting and Attribution:**  
  - *Learning GAN Fingerprints:* Attributing images to specific GANs by learning persistent, unique fingerprints.

### Method Clusters

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Generalized Detection of Synthesized Images:**  
  - Detecting AI-generated images (GANs, VAEs, Diffusion, Autoregressive) across diverse, unseen models and post-processing operations.
  - Reducing overfitting to specific artifacts or generators.

- **Robustness to Post-Processing and Perturbations:**  
  - Ensuring detectors remain effective under compression, resizing, blurring, and other common image manipulations.

- **Attribution and Source Identification:**  
  - Not just detecting fakes, but attributing them to specific generative models (GAN fingerprinting).

- **Fine-Grained Detection and Localization:**  
  - Localizing manipulated regions and classifying forgery attributes at multiple levels.

- **Dataset and Benchmark Construction:**  
  - Addressing the lack of comprehensive, diverse, and realistic datasets for training and evaluation.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Generalization Across Generators:**
  - *HyperDet, MoLE, Universal Fake Image Detectors, GLFF, Fusing Global and Local Features, DIRE* all target generalization, but use different strategies: ensembling, feature fusion, parameter-efficient adaptation, or leveraging pretrained feature spaces.

- **Robustness to Post-Processing:**
  - *Gram-Net, GLFF, HyperDet, Multi-view Completion Representation* explicitly evaluate and improve robustness to image perturbations.

- **Attribution:**
  - *GAN Fingerprints, Co-occurrence Matrices* focus on source attribution, with GAN Fingerprints analyzing the persistence and uniqueness of model-specific fingerprints.

- **Localization:**
  - *HiFi-Net* uniquely addresses pixel-level localization and hierarchical attribute classification.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Binary to Multi-Label and Hierarchical Tasks:**  
  The field is moving from simple real/fake classification to more nuanced tasks (e.g., localization, attribution, hierarchical attribute classification).

- **From Model-Specific to Universal Detection:**  
  Early works focused on detecting fakes from specific models; recent works emphasize universal detection across unknown generators.

- **From Controlled to Real-World Scenarios:**  
  There is increasing emphasis on real-world conditions, including diverse post-processing, unseen generators, and large-scale, diverse datasets.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Handcrafted Features to Deep Learning:**  
  Early works relied on hand-crafted features (e.g., co-occurrence matrices, frequency analysis). The field has shifted toward deep learning, multi-branch architectures, and attention mechanisms.

- **From Model-Specific to Universal Detection:**  
  Initial detectors were often overfitted to specific generators (e.g., GANs). Recent works (HyperDet, MoLE, Universal Fake Image Detectors) emphasize generalization to unseen models, including diffusion and autoregressive models.

- **From Pixel/Frequency Artifacts to Semantic and Structural Features:**  
  As frequency-based methods were shown to be vulnerable, there is a move toward combining pixel, semantic, and structural cues (e.g., HyperDet’s balanced objective, Gram-Net’s global texture, HiFi-Net’s hierarchical attributes).

- **Leveraging Large Pretrained Models:**  
  The use of CLIP and other large vision-language models for feature extraction and detection is a recent and growing trend, enabling strong generalization with minimal training.

- **Parameter and Data Efficiency:**  
  Methods like LoRA, MoLE, and hypernetworks enable efficient adaptation, making large models practical for forensic tasks.

- **Dataset Scale and Diversity:**  
  The field is responding to the need for realistic evaluation by constructing larger, more diverse, and more challenging datasets (e.g., DiFF, DF^3, HiFi-IFDL, DiffusionForensics).

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **LoRA and MoE:**  
  LoRA-based adaptation (MoLE, HyperDet) builds on parameter-efficient fine-tuning, extended by hypernetworks and ensembling.

- **Feature Fusion:**  
  Early two-branch models are extended with attention mechanisms and more sophisticated patch selection.

- **Frequency Analysis:**  
  Initially a key detection signal, now often combined with other cues or used as a cautionary tale about over-reliance on artifacts.

- **Reconstruction-Based Methods:**  
  DIRE and multi-view completion represent a move toward using generative models themselves for detection.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Frequency vs. Semantic/Structural Features:**  
  Frequency-based methods are fast and efficient but vulnerable; semantic/structural features (e.g., from CLIP or hierarchical models) offer better generalization.

- **End-to-End Training vs. Feature Space Probing:**  
  End-to-end training can overfit; probing pretrained feature spaces (CLIP) offers better cross-model generalization.

- **Single-Model vs. Ensemble/Expert Approaches:**  
  Ensembling (HyperDet, MoLE) improves robustness and generalization compared to single-model detectors.
