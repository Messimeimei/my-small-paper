# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission, DuRND, is positioned within the "Random Network Distillation and Intrinsic Motivation" cluster, which includes RND (Burda et al., 2018), DRND, and related extensions. This cluster focuses on scalable, low-overhead intrinsic rewards for exploration in sparse-reward, high-dimensional RL environments.
- DuRND claims to advance this line by introducing a *dual* random network structure: one module for novelty (exploration) and one for "contribution" (exploitation), with a dynamic reward adjustment mechanism that shifts the agent’s focus over time.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **RND (Burda et al., 2018):** The foundational method for random network-based intrinsic rewards.
- **DRND:** Distributional extension of RND, not directly cited but relevant.
- **Never Give Up (Badia et al., 2020):** Combines RND with episodic memory for improved exploration.
- **Reward Shaping Methods:** SORS (Memarian et al., 2021), ROSA (Mguni et al., 2023), ReLara (Ma et al., 2024b), which focus on adaptive or self-supervised reward shaping.
- **Sibling Rivalry, SERENE, SASR:** Methods that dynamically balance exploration and exploitation.

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- DuRND is most closely aligned with the RND/DRND family but incorporates ideas from adaptive reward shaping (dynamic reward adjustment) and exploration/exploitation balancing (as in Sibling Rivalry, SERENE).
- It does *not* directly engage with policy/trajectory-level diversity methods or model-based/hierarchical approaches.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses efficient exploration in sparse-reward, high-dimensional environments (Atari, VizDoom, MiniWorld), focusing on balancing exploration and exploitation with low computational overhead.
- Evaluation follows standard practice: cumulative reward, learning efficiency, and convergence stability, compared to RND, #Exploration, and reward shaping baselines.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is a direct extension of RND, with the main novelty being the dual network structure and dynamic reward scheduling. The "contribution" reward is conceptually similar to value-based or success-based shaping in SORS, ROSA, and ReLara, but implemented via a second random network rather than auxiliary agents or classifiers.
- The dynamic adjustment from novelty to contribution is similar in spirit to meta-scheduling (SERENE) and self-balancing (Sibling Rivalry), though the mechanism differs.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**
- The authors consistently position prior work as either focusing *only* on novelty (e.g., RND) or *only* on value-based/contribution rewards (e.g., SORS, ROSA, ReLara), and claim DuRND uniquely integrates both in a scalable way.
- They emphasize DuRND’s low overhead, contrasting it with methods that require auxiliary agents, buffers, or pseudo-counts.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- **RND (Burda et al., 2018):** Accurately described as providing novelty-based intrinsic rewards. The authors state, "we extend this concept to propose the Dual Random Networks Distillation (DuRND...)"—this is a fair characterization, though the degree of extension is modest (adding a second network).
- **SORS (Memarian et al., 2021), ROSA (Mguni et al., 2023), ReLara (Ma et al., 2024b):** Described as "hidden value based RS approaches" that "primarily develop task-related signals to reveal the extent to which states contribute to achieving higher environmental rewards." This is accurate, but the authors underplay the fact that these methods also often include mechanisms for exploration (e.g., via trajectory ranking or success estimation).
- **#Exploration (Tang et al., 2017), ExploRS (Devidze et al., 2022):** Cited as count-based or exploration bonus methods, which is accurate.
- **Methods with auxiliary agents/buffers/pseudocounts:** The authors claim DuRND avoids these, which is accurate for their method, but the computational overhead of some prior methods (e.g., SORS, ROSA) is not always as high as implied.

**Discrepancies:**
- The authors sometimes overstate the dichotomy between "novelty-only" and "contribution-only" methods. For example, SORS and ROSA can blend exploration and exploitation signals, and some RND extensions (e.g., Never Give Up) already combine multiple intrinsic signals.
- The claim that DuRND is the first to "jointly compute novelty and contribution rewards" is somewhat overstated; prior work has combined multiple reward signals, though not always via dual random networks.

**Rhetoric:**
- The language ("remarkable scalability," "does not rely on auxiliary agents") is somewhat promotional and may overstate the practical differences in overhead or scalability.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

## Claimed Contribution 1: **Dual Random Networks for Joint Novelty and Contribution Rewards**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - RND (Burda et al., 2018): Single random network for novelty.
  - SORS (Memarian et al., 2021), ROSA (Mguni et al., 2023): Use classifiers or auxiliary agents for value/contribution-based shaping.
  - Never Give Up (Badia et al., 2020): Combines RND with episodic memory for directed exploration.

- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - DuRND uses *two* random networks: one for novelty, one for contribution, computed jointly and dynamically.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The use of two random networks is a straightforward extension of RND. The "contribution" network is novel in the sense that it uses a random network to estimate state "contribution" (success/failure), but the conceptual idea of shaping rewards based on success or value is well-established (SORS, ROSA, ReLara).
  - The main technical difference is the use of random networks for both signals, rather than a classifier or auxiliary agent for contribution.
  - The dual-network structure is not fundamentally more complex than RND, and the computational overhead is indeed low.

- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The difference is more architectural (two networks instead of one) than conceptual; the idea of combining exploration and exploitation signals is not new, but the specific implementation via dual random networks is novel in this context.

## Claimed Contribution 2: **Dynamic Reward Adjustment from Novelty to Contribution**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - Sibling Rivalry (Trott et al., 2019): Self-balancing reward shaping.
  - SERENE: Meta-scheduler for exploration/exploitation.
  - SASR, SORS: Adaptive reward shaping based on learning progress.

- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - DuRND dynamically shifts the reward structure from exploration (novelty) to exploitation (contribution) as learning progresses.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The idea of dynamically adjusting the balance between exploration and exploitation is present in Sibling Rivalry, SERENE, and SASR, though the mechanisms differ (meta-scheduling, self-balancing, kernel density estimation).
  - DuRND’s approach (using the outputs of two random networks and a scheduling function) is a new instantiation, but the underlying principle is not unique.

- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The scheduling mechanism is a routine adaptation of existing ideas; the novelty lies in the specific use of dual random networks for this purpose.

## Claimed Contribution 3: **Low Computational Overhead and Scalability**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - RND, DRND, #Exploration: All emphasize low overhead.
  - SORS, ROSA, ReLara: May require auxiliary agents or classifiers.

- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - DuRND requires only two random networks, no auxiliary agents, buffers, or pseudo-counts.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - This claim is accurate relative to SORS, ROSA, and ReLara, which can have higher overhead. However, RND and #Exploration are also low-overhead.
  - The difference is incremental: DuRND is as scalable as RND, with only a minor increase in computation.

- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The improvement is practical but not conceptually transformative.

## Claimed Contribution 4: **Superior Performance and Scalability in High-Dimensional, Sparse-Reward Environments**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - RND, DRND, SORS, ROSA, ReLara, #Exploration.

- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - DuRND outperforms these baselines on Atari, VizDoom, MiniWorld.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Empirical improvements may be due to implementation details, hyperparameter tuning, or the specific reward schedule, rather than the dual-network architecture per se.
  - The field is known for high variance in results; small architectural changes can yield performance gains.

- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The performance gains are evidence of practical value, but the conceptual advance is modest.

  - Emphasis on empirical performance in hard exploration benchmarks.

# 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Differences:**  
  - DuRND introduces a dual random network architecture to compute both novelty and contribution rewards, with a dynamic scheduling mechanism.
  - The use of random networks for both signals is new, but the underlying concepts (novelty, contribution, dynamic scheduling) are well-established.

- **Main Relationships to Existing Research:**  
  - DuRND is a direct extension of RND, with similarities to SORS, ROSA, and ReLara in its use of contribution/success-based shaping.
  - The dynamic reward adjustment is similar in spirit to Sibling Rivalry, SERENE, and SASR.

- **Strongest Differentiation:**  
  - The architectural simplicity (two random networks, no auxiliary agents) and low overhead are practical advantages.
  - The specific use of random networks for both novelty and contribution is a novel implementation detail.

- **Weakest Differentiation:**  
  - The conceptual advance is modest; the combination of exploration and exploitation signals, and dynamic scheduling, are not new.
  - The performance gains may be due to implementation details rather than fundamental innovation.

- **Discrepancies Between Author Characterizations and Independent Assessment:**  
  - The claim of unique joint computation of novelty and contribution is overstated; prior work has blended such signals.
  - The dichotomy between prior methods is somewhat exaggerated.
  - The practical differences in scalability and overhead are incremental, not transformative.

---

**Overall, DuRND is a practical and well-engineered extension of RND, offering a new dual-network implementation for balancing exploration and exploitation. Its main novelty is architectural rather than conceptual, and its empirical gains, while promising, should be interpreted in the context of a field where incremental advances are common and performance is sensitive to implementation details.**
