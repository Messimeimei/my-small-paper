This paper presents DuRND, a novel exploration method for reinforcement learning that builds upon Random Network Distillation (RND). DuRND introduces a dual network structure to differentiate between "successful" and "failed" states, aiming to guide exploration towards promising areas. Some reviewers considered the contribution incremental compared to RND, suggesting the core idea might not be entirely novel. Experiments across various environments demonstrate DuRND's potential to outperform existing novelty-seeking and reward-shaping methods.
