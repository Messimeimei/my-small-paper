# METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

**Main Approaches and Clusters:**

1. **Contrastive Learning-Based Methods**
   - *Papers*: Submission, Related 1 (AutoAugOOD), 2 (OOD-aware SupCon), 3 (OAL), 8 (CSI), 16 (CPC), 17 (MSC)
   - *Description*: These methods leverage contrastive objectives to learn representations that separate in-distribution (ID) from out-of-distribution (OOD) or anomalous data. Variations include:
     - **Adaptive/Hard Augmentations**: Submission, AutoAugOOD, CSI use data-driven or hard augmentations to create challenging negative pairs or OOD-like samples.
     - **Supervised Contrastive Extensions**: OOD-aware SupCon extends supervised contrastive loss with OOD-aware terms and prototype-based classifiers.
     - **Latent/Mutual Information Contrastive Modules**: OAL and others use contrastive learning in latent space or with mutual information objectives.
     - **Patch-wise/Mean-Shifted Contrastive Losses**: CPC and MSC adapt contrastive losses for anomaly segmentation or fine-tuning pre-trained features.

2. **Outlier/OOD Synthesis and Exposure**
   - *Papers*: Submission, 3 (OAL), 4 (Score-based Gen), 5 (MixOOD), 7 (VOS), 10 (NPOS)
   - *Description*: These works generate synthetic OOD or anomalous data to regularize models:
     - **Feature-Space Synthesis**: VOS, NPOS, and OAL synthesize outliers in feature or latent space, often using density estimation or diffusion models.
     - **Pixel/Score-Based Synthesis**: Score-based generative models (Related 4) and Mixup-based approaches (MixOOD) create near-distribution or mixed OOD samples.
     - **Saliency-Guided Distortion**: The submission uniquely distorts core features identified by Grad-CAM, rather than global augmentations.

3. **Knowledge Distillation and Teacher-Student Frameworks**
   - *Papers*: Submission, 3 (OAL)
   - *Description*: The submission and OAL use teacher-student or knowledge distillation frameworks to transfer knowledge and align/diverge outputs for ID/OOD samples, enhancing robustness.

4. **Prototype/Classifier Innovations**
   - *Papers*: 2 (OOD-aware SupCon), 19 (MS-OOD)
   - *Description*: Prototype-based classifiers and model-specific detection frameworks are used to improve OOD detection, moving beyond standard softmax or thresholding.

5. **Unsupervised/One-Class and Density-Based Methods**
   - *Papers*: 6 (AD under Shift), 9 (Adversarial One-Class), 14 (LSA)
   - *Description*: These methods focus on unsupervised or one-class settings, using autoencoders, adversarial training, or density estimation in latent space.

6. **Domain Generalization and Style Perturbation**
   - *Papers*: Submission, 15 (Normalization Perturbation)
   - *Description*: Both address robustness to style/domain shifts, with the submission focusing on novelty detection under style shifts and Related 15 perturbing feature statistics to synthesize domain styles.

**Trends and Relationships:**
- **Contrastive learning** is a dominant paradigm, with increasing sophistication in negative pair generation (adaptive, hard, or synthesized).
- **Synthetic OOD data** is often generated in feature/latent space for efficiency and effectiveness, with a shift from pixel-space GANs to diffusion or non-parametric methods.
- **Knowledge distillation** and **teacher-student frameworks** are emerging for robust novelty/OOD detection, especially when OOD data is unavailable.
- **Prototype-based and model-specific classifiers** are gaining traction for their improved separation of ID/OOD.
- There is a **convergence** between anomaly/novelty detection, OOD detection, and domain generalization, with methods often applicable across these tasks.

# TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

**Progression of Ideas:**
- **Early OOD/Anomaly Detection**: Focused on one-class classification, autoencoders, and adversarial training (e.g., Related 9, 14).
- **Contrastive Learning Emergence**: CSI, CPC, and others showed the power of contrastive objectives, especially with hard or adaptive augmentations.
- **Synthetic OOD Data**: Shift from GAN-based pixel synthesis to feature/latent space synthesis (VOS, NPOS, OAL), with more efficient and effective regularization.
- **Knowledge Distillation and Teacher-Student**: Recent works (Submission, OAL) use teacher-student frameworks and knowledge distillation for robust detection without OOD data.
- **Domain Generalization and Style Robustness**: Methods like Normalization Perturbation and the submission’s saliency-guided distortions address real-world style/domain shifts.
- **Unified and Theoretical Frameworks**: Recent surveys and theoretical works (12, 13, 18, 19) clarify the relationships and boundaries between OOD, anomaly, novelty, and open-set recognition, and connect OOD detection to continual learning.

**Building Blocks and Extensions:**
- **Contrastive learning** is extended with adaptive/hard augmentations, OOD-aware losses, and prototype-based classifiers.
- **Outlier synthesis** evolves from pixel-space GANs to non-parametric and diffusion-based latent space methods.
- **Knowledge distillation** is adapted for OOD/novelty detection, not just for model compression.
- **Domain generalization** methods are now being integrated with OOD/novelty detection pipelines.

**Competing/Complementary Approaches:**
- **Contrastive vs. Outlier Synthesis**: Some methods focus on learning robust representations (contrastive), while others focus on generating challenging OOD data (synthesis); recent works often combine both.
- **Prototype-based vs. Softmax Classifiers**: Prototype-based classifiers are shown to be more effective for OOD detection in some settings.
- **Model-Specific vs. Universal Detection**: Some frameworks (MS-OOD) argue for model-specific detection, while others (AutoAugOOD) aim for universality across models and datasets.
