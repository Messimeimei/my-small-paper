This paper uses the concept of stability gap to explain the initial drop in LLM performance during continual pretraining in a new domain and proposes three training strategies to address the initial instability. The proposed strategies improve the accuracy of the LLM in the new domain when compared to the existing continual pretraining techniques. However, the drop itself does not look significant (less than 1% averaged accuracy), making the observation less strong, and the performance improvement when compared to the baseline seems to be less than 1%, which does not look very significant.
