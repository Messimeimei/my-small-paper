# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission addresses *efficient domain continual pre-training* of LLMs, focusing on the *stability gap* (initial performance drop) and proposing strategies to mitigate it.
- It is positioned within the **Efficient Continual Pre-training and Domain Adaptation** cluster, which is the most active and central area in the current landscape (see Cluster A in the research landscape).
- The submission’s methods—multi-epoch subset training, high-quality token selection, and data mixture matching—are all established or emerging strategies in this cluster.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Efficient Continual Pre-training for Building Domain Specific Large Language Models** (Related 1): Nearly identical in scope and methods; both focus on efficient continual pre-training, data selection, and mixture strategies.
- **PMC-LLaMA** and **MedAlpaca**: Medical LLMs using continual pre-training and instruction tuning, with some overlap in data selection and multi-epoch strategies.
- **Simple and Scalable Strategies to Continually Pre-train Large Language Models** (Ibrahim et al., 2024): Focuses on learning rate scheduling and replay to address continual pre-training challenges.
- **To Repeat or Not To Repeat** (Xue et al., 2024): Examines multi-epoch training and its effects on LLMs, including performance peaks and degradation.

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission’s strategies are not unique to it; multi-epoch subset training and data mixture are present in several recent works (e.g., Related 1, 7, 19, 20).
- The *stability gap* concept is borrowed from vision models but is now being discussed in LLMs (e.g., Xue et al., 2024; Ibrahim et al., 2024).
- Data selection and mixture strategies are a cross-cutting theme in both domain adaptation and data-centric clusters.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission’s focus on *initial performance drop* and *knowledge forgetting* aligns with the field’s concern for stability and catastrophic forgetting.
- Evaluation on both domain-specific (medical/legal) and general instruction-following tasks is standard in the field.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is well-placed within the mainstream of current research, with its main novelty claim being the explicit identification and systematic analysis of the *stability gap* in LLM continual pre-training.
- Its methods are incremental extensions or combinations of existing strategies, rather than fundamentally new techniques.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**
- Authors consistently cite prior works as *motivating* the need for continual pre-training (e.g., Wu et al., 2022; Fu et al., 2024; Zhuang et al., 2024) and as *examples* of domain adaptation.
- They position their work as the first to *systematically analyze* the stability gap in LLMs, referencing vision literature for the concept.
- Prior works on data selection (e.g., Liu et al., 2024, Deita) are cited as related but not as direct competitors to their high-quality token selection strategy.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The authors’ characterizations of prior works are generally accurate in terms of their high-level goals (e.g., domain adaptation, instruction tuning).
- However, there is a tendency to understate the sophistication of prior data selection and mixture strategies, and to overstate the novelty of their own systematic analysis of the stability gap.
- For example, the citation of Xue et al. (2024) and Muennighoff et al. (2024) in the context of multi-epoch training suggests that this is a known and studied approach, not unique to the submission.

**Discrepancies:**
- The claim that the submission is the *first* to identify and systematically analyze the stability gap in LLMs is not fully substantiated; Xue et al. (2024) and Ibrahim et al. (2024) have discussed similar phenomena, albeit perhaps with different terminology.
- The authors’ description of their data selection strategy as novel does not fully acknowledge the breadth of recent work on automatic data selection (e.g., Liu et al., 2024; Deita).

**Rhetoric:**
- The language used sometimes overstates the differences (“first to identify”, “introduce the stability gap concept”) when similar issues have been discussed in recent LLM literature, though perhaps not with the same framing.

- **Independent Assessment:**

- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The difference is primarily in framing and systematicity, not in the identification of a new phenomenon.
