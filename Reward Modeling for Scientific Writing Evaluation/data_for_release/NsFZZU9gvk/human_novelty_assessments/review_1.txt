This paper introduces a benchmark consisting of 100 harmful behaviors for red-teaming web agents, aiming to evaluate the alignment and safety of such agents in practical scenarios. While the authors appear to be among the first to propose a dedicated benchmark for red-teaming web agents by the submission deadline, the novelty of the contribution is limited. There is already a substantial body of related work in this domain, such as Tian et al. (2023), and the paper does not sufficiently discuss how its approach differs from or advances beyond these existing methods. Furthermore, the red-teaming methodology itself lacks novelty, as it primarily involves manually crafting or transforming examples from prior work, without introducing automated or agent-specific techniques. Simply designing a set of 100 examples for red-teaming does not constitute a significant advance, and the paper would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with prior toolkits and a clearer articulation of its unique contributions. Overall, while the benchmark is an early effort in this direction, its incremental nature and lack of methodological innovation limit its impact.
