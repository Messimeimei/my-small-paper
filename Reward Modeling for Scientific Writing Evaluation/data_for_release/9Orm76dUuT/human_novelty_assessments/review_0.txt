This paper presents an approach that extends visual universal adversarial attacks, originally designed for image classification, to large multi-modal models (MLLMs) by optimizing adversarial perturbations in conjunction with a predefined trigger token to elicit specific harmful responses. First, to the best of my knowledge, no prior work has explored attacks in this particular setting, combining adversarial perturbations on images with a triggering token. However, given that existing approaches, such as Dong et al. (2023b), have already demonstrated that visual universal adversarial perturbations (UAPs) can be applied to MLLMs (potentially in a black-box setting as well), the technical novelty of this work appears somewhat limited, especially so given the limited transferability. From a practical perspective, the lack of cross-model transferability significantly reduces the attack's relevance.
