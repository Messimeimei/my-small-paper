## 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Research Landscape:**

The submission, "Test-Time BACKDOOR ATTACKS ON MULTIMODAL LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS," is situated at the intersection of several active research clusters: (a) test-time backdoor attacks, (b) multimodal (vision-language) model security, and (c) black-box attack scenarios. The most closely related prior works are those that:

- Propose test-time or prompt-based backdoor attacks on LLMs (e.g., BadChain, ICLAttack, Composite Backdoor Attack, Jailbreak in Pieces, Visual Role-Play).
- Target multimodal models, especially those using both visual and textual modalities (e.g., BadCLIP, Backdooring Multimodal Learning, Data Poisoning Attacks Against Multimodal Encoders).
- Explore attacks that do not require access to training data or model parameters (e.g., Xiang et al., 2024; Zhao et al., 2024; Zou et al., 2024; Kandpal et al., 2023).

**Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission aligns most closely with the "Test-Time and Prompt-Based Attacks/Defenses" and "Multimodal Backdoor Attacks/Defenses" clusters.
- Its main methodological novelty claim is the decomposition of backdoor setup and activation across modalities, leveraging the visual modality for setup (via universal adversarial perturbations) and the textual modality for activation (via prompt manipulation).

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The problem addressed—test-time backdoor attacks on MLLMs without training data/model access—reflects a realistic and increasingly studied threat model.
- Evaluation metrics (attack success rate, robustness under corruption, ablation on perturbation budgets) are standard and comparable to those in related work.

**Independent Assessment:**
- While the submission claims to be the "first" to demonstrate test-time backdoor attacks on MLLMs without training data/model access, several recent works (e.g., BadChain, ICLAttack, Jailbreak in Pieces, Visual Role-Play) have explored similar black-box, test-time, or prompt-based attacks, including in multimodal settings.
- The specific decomposition of setup/activation across modalities and the use of universal adversarial perturbations for visual triggers may represent a technical variation, but the overall attack paradigm is an extension of existing lines of work.

## 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**

- The authors consistently position their work as extending prior backdoor attacks from unimodal (text or vision) or training-time settings to test-time, multimodal, and black-box scenarios.
- They cite works on test-time attacks in LLMs (e.g., Xiang et al., 2024; Zhao et al., 2024; Zou et al., 2024; Kandpal et al., 2023) and multimodal backdoor attacks (e.g., BadCLIP, Carlini & Terzis, 2022; Jia et al., 2022), often emphasizing that these do not address the specific combination of test-time, multimodal, and black-box constraints.

**Accuracy and Balance:**

- In several cases, the authors accurately describe the general focus of prior work (e.g., "backdoor attacks against multimodal contrastive learning" for BadCLIP).
- However, there is a tendency to understate the flexibility or generality of prior test-time or prompt-based attacks, especially those that have already explored black-box or multimodal settings (e.g., Jailbreak in Pieces, Visual Role-Play).
- For example, the claim that prior test-time attacks do not address multimodal models is not fully accurate: Jailbreak in Pieces and Visual Role-Play both target MLLMs at test time, using compositional or role-playing triggers.

**Discrepancies:**

- The authors' claim of being the "first" to demonstrate test-time backdoor attacks on MLLMs without training data/model access is not fully substantiated, as at least Jailbreak in Pieces and Visual Role-Play have demonstrated related attacks in similar settings.
- The characterization of prior work as "limited to training-time" or "unimodal" is sometimes overstated, given the recent proliferation of test-time, multimodal, and prompt-based attacks.

**Rhetorical Patterns:**

- The authors use language that emphasizes novelty ("first demonstration," "introduce a new paradigm") and downplays the scope of prior work, sometimes by omission (not citing Jailbreak in Pieces, Visual Role-Play, or BDetCLIP) or by generalization.

## 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

### Claimed Contribution 1: **First demonstration of test-time backdoor attacks on MLLMs without access to training data or model parameters**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Jailbreak in Pieces (Bailey et al., 2023), Visual Role-Play, BadChain, ICLAttack.
- **Critical Examination:** Jailbreak in Pieces and Visual Role-Play both demonstrate test-time attacks on MLLMs, using compositional or role-playing triggers, without access to training data or model parameters. BadChain and ICLAttack also explore test-time, black-box attacks, though primarily in LLMs.
- **Actual Delta:** The main technical difference is the explicit decomposition of setup (visual) and activation (textual) and the use of universal adversarial perturbations for the visual trigger. However, the general paradigm of test-time, black-box, multimodal backdoor attacks is not entirely new.
- **Author vs. Independent Assessment:** The claim of being the "first" is overstated; the contribution is better described as a specific instantiation or extension of existing test-time, multimodal attack paradigms.

### Claimed Contribution 2: **Decomposition of backdoor setup (visual) and activation (textual) for flexibility/control**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Jailbreak in Pieces (compositional triggers), Visual Role-Play (role-based triggers), BadCLIP (dual-modality triggers).
- **Critical Examination:** Prior works have explored compositional triggers (Jailbreak in Pieces) and cross-modal triggers (BadCLIP), but the explicit separation of setup and activation across modalities, as operationalized in AnyDoor, appears to be a novel technical detail.
- **Actual Delta:** The decomposition is a technical refinement, allowing for more flexible and dynamic control, but is conceptually similar to compositional or cross-modal triggers in prior work.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The difference is more in the operationalization and flexibility than in the fundamental attack paradigm.

### Claimed Contribution 3: **Dynamic modification of trigger prompts and harmful effects at test time**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Jailbreak in Pieces (dynamic, compositional triggers), Visual Role-Play (role-based, context-adaptive triggers).
- **Critical Examination:** Prior works have demonstrated dynamic or context-adaptive triggers, though perhaps not with the same explicit mechanism of universal adversarial perturbations plus prompt manipulation.
- **Actual Delta:** The ability to change both the visual perturbation and the textual prompt at test time is a technical extension, but the general idea of dynamic, compositional triggers is present in prior work.
- **Implementation vs. Conceptual Advance:** The improvement may be more about implementation flexibility than a fundamentally new attack concept.

## 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Field Maturity:** The area of backdoor attacks/defenses in (multimodal) LLMs is highly active and rapidly evolving, with many papers published in the last 1–2 years.
- **Recent Surveys:** No comprehensive survey is cited in the submission, but several recent literature reviews exist (e.g., "A Survey on Backdoor Attacks and Defenses in Vision-Language Models," arXiv 2023).
- **Trends:** The field is moving toward more realistic, black-box, and test-time threat models, with increasing focus on multimodal and compositional attacks/defenses.
- **Incremental Advances:** Many recent papers propose new variants or extensions of attack/defense paradigms, often differing in trigger design, modality, or operational constraints. Incremental advances are common, and claims of "first" or "novel" should be carefully scrutinized.

## 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Overstated Novelty:** The claim of being the "first" to demonstrate test-time backdoor attacks on MLLMs without training data/model access is not fully supported, given prior work (Jailbreak in Pieces, Visual Role-Play).
- **Alignment of Claims and Evidence:** The main technical novelty is the explicit decomposition of setup/activation and the use of universal adversarial perturbations, which is a refinement rather than a paradigm shift.
- **Empirical Improvements:** Any observed improvements in attack success rate or flexibility may be due to implementation details (e.g., choice of perturbation method) rather than conceptual advances.
- **Terminology Differences:** The use of "decomposition" and "dynamic modification" may mask similarities to compositional or adaptive triggers in prior work.
- **Routine Adaptations:** The extension to new models or datasets is a routine adaptation, not a conceptual novelty.

## 6. RELATED WORK CONSIDERATIONS

- **Omitted Relevant Work:** The submission does not cite several highly relevant recent works, including:
  - Jailbreak in Pieces (Bailey et al., 2023): Test-time, compositional attacks on MLLMs.
  - Visual Role-Play: Universal jailbreak attack on MLLMs via role-playing images.
  - BDetCLIP, AdaShield, Mask and Restore: Recent test-time defenses for multimodal backdoor attacks.
- **Need for Additional Comparisons:** Direct empirical and conceptual comparison with Jailbreak in Pieces and Visual Role-Play is necessary to clarify the actual delta.
- **Potentially Misleading Characterizations:** The claim that prior work is "limited to training-time" or "unimodal" is not fully accurate.
- **Exaggerated Limitations:** The limitations of prior test-time, multimodal attacks are sometimes exaggerated to emphasize the submission's novelty.

## 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Differences:** The explicit decomposition of backdoor setup (visual) and activation (textual), and the use of universal adversarial perturbations for the visual trigger, are the main technical refinements over prior work.
- **Main Relationships to Existing Research:** The submission is an extension of the rapidly growing line of test-time, black-box, and multimodal backdoor attacks, closely related to Jailbreak in Pieces, Visual Role-Play, BadChain, and BadCLIP.
- **Strongest Differentiation:** The operational flexibility enabled by decomposing setup/activation and dynamic modification of triggers.
- **Weakest Differentiation:** The overall attack paradigm (test-time, black-box, multimodal backdoor attacks) is already established in the literature.
- **Discrepancies:** The claim of being the "first" is not substantiated; several recent works have demonstrated similar attacks. The omission of key related work and the characterization of prior work as "unimodal" or "training-time only" are not fully accurate.
- **Reviewer Guidance:** The main contribution is a technical refinement and operational extension, not a paradigm shift. The field is highly active, and incremental advances are common; claims of novelty should be calibrated accordingly.
