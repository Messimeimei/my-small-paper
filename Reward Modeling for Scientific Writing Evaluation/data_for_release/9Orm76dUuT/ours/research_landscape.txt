# PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

**Key Problems Addressed:**
1. **Backdoor Attacks on LLMs and MLLMs:**
   - How to implant or trigger malicious behaviors in large (multimodal) language models, often without access to training data or model parameters.
2. **Backdoor Defenses:**
   - How to detect, mitigate, or remove backdoors, especially in black-box or test-time settings.
3. **Adversarial Robustness:**
   - How robust are VLMs/MLLMs to adversarial or backdoor attacks, and what factors influence this robustness?
4. **Supply Chain Security:**
   - Can plugins/adapters be used to compromise LLMs, and how can this be detected or prevented?
5. **Cross-Modal Vulnerabilities:**
   - How do attacks/defenses differ when targeting single vs. multiple modalities?

**Approaches to Similar Problems:**
- **Backdoor Attacks:** Vary in trigger type (single vs. composite, visual vs. textual, prompt-based, chain-of-thought), attack phase (training vs. test-time), and required access (white-box vs. black-box).
- **Defenses:** Range from data purification (BDMAE), prompt-based shielding (AdaShield), trigger inversion (TIJO), to attention distillation (NAD).
- **Robustness Evaluation:** Some works focus on empirical evaluation (On Evaluating Adversarial Robustness...), while others analyze the impact of pre-training objectives (Effective Backdoor Mitigation...).

**Patterns in Problem Formulation:**
- Increasingly realistic threat models: attacks/defenses are designed for black-box, API-only, or supply chain scenarios.
- Multimodal and compositionality: attacks/defenses often consider the interplay between modalities, not just unimodal settings.
- Emphasis on stealth and generalizability: attacks aim to be stealthy (imperceptible, context-aware) and generalize across tasks/models.

# TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

**Progression of Ideas:**
- **From Training-Time to Test-Time Attacks:** Early works focused on poisoning during training; recent works (AnyDoor, BadChain, ICLAttack) show that attacks can be performed at test time, even in black-box settings.
- **From Unimodal to Multimodal:** The field has evolved from text-only or vision-only attacks to sophisticated multimodal attacks/defenses, reflecting the rise of MLLMs and VLMs.
- **From Simple to Composite/Stealthy Triggers:** Triggers have evolved from simple patterns to composite, context-aware, or dynamic triggers (Composite Backdoor Attack, Hidden Backdoors, AnyDoor).
- **From White-Box to Black-Box Threat Models:** There is a clear shift toward attacks/defenses that do not require model internals, reflecting real-world deployment scenarios.
- **From Static to Adaptive Defenses:** Defenses are moving from static rules to adaptive, prompt-based, or generative approaches (AdaShield, BDMAE, TIJO).

**Building Blocks and Extensions:**
- **Contrastive Learning Defenses:** CleanCLIP builds on the vulnerabilities exposed by BadCLIP and similar attacks.
- **Prompt-Based Defenses:** AdaShield and BDetCLIP extend prompt engineering from attacks to defenses.
- **Trigger Inversion and Attention Distillation:** TIJO and NAD represent new directions in understanding and reversing backdoor effects.

**Competing and Complementary Approaches:**
- **Attack vs. Defense Arms Race:** Many defenses are direct responses to new attack strategies, and vice versa.
- **Complementarity:** Some methods (e.g., prompt-based defenses) can be combined with other approaches (e.g., data purification) for layered security.
- **Competing Paradigms:** Some works focus on detection (BDetCLIP, BDMAE), others on removal/mitigation (CleanCLIP, TIJO, NAD), and some on prevention (pre-training defenses).
