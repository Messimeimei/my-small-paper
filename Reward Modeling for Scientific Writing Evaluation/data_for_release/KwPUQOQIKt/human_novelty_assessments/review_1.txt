This paper introduces OmegaPRM, a divide-and-conquer Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) algorithm designed to automate the collection of process supervision data for large language models (LLMs), with the goal of efficiently identifying the first error in a reasoning chain and thereby improving LLM performance on mathematical reasoning tasks. However, the novelty of OmegaPRM is limited, as the approach primarily combines established techniques—namely, MCTS and binary search. While the integration of these methods streamlines the data collection process and reduces reliance on human annotation, the core algorithmic contribution does not represent a significant departure from existing strategies in the literature. The paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison with prior work that leverages similar divide-and-conquer or search-based approaches, as well as a clearer articulation of how OmegaPRM advances beyond the straightforward combination of MCTS and binary search. Overall, the contribution is incremental, and the novelty is constrained by the reliance on well-known methods.
