## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the following methodological paradigms emerge:

- **Large Language Model (LLM)-Driven Algorithm/Heuristic Generation:**  
  - LLMs are used to generate, evolve, or optimize algorithms and heuristics for combinatorial optimization (CO) problems (e.g., LLM4Solver, AutoSAT, EoH, AEL, ReEvo, OPRO, LLM A*).
- **Evolutionary and Derivative-Free Optimization:**  
  - Evolutionary algorithms, often in combination with LLMs, are used to search the space of algorithms or heuristics (e.g., LLM4Solver, EoH, AEL, ReEvo, AutoSAT).
- **Neural Network-Based Learning:**  
  - Deep learning models (GCNNs, GNNs, DRL, diffusion models) are trained to learn policies, heuristics, or solution distributions for CO (e.g., Neural Diving/Branching, DeepACO, Sym-NCO, DIFUSCO, GNN-guided frameworks).
- **Reinforcement Learning (RL):**  
  - RL is used for learning presolve routines, branching policies, or regularization (e.g., RL4Presolve, Sym-NCO, DeepACO).
- **Hybrid and Search-Based Methods:**  
  - Integration of neural models with classical search (e.g., SGBS, LLM A*), or trust-region search around ML predictions (GNN-guided predict-and-search).

### Methodological Clusters

**Cluster 1: LLM-Driven Algorithm/Heuristic Generation and Evolution**
- **Papers:** LLM4Solver, AutoSAT, EoH, AEL, ReEvo, OPRO, LLM A*
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Use LLMs to generate or optimize algorithms/heuristics, often with evolutionary or search-based frameworks.
  - Some focus on direct code generation (LLM4Solver, AEL), others on heuristic modification (AutoSAT), or open-ended heuristic spaces (ReEvo).
  - OPRO and LLM A* use LLMs as optimizers or in human-in-the-loop settings.

**Cluster 2: Neural Network-Based Learning for CO**
- **Papers:** Neural Diving/Branching, DeepACO, Sym-NCO, GCNN for MILP, DIFUSCO, GNN-guided predict-and-search
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Employ deep learning (GCNN, GNN, DRL, diffusion models) to learn policies, heuristics, or solution distributions.
  - Some focus on learning branching/assignment policies (Neural Diving/Branching, GCNN), others on automating meta-heuristics (DeepACO), or leveraging problem symmetries (Sym-NCO).

**Cluster 3: Reinforcement Learning and Hybrid Search**
- **Papers:** RL4Presolve, SGBS, GNN-guided predict-and-search
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Use RL for presolve routine design or as a component in hybrid search frameworks.
  - Hybrid search methods combine ML predictions with classical search (SGBS, GNN-guided predict-and-search).

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **LLMs as Algorithm Designers:**  
  - There is a clear trend toward using LLMs not just for solution generation, but for the higher-level task of algorithm or heuristic design, often in a derivative-free, evolutionary, or reflective search framework.
- **Integration of ML and Classical Search:**  
  - Many works combine ML-based predictions with classical search or optimization (e.g., GNN-guided predict-and-search, SGBS, LLM A*).
- **Automating and Generalizing Heuristic Design:**  
  - Both LLM and neural network-based approaches aim to reduce human effort and domain knowledge requirements, seeking generalizable, automated solutions.
- **Sample and Training Efficiency:**  
  - Recent methods (EoH, ReEvo, LLM4Solver) emphasize efficiency in terms of computational budget, LLM queries, or training iterations.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Automatic Algorithm/Heuristic Design for CO:**  
  - Automating the design of algorithms or heuristics for combinatorial optimization, reducing reliance on expert knowledge (LLM4Solver, EoH, AEL, AutoSAT, ReEvo, DeepACO).
- **Improving Solver Efficiency and Solution Quality:**  
  - Enhancing the efficiency, solution quality, and generalization of CO solvers (LLM4Solver, Neural Diving/Branching, GCNN, DIFUSCO, SGBS).
- **Reducing Manual Tuning and Domain Knowledge:**  
  - Lowering the barrier for algorithm design by minimizing manual configuration and domain-specific expertise (LLM4Solver, AutoSAT, EoH, DeepACO).
- **Generalization Across Problem Instances and Types:**  
  - Ensuring that learned or generated algorithms generalize well to unseen or heterogeneous problem instances (LLM4Solver, AEL, DeepACO, GCNN).
- **Derivative-Free and Black-Box Optimization:**  
  - Tackling optimization problems where gradients are unavailable or the problem is a black box (LLM4Solver, OPRO, ReEvo).
- **Explainability, Transparency, and Human-in-the-Loop:**  
  - Making algorithmic decisions more transparent and accessible, sometimes involving human feedback (LLM A*).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Algorithm/Heuristic Design:**  
  - LLM4Solver, EoH, AEL, and ReEvo all automate algorithm/heuristic design, but differ in their use of LLMs (direct code generation vs. heuristic modification), evolutionary strategies, and the level of human intervention.
- **Solver Efficiency:**  
  - Neural Diving/Branching and GCNN focus on learning policies for specific solver components (branching, variable assignment), while LLM4Solver and EoH aim to generate entire algorithms or heuristics.
- **Generalization:**  
  - AEL and DeepACO explicitly benchmark generalization to larger or different problem instances, while LLM4Solver demonstrates cross-benchmark generalization.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Component-Level to Algorithm-Level Automation:**  
  - Early works focus on automating specific solver components (branching, presolve), while recent LLM-based methods target the design of entire algorithms or heuristics.
- **From Supervised to Derivative-Free and Evolutionary Search:**  
  - There is a shift from supervised learning (imitation of expert policies) to derivative-free, evolutionary, or black-box search methods, especially in LLM-driven approaches.
- **Emphasis on Efficiency and Scalability:**  
  - Newer methods highlight not just solution quality, but also efficiency (training, inference, sample usage) and scalability to large or diverse problem sets.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **MIPLIB 2017:**  
  - Used by LLM4Solver and Neural Diving/Branching for benchmarking MIP solvers.
- **TSP and Routing Problems:**  
  - TSP (various sizes), CVRP, and related benchmarks are widely used (AEL, Sym-NCO, ReEvo, DIFUSCO, SGBS, DeepACO).
- **SAT Benchmarks:**  
  - AutoSAT uses 12 SAT datasets, including those for MiniSat and Kissat.
- **Other CO Benchmarks:**  
  - Online bin packing, set covering, combinatorial auction, facility location, and maximum independent set problems (EoH, GCNN, DIFUSCO).
- **Industry and Real-World Datasets:**  
  - Neural Diving/Branching and RL4Presolve use real-world datasets (e.g., Google, Huawei).

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Solution Quality:**  
  - Primal-dual gap, primal gap, optimality gap, solution cost/length.
- **Solver Efficiency:**  
  - Training/inference time, number of solved instances, sample efficiency, number of LLM queries.
- **Generalization:**  
  - Performance on unseen or larger instances, cross-benchmark generalization.
- **Task-Specific Metrics:**  
  - For path planning: search space, path optimality, safety (LLM A*).
  - For prompt optimization: task accuracy (OPRO).

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Direct Comparison to SOTA Solvers:**  
  - Most works compare against state-of-the-art human-designed and learning-based solvers (e.g., Gurobi, SCIP, MiniSat, Kissat).
- **Emphasis on Generalization and Efficiency:**  
  - Many papers report not just best-case performance, but also generalization, training efficiency, and sample usage.
- **Ablation and Component Analysis:**  
  - Some works (e.g., DeepACO, ReEvo) analyze the contribution of individual components or extensions.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **LLM-Based Methods:**  
  - Focus on sample/training efficiency, generalization, and performance across diverse benchmarks.
- **Neural Network-Based Methods:**  
  - Emphasize solution quality, scalability, and comparison to expert policies.
- **Hybrid and RL Methods:**  
  - Highlight efficiency improvements and rule extraction for deployment.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Manual to Automated Heuristic/Algorithm Design:**  
  - Early works focused on automating specific solver components (branching, presolve) using supervised or RL-based learning.
  - Recent works (LLM4Solver, EoH, AEL, ReEvo) move toward full automation of algorithm/heuristic design using LLMs and evolutionary search, reducing the need for domain knowledge.
- **From Supervised to Derivative-Free and Evolutionary Search:**  
  - There is a shift from supervised imitation of expert policies to derivative-free, black-box, and evolutionary search methods, especially in LLM-driven approaches.
- **Integration of LLMs and Evolutionary Search:**  
  - LLMs are increasingly used not just for solution generation, but as creative engines for algorithm/heuristic design, often guided by evolutionary or reflective search (LLM4Solver, EoH, ReEvo).
- **Sample and Training Efficiency:**  
  - Newer methods emphasize efficiency, both in terms of computational resources (EoH, LLM4Solver) and sample usage (ReEvo).
- **Generalization and Scalability:**  
  - There is a growing focus on ensuring that learned or generated algorithms generalize across problem types and scales (AEL, DeepACO, LLM4Solver).

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **LLM+Evolutionary Search:**  
  - LLM4Solver, EoH, AEL, and ReEvo extend the idea of using LLMs for code/heuristic generation by integrating evolutionary search for efficient exploration.
- **Neural Policy Learning:**  
  - Neural Diving/Branching, GCNN, and DeepACO build on the idea of learning solver policies, extending to larger scales and more diverse problem types.
- **Hybrid and Reflective Search:**  
  - SGBS and ReEvo introduce hybrid and reflective search strategies, combining ML predictions with classical or evolutionary search.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **LLM-Driven vs. Neural Network-Based:**  
  - LLM-driven methods compete with neural network-based approaches in automating algorithm design, but may be more flexible and require less domain-specific training.
- **Evolutionary vs. RL-Based Search:**  
  - Evolutionary search (LLM4Solver, EoH, ReEvo) and RL-based methods (RL4Presolve, Sym-NCO) offer alternative strategies for exploring algorithm spaces.
- **Human-in-the-Loop vs. Fully Automated:**  
  - LLM A* and similar approaches offer complementary benefits in transparency and accessibility, compared to fully automated methods.

# Conclusion

The research landscape is rapidly evolving from component-level automation using neural networks and RL, toward holistic, LLM-driven algorithm and heuristic design frameworks that emphasize efficiency, generalization, and minimal human intervention. The integration of LLMs with evolutionary and reflective search is a defining trend, with complementary advances in neural network-based learning and hybrid search strategies. Evaluation practices are converging on standardized benchmarks and metrics, with increasing attention to generalization, efficiency, and transparency. The field is moving toward more general, automated, and accessible approaches for combinatorial optimization solver design.
