# TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

**Progression of Ideas:**

1. **From Unimodal to Multimodal:** Early work focused on text (clinicalBERT, Related 8), then expanded to include labs, images, and codes (Related 1, 3, Submission).
2. **From Simple Fusion to Explicit Alignment:** Initial approaches concatenated features; recent work (Submission, Related 2, 3, 6, 10, 13) uses attention, NAS, and graph-based alignment for more effective integration.
3. **From Flat to Structured/Graph Modeling:** GNNs and graph-based methods (Submission, Related 6, 7, 10) allow explicit modeling of relationships (patient similarity, knowledge, molecular structure).
4. **From Black-box to Explainable and Human-centered AI:** Increasing focus on explainability (SHAP, post hoc analysis), fairness, and participatory design (Related 3, 4, 11, 12, 14).
5. **From Handcrafted to Automated Architecture Search:** NAS (Related 2) and systematic fusion/alignment (Related 15) are replacing manual design.

**Building Blocks and Extensions:**

- *ClinicalBERT* (Related 8) is a foundation for many downstream models (Related 3, 9).
- *GNNs* are extended from patient graphs (Submission, Related 6) to knowledge graphs (Related 7) and molecular graphs (Related 10).
- *Fusion strategies* evolve from early/late fusion to hybrid, attention-based, and NAS-optimized methods.

**Competing/Complementary Approaches:**

- *GNN-enhanced LLMs* (Submission, Related 7) vs. *modality-specific encoders* (Related 1, 3): Both aim to enrich LLMs with structured knowledge, but differ in how relationships are modeled.
- *NAS-based fusion* (Related 2) vs. *manual/attention-based fusion* (Related 3, 6): NAS offers automation and potentially better performance, but at higher computational cost.
- *Human-centered design* (Related 12, 14) complements technical advances by ensuring usability and clinical relevance.

# SUMMARY

The research landscape represented by these papers is rapidly evolving toward **deep, explicit, and context-aware integration of multimodal data in healthcare AI**, with a strong emphasis on leveraging LLMs and GNNs. The field is moving from simple feature concatenation to sophisticated fusion/alignment strategies, often automated via NAS or guided by graph-based modeling. There is a parallel trend toward explainability, fairness, and human-centered design, ensuring that technical advances translate into real-world clinical impact. The submission paper (MG-LLM) exemplifies the cutting edge by unifying GNN-driven patient similarity modeling with LLM-based multimodal reasoning, setting a new standard for context-rich, accurate, and explainable healthcare prediction.
