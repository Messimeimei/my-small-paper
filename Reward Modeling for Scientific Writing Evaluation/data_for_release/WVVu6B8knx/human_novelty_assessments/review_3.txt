The paper proposes Supervised Batch Normalization (SBN), a novel normalization technique aimed at enhancing the effectiveness of Batch Normalization (BN) for diverse data distributions by introducing “contexts” to normalize different subsets of data using multiple means and variances. However, the novelty of SBN may be limited, as it could be perceived as a specific case of Mixture Normalization (MN) or Mode Normalization (ModeN). Additionally, the cited literature lacks references from recent years, and SBN lacks comparison with recent methods, which further calls into question the originality and significance of the contribution.
