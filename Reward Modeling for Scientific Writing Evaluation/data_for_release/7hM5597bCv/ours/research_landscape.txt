## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, several methodological themes emerge, especially in the context of **offline reinforcement learning (RL)**:

- **Diffusion Models for Policy/Trajectory Modeling**: Many recent works (DIAR, LDCQ, Diffusion-QL, IDQL, Merlin, DIPO, QVPO) leverage diffusion models to represent policies or trajectory distributions, motivated by their ability to model complex, multimodal distributions.
- **Implicit Q-Learning and Actor-Critic Variants**: Methods like IQL, IDQL, and DIAR (which builds on IQL) focus on implicit policy extraction from Q-functions, often avoiding explicit policy parameterization.
- **Latent/Skill-based Policy Spaces**: Several works (LDCQ, PLAS, CNF, SPiRL, OPAL) propose learning or leveraging latent spaces (via VAEs, normalizing flows, or unsupervised skill discovery) to constrain or structure policy learning.
- **Conservative/Regularized Q-Learning**: CQL, BEAR, and related methods introduce explicit regularization or constraints to prevent overestimation and out-of-distribution (OOD) action selection.
- **Sequence Modeling and Transformers**: Decision Transformer and Decision Mamba use sequence models (Transformers, State Space Models) to model RL as sequence prediction, enabling long-horizon credit assignment.
- **Policy Optimization for Diffusion Models**: DDPO and QVPO adapt RL policy optimization techniques to directly train diffusion models for downstream objectives.

### Method Clusters

- **Cluster 1: Diffusion Model-based RL**
  - **Papers**: DIAR, LDCQ, Diffusion-QL, IDQL, Merlin, DIPO, QVPO
  - **Description**: These methods use diffusion models to represent policies or trajectory distributions, enabling expressive, multimodal action modeling and improved OOD generalization.
  - **Trends**: Increasingly, diffusion models are used not just for generative modeling but as core policy representations, with innovations in how Q-learning or policy improvement is integrated into the diffusion process.

- **Cluster 2: Implicit/Conservative Q-Learning**
  - **Papers**: IQL, IDQL, CQL, BEAR, DIAR
  - **Description**: Focus on learning robust Q-functions and extracting policies implicitly, often with mechanisms to avoid OOD action evaluation and overestimation.
  - **Trends**: Emphasis on in-sample learning, expectile regression, and conservative value estimation.

- **Cluster 3: Latent/Skill-based Policy Spaces**
  - **Papers**: LDCQ, PLAS, CNF, SPiRL, OPAL
  - **Description**: Learn or utilize latent spaces (via VAEs, normalizing flows, or unsupervised skill discovery) to constrain policy learning, improve exploration, or enable temporal abstraction.
  - **Trends**: Shift from explicit action constraints to implicit, data-driven latent space constraints.

- **Cluster 4: Sequence Modeling for RL**
  - **Papers**: Decision Transformer, Decision Mamba
  - **Description**: Model RL as sequence prediction using Transformers or State Space Models, enabling flexible conditioning and long-horizon planning.

- **Cluster 5: Policy Optimization for Diffusion Models**
  - **Papers**: DDPO, QVPO, DIPO
  - **Description**: Adapt RL policy optimization (e.g., policy gradients, Q-weighted objectives) to directly train diffusion models for RL or generative tasks.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Diffusion models** are increasingly central, moving from generative modeling to direct policy representation and optimization in RL.
- **Implicit Q-learning** and **conservative value estimation** are foundational, with many newer methods (e.g., DIAR, IDQL) building on or generalizing these ideas.
- **Latent space modeling** is a recurring theme, with both generative (VAE, normalizing flows) and skill-based approaches aiming to constrain or structure policy learning.
- **Sequence modeling** (Transformers, SSMs) offers an alternative to value-based or policy-based RL, focusing on trajectory-level modeling and credit assignment.
- **Policy optimization for diffusion models** bridges generative modeling and RL, enabling direct optimization of downstream objectives.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Action/State Handling in Offline RL**: Central to almost all papers, addressing the challenge of learning effective policies from static datasets without querying unseen actions.
- **Long-Horizon, Sparse-Reward Decision Making**: Many works (DIAR, LDCQ, Decision Transformer, Merlin) focus on improving performance in tasks with long horizons and sparse rewards, where credit assignment and trajectory stitching are difficult.
- **Q-Value Overestimation and Extrapolation Error**: Addressed by conservative Q-learning (CQL), BEAR, and methods that constrain policy actions to the data distribution (PLAS, CNF).
- **Policy Robustness and Generalization**: Ensuring learned policies generalize beyond the training data, especially in the presence of multimodal or suboptimal behavior policies.
- **Efficient Policy Extraction and Regularization**: Improving the expressiveness and regularization of policy classes (Diffusion-QL, DIPO, QVPO) to avoid suboptimal solutions.
- **Temporal Abstraction and Skill Transfer**: Leveraging skills or primitives (SPiRL, OPAL) to accelerate learning and reduce effective horizon.
- **Efficient Finetuning and Online Adaptation**: Addressing the challenge of transitioning from offline to online RL (Finetuning from Offline RL).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **OOD Handling**: 
  - **Conservative Q-learning** (CQL, BEAR) regularizes Q-values to avoid overestimation.
  - **Latent space constraints** (PLAS, CNF, LDCQ) restrict policy actions to the support of the dataset.
  - **Diffusion models** (DIAR, Diffusion-QL, IDQL) model the data distribution directly, enabling in-distribution sampling.
- **Long-Horizon/Sparse-Reward**:
  - **Sequence modeling** (Decision Transformer, Decision Mamba) and **diffusion-based trajectory modeling** (DIAR, LDCQ) enable better credit assignment and trajectory stitching.
  - **Skill/primitive learning** (SPiRL, OPAL) provides temporal abstraction to reduce horizon.
- **Policy Regularization**:
  - **Diffusion models** (Diffusion-QL, DIPO, QVPO) provide sample-based regularization, capturing multimodality.
  - **Implicit Q-learning** (IQL, IDQL) avoids explicit regularization by never querying OOD actions.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **Offline RL as Distribution Matching**: Many methods frame offline RL as matching the policy distribution to the data distribution, either explicitly (via KL/MMD) or implicitly (via latent spaces or diffusion models).
- **Trajectory-Level Modeling**: Increasing focus on modeling entire trajectories (not just state-action pairs), enabling better handling of long-horizon dependencies.
- **Skill/Primitive Discovery**: Unsupervised learning of skills/primitives from data is seen as a way to accelerate and structure downstream RL.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **D4RL Benchmarks**: The de facto standard for offline RL evaluation, including Maze2D, AntMaze, locomotion, and kitchen tasks.
- **MuJoCo**: Widely used for continuous control tasks.
- **Atari, OpenAI Gym**: Used for sequence modeling and general RL evaluation.
- **Real-robot and manipulation datasets**: Used in some works (PLAS, SPiRL) for real-world validation.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Normalized Score**: Standardized across D4RL and MuJoCo for cross-task comparison.
- **Task Success Rate**: Especially for goal-reaching and manipulation tasks.
- **Average Episode Return**: Common in continuous control and sequence modeling.
- **Sample Efficiency**: Measured as learning speed or performance per environment step.
- **Robustness to Hyperparameters**: Evaluated in some works (IDQL).
- **Computational Efficiency**: (e.g., Merlin) measures speedup over prior methods.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Benchmarking Against State-of-the-Art**: Most works compare to prior SOTA on D4RL/MuJoCo.
- **Emphasis on Long-Horizon/Sparse-Reward Tasks**: Many methods highlight improvements on the most challenging tasks (e.g., AntMaze, Kitchen).
- **Ablation and Robustness Studies**: Some works (IDQL, CQL) emphasize robustness to hyperparameters or data distribution.
- **Real-World Validation**: A few works (PLAS, SPiRL) include real-robot or manipulation experiments.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Diffusion-based methods** often emphasize performance on multimodal or sparse-reward tasks, where their expressiveness is most beneficial.
- **Latent/skill-based methods** highlight improvements in learning efficiency and transfer.
- **Conservative/implicit Q-learning methods** focus on robustness and avoiding OOD overestimation.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Conservative Q-Learning to Diffusion-based Policies**: Early works (CQL, BEAR, IQL) focus on conservative value estimation and in-sample learning. This foundation enables the safe use of more expressive policy classes, leading to the adoption of diffusion models (Diffusion-QL, LDCQ, DIAR).
- **From Explicit Constraints to Implicit Latent Spaces**: PLAS and CNF move from explicit action constraints (KL, MMD) to implicit constraints via learned latent spaces, improving flexibility and performance.
- **From Value-based to Sequence/Trajectory Modeling**: Decision Transformer and Decision Mamba represent a shift from value-based RL to sequence modeling, enabling direct credit assignment and flexible policy conditioning.
- **From Policy Extraction to Direct Policy Optimization**: Methods like DDPO and QVPO adapt RL optimization techniques to directly train diffusion models, bridging generative modeling and RL.
- **From Flat Policies to Hierarchical/Skill-based RL**: SPiRL and OPAL introduce unsupervised skill/primitive discovery, enabling temporal abstraction and transfer.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **IQL** serves as a building block for IDQL and DIAR, with extensions to diffusion-based policy extraction and adaptive revaluation.
- **Diffusion models** are extended from generative modeling (DIPO, DDPO) to policy representation (Diffusion-QL, LDCQ) and further to integrated RL frameworks (DIAR).
- **Latent space modeling** evolves from VAE-based (PLAS) to normalizing flows (CNF) and skill embeddings (SPiRL, OPAL).

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Diffusion-based vs. Latent/Skill-based**: Both aim to address OOD and multimodality, but diffusion models focus on expressiveness, while latent/skill-based methods emphasize structure and abstraction.
- **Conservative Q-learning vs. Sequence Modeling**: Conservative Q-learning methods focus on value estimation robustness, while sequence modeling methods (Decision Transformer) bypass value estimation entirely.
- **Policy Optimization for Diffusion Models**: DDPO and QVPO represent a complementary direction, applying RL optimization to generative models for both RL and non-RL tasks.
