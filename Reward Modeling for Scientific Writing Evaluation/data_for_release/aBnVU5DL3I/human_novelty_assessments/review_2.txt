This paper introduces SaMer, a scenario-aware multi-dimensional evaluator designed to assess the quality of Large Language Model (LLM) responses to open-ended questions, with a flexible framework that adaptively identifies and prioritizes relevant evaluation dimensions based on the query type. While the system effectively combines multiple components—including a large-scale fine-grained preference dataset, a frozen LLaMA-3-8B encoder, and specialized MLP heads for multi-label classification and pairwise preference learning—to create a comprehensive evaluation pipeline, the novelty from a methodological standpoint is limited. The individual techniques employed are standard within the field, and many of the data resources are derived or adapted from existing sources. The primary innovation lies in the application of these established methods to the scenario-aware, multi-dimensional evaluation context, rather than in the development of fundamentally new algorithms or architectures. As such, the contribution is mainly in integrating and tailoring known approaches to address the multifaceted nature of LLM evaluation, rather than in introducing novel methodological advances.
