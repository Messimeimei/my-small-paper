# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission (ICConv) is positioned within the "Synthetic Data Generation & Augmentation" cluster, focusing on large-scale, automated construction of conversational search datasets. Its stated novelty is the integration of multi-intent and context-aware features at scale, with a quality control pipeline leveraging a contrastive-learning-based dialogue discriminator.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **ConvTrans (Mao et al., 2022):** Automated transformation of web search sessions into conversational data.
- **Dialog Inpainting (Dai et al., 2022):** Converts web passages into dialogues by generating questions and using sentences as responses.
- **ConvSDG:** Session data generation for conversational search (not cited).
- **BlendX, MIntRec2.0:** Multi-intent, complex conversational datasets (not cited).
- **LAPS, MMConv:** Multimodal, multi-intent, and personalized conversational data (not cited).

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- **Data Construction & Augmentation:** ICConv’s pipeline (LLM-based query generation, query trees, reverse reformulation, dialogue discriminator) is methodologically similar to ConvTrans, ConvSDG, and Dialog Inpainting, all of which use automated or LLM-based methods for dataset creation.
- **Multi-Intent and Contextual Modeling:** BlendX and MIntRec2.0 explicitly address multi-intent and context, sometimes with multimodal data, which is also a focus of ICConv.
- **Quality Control:** The use of a dialogue discriminator for filtering is a recent trend, but not unique—other works (e.g., LAPS) use LLMs or discriminators for data quality.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses the scarcity of large-scale, high-quality, context- and intent-rich conversational search data, a widely recognized problem.
- Evaluation via human judgment and dataset statistics is standard; the use of six dialogue/search criteria is in line with recent works.

**Independent Assessment:**
- ICConv is a direct extension of the automated data construction paradigm, with incremental advances in multi-intent/contextualization and quality control.
- The field is active, with several recent datasets and methods targeting similar goals, some of which are not cited in the submission.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**ConvTrans (Mao et al., 2022):**
- **Author Characterization:** "Another approach attempts to convert existing web search sessions... into conversations."
- **Citation Context:** Cited as an automated dataset construction method using web search sessions.
- **Independent Assessment:** Accurate in describing the method, but the submission does not discuss the specifics of ConvTrans’s approach to multi-intent or context, nor does it clarify how ICConv’s pipeline substantively differs (e.g., in query tree construction or quality control).
- **Potential Discrepancy:** The submission implies ConvTrans lacks multi-intent/contextualization, but ConvTrans does model session context and multi-turn interactions, though perhaps not with the same granularity.

**Dialog Inpainting (Dai et al., 2022):**
- **Author Characterization:** "Converting a large number of web passages into dialogues... using sentences as responses and generating questions."
- **Citation Context:** Cited as a method for generating dialogue data from passages.
- **Independent Assessment:** Accurate, but the submission does not address whether Dialog Inpainting supports multi-intent or context-aware dialogue, nor does it compare the quality control or filtering steps.
- **Potential Discrepancy:** The submission does not acknowledge that Dialog Inpainting can generate context-dependent dialogues, though perhaps not with explicit multi-intent modeling.

**QReCC (Anantha et al., 2020):**
- **Author Characterization:** Used as a source for query reformulation pairs.
- **Citation Context:** Cited as a dataset for training query reformulation models.
- **Independent Assessment:** Accurate; QReCC is a standard resource for query rewriting, but is not a data construction method per se.

**General Patterns:**
- The authors position their work as a step beyond prior automated dataset construction methods, emphasizing multi-intent/contextualization and quality control.
- There is a tendency to understate the contextual/multi-intent capabilities of prior works (e.g., ConvTrans, Dialog Inpainting).
- Several highly relevant recent works (ConvSDG, BlendX, MIntRec2.0, LAPS) are not cited or discussed, despite clear methodological and problem-space overlap.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

## Claimed Contribution 1: **First large-scale synthetic conversational search dataset with multi-intent and context-aware features**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** BlendX, MIntRec2.0, ConvTrans, ConvSDG
- **Claimed Difference:** Prior datasets lack both scale and explicit multi-intent/contextualization.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Scale:** ICConv’s "over 100,000" conversations is large, but BlendX and MIntRec2.0 also offer large-scale, multi-intent datasets (though sometimes multimodal).
  - **Multi-Intent/Context:** BlendX and MIntRec2.0 explicitly annotate multi-intent and context; ConvTrans and ConvSDG model session context, though perhaps not with explicit intent labels.
  - **Delta:** The main difference is the specific combination of automated multi-intent query generation, context-aware session construction, and scale. However, the claim of being "first" is overstated given the existence of BlendX, MIntRec2.0, and ConvSDG.
  - **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The submission does not acknowledge these recent datasets, which weakens the "first" claim.

## Claimed Contribution 2: **Novel automated data construction method (multi-intent query generation, query trees, reverse reformulation)**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** ConvTrans, ConvSDG, Dialog Inpainting, LAPS
- **Claimed Difference:** Prior methods do not generate multiple natural language queries per keyword query based on latent intents, nor do they contextualize them within sessions using query trees and reverse reformulation.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Query Generation:** ConvTrans and ConvSDG generate conversational queries from sessions, but may not explicitly model latent intents or use query trees.
  - **Query Trees:** The use of query trees for de-contextualized conversation structure is a potentially novel technical detail, though similar graph-based or session-based structures exist in ConvTrans and ConvSDG.
  - **Reverse Reformulation:** This is a variant of query rewriting, which is common (e.g., QReCC, AdaCQR), but the specific reverse process may be new.
  - **Delta:** The combination of these steps is somewhat novel, but each component has precedent. The novelty is incremental and may be more in the pipeline integration than in any single step.
  - **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The submission does not compare the technical details of its pipeline to those of ConvTrans, ConvSDG, or LAPS, making it difficult to assess the true delta.

## Claimed Contribution 3: **Dialogue discriminator with contrastive learning for quality control**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** LAPS, BlendX, MIntRec2.0 (use LLMs/discriminators for data filtering)
- **Claimed Difference:** Prior datasets lack a contrastive-learning-based discriminator for filtering defective conversations.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Quality Control:** LLM-based or classifier-based filtering is increasingly common (LAPS, BlendX). The use of contrastive learning for dialogue discrimination is a technical detail that may be new, but the general idea of automated filtering is not.
  - **Delta:** The specific use of contrastive learning for dialogue discrimination is a minor technical novelty; the overall approach is in line with recent trends.
  - **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The submission overstates the uniqueness of this step; similar quality control mechanisms are present in other recent works.

## Claimed Contribution 4: **ICConv as a new standard for evaluation**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** BlendX, MIntRec2.0, MMConv, LAPS
- **Claimed Difference:** ICConv relieves data scarcity and sets a new standard.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Benchmarking:** The field is rapidly producing new datasets; BlendX and MIntRec2.0 are already used for benchmarking multi-intent and context-aware conversational search.
  - **Delta:** ICConv adds another resource, but the claim of being a "new standard" is aspirational rather than substantiated.
  - **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim is not fully supported given the existence of other recent, large-scale, multi-intent/context datasets.
