**Summary for Reviewer:**

Arctic-SnowCoder is an incremental but well-executed contribution to the data-centric code LLM literature, combining a BERT-based data quality annotator, progressive pretraining, and synthetic data generation to train a small code model that achieves strong empirical results. The work’s main novelty is in the specific combination and detailed ablation of established techniques, rather than in introducing fundamentally new concepts or methods. While the authors claim to be the first to systematically "demystify" data quality in code pretraining, similar systematic studies and model-based data selection approaches have already been explored in works like *Textbooks Are All You Need* and *Magicoder*. The empirical results are robust and the ablation studies are more granular than some prior work, but the overall advances are incremental and should be viewed as practical recipe optimization rather than conceptual breakthroughs. Reviewers should recognize the value of the empirical findings while noting that the claims of novelty and systematic analysis are somewhat overstated relative to the existing literature.
