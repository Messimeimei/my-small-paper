This paper studies the originality of generated content from text-conditional diffusion models, proposing that the number of tokens required to invert a generated image is a strong indicator of originality. The detection method is simple to use and is built upon existing works such as Textual Inversion, which can enable widespread adoption by the community. I like that the idea is motivated by a simple observation of “description complexity” as a measure for originality. While the paper curates a method—and baselines in this regard are difficult to find—I suggest the authors at least provide a discussion around the missing baselines. I believe the method can be useful, but the paper needs to be supplemented with solid applications using it, such as how it can be useful for copyright infringement detection.
