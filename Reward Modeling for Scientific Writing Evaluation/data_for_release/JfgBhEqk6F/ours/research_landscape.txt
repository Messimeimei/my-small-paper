## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, several methodological themes emerge, primarily centered on **resource-efficient federated learning (FL)**, **heterogeneity handling**, **progressive/block-wise training**, **knowledge distillation/ensemble methods**, and **client selection/coordination**. The main approaches can be grouped as follows:

#### **Cluster 1: Progressive/Block-wise Training for Memory Efficiency**
- **Key Methods:** Progressive training (ProFL, NeuLite, SmartFreeze, ProgFed, Honey), block-wise model division, progressive layer freezing, elastic resource harmonization.
- **Papers:** Submission (Honey), Related 1, 2, 3, 4, 11, 21.
- **Description:** These methods divide models into blocks/layers and train them sequentially or progressively, often freezing blocks after convergence to reduce memory usage. Some (Honey, NeuLite) introduce mechanisms to break information isolation between blocks, while others (SmartFreeze) add heterogeneity-aware participant selection and pace control.

#### **Cluster 2: Heterogeneity-Aware FL (Device, Model, Data)**
- **Key Methods:** Model assignment based on device capability (InclusiveFL), model-heterogeneous FL (FedRolex, FedZKT, FedMD, Fed-ET, FedDF), hierarchical management (Harmony), participant selection considering heterogeneity.
- **Papers:** 5, 6, 10, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16.
- **Description:** These works address heterogeneity in device resources, model architectures, and data distributions. Approaches include assigning different model sizes, enabling knowledge transfer/distillation across heterogeneous models, and hierarchical or tiered management for efficient coordination.

#### **Cluster 3: Knowledge Distillation and Ensemble Methods**
- **Key Methods:** Distillation-based FL (DS-FL, FedMD, FedDF, Fed-ET, FedZKT), ensemble distillation, zero-shot knowledge transfer.
- **Papers:** 7, 13, 14, 15, 16.
- **Description:** These methods use knowledge distillation (often with unlabeled or synthetic data) to enable collaboration among heterogeneous models, reduce communication, or improve robustness.

#### **Cluster 4: Client Selection and Coordination**
- **Key Methods:** Ranking-based selection (FedRank), generative selection (FedGCS), tier-based selection (TiFL), hierarchical/global-local coordination (Harmony), RL-based aggregation (FedStitch).
- **Papers:** 5, 6, 8, 9, 20.
- **Description:** Focused on optimizing which clients participate in each round, considering device/data heterogeneity, energy, and system constraints. Some use learning-based or generative models for selection, others use hierarchical or tiered structures.

#### **Cluster 5: Communication and Computation Efficiency**
- **Key Methods:** Gradient sparsification (DEF), communication-efficient algorithms (FedAvg, ProgFed), compression, partial training.
- **Papers:** 11, 17, 18, 19.
- **Description:** These works focus on reducing communication/computation costs via model/gradient compression, partial training, or efficient aggregation.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Progressive Training as a Foundation:** Progressive/block-wise training is a recurring foundation for memory-efficient FL, with each subsequent work (e.g., Honey, NeuLite) adding mechanisms for better block collaboration, resource adaptation, or heterogeneity handling.
- **Synergy and Elasticity:** Recent works (Honey, NeuLite) emphasize breaking block isolation and introducing elastic mechanisms for resource adaptation, moving beyond rigid block assignments.
- **Heterogeneity Handling:** There is a clear trend toward supporting device, model, and data heterogeneity, with methods evolving from simple model partitioning to sophisticated knowledge transfer and aggregation across diverse models and devices.
- **Distillation and Ensemble Learning:** As model heterogeneity becomes more prevalent, knowledge distillation and ensemble methods are increasingly used for robust aggregation and model fusion.
- **Client Selection Evolution:** Client selection has evolved from heuristic and tier-based methods to learning-based, generative, and RL-based approaches, often integrated with heterogeneity-aware frameworks.
- **Complementarity:** Many methods are complementary—progressive training can be combined with distillation, client selection, and compression for further gains.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

1. **Memory and Resource Constraints in FL**
   - Training large models on memory-limited devices (Submission, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 10, 12)
   - Efficient resource utilization and adaptation to device capabilities (Submission, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 12)

2. **Heterogeneity in FL**
   - Device heterogeneity (memory, compute, energy) (4, 5, 6, 9, 10, 12, 13)
   - Model heterogeneity (different architectures/sizes) (10, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16)
   - Data heterogeneity (non-IID, unbalanced) (5, 6, 7, 9, 13, 14, 16, 17, 18)

3. **Communication and Computation Efficiency**
   - Reducing communication/computation costs (11, 17, 18, 19)
   - Efficient aggregation and model fusion (14, 15, 16)

4. **Client Selection and Participation**
   - Efficient, adaptive, and inclusive client selection (5, 6, 8, 9, 20)
   - Ensuring participation of low-end/weak clients (5, 10, 12, 13)

5. **Robustness and Performance under Non-IID/Adversarial Conditions**
   - Robust aggregation under non-IID data (7, 14, 16, 18)
   - Handling malicious clients or corrupted data (7)

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Memory Constraints:** Most works use progressive/block-wise training, but differ in how they handle block collaboration (Honey, NeuLite), freezing (SmartFreeze), or model growing/shrinking (ProFL).
- **Heterogeneity:** Some assign different model sizes (InclusiveFL), others use knowledge distillation (FedMD, FedZKT, FedDF, Fed-ET), or rolling sub-model extraction (FedRolex).
- **Communication Efficiency:** Approaches include model/gradient compression (DEF, ProgFed), distillation (DS-FL), and partial training (FedRolex).
- **Client Selection:** Methods range from heuristic/tier-based (TiFL) to learning-based (FedRank, FedGCS), and hierarchical (Harmony).
- **Robustness:** Distillation and ensemble methods (FedDF, Fed-ET) are used to improve robustness to heterogeneity and adversarial conditions.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- Problems are increasingly formulated with explicit consideration of **heterogeneity** (device, model, data).
- There is a shift from **static** to **dynamic/adaptive** resource allocation and participation.
- **Collaboration and knowledge transfer** across heterogeneous participants is a growing focus, moving beyond simple parameter averaging.

- **Efficiency:** Training speed/acceleration, convergence rate, number of communication rounds, latency.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Dual Focus:** Most works report both model performance (accuracy) and resource efficiency (memory, computation, communication, energy).
- **Relative Improvements:** Results are often reported as percentage improvements over baselines or state-of-the-art.
- **Novel Metrics:** Some introduce new metrics (e.g., effective movement for block convergence, communication cost reduction, inclusiveness).
- **Comprehensive Evaluation:** Recent works (e.g., Honey, NeuLite, FedStitch) evaluate across multiple axes: accuracy, memory, energy, speed, and inclusiveness.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Progressive Training Papers:** Emphasize memory usage and accuracy, sometimes training speed.
- **Distillation/Ensemble Papers:** Focus on accuracy, communication rounds, and robustness.
- **Client Selection Papers:** Highlight accuracy, convergence speed, and energy/latency.
- **Heterogeneity-Aware Papers:** Evaluate inclusiveness, fairness, and performance across device/model types.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Monolithic to Progressive Training:** Early FL methods trained full models on all clients, leading to high memory/computation costs. Progressive/block-wise training (ProFL, SmartFreeze, NeuLite, ProgFed) emerged to address this, with each iteration adding more flexibility (elasticity, harmonization, block collaboration).
- **From Homogeneous to Heterogeneous FL:** Initial works assumed homogeneous models/devices. Later works (FedRolex, InclusiveFL, FedZKT, FedDF, FedMD, Fed-ET) enabled participation of diverse devices and models, using knowledge distillation, ensemble learning, and model partitioning.
- **From Static to Adaptive Resource Allocation:** Early progressive methods assigned fixed blocks; newer methods (Honey, NeuLite) allow dynamic, elastic allocation based on device capacity.
- **From Heuristic to Learning-Based Client Selection:** Client selection evolved from heuristic/tier-based (TiFL) to learning-based (FedRank), generative (FedGCS), and RL-based (FedStitch) approaches.
- **From Parameter Averaging to Knowledge Distillation:** Aggregation methods evolved from simple parameter averaging (FedAvg) to sophisticated distillation and ensemble methods, enabling robust aggregation across heterogeneous models and data.
- **Integration and Complementarity:** Recent works combine multiple techniques—progressive training with distillation, heterogeneity-aware selection with hierarchical management, etc.—for holistic solutions.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Block-wise Training:** Foundation for memory efficiency, extended with block collaboration (Honey), harmonization (NeuLite), and freezing (SmartFreeze).
- **Knowledge Distillation:** Used for model heterogeneity, extended to zero-shot (FedZKT), ensemble (FedDF, Fed-ET), and communication-efficient (DS-FL) settings.
- **Client Selection:** From static to adaptive, learning-based, and generative models.
- **Hierarchical/Global-Local Coordination:** Introduced for efficient management of heterogeneity (Harmony, FedStitch).

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing:** Parameter averaging (FedAvg) vs. distillation/ensemble methods for aggregation; static vs. elastic/progressive training.
- **Complementary:** Progressive training can be combined with distillation, client selection, and compression for further gains; heterogeneity-aware frameworks often integrate multiple techniques.
