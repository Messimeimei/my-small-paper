## 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission addresses the comparative performance of LSTM and Transformer models for high-frequency financial time series forecasting, specifically using limit order book (LOB) data from Binance. It introduces a new LSTM variant (DLSTM with time series decomposition) and a modified Transformer, benchmarking both against standard LSTM and Transformer baselines.
- This places the work squarely within **Cluster A (LOB and High-Frequency Trading Forecasting)** and **Cluster B (Transformer Effectiveness and Adaptation for Time Series)**, as identified in the research landscape.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **DeepLOB-Seq2Seq and DeepLOB-Attention (Zhang and Zohren, 2021):** These models adapt Seq2Seq and attention mechanisms for LOB data, directly targeting multi-horizon and long-term prediction in high-frequency trading.
- **Temporal Fusion Transformer (Hu, 2021):** Applies advanced Transformer architectures to financial time series, including stock and cryptocurrency prediction.
- **iTransformer, LTSF-Linear, and other Transformer variants:** These works critically assess and adapt Transformer architectures for time series, sometimes challenging their necessity or proposing architectural modifications.
- **Hybrid and ensemble models (e.g., CNN-LSTM, BiLSTM-GCN):** These are relevant for their attempts to combine strengths of different architectures for financial forecasting.

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission’s DLSTM is an extension within the RNN/LSTM family, specifically targeting the integration of time series decomposition—a technique also seen in some hybrid models but less so in pure LSTM variants.
- The modified Transformer aligns with the trend of adapting Transformer architectures for time series, as seen in iTransformer, Linear Transformer, and TFT.
- The comparative analysis of LSTM vs. Transformer is a common theme in recent literature, with several works (e.g., LTSF-Linear, iTransformer) providing systematic benchmarks.

**Place in Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The focus on LOB data, mid-price prediction, and simulated trading evaluation is shared with a subset of recent works (e.g., DeepLOB, DeepLOB-Seq2Seq, DeepLOB-Attention).
- The use of multiple metrics (prediction error, R^2, accuracy, profitability) is standard in the field, especially for LOB and high-frequency trading studies.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is not the first to compare LSTM and Transformer models for financial time series or LOB data, nor the first to propose LSTM variants or Transformer modifications for this domain.
- Its main novelty appears to be the specific integration of time series decomposition into an LSTM (DLSTM) and the particular modifications made to the Transformer for financial prediction, as well as the scope and detail of the comparative analysis.
