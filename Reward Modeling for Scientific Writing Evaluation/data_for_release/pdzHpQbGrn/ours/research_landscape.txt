## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the dominant methodological theme is **prompt learning and adaptation for Vision-Language Models (VLMs)**, especially in the context of test-time adaptation, zero-shot/few-shot generalization, and domain shift. The methods can be grouped as follows:

#### **Cluster 1: Test-Time Prompt Learning and Adaptation**
- **Test-Time Prompt Tuning (TPT, C-TPT, DiffTPT, InCPL, VPA, ADAPROMPT, VPTTA, TPGaze, DPCore, ATPL [Submission])**
  - These methods adapt prompts at test time, often without labeled data, to improve generalization under distribution shift.
  - Innovations include: entropy minimization, confidence selection, diffusion-based augmentation, in-context learning, buffer-based sample selection, and dynamic coreset management.

#### **Cluster 2: Active Learning and Annotation Efficiency**
- **Active Test-Time Prompt Learning (ATPL [Submission]), PCB, SALAD**
  - These methods integrate active learning principles, such as uncertainty-based querying and class balancing, to minimize annotation cost during adaptation.
  - The submission paper is unique in applying active learning at test time for VLMs.

#### **Cluster 3: Distribution Alignment and Calibration**
- **PromptAlign, C-TPT, ATPL [Submission]**
  - Focus on aligning feature distributions (mean/variance) or improving calibration (reducing overconfidence) during adaptation.
  - Use of distribution alignment losses and calibration metrics (e.g., ECE, ATFD).

#### **Cluster 4: Visual Prompting and Parameter-Efficient Adaptation**
- **VPA, DPCore, VPTTA, TPGaze**
  - Emphasize adaptation via learnable visual prompt tokens, often with minimal parameter updates and memory/computation overhead.

#### **Cluster 5: Multi-modal and Conditional Prompt Learning**
- **MaPLe, CoCoOp, CoOp, InstructBLIP**
  - Simultaneous adaptation of both vision and language branches, or conditioning prompts on input instances or instructions.

#### **Cluster 6: Training-Free and Plug-and-Play Adaptation**
- **MTA, MEMO**
  - Methods that do not require prompt learning or model parameter updates, instead leveraging test-time augmentations and marginal entropy minimization.

#### **Cluster 7: Domain Adaptation and Transfer Learning**
- **SALAD, ASTTL, EDJAN**
  - Broader domain adaptation methods, sometimes outside the VLM context, focusing on source-free, label-agnostic, or cross-dataset transfer.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Prompt learning** has evolved from static, class-specific prompts (CoOp) to dynamic, instance-conditional (CoCoOp), multi-modal (MaPLe), and test-time adaptive prompts (TPT, ATPL).
- **Test-time adaptation** is increasingly parameter-efficient, leveraging prompts or small buffers rather than full model updates (VPA, DPCore, TPGaze).
- **Active learning** is being integrated into adaptation, not just for training but also for test-time scenarios (ATPL, PCB, SALAD).
- **Distribution alignment and calibration** are recognized as critical for robust adaptation, with explicit losses and metrics being introduced (PromptAlign, C-TPT).
- **Training-free approaches** (MTA, MEMO) offer practical alternatives for black-box or resource-constrained settings.
- **Visual prompting** and **memory/coreset mechanisms** (VPA, DPCore, VPTTA) address continual and efficient adaptation under dynamic domain shifts.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

1. **Test-Time Adaptation under Distribution Shift**
   - How to adapt VLMs (especially CLIP) to new domains or tasks at test time, often without labeled data or with minimal annotation (ATPL, TPT, InCPL, VPA, DPCore, MEMO, VPTTA, TPGaze).

2. **Zero-Shot and Few-Shot Generalization**
   - Improving VLM performance on unseen classes or domains without additional training data (TPT, DiffTPT, MTA, MaPLe, CoCoOp, CoOp, InstructBLIP).

3. **Efficient Annotation and Active Learning**
   - Reducing annotation cost and maximizing adaptation efficiency via active querying and class balancing (ATPL, PCB, SALAD).

4. **Distribution Alignment and Calibration**
   - Addressing overconfidence, poor calibration, and feature distribution mismatch between source and target/test domains (PromptAlign, C-TPT, ATPL).

5. **Parameter-Efficient and Continual Adaptation**
   - Adapting models with minimal parameter updates, memory, and computation, especially in continual or streaming settings (VPA, DPCore, VPTTA, TPGaze).

6. **Domain Adaptation and Transfer Learning**
   - Adapting to new domains without source data, handling label space shifts, and cross-dataset transfer (SALAD, ASTTL, EDJAN).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Test-Time Adaptation**: Some methods update prompts (TPT, ATPL, InCPL), others use visual tokens (VPA, DPCore), and some avoid parameter updates altogether (MTA, MEMO).
- **Active Learning**: ATPL and PCB focus on class balancing and uncertainty, while SALAD combines active selection with domain adaptation.
- **Distribution Alignment**: PromptAlign and ATPL use explicit alignment losses; C-TPT uses feature dispersion for calibration.
- **Continual Adaptation**: DPCore and VPTTA use memory/coreset mechanisms to avoid forgetting and enable efficient adaptation.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- Increasing focus on **test-time, streaming, and continual settings** (single-sample, online, or batch adaptation).
- Emphasis on **label efficiency** and **source-free adaptation** (no access to source data or labels at test time).
- Recognition of **distribution shift** as a central challenge, with explicit methods to measure and mitigate it.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **ImageNet and its variants** (ImageNet-A, ImageNet-C, ImageNet-R): Standard for OOD and domain shift evaluation.
- **Caltech101, Flowers102, DTD, EuroSAT, Oxford-IIIT Pets**: Popular for prompt learning and VLM adaptation.
- **Domain generalization and cross-dataset transfer benchmarks**: Used in ATPL, PromptAlign, and others.
- **Specialized datasets**: Bongard-HOI (visual reasoning), medical images (VPTTA), gaze estimation (TPGaze), EEG (EDJAN), activity recognition (ASTTL), and more.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Accuracy**: Most common, for classification and recognition tasks.
- **Zero-shot/Few-shot accuracy**: For generalization to unseen classes/domains.
- **Expected Calibration Error (ECE), Average Text Feature Dispersion (ATFD)**: For calibration and uncertainty.
- **Annotation budget, buffer capacity**: For active learning and adaptation efficiency.
- **mIoU, mAP**: For segmentation and detection tasks.
- **Computation time, parameter count**: For efficiency and scalability.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Relative improvement over state-of-the-art**: Most papers report gains over prior methods, often in percentage points.
- **Annotation efficiency**: Measured by number of queried labels or buffer size (ATPL, PCB, SALAD).
- **Robustness to distribution shift**: Evaluated via OOD datasets, domain generalization, and continual adaptation settings.
- **Calibration and uncertainty**: Increasingly important, with dedicated metrics (C-TPT).

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Submission paper (ATPL)**: Evaluates on 10 cross-dataset and 4 domain-generalization datasets, reporting accuracy, annotation budget, and buffer capacity.
- **Related works**: Vary in focus—some emphasize accuracy, others annotation efficiency, calibration, or computational cost.
- **Emerging trend**: Multi-faceted evaluation, considering not just accuracy but also efficiency, robustness, and calibration.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **Prompt Learning**: Began with static, class-specific prompts (CoOp), evolved to instance-conditional (CoCoOp), multi-modal (MaPLe), and instruction-based (InstructBLIP) prompts.
- **Test-Time Adaptation**: Shifted from full model updates to prompt-based and visual token-based adaptation (VPA, DPCore, VPTTA), with increasing focus on efficiency and continual adaptation.
- **Active Learning**: Moved from training-time sample selection to test-time active querying (ATPL), with innovations in class balancing and uncertainty-based selection.
- **Distribution Alignment and Calibration**: Emerged as critical for robust adaptation, with explicit losses and metrics (PromptAlign, C-TPT, ATPL).
- **Training-Free Methods**: Developed for scenarios where prompt/model updates are impractical (MTA, MEMO).
- **Memory and Coreset Mechanisms**: Introduced for continual adaptation and to avoid catastrophic forgetting (DPCore, VPTTA).

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **CoOp/CoCoOp**: Foundational for prompt learning, extended by MaPLe (multi-modal), TPT (test-time), and ATPL (active test-time).
- **MaPLe**: Multi-modal prompting, further extended by PromptAlign (distribution alignment).
- **VPA/DPCore**: Visual prompting and coreset mechanisms, enabling efficient continual adaptation.
- **Active Learning**: PCB and SALAD inform ATPL’s active test-time adaptation.
- **Test-Time Augmentation**: MEMO and MTA provide training-free adaptation strategies, sometimes combined with prompt learning.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Prompt Learning vs. Training-Free**: Prompt-based adaptation (TPT, ATPL) competes with training-free methods (MTA, MEMO) in terms of practicality and performance.
- **Textual vs. Visual Prompting**: Some methods focus on textual prompts (TPT, ATPL), others on visual tokens (VPA, DPCore), and some combine both (MaPLe, InCPL).
- **Active Learning vs. Passive Adaptation**: Active querying (ATPL, PCB, SALAD) complements passive adaptation (TPT, VPA).
- **Distribution Alignment/Calibration**: Increasingly integrated into prompt learning and adaptation frameworks for improved robustness.

The research landscape is rapidly evolving toward **efficient, robust, and label-efficient adaptation of vision-language models under distribution shift**, with a strong emphasis on **test-time prompt learning**, **active learning**, and **distribution alignment**.
