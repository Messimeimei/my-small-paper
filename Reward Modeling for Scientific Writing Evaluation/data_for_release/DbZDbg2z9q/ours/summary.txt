This submission introduces OntoGen, an automated ontology generation system, and OntoRAG, an ontology-augmented retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) framework, targeting improved logical consistency and scientific accuracy in LLM outputs for emerging scientific domains. The main conceptual advance is the automation of ontology creation and its integration into RAG, enabling structured knowledge use where curated ontologies do not exist—a gap in prior RAG systems, which typically rely on pre-existing or manually curated ontologies. The work is empirically validated in the novel domain of Single-Atom Catalysis, including the introduction of a new benchmark (SACBench), though this is a standard field practice rather than a conceptual breakthrough. While the authors’ claims of novelty are generally supported, some overstate the lack of structured knowledge use in prior work, and the empirical gains may reflect the general benefits of ontological structure rather than the specific integration method. The submission would benefit from broader comparison to ontology learning literature and a more nuanced discussion of related RAG systems, but overall represents a substantive and timely contribution to automated knowledge integration for scientific LLMs.
