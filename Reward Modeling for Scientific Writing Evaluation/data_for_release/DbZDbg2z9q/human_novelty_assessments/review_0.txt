This paper presents a system that combines an automatically generated ontology, OntoGen, with a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) framework, OntoRAG, to improve the scientific accuracy of large language model (LLM) outputs in specialized domains where curated ontologies do not exist. Additionally, the authors introduce SACBench, a new benchmark dataset for evaluating LLM performance in the context of single atom catalyst (SAC) synthesis within materials science. The novelty of the work is threefold: (1) the design of OntoGen as an automatic ontology generator for domains lacking pre-existing ontologies, (2) the development of OntoRAG, a RAG system that incorporates ontologies as structured input to guide LLM reasoning, and (3) the creation of SACBench to facilitate rigorous assessment of LLM outputs in a highly specialized scientific setting. The paper addresses a novel and interdisciplinary area at the intersection of AI and the natural sciences, and the approach is creative in its attempt to mitigate LLM hallucinations by leveraging domain-specific ontological knowledge. While the integration of automatic ontology generation with RAG for scientific applications is original, the review notes that the novelty primarily lies in the unique combination and application of these components rather than in fundamentally new algorithms. The work stands out for its creative framing and for targeting a challenging, underexplored problem, but the degree of novelty would be further strengthened by more direct comparisons to existing scientific ontologies and by clarifying how OntoGen’s outputs differ from or improve upon established resources. Overall, the contribution is innovative in its interdisciplinary scope and in addressing the limitations of LLMs in scientific domains, though its novelty is most pronounced in the system’s integration and application rather than in methodological breakthroughs.
