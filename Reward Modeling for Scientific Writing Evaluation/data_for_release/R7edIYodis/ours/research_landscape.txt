- **Hybrid and Modular Approaches**: Some works (Papers 4, 7, 9, 14) combine multiple models or modules, each specializing in a subtask (e.g., parsing, reasoning, execution).

  - Shared traits: End-to-end learning, large synthetic datasets, focus on accuracy and generalization.

- **Cluster B: Neuro-Symbolic and Compositional Integration**

  - Papers 4, 5

- **Cluster D: Application of Transformers to Other Domains**

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From Black-Box to Interpretable Models**: Early works (Papers 2, 6) treat symbolic math as a translation problem; the submission and others (Paper 14) move toward stepwise, rule-based, or compositional models for interpretability and faithfulness.
- **From Purely Neural to Neuro-Symbolic**: There is a clear evolution toward integrating symbolic reasoning with neural models, motivated by the need for transparency, user interaction, and domain knowledge incorporation.
- **From Random to Structured Datasets**: Dataset generation evolves from random expression trees to comprehensive, rule-annotated datasets (Submission, Paper 4).
- **From Flat to Structured Representations**: The field moves from flat string representations to tree-based, Polish notation, and other structure-aware encodings (Papers 4, 5).
- **From Single-Model to Modular/Hybrid Systems**: Increasing use of modular architectures, where different components (neural, symbolic, logic-based) are composed for improved performance and interpretability.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Transformers as a Foundation**: Transformers are the backbone for many advances, extended with pretraining (Paper 1), hybridization (Paper 12), or integration with symbolic modules (Submission, Paper 14).
- **Symbolic Reasoning Modules**: Symbolic solvers, logic engines, and program executors are increasingly integrated with neural models for compositional reasoning (Papers 7, 9, 14).
- **Meta-Learning and Model Selection**: ML-based selection of algorithms or models (Paper 5) extends the capabilities of CAS and deep learning systems.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Black-Box vs. Stepwise/Rule-Based**: Black-box seq2seq models compete with stepwise, rule-guided approaches; the latter offer interpretability and efficiency.
- **Pure Neural vs. Neuro-Symbolic**: Pure neural models compete with neuro-symbolic systems; the latter are increasingly favored for tasks requiring faithfulness, transparency, or user interaction.
- **Representation Choices**: Competing approaches to representing mathematical expressions (flat, tree, Polish notation) impact model performance and interpretability.
