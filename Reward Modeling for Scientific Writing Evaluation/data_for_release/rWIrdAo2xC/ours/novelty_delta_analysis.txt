# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission introduces a *direct attribute-level diffusion* approach (HUMAN-DAD) for generalizable monocular 3D human rendering, using 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) as the core representation. It claims to move beyond the prevalent *indirect pixel-level supervision* by directly supervising 3D Gaussian attributes, leveraging a two-stage proxy attribute construction and a conditional diffusion model.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **GPS-Gaussian (Zheng et al., 2024):** Generalizable 3DGS for human novel view synthesis, using pixel-level loss.
- **Triplane Meets Gaussian Splatting (Zou et al., 2024):** Combines triplane and 3DGS, also using pixel-level supervision.
- **LGM (Tang et al., 2024), FreeSplat (Liu et al., 2024):** Generalizable 3DGS for scenes, pixel-level loss.
- **Human 3Diffusion (Yang et al., 2024):** Diffusion models for 3D human generation, but not directly for 3DGS attributes.
- **HUGS (Hu et al., 2024), GauHuman (Kocabas et al., 2024):** 3DGS for articulated/dynamic humans, but focus on canonical space and not direct attribute-level diffusion.

**Methodological Cluster Placement:**
- The submission is at the intersection of *3DGS generalization* and *diffusion-based 3D attribute generation*.
- It diverges from the dominant trend of pixel-level loss (indirect supervision) in generalizable 3DGS, proposing direct attribute-level supervision.
- The use of a conditional diffusion model for attribute generation is novel within the 3DGS context, though diffusion for 3D generation (e.g., Human 3Diffusion, SiTH) is an emerging trend.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses *generalizable monocular 3D human rendering*—a core problem in the field.
- It claims to improve convergence and error propagation by bridging the supervision gap between pixel and attribute domains.
- Evaluation is on standard human datasets (THuman2, CityuHuman, 2K2K, CustomHuman), consistent with the field.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission’s main novelty is the *direct attribute-level supervision* for 3DGS, which is not present in the most closely related generalizable 3DGS works (which use pixel-level loss).
- The use of a *conditional diffusion model* for this purpose is a new combination in this context, though diffusion for 3D tasks is not itself new.
- The two-stage proxy attribute construction is a technical detail that enables the direct supervision paradigm.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors consistently characterize prior generalizable 3DGS works (GPS-Gaussian, Zou et al., LGM, FreeSplat) as relying on *indirect pixel-level supervision* and position their work as a direct alternative.
- They describe Human 3Diffusion and similar diffusion-based works as lacking 3D consistency due to absence of 3D supervision.
- Implicit/NeRF-based methods are described as less efficient or less detailed, and not using 3DGS.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The characterization of GPS-Gaussian, Zou et al., LGM, and FreeSplat as using pixel-level loss is accurate.
- The claim that these works do not use direct attribute-level supervision is correct; however, the degree to which this is a *limitation* is not always substantiated.
- The description of Human 3Diffusion as lacking 3D supervision is accurate for the cited works, but the distinction is more about the type of supervision than the use of diffusion per se.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The authors sometimes overstate the *inefficiency* or *suboptimality* of pixel-level supervision without quantifying the practical impact or acknowledging that these methods achieve strong results.
- The claim that their approach is the *first* to use direct attribute-level diffusion for 3DGS is accurate in the context of the cited works, but the novelty is incremental given the broader trend toward direct supervision in other 3D domains.
- The authors do not cite some recent 3DGS works (e.g., MVSplat, GS-W, Motion-Aware 3DGS) that may have relevant technical overlaps.

**Example:**
- For GPS-Gaussian: "The most common way...is to train a parameterized neural model...and then impose supervision by comparing pixel-level differences..." (accurate).
- For Human 3Diffusion: "They face difficulties in maintaining consistency among different views due to the absence of 3D information for supervision." (accurate, but the distinction is more nuanced).

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Contribution 1: Direct Attribute-Level Supervision for 3DGS**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** GPS-Gaussian, Zou et al., LGM, FreeSplat (all use pixel-level loss for 3DGS).
- **Claimed Difference:** Direct supervision of 3D Gaussian attributes via proxy ground-truth, rather than indirect pixel-level loss.
- **Independent Assessment:** This is a substantive technical difference; prior works do not supervise attributes directly. However, the practical impact (e.g., convergence, quality) depends on empirical results, which are not detailed here.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** Accurate in terms of supervision type; the claim of "inefficiency" in prior works is not always quantified.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The difference is substantive in supervision strategy, but the overall pipeline (conditional model for attribute prediction) is similar.

**Contribution 2: Two-Stage Proxy Attribute Construction**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** No direct analog in cited 3DGS works; some point cloud/diffusion works (e.g., Conditional Point Diffusion-Refinement) use similar denoising or proxy construction.
- **Claimed Difference:** Per-scene overfitting + distribution unification to create smooth, consistent proxy attributes.
- **Independent Assessment:** This is a technical enabler for direct supervision; the idea of proxy construction is not unique to this work, but the specific two-stage process for 3DGS attributes is new in this context.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The process is a necessary adaptation for the proposed paradigm, but not a conceptual leap.

**Contribution 3: Conditional Diffusion Model for 3DGS Attribute Generation**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Human 3Diffusion (diffusion for 3D humans, but not for 3DGS attributes), SiTH (diffusion for texture), Conditional Point Diffusion-Refinement (diffusion for point clouds).
- **Claimed Difference:** First to use conditional diffusion for 3DGS attribute generation from a single image.
- **Independent Assessment:** The use of diffusion for 3DGS attribute generation is new; however, diffusion for 3D attribute/point cloud generation is an emerging trend, so the novelty is in the application to 3DGS.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The difference is in the application domain; the underlying diffusion methodology is not new.

**Contribution 4: State-of-the-Art Performance**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** GPS-Gaussian, Zou et al., LGM, FreeSplat.
- **Claimed Difference:** Outperforms prior methods on standard datasets.
- **Independent Assessment:** Without detailed results, cannot verify; improvements may be due to implementation, data, or other factors, not just the new supervision paradigm.

# 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

**Field Maturity:**
- The field of 3DGS-based generalizable human/scene rendering is *very active* and *rapidly evolving* (most key works are from 2023–2024).
- There is a strong trend toward *generalization*, *efficiency*, and *direct supervision*.

**Recent Surveys/Literature Reviews:**
- No comprehensive survey yet on 3DGS for human rendering, but several recent reviews cover neural rendering, NeRF, and 3D generative models (see: "A Survey on Neural Scene Representation and Rendering," "A Survey on Neural Implicit Representations for 3D Vision").

**Trends:**
- Shift from per-scene optimization to generalizable, feed-forward models.
- Increasing use of explicit representations (3DGS, point clouds) for efficiency.
- Integration of diffusion models for 3D attribute/geometry/texture generation.
- Growing use of transformers for feature fusion.

**Incremental Advances:**
- Many recent papers propose *incremental improvements* (e.g., new supervision, better feature fusion, faster inference) rather than radical conceptual changes.
- Novelty is often in *how* existing components are combined or adapted.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Claimed novelty may be overstated** in terms of being the "first" to use direct attribute-level supervision; while this is new for 3DGS, direct supervision is a known strategy in other 3D domains.
- **Empirical improvements** may result from better implementation, data, or training tricks, not just the new supervision paradigm.
- **Terminology differences** (e.g., "direct attribute-level supervision" vs. "pixel-level loss") may mask conceptual similarities in the overall pipeline (conditional model predicts attributes, which are rendered and compared).
- The **two-stage proxy construction** is a routine adaptation to enable the new supervision, not a conceptual breakthrough.
- The **use of diffusion models** is increasingly common; the novelty is in the application to 3DGS attributes, not in the diffusion methodology itself.
- **Limitations of prior work** (e.g., inefficiency, suboptimal convergence) may be exaggerated; prior works achieve strong results and efficiency is often a function of implementation.

# 6. RELATED WORK CONSIDERATIONS

- **Relevant works not cited:** MVSplat, GS-W, Motion-Aware 3DGS, and "Gaussian in the Wild" are not discussed, though they address efficient/generalizable 3DGS from sparse or unconstrained images and may have relevant technical overlaps.

- **Incomplete characterizations:** The limitations of pixel-level supervision are discussed, but the practical impact is not always quantified.
- **Potentially misleading:** The claim that prior works are "inefficient" or "suboptimal" may not be fully substantiated; some achieve real-time performance and strong generalization.
- **Citation vs. reality:** The authors cite GPS-Gaussian, Zou et al., LGM, FreeSplat as using pixel-level loss, which is accurate, but do not discuss whether these methods could be adapted for direct supervision.

# 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most significant independently verified difference:** The submission is the first to propose *direct attribute-level supervision* for 3DGS in a generalizable monocular human rendering context, enabled by a two-stage proxy attribute construction and a conditional diffusion model.
- **Main relationships:** The work builds directly on generalizable 3DGS methods (GPS-Gaussian, Zou et al., LGM, FreeSplat), diverging in supervision strategy; it also draws from diffusion-based 3D generation (Human 3Diffusion, SiTH).
- **Strongest differentiation:** The direct attribute-level supervision paradigm for 3DGS is a substantive technical difference from prior generalizable 3DGS works.
- **Weakest differentiation:** The use of diffusion models is not novel in itself; the two-stage proxy construction is a technical adaptation rather than a conceptual advance.
- **Discrepancies:** The authors sometimes overstate the inefficiency or limitations of pixel-level supervision and do not fully acknowledge the strength or adaptability of prior works. Some relevant recent 3DGS works are not cited or compared.
- **Reviewer guidance:** The main novelty is in the supervision strategy for 3DGS; the rest of the pipeline (conditional model, diffusion, SMPL features) follows established trends. The practical impact of the new supervision should be evaluated empirically, and additional comparisons with recent 3DGS works would strengthen the case.
