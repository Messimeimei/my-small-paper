# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission, SQUBA, proposes a Mamba-based end-to-end speech summarization model with a query-attention projector for compressing acoustic features, targeting efficient summarization of long speech inputs.
- Within the research landscape, SQUBA sits at the intersection of three active clusters:
  - **SSM/Mamba-based sequence models** (e.g., Mamba, Dual-path Mamba, Cobra, VL-Mamba)
  - **Speech summarization and modality bridging** (e.g., Q-Former-based models, SALMONN, BASS, End-to-End Speech Summarization Using Restricted Self-Attention)
  - **Training/alignment strategies** (e.g., DPO, TDPO)

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Shang et al. (2024):** Uses Q-Former for speech-to-text summarization, employing querying-attention for compact abstraction.
- **Kang & Roy (2024):** Employs HuBERT and MiniChat2-3b connected via Q-Former for end-to-end speech summarization.
- **Tang et al. (2024) (SALMONN):** Integrates auditory inputs using windowing Querying Transformer (Q-Former).
- **Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023):** Introduces the SSM-based Mamba architecture for efficient sequence modeling.
- **Cobra (Zhao et al., 2024), VL-Mamba (Qiao et al., 2024):** Extend Mamba to multimodal LLMs for efficient inference.

**Methodological Clusters:**
- SQUBA is most closely aligned with SSM-based models that use cross-modal projectors (notably Q-Former) for modality bridging, but distinguishes itself by:
  - Using a Mamba backbone (rather than Transformer) for the LLM.
  - Introducing a query-attention Mamba projector for speech compression.
  - Employing a two-stage training process with bootstrapped DPO.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses efficient abstractive speech summarization for long inputs, focusing on reducing computational/memory overhead and improving inference speed.
- Evaluation is in line with field standards: inference speed and summarization quality (e.g., ROUGE).

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is an incremental extension of recent trends: replacing Transformer LLMs with Mamba, and adapting Q-Former-style projectors for speech.
- The main technical novelty is the specific integration of a query-attention Mamba projector with a Mamba LLM, and the use of DPO in training.
- The problem formulation and evaluation approach are standard for the field.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors consistently frame prior work as either:
  - Cascade (ASR + summarization) or end-to-end, with end-to-end being superior.
  - Transformer-based or SSM-based, with SSM (Mamba) offering efficiency.
  - Using Q-Former or similar projectors for modality bridging, with SQUBA’s approach being “inspired by” but distinct from these.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- **Shang et al. (2024):** Authors acknowledge similarity in using querying-attention for compact abstraction, but do not detail the technical differences between Q-Former and their Q-Mamba projector.
- **Kang & Roy (2024):** Authors compare against this model, noting its use of HuBERT and MiniChat2-3b with Q-Former, and claim superior performance.
- **Tang et al. (2024) (SALMONN):** Cited as inspiration for windowing Q-Mamba, but the distinction between Q-Former and Q-Mamba is not clearly articulated.
- **Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023):** Cited as the backbone, with standard description of its efficiency.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The authors’ characterizations are generally accurate in terms of broad categories (cascade vs. end-to-end, Transformer vs. SSM, Q-Former vs. Q-Mamba), but:
  - The technical distinction between Q-Former and Q-Mamba is not clearly specified, potentially overstating the novelty of the projector.
  - The claim that SQUBA “extends Mamba-based LLMs to efficiently handle long speech inputs” is accurate in implementation, but the conceptual leap is incremental, as similar modality bridging has been done with Q-Former and Transformers.
  - The use of “inspired by” and “similar to” in reference to Q-Former-based models may understate the degree of overlap.

**Substantiation of Claims:**
- Claimed improvements in speed and competitive performance are supported by empirical results, but the technical source of these improvements (projector design vs. backbone choice) is not disentangled.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claim 1: Query-attention Mamba projector for compressing long speech prompts**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Shang et al. (2024), Tang et al. (2024), Li et al. (2023) (Q-Former)
- **Claimed Difference:** SQUBA uses a “query-attention Mamba projector” instead of Q-Former.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - Q-Former is a learnable query-based cross-attention module for compressing modality features (used in vision and speech).
  - The submission’s Q-Mamba projector appears to be a direct adaptation of this idea, but with Mamba layers instead of Transformer layers.
  - The main difference is the use of Mamba for the projector, which may offer efficiency, but the conceptual approach (learnable queries, cross-attention) is the same.
  - The novelty is thus in the architectural substitution (Mamba for Transformer) rather than a new compression paradigm.
  - The authors do not provide ablation or analysis to show that the Q-Mamba projector is fundamentally different or superior to Q-Former.

**Claim 2: Extension of Mamba-based LLMs to long speech summarization**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Cobra (Zhao et al., 2024), VL-Mamba (Qiao et al., 2024), Dual-path Mamba (Jiang et al., 2024)
- **Claimed Difference:** SQUBA is the first to apply Mamba LLMs to speech summarization with modality bridging.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - Cobra and VL-Mamba extend Mamba to multimodal LLMs, including speech and vision, but focus on general-purpose tasks.
  - Dual-path Mamba applies SSMs to speech separation, not summarization.
  - SQUBA’s application to speech summarization is a logical extension, but not a conceptual leap; it is a new application of an existing architecture.
  - The main delta is the specific task (speech summarization) and the integration with a query-attention projector.

**Claim 3: Two-stage training with bootstrapped DPO**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Token-level DPO, curriculum learning in speech LLMs
- **Claimed Difference:** Bootstrapped DPO for improved summarization quality and efficiency.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - DPO and TDPO are established for LLM alignment; their application to speech summarization is a natural extension.
  - The “bootstrapped” aspect is not detailed; it is unclear if this is a novel training regime or a standard application of DPO.
  - The contribution is an adaptation of known alignment techniques to a new task, rather than a new algorithm.

**Claim 4: Empirical demonstration of competitive performance with faster inference**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Mamba, Cobra, VL-Mamba, Q-Former-based models
- **Claimed Difference:** SQUBA is faster than Transformer-based models with competitive quality.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - Mamba-based models are known to be faster than Transformers for long sequences.
  - The speedup is likely attributable to the Mamba backbone and token compression, not a fundamentally new method.
  - The empirical results support the claim, but the source of improvement is architectural efficiency, not a new algorithmic insight.

- Recent survey/review papers:

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Claimed novelty in the Q-Mamba projector may be overstated:** The main difference from Q-Former is the use of Mamba layers; the core idea (learnable queries, cross-attention) is not new.
- **Empirical improvements may be due to backbone efficiency:** The speedup is likely from the Mamba backbone and token compression, not a fundamentally new compression method.
- **Terminology differences may mask conceptual similarities:** “Q-Mamba” vs. “Q-Former” may suggest more novelty than actually exists.
- **Extensions may be routine adaptations:** Applying DPO to speech summarization is a straightforward extension of its use in text LLMs.
- **Lack of ablation or analysis:** The submission does not provide evidence that the Q-Mamba projector is superior to Q-Former, or that bootstrapped DPO is novel.

# 6. RELATED WORK CONSIDERATIONS

- **Relevant work not addressed:**
  - **DeCo (Decoupling Token Compression from Semantic Abstraction):** Proposes a parameter-free projector for multimodal LLMs, directly relevant to the projector design space.
  - **Token-level DPO:** Fine-grained preference optimization, relevant to the training strategy.
  - **Can Mamba Learn How to Learn?:** Comparative study of Mamba’s in-context learning, relevant for understanding Mamba’s capabilities in LLM settings.
- **Additional comparisons needed:**
  - Direct ablation between Q-Former and Q-Mamba projectors.
  - Comparison with DeCo and MMC for projector efficiency and explainability.
- **Potentially misleading characterizations:**
  - The distinction between Q-Former and Q-Mamba is not clearly articulated, possibly exaggerating the novelty.
  - The limitations of prior work (e.g., Q-Former) may be overstated by omission of recent advances (e.g., DeCo).
- **Citation practices:**
  - Authors cite Q-Former as inspiration but do not engage with the full range of recent projector designs.

**In summary:**  
The submission is a well-executed adaptation of recent advances (Mamba LLMs, Q-Former-style projectors, DPO) to the task of efficient speech summarization. The main technical delta is the use of Mamba layers in both the LLM and the projector, resulting in improved speed. However, the conceptual advances are incremental, and the novelty of the projector is likely overstated relative to Q-Former and recent projector designs (e.g., DeCo). The empirical results are promising, but the source of improvement is architectural efficiency rather than a fundamentally new method. Additional comparisons and more precise characterization of prior work would strengthen the submission’s claims.
