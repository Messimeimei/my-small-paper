The authors propose an equivariant Topological Deep Learning framework that deals with geometric node features and can be generalized to many topological domains including simplicial, cell, combinatorial, and path complexes. The authors add an important piece of work for the Topological Deep Learning (TDL) community as there is not much literature on Equivariant TDL, and the novel benchmark based on geospatial information is novel. However, novelty is the key disadvantage of the paper, as it seems that the work just extends prior works on graphs to TDL; even though the theoretical insights are important, they are mostly an extension from graphs. Furthermore, the property of “heterogeneous interactions” was actually mentioned in prior literature, so it is not fair to claim that ETNNs are set up for this characteristic, but more like TDL in general already possesses this property.
