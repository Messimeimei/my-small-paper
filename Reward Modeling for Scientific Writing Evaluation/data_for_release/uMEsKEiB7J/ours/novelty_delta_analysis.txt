# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating NovelQA in the Research Landscape:**

- **Benchmark Focus:** NovelQA is positioned as a long-context question answering (QA) benchmark, specifically targeting narrative texts (English novels) with contexts exceeding 200,000 tokens. This places it within the "Long-Context QA and Comprehension Benchmarks" cluster, alongside works like LV-Eval, BAMBOO, LongBench, Ada-LEval, and Narrative XL.
- **Most Closely Related Works:**
  - **LV-Eval**: Supports up to 256k tokens, includes multi-hop/detail QA, and emphasizes length scaling and robustness.
  - **Narrative XL**: Focuses on novels, but uses LLMs for automatic question generation and scene summarization.
  - **LongBench, BAMBOO, Ada-LEval**: Provide long-context QA but with shorter contexts (typically up to 32k–128k tokens) and broader domains.
- **Methodological Clusters:**
  - **Manual Annotation & Narrative Focus:** NovelQA claims expert, manual annotation for narrative texts, contrasting with Narrative XL’s LLM-generated questions and more generic or multi-domain approaches in other benchmarks.
  - **Ultra-Long Contexts:** Only LV-Eval and Ada-LEval approach or exceed NovelQA’s context length, but with different domains and annotation strategies.
- **Problem Space:** NovelQA addresses the gap in evaluating LLMs’ deep understanding and reasoning over ultra-long, coherent narratives, a problem not fully covered by prior benchmarks that either use shorter contexts, synthetic data, or non-narrative domains.
- **Evaluation Approaches:** NovelQA uses accuracy (multi-choice/generative), breakdowns by question type, and a public leaderboard. This is similar to LV-Eval and BAMBOO, but less sophisticated than L-Eval or LongGenBench, which use human/LLM judges for open-ended tasks.

**Independent Assessment:**  
NovelQA’s main differentiator is the combination of ultra-long narrative contexts and expert manual annotation. However, LV-Eval matches or exceeds the context length (256k tokens) and includes multi-hop/detail QA, while Narrative XL shares the narrative domain but relies on LLM-generated questions. The field is rapidly converging on longer contexts and more complex tasks, so the degree of novelty depends on the specifics of annotation quality, question diversity, and evaluation rigor.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**How Authors Characterize Related Work:**

- **L-Eval:** Cited as instituting standardized evaluation for long-context LLMs. Authors mention it in the context of the rise of long-context LLMs, but do not directly compare evaluation methodologies or context lengths.
- **LongBench:** Described as offering diverse, multi-task, bilingual benchmarks across languages and domains. Authors position NovelQA as addressing a gap in narrative, ultra-long contexts.
- **LongHealth:** Cited as a long-context QA benchmark in the clinical domain. Authors claim NovelQA is unique in addressing "extremely long-context understanding" in narrative texts.
- **Narrative XL:** Characterized as using GPT-3.5 to summarize scenes and auto-annotate questions for 1,500 books. Authors imply that Narrative XL’s questions are not manually crafted or expert-annotated.

**Patterns in Positioning:**
- Authors consistently emphasize NovelQA’s manual annotation, narrative focus, and ultra-long context as unique.
- They contrast NovelQA with prior work by highlighting either domain (narrative vs. clinical/generic), annotation method (manual vs. automatic), or context length.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The characterization of Narrative XL as using LLM-generated questions is accurate, but the implication that this makes NovelQA uniquely "expert-annotated" is somewhat overstated, as other benchmarks (e.g., LV-Eval, BAMBOO) also use human annotation.
- The claim that prior benchmarks do not address "extremely long-context understanding" is only partially accurate: LV-Eval and Ada-LEval do support ultra-long contexts, though not in the narrative domain.
- There is no mention of LV-Eval, Ada-LEval, or other recent ultra-long context benchmarks, which is a notable omission.

**Discrepancies:**
- The authors understate the context length and annotation quality of some recent benchmarks (e.g., LV-Eval).
- The distinction between "manual" and "automatic" annotation is used to emphasize NovelQA’s uniqueness, but this is not exclusive to NovelQA.

**Rhetoric:**
- The language sometimes overstates differences (e.g., "first" or "unique") without fully acknowledging the nuances of prior work.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contribution 1:**  
*NovelQA is the first long-context QA benchmark with manually crafted questions, golden answers, and evidences, with contexts exceeding 200,000 tokens.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** LV-Eval (256k tokens, multi-hop/detail QA, human annotation), Narrative XL (novels, LLM-generated questions).
- **Claimed Difference:** Manual annotation and narrative focus at ultra-long context length.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - LV-Eval matches/exceeds context length and includes human annotation, but is not narrative-focused.
  - Narrative XL is narrative-focused but uses LLM-generated questions.
  - The combination of narrative domain, ultra-long context, and manual annotation is relatively unique, but not entirely unprecedented (LV-Eval’s annotation quality and question types are similar, though in a different domain).
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The domain (narrative) and annotation method (manual) are substantive differences from LV-Eval and Narrative XL, but the claim of being "first" is overstated given the existence of LV-Eval and Ada-LEval for context length and human annotation.
  - The difference from Narrative XL is more substantive (manual vs. LLM-generated questions).

**Claimed Contribution 2:**  
*NovelQA provides a unique evaluation tool for LLMs' deep understanding of extended, complex narratives, addressing a gap in current benchmarks.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Narrative XL (narrative domain), LV-Eval (deep, multi-hop reasoning).
- **Claimed Difference:** Focus on deep narrative understanding at scale.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Narrative XL’s questions may be less challenging or nuanced due to LLM generation, but this is not empirically demonstrated in the submission.
  - LV-Eval and others do not focus on narrative, but do address deep reasoning in other domains.
  - The "gap" is partially addressed by Narrative XL (narrative, but auto-generated) and LV-Eval (ultra-long, but not narrative).
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The narrative focus is a substantive difference; the claim of "unique" evaluation is less clear without direct comparison to Narrative XL’s question quality.

**Claimed Contribution 3:**  
*NovelQA includes diverse question types (multi-hop, single-hop, detail) and is constructed from a wide range of English novels, annotated by experts.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** LV-Eval (multi-hop, detail, human annotation), BAMBOO (multi-domain, multi-hop), Narrative XL (narrative, LLM-generated).
- **Claimed Difference:** Diversity of question types in narrative domain, expert annotation.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - LV-Eval and BAMBOO also have diverse, human-annotated questions, but not in the narrative domain.
  - Narrative XL covers the narrative domain but with LLM-generated questions.
  - The combination is relatively unique, but not all elements are novel individually.

**Claimed Contribution 4:**  
*NovelQA is publicly released with an evaluation system and leaderboard, supporting both public domain and all-novel evaluations.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** LongHealth, LongSafety, LV-Eval (public leaderboards).
- **Claimed Difference:** Leaderboard for narrative, ultra-long QA.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Public leaderboards are now common; the novelty is in the domain/context length combination.

# 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Field Maturity:**  
  The field is highly active and rapidly evolving, with multiple new benchmarks released in the past 12–18 months. There is a trend toward ever-longer contexts, more realistic data, and more sophisticated evaluation.
- **Recent Surveys/Literature Reviews:**  
  No explicit survey cited, but the research landscape is well-mapped by recent benchmarks (LV-Eval, Ada-LEval, BAMBOO, LongBench, Narrative XL).
- **Trends:**  
  - Increasing context length (now up to 256k tokens).
  - Shift from synthetic to real-world data (novels, clinical, legal).
  - Emphasis on manual annotation for quality.
  - Move toward public leaderboards and community benchmarking.
- **Incremental Advances:**  
  Many new benchmarks are incremental, differing in domain, annotation method, or context length, rather than introducing fundamentally new evaluation paradigms.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Overstated Novelty:**  
  - The claim of being the "first" or "unique" in combining ultra-long context, manual annotation, and narrative focus is somewhat overstated, as LV-Eval and Ada-LEval match/exceed context length and annotation quality (though not in the narrative domain), and Narrative XL covers the narrative domain (though with LLM-generated questions).
- **Alignment of Claims and Evidence:**  
  - The submission does not empirically compare question quality or challenge level with Narrative XL or LV-Eval, making it difficult to assess the practical impact of manual annotation.
- **Empirical Improvements:**  
  - Reported accuracy improvements may be due to dataset construction, annotation style, or implementation details, not just conceptual advances.
- **Terminology Differences:**  
  - "Manual annotation" and "expert annotation" are used to distinguish from LLM-generated questions, but the practical difference in question quality is not demonstrated.
- **Routine Extensions:**  
  - The addition of a leaderboard and public release is now standard practice and not a substantive novelty.

# 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Differences:**
  - NovelQA is the only benchmark combining ultra-long (200k+) narrative contexts with manual, expert annotation and a public leaderboard.
  - The narrative focus and manual annotation are substantive differences from LV-Eval (which is not narrative-focused) and Narrative XL (which is not manually annotated).
- **Main Relationships to Existing Research:**
  - Closest to LV-Eval (context length, annotation) and Narrative XL (domain), but not directly comparable to both on all axes.
  - Other benchmarks (BAMBOO, LongBench, Ada-LEval) are similar in task diversity and annotation, but differ in domain or context length.
- **Strongest Differentiation:**
  - Narrative domain + ultra-long context + manual annotation.
- **Weakest Differentiation:**
  - Annotation method (manual annotation is not unique), leaderboard/public release (now standard), question diversity (common in recent benchmarks).
- **Discrepancies:**
  - Overstatement of being "first" or "unique" without acknowledging LV-Eval and Ada-LEval.
  - Understatement of the annotation quality and context length in recent benchmarks.
  - Lack of empirical comparison with Narrative XL and LV-Eval on question quality or challenge level.

---

**In summary:**  
NovelQA’s main contribution is the combination of ultra-long narrative contexts and manual annotation, but the degree of novelty is less than claimed due to the existence of LV-Eval (ultra-long, manual annotation) and Narrative XL (narrative, large scale). The field is rapidly converging on these features, and the submission would benefit from a more nuanced and comparative discussion of recent benchmarks. The strongest differentiation is the intersection of narrative domain, ultra-long context, and manual annotation, but each element is present in prior work individually. The weakest differentiation is in annotation method and leaderboard, which are now common. The omission of LV-Eval and Ada-LEval is a significant gap in the related work discussion.
