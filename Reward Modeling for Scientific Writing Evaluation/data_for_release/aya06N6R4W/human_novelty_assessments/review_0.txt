This paper presents an end-to-end pipeline that leverages large language models (LLMs) to extract causal variables from unstructured text, construct causal graphs, merge graphs from multiple data sources, and ultimately perform counterfactual inference on the resulting structures. While the approach is comprehensive and integrates several causal tasks into a unified framework, the novelty is somewhat limited, as each individual component—causal graph discovery and counterfactual inference using LLMs—has been addressed in prior work. For example, causal discovery with LLMs has been explored in "Efficient Causal Graph Discovery Using Large Language Models" and "Causal Graph Discovery with Retrieval-Augmented Generation based Large Language Models," while counterfactual inference with LLMs has been studied in works such as "Using LLMs for Explaining Sets of Counterfactual Examples to Final Users" and "Do Models Explain Themselves? Counterfactual Simulatability of Natural Language Explanations." The primary contribution of this paper lies in combining these tasks into a novel end-to-end pipeline, which does offer some merit in terms of methodological integration. However, since the individual steps are not themselves new, the overall advance is incremental rather than groundbreaking. The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work to better clarify the specific distinctions and contributions of the proposed framework relative to existing approaches.
