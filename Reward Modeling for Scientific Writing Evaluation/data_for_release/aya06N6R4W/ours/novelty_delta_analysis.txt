# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission proposes an end-to-end, explainable pipeline for causal structure discovery and counterfactual inference directly from unstructured natural language (news articles) using LLMs. It includes LLM-based variable extraction, graph construction, multi-source graph merging, and counterfactual inference.

**Closest Prior Works:**
- **Cluster 1 (End-to-End LLM Causal Inference):** The most closely related works are those that use LLMs for direct extraction and inference from unstructured text, notably:

  - *Causal Inference Using LLM-Guided Discovery*
  - *ALCM: Autonomous LLM-Augmented Causal Discovery Framework*
  - *Large Language Models for Constrained-Based Causal Discovery*
  - *LLM-initialized Differentiable Causal Discovery*
  - *Causal Graph Discovery with Retrieval-Augmented Generation based LLMs*
  - *From Query Tools to Causal Architects: Harnessing LLMs for Advanced Causal Discovery from Data*

- The submission is firmly in **Cluster 1** (LLM-only, end-to-end pipelines), with some overlap with **Cluster 2** (hybrid approaches) in its use of graph merging and explainability.
- Unlike hybrid methods, the submission does not appear to integrate statistical or data-driven causal discovery algorithms, instead relying on LLMs for all steps.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses the challenge of extracting and reasoning about causality from unstructured text, a problem also tackled by several recent works.
- Its focus on counterfactual inference from LLM-extracted graphs is less common, though not unique (see *Causal Inference Using LLM-Guided Discovery* and *ALCM*).
- The use of real-world news articles as data is a distinguishing but not unique feature; other works use clinical or manufacturing text.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is not the first to propose end-to-end LLM pipelines for causal discovery from text, but it is among the few to explicitly include multi-source graph merging and a detailed analysis of LLM bottlenecks in counterfactual reasoning.
- The field is active, with several recent works (2023–2024) proposing similar pipelines, often in different domains or with different LLM prompting strategies.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

## Claimed Contribution 1: End-to-end, explainable causal structure discovery and counterfactual inference from unstructured text using LLMs

- **Most similar prior work:** *ALCM: Autonomous LLM-Augmented Causal Discovery Framework*, *Causal Inference Using LLM-Guided Discovery*, *Causality extraction from medical text using LLMs*.
- **Claimed difference:** End-to-end pipeline, explainability, direct application to unstructured news text, and counterfactual inference.
- **Independent assessment:**
  - *ALCM* and *LLM-Guided Discovery* both propose end-to-end pipelines for causal discovery and inference from text, including counterfactuals.
  - *Gopalakrishnan et al.* focus on extraction, not inference.
  - The submission’s novelty is not in the existence of an end-to-end pipeline, but possibly in the specific combination of steps (e.g., multi-source graph merging) and the detailed analysis of LLM bottlenecks.
  - The explainability claim is common in recent works, many of which emphasize interpretable outputs.
  - The use of news articles is a domain choice, not a methodological advance.

## Claimed Contribution 2: Merging causal graphs from multiple sources using a second LLM

- **Most similar prior work:** *Causal Graph Discovery with Retrieval-Augmented Generation based LLMs*, *ALCM*, *LLM-initialized Differentiable Causal Discovery*.
- **Claimed difference:** Use of a second LLM to merge graphs for a more exhaustive representation.
- **Independent assessment:**
  - Several recent works propose merging or integrating causal knowledge from multiple sources, sometimes using LLMs for aggregation or consensus.
  - The specific use of a "second LLM" for merging is a technical detail; the conceptual advance (multi-source graph integration) is present in prior work.
  - The submission does not provide evidence that its merging approach is fundamentally different from, or more effective than, existing aggregation/consensus methods.

## Claimed Contribution 3: Disentangling steps in LLM-based counterfactual reasoning and identifying bottlenecks

- **Most similar prior work:** *Causal Inference Using LLM-Guided Discovery*, *Large Language Models are Effective Priors for Causal Graph Discovery*, *LLM Causal Reasoning Benchmarking*.
- **Claimed difference:** Detailed analysis of where LLMs fail in counterfactual reasoning, showing that prediction is the main bottleneck.
- **Independent assessment:**
  - Several benchmarking and analysis papers (e.g., *LLM Causal Reasoning Benchmarking*) systematically evaluate LLMs’ performance on causal reasoning, including counterfactuals.
  - The submission’s stepwise analysis (disentangling reasoning steps) is a useful contribution, but not unique; similar analyses exist in benchmarking papers.
  - The identification of the prediction step as the bottleneck is a specific empirical finding, but may depend on implementation and prompt design.
