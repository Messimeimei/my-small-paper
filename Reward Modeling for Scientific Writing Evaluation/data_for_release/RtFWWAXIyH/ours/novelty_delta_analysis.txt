# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**IM-3D (Melas-Kyriazi et al. 2024):**

- *Independent Assessment*: IM-3D also fine-tunes video diffusion models for multi-view/3D generation and introduces iterative feedback for consistency. The author’s characterization is accurate but omits specifics, possibly to avoid direct comparison.

**Wonder3D (Long et al. 2023):**
- *Author Characterization*: Groups Wonder3D with MVDream as using "attention layers learned from limited 3D data" to boost consistency; also credits Wonder3D as inspiration for camera embedding.
- *Pattern*: Positions prior work as limited by data and as inspiration for a component.
- *Independent Assessment*: Wonder3D uses cross-domain diffusion and hybrid 2D/3D priors, not solely attention layers. The characterization is partially accurate but simplifies Wonder3D’s contributions. The use of camera embedding is a common technique, not unique to Wonder3D.

**LGM (Tang et al. 2024):**
- *Author Characterization*: Cites LGM for 3D Gaussian splitting, fast convergence, and Plücker ray embeddings; claims difference in data augmentation vs. direct use of model outputs.
- *Pattern*: Positions LGM as a reconstruction baseline and highlights a procedural difference.
- *Independent Assessment*: LGM is a feed-forward 3D generative model trained on synthetic data, often using data augmentation. The submission’s use of model outputs for training the reconstruction module is a procedural difference, but the core idea of using 3D Gaussian representations is shared. The distinction is accurate but may overstate the novelty of the procedural change.

**RenderDiffusion (Anciukevičius et al. 2024):**
- *Author Characterization*: "VideoMV is also different from RenderDiffusion... which do not use pretrained 2D diffusion models due to the structure difference."
- *Pattern*: Emphasizes architectural difference.
- *Independent Assessment*: RenderDiffusion integrates 3D reconstruction into the diffusion process but does not rely on pre-trained 2D models. The distinction is accurate, but the practical impact of this difference is not fully explored.

**General Patterns:**
- The authors tend to acknowledge similarity with concurrent work but avoid detailed, head-to-head technical comparisons.
- Differences are often framed as procedural or architectural, sometimes without clear evidence of substantive impact.
- There is a tendency to group prior work by a single characteristic, which may understate their full contributions.
