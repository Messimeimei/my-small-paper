This submission systematically investigates whether gender bias present in pre-trained causal LLMs (such as Mistral, Falcon, and Llama) transfers to downstream tasks (pronoun co-reference resolution) when models are adapted via prompting, rather than fine-tuning. The key empirical finding is a strong, robust correlation (≥0.90) between intrinsic and downstream gender bias in prompt-adapted causal LLMs, in contrast to prior work on fine-tuned masked language models, which found weak or no such transfer. The study’s methodological novelty lies in its focus on prompt-based adaptation in causal LLMs and its systematic variation of prompt and few-shot factors, providing robustness evidence for the main result. While the authors’ claims of being the first to systematically analyze bias transfer in this adaptation regime are largely accurate, the distinction from some recent related work (e.g., Ladhak et al., 2023) could be more clearly articulated. Overall, the submission offers a substantive empirical advance in understanding bias transfer dynamics in modern LLMs, though its conceptual novelty is primarily in the adaptation regime and model type rather than in the general framework of bias transfer analysis.
