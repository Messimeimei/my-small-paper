This submission introduces ML4MILP, a large-scale, open-source MILP benchmark featuring over 100,000 instances and a novel, embedding-based instance classification scheme that combines graph statistics and GNN-derived embeddings. Its main methodological advance is the use of GNN-based embeddings for fine-grained instance categorization, which, while new for MILP datasets, is an incremental extension of existing clustering and representation learning techniques. The dataset’s scale and the embedding-based classification provide more nuanced groupings than prior benchmarks (e.g., Distributional MIPLIB, ML4CO), though the underlying problem diversity remains similar. Some claims of “first” or uniquely fine-grained classification are somewhat overstated, as prior datasets do offer domain and hardness labels, and the provision of high-quality solutions and benchmarking protocols largely follow established practice. Overall, the submission’s primary contribution is infrastructural—advancing benchmark scale and classification methodology—rather than introducing fundamentally new algorithmic concepts.
