## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Manual to Learned Policies:** Early work relied on hand-crafted heuristics; recent work uses GNNs/GCNs and RL/imitation learning to learn policies directly from data.
- **From Synthetic to Real-world Benchmarks:** There is a clear shift toward large, diverse, and real-world datasets (Submission, 1, 7, 13, 16).
- **From Single-task to Multi-task and Adaptive Methods:** Methods are evolving from targeting single tasks (e.g., branching) to more holistic approaches (e.g., LNS, solver configuration, instance classification).
- **From Static to Adaptive and Hybrid Models:** Hybrid architectures (GNN+MLP), adaptive heuristic selection (multi-armed bandit), and curriculum/self-paced learning are being adopted for efficiency and generalization.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Graph-based Representations:** Now foundational for encoding MILP structure in learning-based methods.
- **Learning-based Heuristics:** RL and imitation learning are being extended to more complex solver components (LNS, node selection, reoptimization).
- **Benchmarking Infrastructure:** New datasets and evaluation frameworks are enabling more rigorous and reproducible research.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Imitation Learning vs. Reinforcement Learning:** Both are used for learning solver policies; some works combine them (e.g., RL with demonstration data).
- **GNNs vs. Hybrid Models:** GNNs are powerful but computationally expensive; hybrid models (GNN+MLP) offer efficiency for large-scale or CPU-only settings.
- **Heuristic vs. ML-based LNS:** Traditional LNS is being augmented or replaced by learning-based policies, with evidence of superior performance and scalability.
