# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission, *SceneLock*, proposes a reversible adversarial learning framework for protecting camera-based autonomous driving data, integrating adversarial perturbations with image steganography for robust, reversible data protection.
- Within the research landscape, this work is most closely aligned with the **Reversible Adversarial Example (RAE) Generation (Image Domain)** cluster, and also draws from the **Steganography and Data Hiding** cluster. Its application focus is on **autonomous driving**, intersecting with the **Privacy and Protection in Autonomous Driving** cluster.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **RAE-VWP**: Reversible adversarial example-based privacy and copyright protection (medical images, but conceptually similar).
- **Frequency domain-based reversible adversarial attacks**: Focus on privacy protection in IoT, using frequency domain embedding.
- **Reversible Adversarial Examples based on Self-Embedding Watermark**: Embeds watermarks for reversibility.
- **RAEDiff**: Uses diffusion models for reversible adversarial examples.
- **Privacy Protection for Image Sharing Using Reversible Adversarial Examples**: General image privacy.
- **Reversible Adversarial Attack based on Reversible Image Transformation**: Focuses on transformation-based reversibility.
- **Seek-and-Hide: Adversarial Steganography via Deep Reinforcement Learning**: Integrates adversarial learning and steganography.
- **Privacy of Autonomous Vehicles: Risks, Protection Methods, and Future Directions**: Survey of privacy in AVs.

**Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission’s dual-module (NSE/NSD) system for reversible encoding/decoding is a variant of the RAE paradigm, but the explicit integration of advanced steganography for perturbation embedding is less common in prior AV-focused works.
- The problem space—protecting AV data from unauthorized use while ensuring full recoverability for authorized users—has been addressed in other domains (medical, IoT), but not specifically for AV camera data with this combination of techniques.

**Evaluation Approaches:**
- The use of KITTI, nuScenes, and Waymo aligns with standard AV benchmarks.
- Metrics (image degradation for unauthorized users, restoration fidelity, obstruction of manual annotation) are consistent with dual-objective evaluation in the RAE literature.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is not the first to propose reversible adversarial examples or to combine adversarial learning with steganography, but it appears to be the first to do so specifically for AV camera data with a dual-module system and explicit focus on both AI model and manual annotation obstruction.
- The technical novelty is incremental in the context of the broader RAE and steganography literature, but the application to AV data and the specific integration of modules may represent a new combination.
