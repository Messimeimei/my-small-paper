This assessment finds that the EDiT submission primarily advances the field through a specific integration of Local SGD, model sharding, and layer-wise parameter synchronization, targeting communication efficiency and heterogeneity in large-scale LLM training. While the pseudo gradient penalty and fully asynchronous variant (A-EDiT) are presented as novel, similar stability mechanisms and asynchrony strategies are well-established in prior literature; the main contribution is their tailored application and empirical validation at LLM scale. The authors’ characterizations of related work sometimes understate the sophistication of existing solutions, and several relevant works (e.g., Qsparse-Local-SGD, SHAT, Pisces) are omitted, potentially overstating EDiT’s uniqueness. The most substantive differentiation lies in the system-level engineering and comprehensive large-scale experiments, rather than in fundamental algorithmic innovation. Reviewers should calibrate novelty expectations accordingly, recognizing that the field increasingly values robust system integration and empirical scale, with many recent advances being incremental combinations of established techniques.
