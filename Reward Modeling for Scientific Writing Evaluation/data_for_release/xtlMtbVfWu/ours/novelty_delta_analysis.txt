# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission, EDiT, is positioned at the intersection of communication-efficient distributed training (especially for LLMs), Local SGD variants, and asynchrony/heterogeneity-aware methods.
- It claims to advance the state of the art by integrating Local SGD with model sharding and layer-wise parameter synchronization, introducing a pseudo gradient penalty for stability, and providing a fully asynchronous variant (A-EDiT) for heterogeneous clusters.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Local SGD and its Variants:** Lin et al. (2019), Wang et al. (2019), Douillard et al. (2023), and the scaling law analysis for Local SGD (uncited).
- **Asynchronous Local SGD:** Liu et al. (2024), SHAT (uncited), and CO2 (Sun et al., 2023).
- **Communication-Overlap and Model Sharding:** CO2 (Sun et al., 2023), Qsparse-Local-SGD (uncited).
- **Federated/Heterogeneity-Aware Training:** TimelyFL (Zhang et al., 2023), Pisces (uncited).

**Methodological Clusters:**
- EDiT bridges the Local SGD cluster (communication reduction via local updates), the asynchrony/heterogeneity cluster (robustness to stragglers), and the communication-overlap/model sharding cluster (overlapping communication and computation, memory efficiency).
- The pseudo gradient penalty aligns with the stability/robustness enhancement cluster.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- EDiT addresses communication bottlenecks, straggler/heterogeneity issues, and instability in LLM training—problems widely recognized in the field.
- Its evaluation metrics (convergence, generalization, acceleration, scalability, stability) are standard, but the focus on large-scale LLMs and asynchronous pre-training is relatively recent and less explored in prior work.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission’s integration of Local SGD with model sharding and layer-wise sync is a logical extension of existing trends, but similar ideas (e.g., communication overlap, quantization, sparsification) have been explored.
- The pseudo gradient penalty is a new stability mechanism, but the field has seen various penalties and momentum strategies for similar purposes.
- The asynchronous variant (A-EDiT) is conceptually similar to prior asynchronous Local SGD and federated learning approaches, though its application to LLMs and specific implementation details may differ.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**CO2 (Sun et al., 2023):**
- **Author Characterization:** "In most existing Local SGD methods, parameter synchronization operations will introduce non-overlapped communication overhead."
- **Pattern:** Authors use CO2 to highlight the lack of communication-computation overlap in prior Local SGD methods.
- **Independent Assessment:** CO2 specifically addresses communication-computation overlap, but other works (e.g., Qsparse-Local-SGD, not cited) also address communication efficiency. The characterization is accurate for many Local SGD variants, but not all.
- **Discrepancy:** The authors do not acknowledge that CO2 and similar works already address overlap, potentially overstating EDiT’s uniqueness in this aspect.

**Asynchronous Local-SGD Training for Language Modeling (Liu et al., 2024):**
- **Author Characterization:** "Current Local SGD methods diminish the impact of random stragglers, but still struggle with consistent stragglers within heterogeneous devices."
- **Pattern:** Authors position their asynchronous variant (A-EDiT) as addressing a limitation in prior asynchronous Local SGD.
- **Independent Assessment:** Liu et al. (2024) do address stragglers, but the distinction between "random" and "consistent" stragglers is not always clear-cut. Other works (e.g., SHAT, TimelyFL) also address persistent heterogeneity.
- **Discrepancy:** The characterization may understate the sophistication of prior asynchronous methods in handling heterogeneity.

**TimelyFL (Zhang et al., 2023):**
- **Author Characterization:** "TimelyFL dynamically adjusts the local training workload according to the real-time resource situation."
- **Pattern:** Authors cite TimelyFL as an example of adaptive workload in federated learning.
- **Independent Assessment:** Accurate, but TimelyFL also addresses staleness and partial model training, which are not discussed.
- **Discrepancy:** The citation is accurate but incomplete, omitting other relevant aspects of TimelyFL.

**Don't Use Large Mini-Batches, Use Local SGD (Lin et al., 2019):**
- **Author Characterization:** "Subsequent studies have improved upon this foundational paradigm to improve the performance."
- **Pattern:** Authors acknowledge Local SGD as foundational, with subsequent improvements.
- **Independent Assessment:** Accurate, but the submission does not discuss more recent or advanced Local SGD variants (e.g., Qsparse-Local-SGD, SlowMo, scaling law studies).
- **Discrepancy:** The landscape of Local SGD improvements is broader than acknowledged.

**General Patterns:**
- Authors tend to highlight limitations in prior work to emphasize their own contributions, sometimes omitting nuanced capabilities of cited works.
- There is a tendency to understate the breadth of existing solutions for communication overlap, asynchrony, and heterogeneity.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

## 1. **Integration of Local SGD with Model Sharding and Layer-wise Parameter Synchronization**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** CO2 (Sun et al., 2023), Qsparse-Local-SGD (uncited), and general Local SGD variants.
- **Claimed Difference:** EDiT uniquely combines Local SGD with model sharding and performs layer-wise parameter synchronization during the forward pass, reducing communication/memory overhead and enabling overlap.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Model Sharding:** Model sharding is a known technique (e.g., in DeepSpeed, Megatron-LM), but its integration with Local SGD and layer-wise sync is less common.
  - **Layer-wise Sync During Forward Pass:** This is a specific implementation detail that may reduce peak memory and enable overlap, but similar ideas (e.g., pipelined or overlapping communication) have been explored.
  - **Delta:** The combination is novel in the context of Local SGD for LLMs, but the individual components are not new. The main difference is the specific integration and application to LLMs.
  - **Superficial vs. Substantive:** The difference is more in system design/engineering than in fundamental algorithmic innovation.

## 2. **Pseudo Gradient Penalty for Training Stability**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** CO2 (staleness gap penalty), SlowMo (momentum strategies), and other stability mechanisms.
- **Claimed Difference:** EDiT introduces a "novel" penalty to suppress loss spikes and improve stability.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Penalty Mechanisms:** Penalties for staleness, gradient noise, or instability are common. The specific form of the "pseudo gradient penalty" is not detailed here, but the concept is not fundamentally new.
  - **Delta:** The novelty lies in the specific formulation and application to LLM training with Local SGD. The idea of penalizing unstable updates is established.
  - **Superficial vs. Substantive:** Likely a routine adaptation unless the penalty is shown to have unique theoretical or empirical properties.

## 3. **A-EDiT: Fully Asynchronous Variant for Heterogeneous Clusters**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Asynchronous Local SGD (Liu et al., 2024), SHAT (uncited), TimelyFL, Pisces.
- **Claimed Difference:** A-EDiT handles "consistent stragglers" in heterogeneous clusters.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Asynchrony for Heterogeneity:** Many prior works address asynchrony and heterogeneity, including persistent stragglers.
  - **Delta:** The main difference may be in the specific implementation for LLMs and the integration with EDiT’s other features.
  - **Superficial vs. Substantive:** The conceptual advance is incremental; the main novelty is in system integration and empirical validation at LLM scale.

## 4. **Comprehensive Large-Scale Verification and Analysis**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Scaling law studies (uncited), large-scale experiments in Pisces, TimelyFL, CO2.
- **Claimed Difference:** First to provide comprehensive large-scale analysis of asynchronous pre-training for LLMs.
- **Independent Assessment:**
  - **Empirical Scope:** Large-scale LLM experiments are becoming more common, but comprehensive asynchronous pre-training studies are still rare.
  - **Delta:** The scale and thoroughness of evaluation may be a distinguishing factor, but not a conceptual advance.
  - **Superficial vs. Substantive:** This is a contribution in empirical validation, not in methodology.
