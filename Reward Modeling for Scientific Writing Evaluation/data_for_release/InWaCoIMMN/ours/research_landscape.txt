## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the papers, several methodological paradigms emerge, often with overlapping or complementary aims:

- **Causal Probing and Interventions**: Methods that intervene on model representations or inputs to assess causal relationships between linguistic properties and model behavior (e.g., CALM, GBIs, naturalistic causal probing, interchange interventions, path patching, counterfactual embeddings).
- **Probing Classifiers**: Use of linear or non-linear classifiers (probes) to test for the presence of linguistic properties in model representations (e.g., Holmes, usage-based probing, LEACE, INLP).
- **Benchmarking and Evaluation Frameworks**: Construction of large-scale, systematic benchmarks for evaluating linguistic competence, robustness, and interpretability (e.g., Holmes, CausalGym, GLUE-X, BEAR).
- **Concept Erasure and Fairness**: Methods for removing or controlling for specific information (e.g., gender, number) in representations to study bias or fairness (e.g., INLP, LEACE, concept scrubbing).
- **Circuit and Mechanistic Analysis**: Tracing and reverse-engineering model components responsible for specific behaviors (e.g., circuit analysis, path patching).
- **Pattern-based and Prompting Approaches**: Use of discrete or continuous patterns, prompts, or templates to elicit or evaluate specific knowledge (e.g., CONAN, prompting for hypernymy, pattern-based relation induction).
- **Causal Abstraction and Training**: Methods that align or train models to realize explicit causal structures (e.g., Interchange Intervention Training, causal abstraction analysis).

### Method Clusters

- **Cluster 1: Causal Probing and Interventions**
  - Submission Paper, Related Papers 1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 9, 11, 13, 12
  - These works focus on understanding not just what is encoded, but what is causally used by models, often via interventions (gradient-based, input-level, or representation-level).
  - They differ in the granularity (input vs. representation), the type of intervention (gradient-based, naturalistic, path patching), and the linguistic phenomena targeted.

- **Cluster 2: Probing Classifiers and Benchmarks**
  - Related Papers 2, 5, 7, 8, 13, 14, 15
  - These works use classifier-based probes and/or large-scale benchmarks to systematically evaluate linguistic competence, knowledge, or bias.
  - They vary in the scope (number of phenomena, tasks, or models), the type of probe (linear, information-theoretic), and the focus (competence, fairness, robustness).

- **Cluster 3: Concept Erasure and Fairness**
  - Related Papers 14, 15, 9
  - Focused on removing or controlling for specific properties in representations, often to study or mitigate bias.

- **Cluster 4: Mechanistic and Circuit Analysis**
  - Related Papers 12, 3, 11
  - These works aim to reverse-engineer or mechanistically explain model behaviors, often using causal interventions and tracing.

- **Cluster 5: Pattern/Prompt-based Knowledge Probing**
  - Related Papers 19, 20, 24
  - Use of patterns, prompts, or continuous pattern representations to probe or improve model knowledge, especially for lexical inference.

- **Cluster 6: Causal Abstraction and Training**
  - Related Papers 3, 10
  - Move beyond analysis to training models to realize explicit causal structures.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Shift from Correlational to Causal Analysis**: There is a clear trend from correlational probing (what is encoded) to causal probing (what is used and how interventions affect behavior).
- **From Black-box to Mechanistic Interpretability**: Methods are moving from black-box evaluation to more mechanistic, component-level explanations (e.g., circuit analysis, path patching).
- **Unified and Scalable Benchmarks**: There is a push for comprehensive, scalable benchmarks (Holmes, CausalGym, GLUE-X, BEAR) to enable systematic comparison.
- **Bridging Analysis and Training**: Some works (e.g., IIT) aim to not just analyze but also train models to realize interpretable, causal structures.
- **Fairness and Bias Mitigation**: Concept erasure methods are increasingly used to study and mitigate bias, often evaluated with causal or information-theoretic metrics.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Interpretability of LLMs**: Understanding what linguistic properties are encoded and used by language models, and how these relate to model behavior.
- **Causal Structure and Competence**: Distinguishing between causal and spurious correlations in model predictions; measuring true linguistic competence.
- **Robustness and Generalization**: Evaluating and improving model robustness to input perturbations, adversarial attacks, and distribution shifts.
- **Bias and Fairness**: Identifying and mitigating bias in model representations and predictions, especially regarding protected attributes.
- **Mechanistic Explanations**: Providing detailed, component-level explanations for specific model behaviors.
- **Evaluation and Benchmarking**: Developing comprehensive, reliable benchmarks for linguistic competence, robustness, and interpretability.
- **Knowledge Probing**: Assessing and improving models' knowledge of lexical relations, inference, and reasoning.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Interpretability and Causal Analysis**: Submission Paper, Related Papers 1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 9, 11, 12, 13 all tackle interpretability, but differ in their focus (e.g., competence, bias, specific linguistic phenomena) and methods (causal probing, circuit analysis, information-theoretic frameworks).
- **Robustness**: Related Papers 18, 21, 22, 23 focus on robustness, using input perturbations, OOD benchmarks, adversarial evaluation, and analysis of evaluation assumptions.
- **Bias/Fairness**: Related Papers 14, 15, 9 address bias via concept erasure and information-theoretic analysis.
- **Knowledge Probing**: Related Papers 2, 5, 19, 20, 24 use probing, pattern-based, or prompting methods to assess knowledge of linguistic phenomena.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Encoding to Usage**: Early works focus on what is encoded; recent works emphasize what is used causally for predictions.
- **From Task Performance to Underlying Competence**: There is a distinction between surface-level task performance and deeper linguistic competence, with new metrics and benchmarks aiming to disentangle the two.
- **From Single Phenomena to Holistic Evaluation**: Movement from probing single properties to comprehensive, multi-phenomena benchmarks (e.g., Holmes, CausalGym).

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets and Evaluation Methods

- **Lexical Inference Tasks**: Used in the submission and several related papers (e.g., SherLIiC, Levy/Holt, Holmes).
- **Probing Benchmarks**: Holmes (208 datasets), CausalGym, GLUE-X, BEAR.
- **Custom Counterfactual or Intervention Datasets**: For causal probing (e.g., naturalistic counterfactuals in Spanish, IOI task for GPT-2).
- **Standard NLP Benchmarks**: SQuAD, ANLI, AdvGLUE, GLUE tasks.
- **Specialized Datasets**: MQNLI (Multiply Quantified NLI), CEBaB (controlled generation), number agreement, aspect in Russian, etc.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Competence/Alignment Metrics**: Alignment between ground-truth causal structure and model representations (CALM, causal abstraction).
- **Probe Accuracy**: Performance of classifiers on probing tasks.
- **Causal Efficacy**: Ability of interventions to affect model predictions.
- **Fairness/Bias Metrics**: Linear separability, reduction in bias, fairness in classification.
- **Robustness Metrics**: Accuracy under perturbations, OOD accuracy, performance drop.
- **Information-theoretic Metrics**: Mutual information, ν-information, intrinsic information between subspaces and concepts.
- **Task-specific Metrics**: Faithfulness, completeness, minimality (circuit analysis), rationale overlap, translation quality.

### Patterns and Comparisons

- **Shift to Causal and Information-theoretic Metrics**: Increasing use of metrics that go beyond accuracy to measure causal alignment, information content, and robustness.
- **Comprehensive, Multi-faceted Evaluation**: Benchmarks like Holmes and CausalGym evaluate across many tasks and phenomena, often with control tasks and efficiency considerations.
- **Robustness and OOD Generalization**: Growing emphasis on evaluating models under distribution shift, adversarial conditions, and input perturbations.
- **Fairness and Bias**: Use of concept erasure and fairness metrics to assess and mitigate bias.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From Correlational to Causal Probing**: Early probing methods focused on what is encoded; recent work (CALM, GBIs, naturalistic interventions) emphasizes causal relationships and competence.
- **From Black-box to Mechanistic Interpretability**: The field is moving toward mechanistic explanations (circuit analysis, path patching) and explicit causal models (IIT, causal abstraction).
- **From Single-task to Comprehensive Benchmarks**: Evaluation has evolved from single-task or single-phenomenon probing to large-scale, multi-phenomena benchmarks (Holmes, CausalGym, GLUE-X, BEAR).
- **From Analysis to Training for Causality**: Methods like IIT train models to realize causal structures, not just analyze them post hoc.
- **From Discrete to Continuous Pattern-based Probing**: Pattern-based methods have evolved from discrete templates to continuous, optimized patterns (CONAN), improving efficiency and performance.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Causal Probing**: CALM and GBIs extend causal probing to arbitrarily-encoded properties and provide quantitative competence metrics.
- **Concept Erasure**: INLP and LEACE provide increasingly principled and efficient methods for erasing concepts, with LEACE offering closed-form guarantees.
- **Benchmarks**: Holmes, CausalGym, and BEAR build on earlier probing and evaluation frameworks, expanding coverage and enabling fairer comparisons.
- **Mechanistic Analysis**: Circuit analysis and path patching extend causal interventions to the component level, enabling detailed explanations.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Causal vs. Correlational Probing**: Causal methods challenge the sufficiency of correlational probes, showing that encoding does not imply usage.
- **Pattern-based vs. Probing Approaches**: Pattern/prompt-based methods offer alternative ways to probe knowledge, sometimes outperforming traditional probes for specific tasks.
- **Fairness via Erasure vs. Causal Control**: Concept erasure methods (INLP, LEACE) and causal control frameworks (do-intervention, information-theoretic) offer complementary tools for bias mitigation.
