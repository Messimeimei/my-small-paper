### Patterns in Evaluation

- **Regret and cost minimization are the dominant metrics** for online inventory/control.
- **Empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world data** is common, with increasing use of large-scale, operational datasets.
- **Theoretical guarantees (regret bounds, optimality gaps, competitive ratios)** are often provided alongside empirical results.
- **Service-level and operational metrics** are used in joint optimization and supply chain contexts.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Online learning papers** focus on regret and cost relative to optimal or clairvoyant policies.
- **Scenario-based and stochastic programming papers** emphasize cost gap reduction and service metrics.
- **Nonparametric/data-driven papers** highlight generalization error and robustness to demand uncertainty.
- **Classical policy analysis** uses optimality gaps and asymptotic results.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Classical to Online and Adaptive:**  
  - Early work focused on base-stock and (s, S) policies (Cluster 5), with later work extending these to perishable, censored, and multi-product settings.
  - The field has shifted toward online, adaptive, and data-driven methods (Clusters 1, 2), capable of handling nonstationary, adversarial, and censored demand.

- **From Parametric to Nonparametric and Distribution-Free:**  
  - Movement away from strong distributional assumptions (i.i.d., known demand) toward nonparametric, robust, and distribution-free algorithms (Clusters 2, 4).

- **Integration of Learning and Optimization:**  
  - Increasingly, prediction (ML, feature engineering) and optimization are integrated (Cluster 3, 4), with scenario-based and decision-focused learning approaches.

- **Theoretical Foundations and Guarantees:**  
  - Theoretical analysis (regret, generalization error, optimality gap, competitive ratio) has become central, with new bounds and guarantees for increasingly complex settings.

- **Bridging Control and Online Optimization:**  
  - Recent work (e.g., GAPS, Paper 10) bridges online optimization and control theory, enabling regret guarantees for policy selection in dynamical systems.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Base-stock policies** serve as a recurring building block, extended to perishable, censored, and multi-product settings.
- **Online convex optimization and regret minimization** are foundational, with extensions to RL, bandits, and nonconvex settings.
- **Feature engineering and ML** are increasingly used to enhance policy learning and prediction.
- **Scenario-based optimization** extends classical stochastic programming to data-driven, distribution-free settings.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing:**  
  - Online learning vs. classical heuristics (Submission, Papers 1, 2 vs. 5, 9)
  - Parametric vs. nonparametric learning (Papers 3, 11, 14, 15)
  - RL/bandit vs. online convex optimization (Papers 2, 7, 8 vs. 1, 10)

- **Complementary:**  
  - Feature-enhanced policies (Submission, Paper 3) complement online learning by improving expressiveness and generalization.
  - Scenario-based optimization (Paper 4) complements online learning by integrating robust prediction with operational decision-making.
  - Theoretical analysis (Paper 3, 5, 7, 13) underpins and justifies practical algorithm design.
