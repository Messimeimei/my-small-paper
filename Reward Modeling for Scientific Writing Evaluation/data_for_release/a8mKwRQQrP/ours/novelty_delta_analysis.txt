**Discrepancies:**

- The adaptation of GAPS to inventory is presented as a major conceptual advance, but the technical changes (feature enhancement, AdaGrad, handling non-differentiability) are incremental and implementation-focused.

**Rhetoric:**

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Contribution 1: GAPSI Algorithm (Feature-Enhanced Base-Stock Policy with AdaGrad and Non-Differentiability Handling)**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** GAPS (Lin et al., 2024), base-stock policy analysis (Snyder & Shen, 2019; Bu et al., 2023; Xie et al., 2024), AdaGrad (Streeter & McMahan, 2010; Duchi et al., 2011).
- **Claimed Difference:** GAPSI adapts GAPS to inventory by using a feature-enhanced base-stock policy, AdaGrad learning rates, and handling non-differentiability.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The main technical difference is the use of a feature-enhanced base-stock policy (target inventory as a linear function of features) and AdaGrad learning rates within the GAPS/OPS framework.
  - The handling of non-differentiability is via approximated gradients, as in GAPS, but with inventory-specific partial derivatives.
  - These are routine adaptations rather than conceptual breakthroughs; the core algorithmic structure is inherited from GAPS.
  - The use of AdaGrad is standard in online learning; its application here is natural but not novel.
  - The feature-enhanced policy is a straightforward extension, similar to approaches in policy generalization (see Paper 3).

**Summary of Deltas:**
