## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the following methodological paradigms dominate:

- **Language Models for Molecules**: Leveraging transformer-based or autoregressive models trained on molecular string representations (SELFIES, SMILES) for molecule generation and property prediction (e.g., MoLlama in NExT-Mol, MOLGEN, SELFormer, MolGPT, FusionDTI).
- **Diffusion Models for 3D Generation**: Employing denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs) for generating 3D molecular structures, often with equivariant neural networks to respect molecular symmetries (e.g., DMT in NExT-Mol, EQGAT-diff, GeoLDM, MDM, MiDi, TextSMOG).
- **Joint/Hybrid Models**: Integrating multiple modalities or tasks, such as combining 1D language modeling with 3D diffusion (NExT-Mol), or jointly generating 2D graphs and 3D conformers (JODO, MiDi).
- **Latent Space Modeling**: Using autoencoders or latent diffusion to reduce dimensionality and improve controllability (GeoLDM).
- **Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)**: Applying GNNs for molecular graph representation and generation, sometimes with equivariant properties (GeoMol, GraphAF, SimSGT).
- **Multi-modal and Cross-modal Learning**: Bridging different molecular representations (1D, 2D, 3D, text) via cross-modal projectors or fusion modules (NExT-Mol, TextSMOG, nach0-pc, FusionDTI).
- **Feedback and Control Mechanisms**: Incorporating self-feedback, chemical feedback, or property conditioning to guide generation towards desired properties (MOLGEN, MolGen, GraphAF, TextSMOG).

### Method Clusters

- **Cluster 1: Language Model-based Molecular Generation**
  - NExT-Mol (MoLlama), MOLGEN, SELFormer, MolGPT, FusionDTI, MolGen, nach0-pc
  - Focus: Pretraining/fine-tuning on large molecular string corpora, often using SELFIES for robustness, with downstream tasks in generation or property prediction.

- **Cluster 2: Diffusion-based 3D Molecular Generation**
  - NExT-Mol (DMT), EQGAT-diff, GeoLDM, MDM, MiDi, TextSMOG, APMG
  - Focus: Denoising diffusion models for 3D conformer generation, with innovations in equivariant architectures, latent space modeling, and property conditioning.

- **Cluster 3: Joint/Hybrid and Cross-modal Models**
  - NExT-Mol (integration of 1D and 3D), JODO, MiDi, TextSMOG, nach0-pc, FusionDTI
  - Focus: Simultaneous or sequential modeling of multiple molecular modalities (e.g., 1D+3D, 2D+3D, text+3D), often with transfer learning or cross-modal projectors.

- **Cluster 4: Graph-based Generative Models**
  - GraphAF, GeoMol, SimSGT
  - Focus: Autoregressive, flow-based, or masked modeling of molecular graphs, sometimes with property optimization or improved tokenization/decoding.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **SELFIES over SMILES**: Increasing adoption of SELFIES for string-based molecular representations due to its robustness and 100% validity guarantee (NExT-Mol, SELFormer, FusionDTI).
- **Equivariance in 3D Models**: Strong trend towards E(3)/SE(3)-equivariant neural networks in 3D diffusion models to respect molecular symmetries (EQGAT-diff, GeoLDM, DMT).
- **Transfer and Cross-modal Learning**: Growing interest in leveraging large-scale 1D data to improve 3D generation (NExT-Mol), or fusing textual/structural information (TextSMOG, nach0-pc, FusionDTI).
- **Latent and Hybrid Diffusion**: Movement from direct feature-space diffusion to latent space (GeoLDM) and hybrid graph+3D diffusion (MiDi, JODO).
- **Feedback and Control**: Emergence of feedback mechanisms (self-feedback, chemical feedback) and property conditioning for more targeted molecule generation (MOLGEN, MolGen, GraphAF, TextSMOG).

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **3D Molecular Conformer Generation**: Generating valid, realistic, and diverse 3D molecular structures for drug discovery and material design (NExT-Mol, EQGAT-diff, GeoLDM, MDM, MiDi, APMG, GeoMol).
- **Molecular Graph Generation**: Creating chemically valid molecular graphs, often with property optimization (GraphAF, SimSGT, JODO, MiDi).
- **Bridging Modalities**: Connecting 1D (string), 2D (graph), and 3D (geometry) molecular representations for improved generative modeling (NExT-Mol, JODO, MiDi, nach0-pc).
- **Property-Conditioned/Controlled Generation**: Generating molecules with specific properties or in response to textual prompts (TextSMOG, GraphAF, MOLGEN, MolGen, MolGPT).
- **Benchmarking and Evaluation**: Establishing standardized datasets and metrics for fair comparison (MOSES).
- **Representation Learning**: Learning robust, valid, and expressive molecular representations for downstream tasks (SELFormer, SimSGT, FusionDTI).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **3D Conformer Generation**:
  - *Diffusion Models*: NExT-Mol, EQGAT-diff, GeoLDM, MDM, MiDi, APMG use various forms of diffusion models, differing in their handling of equivariance, latent space, and force modeling.
  - *Graph-based Approaches*: GeoMol uses MPNNs and torsion angle prediction; JODO and MiDi combine graph and 3D generation.
  - *Hybrid/Transfer Learning*: NExT-Mol uniquely leverages transfer from 1D language models to 3D diffusion.

- **Property-Conditioned Generation**:
  - *Language Model Conditioning*: MOLGEN, MolGen, MolGPT, TextSMOG use property or text conditioning in language models.
  - *Diffusion Model Conditioning*: TextSMOG, MDM, MiDi incorporate property or text guidance in diffusion processes.

- **Bridging Modalities**:
  - *Cross-modal Projectors*: NExT-Mol uses a cross-modal projector for 1D-to-3D transfer.
  - *Joint Diffusion*: JODO and MiDi perform joint 2D/3D or graph/3D diffusion.
  - *Multi-modal Fusion*: nach0-pc and FusionDTI integrate spatial and sequence information.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **Validity and Realism**: Emphasis on generating molecules that are both syntactically and chemically valid, with some works (NExT-Mol, GraphAF, SELFormer) guaranteeing 100% validity.
- **Scalability and Diversity**: Addressing the challenge of generating large, diverse, and complex molecules (GeoLDM, MDM, EQGAT-diff).
- **Controllability**: Increasing focus on controllable generation—either via property conditioning, text prompts, or feedback mechanisms.
- **Bridging Data Modalities**: Recognizing the value in leveraging large-scale 1D data for 3D tasks, and in integrating multiple molecular representations.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **QM9**: Widely used for small molecule generation and property prediction (NExT-Mol, EQGAT-diff, JODO, MDM, TextSMOG).
- **GEOM-DRUGS/GEOM-QM9**: Used for 3D conformer generation and evaluation (NExT-Mol, JODO, EQGAT-diff, MiDi).
- **PubChem3D, Crossdocked**: Used for large-scale pretraining and docking evaluation (EQGAT-diff, APMG).
- **MOSES**: Standardized benchmarking platform for molecular generation (MOSES).
- **ZINC, BindingDB, Davis, KIBA**: Used for property prediction, DTI, and graph generation (GraphAF, FusionDTI).

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Validity, Uniqueness, Novelty**: Core metrics for generative models, measuring chemical correctness, diversity, and originality (NExT-Mol, MDM, MiDi, MolGPT, MOSES).
- **3D-specific Metrics**: 3D Fréchet ChemNet Distance (FCD), COV-R, geometric similarity, stability (NExT-Mol, EQGAT-diff, MiDi).
- **Property-based Metrics**: QED, penalized logP, docking scores, synthetic accessibility (MolGen, TextSMOG, APMG).
- **Task-specific Metrics**: Prediction accuracy (SELFormer, FusionDTI), training/inference speed (EQGAT-diff, nach0-pc), downstream property prediction (SimSGT).

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Emphasis on 3D Quality**: Recent works prioritize 3D-specific metrics (FCD, COV-R, stability) over traditional 2D graph metrics.
- **Validity as a Baseline**: 100% validity is increasingly expected, especially with SELFIES-based or chemically-aware models.
- **Diversity and Novelty**: Diversity is measured both in terms of unique structures and property distributions.
- **Benchmarking Platforms**: MOSES and GEOM datasets are common for standardized comparison.
- **Property and Task Conditioning**: Evaluation often includes conditional generation or property optimization tasks.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **NExT-Mol**: Uses advanced 3D metrics (FCD, COV-R) and demonstrates relative improvements over prior SOTA.
- **Diffusion Models**: Often report stability and geometric similarity, with some (MiDi) showing dramatic improvements in stable molecule generation.
- **Language Models**: Focus on validity, diversity, and property prediction accuracy.
- **Hybrid Models**: Evaluate both graph and 3D aspects, sometimes with joint or multi-modal metrics.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From 1D to 3D**: Early works focused on 1D/2D representations (SMILES, graphs). Recent advances (NExT-Mol, MiDi, JODO) bridge these with 3D generation, leveraging transfer learning and joint modeling.
- **SELFIES Adoption**: Shift from SMILES to SELFIES for robust, valid string representations, enabling 100% validity in generative models (SELFormer, NExT-Mol, FusionDTI).
- **Diffusion Model Innovations**: Progression from direct feature-space diffusion (MDM) to latent space (GeoLDM), joint graph+3D diffusion (MiDi, JODO), and property/text-conditioned diffusion (TextSMOG).
- **Equivariance and Scalability**: Increasing use of E(3)/SE(3)-equivariant networks for 3D tasks, with focus on scalability to larger molecules (EQGAT-diff, GeoLDM, MDM).
- **Feedback and Control**: Emergence of self-feedback, chemical feedback, and property/text conditioning for more targeted and controllable generation (MOLGEN, MolGen, TextSMOG, GraphAF).
- **Multi-modal and Cross-modal Learning**: Recent works (NExT-Mol, nach0-pc, FusionDTI) integrate multiple data modalities, enabling richer representations and improved generative performance.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Language Models**: MoLlama (NExT-Mol) builds on the transformer LM paradigm, extending it to 3D tasks via transfer learning.
- **Diffusion Models**: DMT (NExT-Mol) extends diffusion models with relational attention and cross-modal transfer; MiDi and JODO extend to joint graph+3D generation.
- **Latent Space Modeling**: GeoLDM introduces latent diffusion for 3D molecules, improving scalability and controllability.
- **Equivariant Networks**: EQGAT-diff, GeoLDM, and DMT push the boundaries of equivariant modeling for 3D tasks.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing**: Direct 3D diffusion (MDM, EQGAT-diff) vs. latent diffusion (GeoLDM); graph-only (GraphAF, SimSGT) vs. joint graph+3D (MiDi, JODO).
- **Complementary**: Language models and diffusion models are increasingly combined (NExT-Mol, TextSMOG, nach0-pc); feedback mechanisms can be layered atop any generative paradigm.
