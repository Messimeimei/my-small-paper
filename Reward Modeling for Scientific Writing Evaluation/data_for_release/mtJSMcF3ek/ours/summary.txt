This submission offers a comprehensive and systematic empirical/theoretical study of LLM self-improvement, with its main novelty being the formalization and central use of the "generation-verification gap" (GV-Gap) as a unifying metric. While the concept of a gap between generation and verification is present in prior work, the explicit metric and its application across models and tasks, as well as the discovery of a scaling law for GV-Gap, are new contributions. The paper also provides a detailed, cross-model analysis of verification mechanisms, including ensemble verification, which is a substantive but incremental extension of existing meta-judging and reward aggregation methods. However, the submission tends to overstate the lack of systematic analysis and diversity in prior work, and does not fully engage with risks such as bias and diversity collapse highlighted in the literature. Overall, the work represents a significant incremental advance in empirical rigor and formalization, rather than a fundamentally new paradigm for LLM self-improvement.
