## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the following methodological themes emerge:

- **Pixel-level and Spatio-temporal Grounding in LMMs**: Large multimodal models (LMMs) that perform fine-grained (pixel-level or mask-level) grounding, often with dual encoders and adapters for spatial-temporal alignment (Related 2, 5, 8, 9).
- **Dense Video Captioning & Event Localization**: Approaches that jointly or sequentially model event segmentation and captioning, often leveraging transformers, sequence-to-sequence models, or reinforcement learning (Related 4, 12, 13, 14).
- **Contrastive and Cross-modal Learning**: Methods that use contrastive losses, token-aware objectives, or cross-modal transformers to align video and text representations (Related 6, 16, 15).
- **Automatic Annotation Pipelines**: Use of LLMs and automated pipelines to generate large-scale, high-quality training data for grounding and captioning tasks (Submission, Related 2, 9, 10, 8).
- **Audio-Visual Integration**: Incorporation of audio cues (e.g., ASR transcripts) to enrich video understanding (Related 5, 10).

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Grounded Video Caption Generation**: Generating captions for videos where entities/objects are explicitly grounded in the visual content (Submission, Related 1, 3, 7).
- **Fine-grained Visual Grounding**: Achieving pixel-level or spatio-temporal grounding of language in video, including object segmentation and temporal localization (Related 2, 5, 8, 9, 15).
- **Dense Video Captioning**: Jointly segmenting events and generating captions for each event in long videos (Related 4, 12, 13, 14).
- **Video-Text Alignment**: Improving the alignment between video and text representations for retrieval and understanding (Related 6, 16).
- **Data Scarcity for Grounded Tasks**: Lack of large-scale, high-quality datasets for grounded video captioning and grounding (Submission, Related 2, 9, 10, 8).
- **Zero-shot and Weakly Supervised Event Understanding**: Event detection and captioning with minimal or no supervision (Related 11, 12).
- **Audio-Visual Integration**: Leveraging audio signals for richer video understanding (Related 5, 10).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Grounded Captioning**: Submission and Related 1 both address the lack of grounded video captioning datasets and models, but Submission introduces a new task and dataset (GROC, HowToGround), while Related 1 focuses on noun phrase grounding with ActivityNet-Entities.
- **Fine-grained Grounding**: Related 2, 5, 8, 9, and 15 all tackle fine-grained grounding, but differ in granularity (pixel-level vs. bounding box), modality (video vs. image), and model architecture (dual encoders, adapters, transformers).
- **Dense Captioning**: Related 4, 12, 13, 14 all address dense video captioning, but differ in supervision (fully supervised, weakly supervised), modeling (sequence-to-sequence, bidirectional context, reinforcement learning), and integration of event localization and captioning.
- **Data Generation**: Submission, Related 2, 9, 10, and 8 all propose automatic annotation pipelines, but with different focuses (captioning, grounding, QA, conversation).

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **Joint Tasks**: Increasingly, problems are formulated as joint tasks (e.g., captioning + grounding, event localization + captioning).
- **Granularity**: There is a shift from coarse (scene-level) to fine-grained (object, pixel, temporal) grounding and understanding.
- **Multimodality**: Problems are being expanded to include multiple modalities (audio, video, text) and tasks (QA, retrieval, segmentation).

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **ActivityNet-Entities / ActivityNet Captions**: Widely used for grounded video description, captioning, and grounding tasks (Related 1, 3, 6, 7, 12, 13, 14).
- **HowTo100M / HowToGround / HowToCaption**: Large-scale instructional video datasets, with HowToGround and HowToCaption as new, automatically annotated resources (Submission, Related 10).
- **Vid-STG / HC-STVG**: Used for spatio-temporal video grounding (Related 5, 15).
- **YouCook2, MSR-VTT, MSVD, LSMDC**: Standard video captioning and retrieval datasets (Related 4, 10, 16).
- **GranD, GranD_f**: Large-scale, densely annotated datasets for grounded conversation and pixel-level grounding (Related 9).
- **TRECVID MEDTest/MEC**: For event detection and captioning (Related 11).

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Captioning Metrics**: BLEU, METEOR, CIDEr, ROUGE—standard for caption quality.
- **Grounding Metrics**: Grounding accuracy (linking phrases to boxes/masks), mAP, localization accuracy.
- **Retrieval Metrics**: Recall@K, text-video retrieval accuracy.
- **Segmentation Metrics**: For pixel-level grounding and referring segmentation.
- **Dense Captioning Metrics**: Event localization accuracy, joint segmentation-captioning performance.
- **Grounded Conversation Generation (GCG)**: Newer metrics for evaluating visually grounded conversations (Related 2, 9).

### Patterns in Evaluation

- **Multi-metric Evaluation**: Most works report both captioning and grounding metrics, reflecting the joint nature of tasks.
- **Benchmarking on New Datasets**: Many papers introduce new datasets and corresponding benchmarks, often with new or adapted metrics (e.g., GROC, HowToGround, GranD, ASRL).
- **Zero-shot and Transfer Evaluation**: Some works emphasize zero-shot or transfer performance, especially for retrieval and event detection (Related 11, 16).

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From Captioning to Grounded Captioning**: Early works focused on captioning; recent works unify captioning with explicit grounding, moving from image to video, and from bounding boxes to pixel-level masks.
- **From Manual to Automated Annotation**: The field is moving from small, manually annotated datasets to large-scale, automatically annotated datasets using LLMs and pipelines.
- **From Coarse to Fine-grained Grounding**: There is a clear evolution from scene-level or object-level grounding to pixel-level and spatio-temporal grounding, enabled by advances in model architectures and data.
- **From Single-task to Multitask/Conversational Models**: Models are evolving to handle multiple tasks (captioning, grounding, QA, conversation) in a unified framework.
- **From Two-stage to End-to-end Models**: There is a shift from pipeline-based (two-stage) approaches to end-to-end, unified models, often leveraging transformers and LMMs.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Attention Mechanisms**: Used for modeling object interactions, temporal dependencies, and cross-modal alignment.
- **Transformers and LMMs**: Serve as the backbone for most recent models, enabling flexible, scalable, and unified modeling.
- **Adapters and Dual Encoders**: Innovations like spatio-temporal adapters and dual encoders improve alignment and grounding.
- **Automatic Annotation Pipelines**: LLMs and semi-automatic methods are now standard for dataset creation.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing**: Some works compete on the granularity of grounding (bounding box vs. pixel-level), the scale of data, or the integration of modalities (audio, text, video).
- **Complementary**: Data generation works (e.g., Submission, Related 10) complement modeling works by providing the necessary training data. Contrastive learning methods (Related 16) can be integrated into larger grounding or captioning models.
