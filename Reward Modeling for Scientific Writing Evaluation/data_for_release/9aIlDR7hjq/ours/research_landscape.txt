## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the dominant methodological theme is **synthetic data generation for vision tasks**, primarily using **diffusion models** and **GANs**, often in combination with **conditioning mechanisms** (text, images, or both). Several papers also explore **feedback loops**, **prompt engineering**, and **automated augmentation pipelines**.

#### Method Clusters

**Cluster 1: Diffusion Model-Based Synthetic Data Generation**
- Papers: Submission, 2, 5, 6, 10, 11, 12, 14, 18
- Approaches: Use of pretrained or fine-tuned diffusion models (e.g., Stable Diffusion, Imagen) to generate synthetic images for data augmentation, classification, or representation learning. Conditioning is achieved via text prompts, real images, or both. Some methods (e.g., submission, 14) avoid fine-tuning by leveraging augmentations or token insertion.

**Cluster 2: GAN-Based and Hybrid Generative Approaches**
- Papers: 7, 13, 15, 17, 19
- Approaches: Use of GANs (conditional or unconditional) for synthetic data generation, sometimes combined with diffusion models (e.g., GANDiffFace). Methods include explicit diversity regularization, latent code optimization, and multi-conditional GANs for medical imaging.

**Cluster 3: Controllable and Automated Augmentation Pipelines**
- Papers: 3, 4, 18
- Approaches: Pipelines that combine text-to-text and text-to-image models (TTIDA), interventional prompting (IDA), or automated language-guided augmentation (ALIA) to generate diverse, controllable, and domain-adaptive synthetic data.

**Cluster 4: Fairness, Bias, and Diversity in Generation**
- Papers: 8, 16
- Approaches: Conditioning on external reference images for fairness (FairRAG), post-hoc debiasing, and comprehensive surveys of bias in T2I models.

#### Methodological Trends and Patterns

- **Shift from GANs to Diffusion Models:** Early works (e.g., 13, 15, 17, 19) focus on GANs, but recent papers overwhelmingly favor diffusion models for their superior fidelity and diversity.
- **Conditioning Mechanisms:** There is a trend toward richer conditioning—using text, real images, or both (submission, 5, 8, 14)—to control generation and improve domain alignment.
- **Feedback and Guidance:** Several methods (5, 2, 10) incorporate feedback from classifiers or use guidance scales to improve the utility of synthetic data.
- **Automated and Controllable Pipelines:** Newer works (3, 18) automate the augmentation process, reducing reliance on manual prompt engineering or finetuning.
- **Fairness and Bias Mitigation:** Recent attention (8, 16) is given to demographic fairness and bias, with methods to ensure diversity and representation.

#### Relationships Between Approaches

- **Complementary Approaches:** Methods that combine text and image conditioning (submission, 5, 8) or integrate GANs and diffusion models (7) leverage the strengths of both paradigms.
- **Competing Approaches:** Some methods (e.g., 2 vs. submission) differ in whether they require fine-tuning of generative models or operate in a zero-shot/frozen setting.
- **Building Blocks:** Many methods build on advances in diffusion models (12), classifier-free guidance, and prompt engineering, extending them for specific tasks (classification, fairness, robustness).

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From GANs to Diffusion Models:** Early reliance on GANs (13, 15, 17, 19) gave way to diffusion models (2, 6, 10, 11, 12, 14) as they demonstrated superior fidelity and diversity.
- **From Unconditional to Rich Conditioning:** Conditioning evolved from simple class labels or text prompts to include real images, augmentations, and demographic attributes (submission, 5, 8).
- **From Manual to Automated Pipelines:** Automation of augmentation (18) and controllable pipelines (3, 4) reduce human effort and increase scalability.
- **From Data Generation to Downstream Impact:** Increasing focus on how synthetic data affects downstream tasks, including fairness, robustness, and transferability.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Diffusion Model Advances:** Techniques like classifier-free guidance, dynamic thresholding, and efficient U-Nets (12) are widely adopted and extended.
- **Feedback Mechanisms:** Feedback-guided synthesis (5) and multi-positive contrastive learning (10) represent new ways to align synthetic data with downstream needs.
- **Fairness and Bias Mitigation:** Conditioning on reference images (8) and systematic bias surveys (16) inform new fairness-aware generation strategies.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Fine-Tuning vs. Frozen Models:** Some methods require fine-tuning generative models (2, 5, 6), while others (submission, 14, 18) achieve strong results with frozen models and clever conditioning.
- **Text vs. Image Conditioning:** Approaches differ in whether they rely on text, images, or both for conditioning, with hybrid methods (submission, 5, 8) showing strong performance.
- **GANs vs. Diffusion Models:** While GANs remain relevant for certain domains (medical, face), diffusion models are now the dominant paradigm for general-purpose synthetic data generation.
