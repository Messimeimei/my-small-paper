## 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Landscape:**

- **Methodological Cluster:**  
  The submission, MetaTST, is best situated at the intersection of two active clusters: (a) LLM-based/reprogrammed time series models (e.g., Time-LLM, S2IP-LLM, LLM4TS, AutoTimes), and (b) Transformer-based time series models that incorporate exogenous/contextual information (e.g., TimeXer, Tsformer).
- **Closest Prior Work:**  
  - **Time-LLM** and **LLM4TS**: Both adapt LLMs for time series forecasting, but focus on reprogramming or fine-tuning LLMs for direct sequence modeling.
  - **S2IP-LLM**: Uses LLMs to align time series with semantic anchors, integrating metadata/semantic information.
  - **TimeXer**: Integrates exogenous variables (calendar, domain features) into Transformer-based forecasting.
- **Distinctive Aspects:**  
  MetaTST’s main distinction is the systematic, multi-level (dataset, task, sample) metadata integration using LLMs as *fixed* encoders, with metadata encoded as natural language and injected as tokens into the Transformer. This is a nuanced extension of both LLM-based and metadata/context integration approaches.
- **Problem Space:**  
  The submission addresses the challenge of leveraging unstructured, multi-level metadata for improved forecasting, especially in large-scale, heterogeneous settings—a problem recognized but not fully solved in prior work.
- **Evaluation Approach:**  
  The use of standard forecasting benchmarks and SOTA comparisons is typical for the field; the multi-dataset and multi-scenario evaluation aligns with recent trends (e.g., Moirai, UniTS).

**Verification of Author Positioning:**  
While the authors claim to be the first to systematically incorporate multi-level metadata via LLMs in time series forecasting, prior work (notably S2IP-LLM and TimeXer) has addressed related aspects (semantic anchors, exogenous/contextual features). The submission’s novelty is in the *combination* of multi-level metadata, natural language encoding, and fixed LLM encoders, rather than the individual components.

## 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Citation Patterns and Characterizations:**

- **Time-LLM (Zhou et al., 2023; Jin et al., 2024):**  
  - *Author claim:* Prior LLM4TS works use fine-tuning and representation alignment; MetaTST uses LLMs as fixed encoders to preserve their “original understanding capability.”
  - *Independent assessment:* Time-LLM indeed focuses on reprogramming LLMs for time series, often involving fine-tuning. However, the distinction between “fixed” and “fine-tuned” LLMs is somewhat overstated, as some prior works (e.g., S2IP-LLM, AutoTimes) also explore frozen LLMs or lightweight adaptation.
- **LLM4TS (Chang et al., 2023):**  
  - *Author claim:* LLM4TS uses a two-stage fine-tuning strategy for time series alignment and forecasting.
  - *Independent assessment:* Accurate; LLM4TS does focus on fine-tuning, but the submission does not acknowledge that LLM4TS and others also experiment with prompt-based or frozen LLMs.
- **Enhancing Locality (Li et al., 2019):**  
  - *Author claim:* Cited as a dataset source (ECL), not for methodology.
  - *Independent assessment:* No methodological comparison; citation is factual.
- **Unified Training of Universal Transformers (Das et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024d; etc.):**  
  - *Author claim:* Cited as evidence of interest in large-scale, universal time series models.
  - *Independent assessment:* Accurate, but the submission does not directly compare its approach to these universal models, which also address heterogeneity and context integration.
- **A Time Series is Worth 64 Words (Nie et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2024b; Dong et al., 2024):**  
  - *Author claim:* Cited for advances in deep time series models.
  - *Independent assessment:* Factual, but no direct methodological comparison.

**Patterns and Discrepancies:**
- The authors consistently position their work as more “systematic” or “general” in metadata integration, but do not fully acknowledge the breadth of prior work on semantic/contextual integration (e.g., S2IP-LLM, TimeXer).
- The distinction between “fixed” and “fine-tuned” LLMs is emphasized, but the field contains a spectrum of adaptation strategies, not a strict dichotomy.
- Some relevant works (S2IP-LLM, Tsformer) are omitted, despite clear methodological overlap.

## 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

### Contribution 1: Systematic Multi-Level Metadata Integration with Transformers

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - **S2IP-LLM:** Aligns time series with semantic anchors using LLMs; incorporates metadata/semantic information.
  - **TimeXer:** Integrates exogenous/contextual variables (calendar, domain) into Transformer forecasting.
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - MetaTST is the first to *systematically* incorporate multi-level (dataset, task, sample) metadata into time series forecasting with Transformers.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - S2IP-LLM and TimeXer both integrate metadata/context, but not in a multi-level, template-driven, or LLM-encoded fashion.
  - The *systematic* aspect (multi-level, natural language encoding, LLM as encoder) is a genuine extension, but the conceptual idea of metadata/context integration is not new.
  - The novelty is in the *combination* and *operationalization* of these elements, not in the individual components.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The multi-level, template-based approach is a substantive extension, but the claim of being “first” is somewhat overstated given prior work on semantic/context integration.

### Contribution 2: Encoding Unstructured Metadata as Natural Language and Using LLMs as Fixed Metadata Encoders

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - **Time-LLM:** Encodes time series as text for LLM input.
  - **S2IP-LLM:** Uses LLMs to align time series with semantic anchors.
  - **AutoTimes:** Uses frozen LLMs for time series adaptation.
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - MetaTST encodes metadata as natural language and uses LLMs as *fixed* encoders, rather than fine-tuning.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The use of natural language templates for metadata is a novel operational detail, but the use of frozen LLMs is not unique (AutoTimes, S2IP-LLM).
  - The distinction from Time-LLM and LLM4TS (which focus on fine-tuning) is valid, but the field contains a range of adaptation strategies.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The template-driven, multi-level metadata encoding is a substantive operational difference; the use of frozen LLMs is less novel.

### Contribution 3: Metadata-Enriched Representations Improve Forecasting in Diverse/Large-Scale Scenarios

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - **Moirai, UniTS:** Universal models for diverse datasets/tasks.
  - **S2IP-LLM, TimeXer:** Metadata/context integration for improved generalization.
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - MetaTST demonstrates improved accuracy and certainty in diverse, large-scale scenarios.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The empirical claim is plausible, but similar improvements have been reported in S2IP-LLM, Moirai, and UniTS for context/metadata integration.
  - The improvement may be due to the richer context, but could also result from implementation details or increased model capacity.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The improvement is likely substantive, but not unique to this approach.

### Contribution 4: Adaptability to Single- and Multi-Dataset Settings (Toward Foundation Models)

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - **Moirai, UniTS:** Explicitly target universal/foundation models for time series.
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - MetaTST is adaptable to both single- and multi-dataset settings, suggesting potential as a foundation model.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Moirai and UniTS already address this goal, with more explicit multi-task and multi-domain evaluation.
  - MetaTST’s approach is compatible with this trend, but not uniquely so.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The adaptability is a necessary property for foundation models, but not a unique contribution.

## 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Field Maturity:**  
  The field is highly active and rapidly evolving, with a surge in LLM-based and universal time series models since 2022–2023.
- **Recent Surveys:**  
  - “A Survey on Transformer Models for Time Series Forecasting” (2023)
  - “Large Language Models for Time Series: A Survey” (2024)

  - Increasing integration of semantic/contextual information (metadata, prompts, anchors).

- **Incremental Advances:**  
  - Many recent papers claim SOTA via architectural tweaks, context integration, or LLM adaptation.
  - The field values both conceptual novelty and operational/empirical improvements.

- **Routine Extensions:**  
  - The use of templates and LLMs as encoders is a logical extension of existing trends, rather than a radical departure.

## 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Differences:**  
  - MetaTST’s systematic, multi-level metadata integration using natural language templates and LLMs as encoders is a substantive operational advance, though conceptually related to prior work.
- **Main Relationships to Existing Research:**  
  - Closely related to S2IP-LLM (semantic anchors), TimeXer (contextual variables), and LLM4TS/Time-LLM (LLM adaptation).
  - Shares the goal of universal/foundation models with Moirai and UniTS.
- **Strongest Differentiation:**  
  - The multi-level, template-driven metadata encoding and integration into the Transformer pipeline.
- **Weakest Differentiation:**  
  - Use of frozen LLMs as encoders (already explored in AutoTimes, S2IP-LLM).
  - Adaptability to multi-dataset settings (already a focus in Moirai, UniTS).
- **Discrepancies Between Author Claims and Independent Assessment:**  
  - Overstatement of being “first” in systematic metadata integration.
  - Understatement of prior work on semantic/context integration and frozen LLMs.
  - Omission of closely related recent work (S2IP-LLM, Tsformer, AutoTimes).

---

**Summary for Reviewers:**  
MetaTST represents a meaningful operational advance in metadata integration for time series forecasting, combining multi-level, template-based natural language encoding with LLMs as metadata encoders. However, the conceptual novelty is less pronounced than claimed, as several recent works address similar goals using related techniques. The main delta lies in the systematic, multi-level approach and the specific operationalization, rather than in the underlying idea of metadata/context integration or LLM adaptation. The field is highly active, and incremental advances are common; reviewers should calibrate expectations accordingly and consider the omitted related work when assessing novelty.
