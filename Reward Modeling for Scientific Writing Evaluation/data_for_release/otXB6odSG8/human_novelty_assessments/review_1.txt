This study uses a broad range of ML models, including CatBoost, Neural ODEs, CNNs, GRUs, and their polynomial activation counterparts, to assess the performance of different architectures in emulating radiation and to plug the best scheme into a regional climate model for speedup assessment. Apart from speedup in radiation emulation, the novelty of the work is not clear to me, since the central idea behind the work has been present by past papers and the paper pretty much uses established neural networks in their out-of-box configuration to emulate radiation. The idea that ML can solve this computational deadlock has been known for a while and some works mentioned in the study have also shown some preliminary progress in this direction. Because I find the ML novelty lacking and because the ideas have already been proposed in past studies, in my opinion, the paper might better fit in a physical science journal which would allow the authors to focus on the scientific merits of their particular analysis.
