# METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

**Main Methodological Approaches:**
Across the collection, the dominant methodological trend is the integration of large language models (LLMs) or vision-language models (VLMs) with reinforcement learning (RL) to improve exploration, goal generation, and sample efficiency, especially in sparse-reward, long-horizon, or open-ended environments. Hierarchical RL, model-based RL, intrinsic reward shaping, and reward relabeling are also prominent.

**Method Clusters:**

- **Cluster 1: LLM/VLM-Guided Goal Generation and Exploration**
  - *Papers:* Submission, Related 1 (DLLM), 3 (DECKARD), 4 (ELLM), 8 (LLM Augmented Hierarchical Agents), 7 (ExploRLLM), 2 (LGE), 6 (IGE-LLMs)
  - *Description:* These methods use LLMs/VLMs to generate goals, subgoals, or action suggestions for RL agents. The guidance can be direct (LLM proposes goals or actions) or indirect (LLM provides priors, hints, or intrinsic rewards). Hierarchical policies are common, with high-level LLM-driven goal selection and low-level RL execution.
  - *Trends:* Increasing sophistication in integrating LLMs, from simple prompting to multi-modal world models and modular architectures. Emphasis on grounding LLM knowledge and correcting for LLM errors (e.g., DECKARD, AdaRefiner).

- **Cluster 2: Language-Guided Reward Shaping and Relabeling**
  - *Papers:* 5 (LGR2), 11 (LLM Background Knowledge), 6 (IGE-LLMs)
  - *Description:* These works use LLMs to generate or relabel reward functions, often in hierarchical RL. LLMs translate instructions or background knowledge into reward shaping signals, which are then used to densify sparse rewards or guide exploration.
  - *Trends:* Focus on data efficiency, stability, and leveraging language for more meaningful reward signals.

- **Cluster 3: Model-Based RL with Language/Visual Guidance**
  - *Papers:* Submission, 1 (DLLM), 3 (DECKARD), 12 (PR2L)
  - *Description:* These methods combine model-based RL (learning a world model) with LLM/VLM guidance, either by integrating language into the model’s state/transition space or using VLMs for semantic representations.
  - *Trends:* Multi-modal world models, promptable representations, and chain-of-thought reasoning are increasingly used to bridge the gap between high-level language and low-level environment dynamics.

- **Cluster 4: Reward from Failure and Hindsight Experience**
  - *Papers:* 10 (Failure Experience), 5 (LGR2), 14 (CoL)
  - *Description:* These works focus on leveraging failed experiences, hindsight relabeling, or demonstration data to improve learning in sparse-reward settings.
  - *Trends:* Combining behavior cloning, RL, and reward relabeling for stable and efficient learning.

- **Cluster 5: Vision-Language and Representation Learning**
  - *Papers:* 12 (PR2L), 9 (Bbox-HGG)
  - *Description:* Use of VLMs or self-supervised visual encoders to provide semantic, promptable, or object-centric representations for RL agents.
  - *Trends:* Emphasis on grounding high-level knowledge into actionable, low-level representations.

**Relationships:**
- Many methods are complementary: e.g., LLM-guided goal generation can be combined with language-guided reward shaping or model-based RL.
- There is a progression from using LLMs for simple action filtering (LGE) to more sophisticated, multi-modal, and hierarchical integrations (DLLM, DECKARD, Submission).
- Some methods (e.g., AdaRefiner) focus on refining LLM outputs using RL feedback, addressing the challenge of LLM errors or lack of grounding.

# PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

**Key Problems Addressed:**
- **Sparse Reward and Inefficient Exploration:** Nearly all papers address the challenge of RL in environments where rewards are rare or delayed, making exploration difficult.
- **Long-Horizon, Open-World, and Complex Tasks:** Many works focus on tasks with long temporal dependencies, large state/action spaces, or open-ended objectives (e.g., Minecraft, Crafter, robotics).
- **Bridging Language and Environment Granularity:** Several papers (Submission, DLLM, DECKARD) highlight the mismatch between high-level language and low-level environment transitions.
- **Sample Efficiency and Generalization:** Improving learning speed and the ability to generalize to new tasks or environments is a recurring theme.
- **Grounding and Correcting LLM Knowledge:** Addressing the unreliability or lack of grounding in LLM outputs (DECKARD, AdaRefiner).

**Approaches to Similar Problems:**
- *Sparse Rewards:* Tackled via intrinsic rewards (IGE-LLMs, DLLM), reward relabeling (LGR2), LLM-guided goal generation (Submission, ELLM), and leveraging failed experiences (Failure Experience).
- *Long-Horizon Tasks:* Hierarchical policies (Submission, LGR2, LLM Augmented Agents) and modular subgoal learning (DECKARD) are common.
- *Language-Environment Mismatch:* Model-based RL with language integration (Submission, DLLM), and verification/correction mechanisms (DECKARD, AdaRefiner).
- *Generalization:* Use of contrastive language models (LGE), promptable VLMs (PR2L), and background knowledge extraction (LLM Background Knowledge).

**Patterns in Problem Formulation:**
- Problems are increasingly formulated in terms of bridging human-like reasoning (via LLMs) with low-level RL, rather than treating RL as a tabula rasa process.
- There is a shift from focusing solely on reward engineering to leveraging external knowledge (language, vision) for both exploration and policy learning.

# TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

**Progression of Ideas:**
- **Early Stage:** Use of LLMs for action filtering or simple goal suggestion (LGE, ELLM).
- **Hierarchical and Model-Based Integration:** Emergence of hierarchical policies and model-based RL with LLM guidance (Submission, DLLM, DECKARD, LLM Augmented Agents).
- **Reward Shaping and Relabeling:** Shift towards using LLMs for reward generation and relabeling, improving data efficiency and stability (LGR2, LLM Background Knowledge, IGE-LLMs).
- **Multi-Modal and Representation Learning:** Incorporation of VLMs and promptable representations for richer semantic grounding (PR2L, Bbox-HGG).
- **Refinement and Adaptation:** Addressing LLM limitations via RL feedback and adapter modules (AdaRefiner).
- **Learning from Failure and Demonstrations:** Combining RL with failed experiences and demonstration data for improved learning in sparse-reward settings (Failure Experience, CoL).

**Building Blocks and Extensions:**
- Hierarchical RL and model-based RL serve as foundational frameworks, extended by integrating LLM/VLM guidance.
- Intrinsic reward methods are extended by using LLMs to generate more meaningful, goal-directed signals.
- Modular and compositional architectures (e.g., DECKARD, AdaRefiner) allow for correction and adaptation of LLM outputs.

**Competing and Complementary Approaches:**
- Some methods compete in how LLMs are used: as direct policy guides (goal/action suggestion) vs. as sources of reward shaping or representation learning.
- Many approaches are complementary: e.g., LLM-guided goal generation can be combined with language-guided reward shaping and model-based RL.
- There is a trend toward modularity and composability, allowing different LLM-guided components to be integrated as needed.
