# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Landscape:**

The submission, "Generate explorative goals with large language model guidance," proposes LanGoal, a model-based RL method with a hierarchical policy that integrates LLM guidance for goal generation and intrinsic exploration rewards. This situates the work squarely within the most active and rapidly evolving cluster of the field: **LLM-guided hierarchical and model-based RL for exploration** (see Cluster A in the landscape).

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **DLLM (Lin et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024):** Model-based RL with LLM hints for goal achievement.
- **ELLM (Du et al., 2023):** LLM-guided exploration in RL.
- **LLM Augmented Hierarchical Agents (Prakash et al., 2023):** Hierarchical RL with LLM-driven skill selection.
- **AdaRefiner (Zhang & Lu, 2024):** RL refinement of LLM outputs.
- **DECKARD:** Modular, LLM-guided subgoal generation and correction.

**Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission is most closely aligned with works that combine **hierarchical RL**, **model-based RL**, and **LLM-guided goal generation** (DLLM, ELLM, LLM Augmented Agents).
- It also shares concerns with works addressing the **granularity mismatch** between language and environment transitions (DECKARD, AdaRefiner).

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The focus on **sparse reward, long-horizon, open-ended environments** (Crafter) and **goal-reaching ability** is standard in this cluster.
- The use of **model-based RL** and **hierarchical policies** is a common approach to address exploration inefficiency and long-horizon credit assignment.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission does not introduce a fundamentally new problem or evaluation paradigm but proposes a new method within a well-established and highly active research area.
- Its closest methodological relatives are DLLM, ELLM, and LLM Augmented Hierarchical Agents, all of which use LLMs for high-level guidance in RL, often with hierarchical or model-based elements.

  - *Citation Context:* Used to describe the world model architecture: "We refer to (Lin et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024) to give a concise expression of the world model, consists several networks that are optimized jointly."

- **Guiding Pretraining in RL with LLMs (ELLM, AdaRefiner, Dynalang):**
  - *Citation Context:* "We consider employing ELLM (Du et al., 2023), Dynalang (Lin et al., 2024), and AdaRefiner (Zhang & Lu, 2024) as baselines that include natural language information in RL methods."
  - *Analysis:* The authors group these as baselines using language in RL, but do not detail the specific mechanisms or how their approach substantively differs. There is no explicit discussion of how LanGoal's hierarchical integration or model-based aspects compare to these works.

- **LLM Augmented Hierarchical Agents (Prakash et al., 2023):**
  - *Citation Context:* "Prakash et al. (2023) train hierarchical policy as skills with LLM decide which skill to use next."
  - *Analysis:* The authors acknowledge the use of LLMs for high-level skill selection but do not clarify whether their own hierarchical structure or integration of model-based RL is novel relative to this work.

- **Vision-Language Models Provide Promptable Representations for RL (Chen et al., 2024; Zhang & Lu, 2024):**
  - *Citation Context:* "LLMs can also provide promptable representation or exploration guidance with semantic meaning to the RL policy..."
  - *Analysis:* The authors mention promptable representations as a general capability of LLMs/VLMs, but do not specify how their approach to integrating LLM guidance for goal generation is distinct from these prior works.

**Patterns:**
- The authors tend to cite related work as background or as baselines, with limited critical comparison.
- There is a tendency to describe prior work in general terms (e.g., "includes natural language information") rather than specifying technical similarities or differences.
- The most direct competitors (DLLM, ELLM, LLM Augmented Agents) are not discussed in detail regarding their hierarchical or model-based integration.

**Discrepancies:**
- The characterization of DLLM as merely a reference for world model structure underplays its role as a direct competitor in LLM-guided, model-based RL.
- The grouping of ELLM, AdaRefiner, and Dynalang as generic "language in RL" baselines may obscure important differences in how language is used (e.g., for goal generation, reward shaping, or policy refinement).

- **Independent Assessment:**

  - **LLM Augmented Hierarchical Agents** use LLMs for high-level skill selection in a hierarchical RL framework, which is conceptually similar.
  - **DECKARD** addresses the language-environment mismatch with modular subgoal generation and correction, which overlaps with the submission's stated motivation.
- **Delta:** The submission's hierarchical policy and model-based integration are not fundamentally new compared to DLLM or LLM Augmented Agents; the novelty may lie in specific architectural or training details, which are not fully elaborated.

- **Delta:** The improvement may be due to implementation details or better integration, rather than a conceptual advance.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** The claim of a "new method" for improved goal-reaching is not strongly substantiated as a conceptual leap over prior work.

**Claimed Contribution 3:** *Demonstrate effectiveness on open-ended environment (Crafter) compared to various RL and LLM baselines.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** DLLM, ELLM, AdaRefiner, LLM Augmented Agents (all use Crafter or similar environments).
