# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission, ProCEED, is positioned within the *exemplar-free class-incremental learning* (EFCIL) literature, specifically targeting catastrophic forgetting and prototype drift without storing previous task samples.
- It draws from and extends two major methodological clusters: (a) *prototype-based/analytic classifier methods* (e.g., FeCAM, PASS, FeTrIL, EFC, ADC), and (b) *ensemble/expert-based approaches* (e.g., EASE, SEED), while also incorporating elements of *knowledge distillation* (e.g., "Adapt Your Teacher", REAL).

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **FeCAM (Goswami et al., 2024):** Prototype-based, analytic classifier, addresses feature drift, and is cited as the main baseline.
- **PASS (Zhu et al., 2021b):** Prototype-based, uses self-supervised learning for representation robustness.
- **EFC, ADC, FeTrIL, FeTrIL++:** All address prototype drift and analytic classification in EFCIL.
- **Ensemble/Expert-based:** EASE, SEED, and to some extent RanPAC, though these are less directly cited.
- **Knowledge Distillation:** "Adapt Your Teacher", "Class-Incremental Learning by Knowledge Distillation", REAL.

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- ProCEED is best viewed as a *hybrid* of prototype-based drift compensation and ensemble-based inference, with a novel knowledge distillation mechanism. Its main technical novelty claims (prototype realignment, ensemble of deep Gaussian classifiers, and MoE-based distillation) are all present in some form in the landscape, but the specific combination and implementation details are the focus.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses the standard EFCIL problem: learning new classes incrementally without exemplars, mitigating catastrophic forgetting, and compensating for prototype drift.
- Evaluation is on standard datasets (CIFAR100/20, TinyImageNet), using accuracy as the main metric, consistent with the field.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is most closely related to FeCAM, PASS, FeTrIL, EFC, and ADC in terms of prototype drift compensation, and to EASE/SEED in terms of ensemble-based approaches.
- The knowledge distillation aspect is related to "Adapt Your Teacher" and REAL, though the specific MoE-based distillation is less common.
- The field is mature, with many recent works addressing similar problems with overlapping techniques.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contribution 1: Prototype Consolidation via Dynamic Realignment**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** FeCAM, ADC, EFC, FeTrIL, FeTrIL++
- **Claimed Difference:** ProCEED realigns old task representations into the latest subspace to prevent feature drift, "without extra optimization overhead."
- **Independent Assessment:** 
  - FeCAM and ADC both address prototype drift, with FeCAM using analytic compensation and ADC using adversarial drift compensation.
  - The specific mechanism of "inter-subspace angular drifts" is not detailed in the excerpt, but similar ideas (e.g., aligning prototypes, compensating for feature space changes) are present in FeCAM, ADC, and EFC.
  - The claim of "no extra optimization overhead" may be a minor implementation detail; FeCAM and EFC also use analytic (non-optimized) adjustments.
  - The delta appears to be in the specific analytic form of the realignment, but the conceptual approach is not fundamentally new.

**Claimed Contribution 2: Knowledge Distillation from a Mixture of Experts (MoE)**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** "Adapt Your Teacher", "Class-Incremental Learning by Knowledge Distillation", REAL
- **Claimed Difference:** A single expert is fine-tuned and inherits knowledge from others without explicit optimization of the whole ensemble.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
  - Prior works use various forms of knowledge distillation, including teacher adaptation and feature map weighting.
  - The use of a MoE for distillation is less common, but the idea of transferring knowledge from multiple sources is present in the literature.
  - The claim of "no explicit optimization" may refer to not jointly optimizing the ensemble, but this is an implementation detail rather than a conceptual advance.
  - The main delta is the specific way the MoE is used for distillation, but the broader concept is not unique.

**Claimed Contribution 3: Prototype-based Ensemble of Deep Gaussian Classifiers**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** FeCAM, PASS, FeTrIL, EFC, ADC, EASE, SEED
- **Claimed Difference:** Use of a prototype-based ensemble of deep Gaussian classifiers for improved plasticity and generalization.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
  - FeCAM, FeTrIL, and EFC use analytic (often Gaussian) classifiers on prototypes.
  - Ensemble-based inference is present in EASE and SEED, though not always with Gaussian classifiers.
  - The combination of prototype-based Gaussian classifiers in an ensemble is a minor extension of existing ideas.
  - The delta is in the specific ensemble structure, but the underlying components are well-established.

**General Observations:**
- Many claimed differences are in implementation details or specific combinations, rather than fundamentally new concepts.
- The authors do not discuss how their approach relates to recent advances in representation expansion, self-supervision, or adapter-based ensembles.
- Improvements in accuracy may be due to careful engineering or hyperparameter tuning, rather than conceptual novelty.
