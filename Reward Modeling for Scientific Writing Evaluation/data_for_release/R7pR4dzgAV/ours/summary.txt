CALF introduces a new, expert-annotated, reference-based benchmark for long-form question answering (LFQA) using Chinese examination questions, addressing the underrepresentation of Chinese and domain-specific LFQA datasets. While the dataset is novel in its specific domain and data source, the overall methodology—including expert annotation, reference-based evaluation, and comprehensive metric assessment—closely parallels recent benchmarks in other domains and languages (e.g., LongBench, FinTextQA, MME). The paper’s structuring of three comparison modes (human vs. human, human vs. model, model vs. model) is a useful but incremental methodological detail, rather than a conceptual innovation. The authors’ claims of being "first" are accurate only for the specific combination of Chinese exam data and LFQA, not for the general approach, and the omission of direct comparisons to similar recent benchmarks somewhat overstates the novelty. Overall, CALF’s main contribution is filling a gap in Chinese LFQA evaluation, but its advances are primarily in dataset scope rather than evaluation methodology, reflecting the field’s current trend of domain- and language-specific benchmark proliferation.
