# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission paper is positioned at the intersection of predictive coding, biologically plausible learning, and the computational neuroscience of spatial representations. It specifically extends predictive coding networks (PCNs)—previously applied mainly to sensory and associative memory domains—into the spatial navigation domain, focusing on the emergence of grid cells in the medial entorhinal cortex (MEC).

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Predictive Coding and Biologically Plausible Learning:**  
  - *Salvatori et al., 2021; Tang et al., 2023, 2024; Barron et al., 2020; Millidge et al., 2024* (PCNs/tPCNs for memory, not spatial navigation)
- **Sparse/Non-Negative Coding for Grid Cells:**  
  - *Dordek et al., 2016; Sorscher et al., 2023; Schaeffer et al., 2022* (non-negative PCA, sparse coding for grid emergence)
- **RNNs/Attractor Models for Path Integration:**  
  - *Cueva & Wei, 2018; Banino et al., 2018; Whittington et al., 2020* (RNNs trained for path integration, grid cell emergence)
- **Biologically Plausible Learning Rules for Grid Cells:**  
  - *Kropff & Treves, 2008; Weber & Sprekeler, 2018; Widloski & Fiete, 2014* (Hebbian, STDP, E/I plasticity)

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission is most closely aligned with **Cluster A (Predictive Coding and Biologically Plausible Learning)** and **Cluster B (Sparse/Non-Negative Coding)**, but also draws on **Cluster C (RNNs for Path Integration)** by extending PCNs to temporal domains (tPCN).
- It is distinct from prior PCN work by focusing on spatial (rather than visual or associative) representations and from prior grid cell models by using predictive coding as the core learning principle.

**Place in Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- Addresses the biological plausibility of grid cell emergence, a central open question.
- Evaluates emergence of hexagonal grid patterns, robustness to architecture/input, and learning without velocity inputs—metrics common in the field.
- Analytical comparison of tPCN Hebbian learning to truncated BPTT is a novel normative link.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is not the first to use non-negative/sparse coding for grid cells, nor the first to use biologically plausible learning for spatial representations. Its main novelty is the *combination* of predictive coding, spatial navigation, and local learning rules, and the analytical link to BPTT.
- The field is mature, with many models for grid cell emergence; the submission’s main delta is the predictive coding framework applied to spatial tasks with local learning.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors consistently position their work as the first to demonstrate grid cell emergence in PCNs with biologically plausible learning, and as extending predictive coding to spatial domains.
- Prior work is often described as either lacking spatial focus (for PCNs) or lacking biological plausibility (for RNN/grid cell models).

**Accuracy and Balance of Characterizations:**
- *Dordek et al., 2016; Sorscher et al., 2023; Schaeffer et al., 2022:*  
  - Accurately cited as showing non-negative PCA/sparse coding yields grid cells.  
  - Authors note that these are feedforward/statistical models, not dynamical or predictive coding.
- *Cueva & Wei, 2018; Banino et al., 2018; Whittington et al., 2020:*  
  - Accurately cited as RNNs trained for path integration yielding grid cells, but authors emphasize these use backpropagation, not local learning.
- *Salvatori et al., 2021; Tang et al., 2023, 2024; Barron et al., 2020:*  
  - Cited as PCNs for memory, not spatial navigation; this is accurate.
- *Sorscher et al., 2023:*  
  - Cited for analytical results on non-negativity and grid emergence; authors use these results as a baseline.
- *Weber & Sprekeler, 2018; Widloski & Fiete, 2014; Kropff & Treves, 2008:*  
  - Cited as biologically plausible learning for grid cells, but authors claim these are “highly specialized” or not generalizable.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The claim that prior PCN work did not address spatial learning is accurate.
- The claim that prior grid cell models lack biological plausibility is somewhat overstated; several prior works (e.g., Weber & Sprekeler, 2018; Widloski & Fiete, 2014) do use local learning, though not in a predictive coding framework.
- The distinction between “predictive coding” and “RNNs” is sometimes blurred, as both can be implemented with similar architectures; the main difference is in the learning rule and objective.

**Substantiation of Claimed Improvements:**
- The analytical link between tPCN Hebbian learning and truncated BPTT is a new result, not present in prior work.
- The demonstration of grid cell emergence in PCNs/tPCNs is new for predictive coding, but similar emergence has been shown in other frameworks.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

## Contribution 1: Grid cells emerge in PCNs/tPCNs with biologically plausible learning

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *Dordek et al., 2016; Sorscher et al., 2023; Schaeffer et al., 2022* (non-negative PCA/sparse coding, grid emergence)
  - *Cueva & Wei, 2018; Banino et al., 2018* (RNNs, grid emergence via backprop)
  - *Weber & Sprekeler, 2018; Widloski & Fiete, 2014* (biologically plausible learning for grid cells)
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - First to show grid cells emerge in *predictive coding* networks with local, Hebbian learning.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Prior work has shown grid emergence with non-negative/sparse coding (feedforward), and with RNNs (backprop), and with some local learning rules (but not in predictive coding).
  - The main delta is the *predictive coding* framework and the specific local learning rule.
  - The emergence of grid cells from place cell inputs with non-negativity is not new; the novelty is in the predictive coding implementation.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The difference is substantive in terms of the learning framework (predictive coding vs. others), but the *phenomenon* (grid emergence from place cells with non-negativity) is well established.

## Contribution 2: Extension of predictive coding theory to spatial (MEC) representations

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *Salvatori et al., 2021; Tang et al., 2023, 2024; Barron et al., 2020* (PCNs for memory, not spatial)
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - First to apply predictive coding to grid cell emergence in spatial navigation.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Prior PCN work did not address spatial navigation or grid cells; this is a genuine extension.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - Substantive extension of predictive coding to a new domain.

## Contribution 3: Analytical demonstration that tPCN Hebbian learning approximates truncated BPTT

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *Whittington & Bogacz, 2017; Song et al., 2024; Pinchetti et al., 2024* (predictive coding approximates backprop in static/visual domains)
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Analytical link for *temporal* predictive coding (tPCN) in path integration tasks.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Prior work has shown PCN ≈ backprop for static tasks; this is the first for tPCN in spatial navigation.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - Substantive, as it extends the equivalence to a new domain and learning rule.

## Contribution 4: Robust grid cell emergence in tPCN without velocity inputs and under various architectures

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *Sorscher et al., 2023; Dordek et al., 2016* (grid emergence with non-negativity, but typically with velocity inputs)
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Grid cells emerge even without velocity inputs, and under various architectures.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Prior work often used velocity inputs; showing emergence without them is a new robustness result, though the underlying mechanism (non-negative coding of place cells) is not new.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The robustness claim is incremental but useful; the core mechanism is not new.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Potential Overstatement of Novelty:**  
  - The claim of “first demonstration” of grid cell emergence with local learning is only accurate for *predictive coding* networks; similar results exist in other frameworks.
  - The analytical link to BPTT is new for tPCN, but similar equivalences have been shown for static PCNs.
- **Empirical Improvements:**  
  - Improvements in robustness or emergence may be due to architectural or input choices (e.g., non-negativity, DoS encoding) rather than the predictive coding framework per se.
- **Terminology Differences:**  
  - “Predictive coding” vs. “RNN” may mask architectural similarities; the main difference is in the learning rule/objective.
- **Routine Extensions:**  
  - Extending predictive coding to spatial domains is a natural progression, given prior work in memory and sequence learning.
