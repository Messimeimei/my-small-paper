# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Research Landscape:**

- **Problem Focus:** The submission addresses *image-level memorization detection* in text-to-image diffusion models, specifically *without access to the original prompts or metadata*. This is a more challenging and realistic scenario than prompt-level detection, as it reflects real-world cases where only the image is available.
- **Methodological Cluster:** The method falls within the *inversion-based detection* cluster, leveraging unconditional DDIM inversion and inference perturbation. This is distinct from prior work that uses inversion primarily for editing (e.g., EDICT, AIDI, Eta Inversion), but conceptually related in its use of inversion to analyze model behavior.
- **Closest Prior Works:**
  - **Prompt-level memorization detection:** Wen et al. (2024), Ren et al. (2024) – both focus on detecting memorization at the prompt level, using cross-attention or text-conditional noise prediction (TCNP/MTCNP).
  - **Membership inference and extraction:** Carlini et al. (2023), Kong et al. (2023), Dubiński et al. (2024) – focus on membership inference attacks, sometimes using inversion (e.g., DDIM inversion in Kong et al.).
  - **Inversion/editing methods:** EDICT, IterInv, Eta Inversion – focus on high-fidelity inversion for editing, not detection.
- **Problem Space:** The submission is situated at the intersection of *privacy/memorization detection* and *inversion-based analysis*, extending detection from prompt-level to image-level, and from conditional to unconditional settings.
- **Evaluation Approach:** Uses standard detection metrics (AUC, TPR, etc.) and tests robustness to data augmentation, which is a recent trend in the field.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is most closely related to Wen et al. (2024) and Ren et al. (2024) in terms of detection, but differs in its focus on image-level detection without prompts.
- It is methodologically related to inversion/editing works, but repurposes inversion for detection rather than editing.
- The approach is distinct from membership inference attacks (Carlini et al., Kong et al.), which often require more information or different threat models.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**

- **Wen et al. (2024):** Authors repeatedly characterize this work as *prompt-level* detection, requiring access to the original prompt, and as using MTCNP as a detection metric. They contrast their own method as *image-level* and *prompt-free*.
  - *Example:* "Existing memorization detecting methods primarily focus on identifying whether specific prompts lead to memorized outputs (Wen et al., 2024; Ren et al., 2024)... making existing detection methods for memorized prompts... struggle to address this challenge."
  - *Example:* "Wen et al. (2024) minimizes the magnitude of text-conditional predictions to perturb the original prompt embedding at the initial time step during generation. We perturb the text embedding in the latter stage of inference..."
- **Ren et al. (2024):** Similarly characterized as prompt-level, using cross-attention analysis.
- **Carlini et al. (2023), Kong et al. (2023), Dubiński et al. (2024):** Cited as membership inference or extraction attacks, sometimes using inversion, but not directly compared in terms of detection methodology.
- **Webster (2023):** Used as a source for memorized image sets, not for detection methodology.

**Assessment of Accuracy and Balance:**
- The authors accurately describe Wen et al. (2024) and Ren et al. (2024) as prompt-level detection methods, but may understate the flexibility of these methods (e.g., whether they could be adapted to image-level detection with additional engineering).
- The distinction between perturbing at the initial vs. later inference steps is technically accurate, but the practical impact of this difference is not fully explored.
- The authors do not discuss inversion-based membership inference works (e.g., Kong et al., 2023) in detail, missing an opportunity to clarify differences.
- There is a tendency to frame prior work as less robust or less general, which may overstate the novelty of the submission.

**Discrepancies:**
- Wen et al. (2024) is described as only applicable to prompt-level detection, but their metric (MTCNP) is also used in the submission for image-level detection, suggesting more conceptual overlap than acknowledged.
- The authors do not address whether prior inversion-based membership inference methods could be adapted for image-level detection.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contributions and Independent Assessment:**

### (a) *First to formalize and address image-level memorization detection for text-to-image diffusion models without requiring original prompts or metadata.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Wen et al. (2024), Ren et al. (2024), Kong et al. (2023)
- **Claimed Difference:** Prior work requires prompts; submission does not.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
  - Wen et al. (2024) and Ren et al. (2024) are indeed prompt-level, but the underlying metrics (e.g., MTCNP) are also used in the submission for image-level detection, indicating conceptual continuity.
  - Kong et al. (2023) uses DDIM inversion for membership inference, which is related but not directly compared.
  - The submission's main novelty is the *systematic use of inversion and inference perturbation* for image-level detection, not the use of MTCNP per se.
  - The "first" claim is mostly accurate in terms of *published, systematic* image-level detection, but the conceptual gap is smaller than implied.

### (b) *Proposes the IIP framework leveraging unconditional DDIM inversion and inference perturbation with optimized random prompts for effective detection.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Kong et al. (2023) (DDIM inversion for membership inference), Wen et al. (2024) (perturbation for detection)
- **Claimed Difference:** Use of unconditional inversion and perturbation for detection, not editing or membership inference.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
  - The use of unconditional DDIM inversion is a technical adaptation, but inversion itself is well-established.
  - Perturbation with random prompts is a novel twist, but the core idea of using perturbation for detection is present in Wen et al. (2024).
  - The combination is new, but each component is incremental.

### (c) *Uncovers two new characteristics of memorized images under inference perturbation: lower similarity and higher TCNP magnitude.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Wen et al. (2024) (MTCNP for memorized prompts)
- **Claimed Difference:** Applies these metrics to images, not prompts.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
  - The metrics themselves are not new; their application to image-level detection is the main extension.
  - The finding that these metrics are robust to data augmentation is a useful empirical contribution.

### (d) *Demonstrates strong robustness of the method against data augmentation attacks.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Not directly addressed in Wen et al. (2024), but robustness is a common evaluation axis.
- **Claimed Difference:** Explicit robustness to augmentation.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
  - This is a valuable empirical result, but not a conceptual advance.

**Summary:** The main delta is the *systematic, prompt-free, image-level application* of inversion-based perturbation and detection metrics. The technical components are mostly adaptations of existing ideas, but the combination and empirical validation are new.
