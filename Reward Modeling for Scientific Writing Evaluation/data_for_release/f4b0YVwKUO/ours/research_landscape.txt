## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the dominant methodological focus is on **pruning and compression of large language models (LLMs)**, with a strong emphasis on structured pruning, efficient importance metrics, and post-training methods. Several papers also explore **layer-wise, block-wise, and semi-structured sparsity**, as well as **architecture search** and **integration with parameter-efficient fine-tuning**.

#### Method Clusters

**Cluster 1: Structured Pruning Frameworks for LLMs**
- **FASP (Submission), BESA, FLAP, LLM-Pruner, SliceGPT, Bonsai, FISTAPruner, Pruner-Zero, LoRAPrune, FinerCut**
  - All propose structured pruning methods, differing in their granularity (layer, block, row/column, attention/FFN), importance metrics, and whether they require retraining.
  - **FASP** introduces interlinked sequential layer pruning with a Wanda-inspired metric and restoration.
  - **BESA** uses differentiable blockwise masks and joint quantization.
  - **FLAP** and **LLM-Pruner** focus on retraining-free, hardware-friendly, or task-agnostic structured pruning.
  - **SliceGPT** and **FISTAPruner** emphasize post-training, efficient, and scalable pruning.
  - **Bonsai** is unique in being gradient-free, using only forward passes.
  - **Pruner-Zero** automates metric discovery via genetic programming.
  - **LoRAPrune** unifies LoRA-based fine-tuning with structured pruning.
  - **FinerCut** offers fine-grained, interpretable, and global output-based pruning.

**Cluster 2: Layer/Depth Pruning and Layer Collapse**
- **LaCo, Shortened LLaMA, ShortGPT, FinerCut**
  - Focus on removing entire layers or blocks, sometimes with merging (LaCo) or fine-grained selection (FinerCut).
  - **Shortened LLaMA** and **ShortGPT** analyze depth vs. width pruning and layer redundancy.
  - **LaCo** collapses layers to preserve structure.

**Cluster 3: Importance Metric Innovations**
- **Wanda, Pruner-Zero, FLAP, BESA, FASP**
  - Propose new or automated importance metrics for pruning (e.g., Wanda’s weight-activation product, Pruner-Zero’s symbolic search, FLAP’s fluctuation metric, BESA’s differentiable masks, FASP’s Wanda-inspired metric).

**Cluster 4: Semi-Structured and Learnable Sparsity**
- **MaskLLM, SparseGPT, BESA**
  - Focus on N:M or semi-structured sparsity, with MaskLLM introducing learnable mask distributions and transferability.

**Cluster 5: Architecture Search and Reformation**
- **Search for Efficient LLMs, Sheared LLaMA**
  - Go beyond pruning to search for efficient sub-architectures or target architectures, often training-free.

**Cluster 6: Pruning in Other Domains**
- **Diff-Pruning, IRPruneDet, DiffBIR, Diffuse and Restore**
  - Apply structured pruning or related compression to diffusion models or vision tasks, showing cross-domain methodological transfer.

#### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Retraining-Free and Post-Training Pruning:** A strong trend is toward methods that avoid retraining or require minimal post-pruning recovery (FASP, Wanda, FLAP, SliceGPT, FISTAPruner, Pruner-Zero, Bonsai, FinerCut).
- **Global vs. Local Pruning:** Newer methods (FinerCut, Bonsai) move from local (layer/block) to global, holistic pruning strategies.
- **Automated and Learnable Metrics:** There is a shift from hand-crafted to learnable or automatically discovered importance metrics (MaskLLM, Pruner-Zero).
- **Integration with PEFT:** LoRAPrune and related works combine pruning with parameter-efficient fine-tuning, reflecting practical deployment needs.
- **Hardware-Aware and Efficient Pruning:** Many methods (FLAP, SliceGPT, Bonsai) are designed for practical speedups on real hardware, not just theoretical sparsity.
- **Cross-Layer and Interlinked Pruning:** FASP and similar methods interlink pruning decisions across layers for better performance and efficiency.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

1. **Efficient Compression of LLMs for Deployment**
   - Reducing computational and memory demands to enable LLMs on resource-constrained devices (FASP, BESA, FLAP, LLM-Pruner, SliceGPT, MaskLLM, Bonsai, FISTAPruner, Pruner-Zero, LoRAPrune, Search for Efficient LLMs).

2. **Maintaining Model Performance after Pruning**
   - Avoiding significant accuracy/perplexity loss, especially at high sparsity or aggressive pruning ratios (FASP, BESA, Wanda, FLAP, LaCo, Shortened LLaMA, FinerCut, MaskLLM, SparseGPT).

3. **Speed and Practicality of Pruning**
   - Reducing pruning time, memory, and hardware requirements; enabling pruning on single GPUs (FASP, SliceGPT, Bonsai, FISTAPruner, LoRAPrune).

4. **Automating and Improving Importance Metrics**
   - Overcoming reliance on hand-crafted or heuristic metrics for pruning (Wanda, Pruner-Zero, FLAP, BESA, MaskLLM).

5. **Layer/Block Redundancy and Structure Preservation**
   - Exploiting redundancy in LLM layers/blocks and preserving model structure for compatibility (LaCo, ShortGPT, FinerCut).

6. **Task-Agnostic and General-Purpose Pruning**
   - Ensuring pruned models retain general capabilities, not just task-specific performance (LLM-Pruner, FinerCut).

7. **Integration with Fine-Tuning and Adaptation**
   - Combining pruning with LoRA or other PEFT methods for efficient adaptation (LoRAPrune).

8. **Hardware-Aware and Real-World Speedup**
   - Ensuring that pruning leads to actual inference speedup on real hardware, not just theoretical FLOPs reduction (FLAP, SliceGPT, Shortened LLaMA, Bonsai).

9. **Pruning in Other Domains**
   - Extending structured pruning to diffusion models and vision tasks (Diff-Pruning, IRPruneDet, DiffBIR, Diffuse and Restore).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Compression without Retraining:** Wanda, FASP, FLAP, SliceGPT, FISTAPruner, Pruner-Zero, Bonsai, FinerCut all propose methods that avoid or minimize retraining, but differ in their importance metrics, granularity, and restoration mechanisms.
- **Layer/Block Redundancy:** LaCo, ShortGPT, Shortened LLaMA, and FinerCut all address layer redundancy, but with different strategies (collapse, removal, fine-grained selection).
- **Automated Metric Discovery:** Pruner-Zero and MaskLLM automate metric or mask discovery, while others (Wanda, FLAP, BESA) propose new hand-crafted metrics.
- **Hardware-Aware Pruning:** FLAP, SliceGPT, Bonsai, and Shortened LLaMA explicitly target hardware efficiency, with Bonsai and SliceGPT focusing on forward-only or post-training approaches.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Local to Global:** Early works focus on local (layer/block) pruning; newer works (FinerCut, Bonsai) emphasize global, holistic pruning.
- **From Heuristic to Learnable/Automated:** There is a clear evolution from heuristic metrics to learnable or automatically discovered metrics.
- **From Task-Specific to Task-Agnostic:** Increasing emphasis on task-agnostic pruning to preserve general LLM capabilities.
- **From Theoretical to Practical Efficiency:** Shift from theoretical sparsity to real-world speedup and hardware compatibility.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **LLM Families:** LLaMA, LLaMA-2, LLaMA-3, OPT, Vicuna, ChatGLM, Phi-2, Tiny-LLaMA, BLOOM, GPT-3, Nemotron-4.
- **Benchmarks:** WikiText2, PTB (Penn Treebank), C4, MMLU, VTAB-1k, various downstream tasks (commonsense, reading comprehension, world knowledge), zero-shot classification/generation datasets.
- **Other Domains:** LSUN Church (diffusion), IRSTD benchmarks (vision), synthetic/real face datasets (restoration).

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Language Model Metrics:** Perplexity, accuracy, zero-shot performance, task-specific accuracy (e.g., MMLU), mean Top-1 accuracy.
- **Compression Metrics:** Pruning rate, sparsity level, percentage of parameters/layers removed, model size reduction.
- **Efficiency Metrics:** Pruning time, inference speed (latency, tokens/sec, throughput), memory usage, compute cost.
- **Quality Metrics (Other Domains):** IoU, nIoU (vision), PSNR/SSIM (restoration), FLOPs, training cost, perceptual quality, face-recognition scores.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Perplexity and Accuracy Dominate:** Most LLM pruning papers use perplexity and/or accuracy on standard benchmarks as primary metrics.
- **Compression and Speedup:** Pruning rate, model size reduction, and actual inference speedup are increasingly reported, reflecting practical deployment concerns.
- **Hardware-Specific Results:** Several works (FLAP, SliceGPT, Bonsai, Shortened LLaMA) report speedup on specific GPUs or hardware simulators.
- **No/Minimal Retraining:** Many methods emphasize performance retention without retraining, reporting results immediately post-pruning.
- **Comparisons to SOTA:** Most papers benchmark against prior SOTA methods (e.g., SparseGPT, Wanda, LLM-Pruner), often on the same datasets.

### Evaluation Approach Comparison

- **Retraining-Free vs. Retraining-Required:** Some methods (Wanda, FASP, FLAP, SliceGPT, FISTAPruner, Pruner-Zero, Bonsai, FinerCut) are evaluated without retraining, while others (Shortened LLaMA, LaCo, LoRAPrune) include post-pruning fine-tuning or adaptation.
- **Layer/Block vs. Weight-Level Metrics:** Layer/block pruning methods often report percentage of layers removed and performance retention, while weight-level methods focus on sparsity and perplexity.
- **Task-Agnostic vs. Task-Specific:** Some frameworks (LLM-Pruner, FinerCut) emphasize task-agnostic evaluation, while others focus on specific downstream tasks.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Unstructured to Structured Pruning:** Early works (e.g., magnitude pruning) gave way to structured approaches (rows, columns, blocks, layers) for better hardware compatibility and speedup.
- **From Heuristic to Learnable/Automated Metrics:** Wanda introduced a simple, effective metric; BESA and FLAP proposed differentiable or fluctuation-based metrics; Pruner-Zero and MaskLLM automate metric/mask discovery.
- **From Local to Global and Fine-Grained Pruning:** Methods evolved from layer/block-level pruning to global, holistic (Bonsai) and fine-grained (FinerCut) approaches.
- **From Retraining-Heavy to Retraining-Free:** There is a clear trend toward methods that avoid retraining or require minimal post-pruning recovery, making them practical for billion-scale LLMs.
- **Integration with PEFT and Adaptation:** LoRAPrune and related works combine pruning with parameter-efficient fine-tuning, reflecting real-world deployment needs.
- **Hardware-Aware and Real-World Speedup:** Recent methods (FLAP, SliceGPT, Bonsai, Shortened LLaMA) focus on actual speedup on real hardware, not just theoretical metrics.
- **Automated Architecture Search:** Search for Efficient LLMs and Sheared LLaMA move beyond pruning to automated architecture search and reformation.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Wanda’s Metric:** Inspired FASP and influenced other importance metric designs.
- **SparseGPT:** Set a baseline for one-shot, retraining-free pruning, influencing subsequent methods.
- **Block/Layer Pruning:** Evolved from simple removal (ShortGPT, Shortened LLaMA) to merging (LaCo) and fine-grained selection (FinerCut).
- **Differentiable and Learnable Approaches:** BESA, MaskLLM, and Pruner-Zero extend pruning to differentiable or learnable frameworks.
- **Restoration and Compensation:** FASP, FLAP, and others introduce restoration/compensation mechanisms to recover performance post-pruning.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing:** Different importance metrics (Wanda, BESA, FLAP, Pruner-Zero) and pruning granularity (weight, block, layer, global) compete for best trade-off between efficiency and performance.
- **Complementary:** Methods like FinerCut (fine-grained pruning) and LoRAPrune (PEFT integration) can be combined with other pruning strategies; MaskLLM’s learnable masks can complement structured pruning.

# **Conclusion**

The research landscape is rapidly evolving from simple, heuristic, and retraining-heavy pruning methods to **efficient, structured, retraining-free, and hardware-aware approaches**. There is a clear trend toward **automated and learnable importance metrics**, **fine-grained and global pruning strategies**, and **integration with parameter-efficient fine-tuning**. The field is also expanding into **architecture search** and **cross-domain applications** (e.g., diffusion models, vision). The submission paper, **FASP**, is well-positioned within the core structured pruning cluster, advancing the state-of-the-art with its interlinked layer pruning, efficient metric, and restoration mechanism, and is both influenced by and complementary to several recent innovations in the field.
