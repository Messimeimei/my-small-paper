# Novelty Delta Analysis for Reviewer Support

## 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Submission Positioning (Independent Assessment):**
- The submission conducts a systematic empirical study of LLM deployment on resource-constrained edge devices, focusing on the interplay between model size, fine-tuning method (PEFT vs. RAG), data amount, compression (distillation, quantization, pruning), and fine-tuning time.
- It aims to provide practical guidelines for practitioners, emphasizing trade-offs between learning efficiency, accuracy, and stability under edge constraints.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Empirical Benchmarking & Guidelines:** Pu et al. (2023), Lin et al. (2024a), Liu et al. (2024), and SLoRA (Xu et al., 2024) all provide systematic empirical studies or frameworks for method selection and benchmarking, often in edge or resource-constrained settings.
- **PEFT & Compression Integration:** QA-LoRA (Sun et al., 2024), LLM-Adapters (Hu et al., 2023), and SLoRA (Xu et al., 2024) explore parameter-efficient fine-tuning, sometimes in combination with quantization or federated learning.
- **Edge/On-Device Customization:** Crayon, EdgeMoE, and Bevilacqua et al. (2023) address on-device LLM adaptation, hybrid inference, and personalization.
- **Model Compression:** Sheared LLaMA (Xia et al., 2023), LLM-Pruner (Ma et al., 2023), and MobileLLM (Lin et al., 2024a) focus on pruning, quantization, and efficient architectures for edge deployment.

**Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission sits at the intersection of **Cluster A (PEFT/Adapter-based methods)**, **Cluster B (Model Compression)**, and **Cluster D (On-Device Customization)**, with a strong empirical benchmarking component (Cluster F).
- Unlike some prior works that focus on a single axis (e.g., only PEFT, only quantization), the submission claims to systematically explore the *joint* effects and trade-offs across multiple axes.

**Problem Space & Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses the practical problem of *how to select and combine methods* for LLM deployment on edge devices, given real-world constraints.
- Its evaluation approach (systematic variation of multiple factors, empirical benchmarking) is similar to recent works (e.g., Pu et al., SLoRA), but claims broader scope.

**Verification of Positioning:**
- While the submission claims to be the "first" to systematically address the *full range* of constraints, several prior works (e.g., Pu et al., SLoRA, MobileLLM) have conducted systematic empirical studies, though perhaps not with the exact same breadth or combination of axes.
- The field is characterized by rapid, incremental advances, with many works providing empirical guidelines or frameworks for method selection.

## 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**
- Authors generally cite prior work as addressing *individual* aspects (e.g., PEFT, quantization, pruning, on-device adaptation), positioning their own work as the first to *systematically* benchmark and synthesize these for edge deployment.
- Citations to PEFT (SLoRA, LLM-Adapters, QA-LoRA) are described as "dominant approaches" for parameter-efficient adaptation, with technical details (e.g., LoRA, quantization-aware variants) accurately summarized.
- Model compression works (Sheared LLaMA, LLM-Pruner) are cited as examples of pruning/quantization, with Sheared LLaMA noted for "decent performance."
- On-device personalization (Bevilacqua et al., Luo et al.) is cited in the context of privacy and real-world deployment needs.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The technical descriptions of PEFT, LoRA, and quantization-aware methods are generally accurate.
- However, the submission tends to understate the *scope* of prior empirical studies (e.g., Pu et al., SLoRA, MobileLLM), which also conduct systematic benchmarking and provide deployment guidelines, albeit sometimes with a narrower or different focus.
- The claim that prior works do not address the "full range" of constraints is not always substantiated; some prior works do consider multiple axes, though perhaps not in the exact same combinations.

**Discrepancies:**
- For example, the citation of Pu et al. (2023) as only providing a "framework for choosing optimal fine-tuning techniques" omits that Pu et al. also conduct extensive empirical benchmarking across tasks, data regimes, and methods.
- Similarly, SLoRA and QA-LoRA are described as focusing on federated or quantization-aware PEFT, but both also include systematic empirical studies relevant to edge deployment.

**Rhetorical Patterns:**
- The submission uses language such as "first exploratory study to systematically address the full range of constraints," which may overstate the novelty relative to prior systematic studies.
- Differences are sometimes framed as categorical (e.g., "prior work only addresses X, we address X+Y+Z"), when in fact the distinction may be one of *degree* or *scope* rather than kind.

## 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

### Claimed Contribution 1: "First exploratory study to systematically address the full range of constraints for deploying LLMs on edge devices"

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Pu et al. (2023), SLoRA (Xu et al., 2024), MobileLLM (Lin et al., 2024a), QA-LoRA (Sun et al., 2024)
- **Claimed Difference:** Prior works address only individual axes (e.g., PEFT, quantization, pruning), while the submission jointly benchmarks all key factors (model size, learning method, data amount, compression, fine-tuning time).
- **Independent Assessment:** 
    - Pu et al. (2023) and SLoRA both conduct systematic empirical studies across multiple axes (task, data, method, device), and provide guidelines for method selection under resource constraints.
    - MobileLLM benchmarks sub-billion parameter models for on-device use, considering architecture, quantization, and fine-tuning.
    - The submission's main delta is the *joint* empirical exploration of all these axes in a single study, but the difference is one of *breadth and synthesis* rather than a fundamentally new methodology.
    - The "first" claim is somewhat overstated; the novelty is in the *comprehensiveness* and *integration* of prior axes, not in the individual methods or empirical approach.

### Claimed Contribution 2: "Provides empirical guidelines for balancing key design factors under resource constraints"

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Pu et al. (2023), SLoRA, MobileLLM, QA-LoRA
- **Claimed Difference:** Prior works do not provide actionable guidelines that synthesize all key factors for edge deployment.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
    - Prior works do provide empirical guidelines, but often focused on a subset of factors (e.g., only PEFT vs. full fine-tuning, or only quantization).
    - The submission's guidelines are broader in scope, but the approach (empirical benchmarking leading to guidelines) is not new.
    - The main delta is the *integration* of multiple axes into a single set of guidelines, which may be practically useful but is an incremental extension.

### Claimed Contribution 3: "Offers new insights into the trade-offs between learning efficiency and accuracy for edge LLM deployment"

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** QA-LoRA, SLoRA, Sheared LLaMA, LLM-Pruner, MobileLLM
- **Claimed Difference:** Prior works do not systematically analyze the trade-offs between learning efficiency and accuracy across all methods and constraints.
- **Independent Assessment:** 
    - Prior works (e.g., QA-LoRA, SLoRA) do analyze trade-offs between efficiency and accuracy, often in the context of specific methods (e.g., quantization-aware PEFT).
    - The submission's contribution is to *synthesize* these trade-offs across a broader set of methods and constraints.
    - The difference is again one of *scope and integration* rather than a fundamentally new insight.

**General Observation:** In all cases, the submission's main delta is the *comprehensive, integrated empirical benchmarking* and synthesis of guidelines, rather than the introduction of new methods or fundamentally new empirical approaches. The novelty is incremental and integrative.
