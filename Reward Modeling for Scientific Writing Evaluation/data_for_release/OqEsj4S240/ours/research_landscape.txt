# Research Landscape Analysis

## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, several methodological themes emerge, notably:
- **Topological Data Analysis (TDA) of Neural Representations**
- **Layer and Block Pruning in LLMs**
- **Similarity and Importance Metrics for Layers**
- **Visualization and Interpretability Tools**
- **Resource-Efficiency Surveys and Taxonomies**

#### Cluster 1: Topological Analysis of Neural Representations
- **Papers:** Submission, Related 1, 5, 8, 13, 14, 15
- **Description:** These works leverage tools from algebraic topology (e.g., persistent homology, Betti numbers, zigzag persistence) to analyze the geometry and topology of neural network representations across layers or over time. The submission paper introduces a zigzag persistence framework and persistence similarity for LLMs, extending prior work (e.g., Related 1, 8, 15) that used TDA for DNNs and dynamic graphs. Related 13 and 14 focus on visualization and uncertainty estimation using topological summaries.
- **Trends:** Increasing sophistication in TDA methods (from Betti numbers to zigzag persistence), movement from static to dynamic/topological tracking, and application to both interpretability and practical tasks (e.g., pruning, OOD detection).
- **Relationships:** The submission builds directly on the TDA lineage, extending from static (Related 8) and local (Related 1) topological metrics to dynamic, trajectory-based descriptors (persistence similarity). Related 15 provides the mathematical foundation for zigzag persistence.

#### Cluster 2: Layer/Block Pruning and Redundancy in LLMs
- **Papers:** Submission, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 11
- **Description:** These works propose various strategies for reducing LLM size and computational cost by identifying and removing redundant layers or blocks. Methods include importance metrics (Block Influence, Shapley value, cosine similarity), structured and fine-grained pruning (block, layer, attention, MLP, FFN), and post-pruning recovery (LoRA, PEFT).
- **Trends:** Shift from coarse (block-level) to fine-grained (layer, attention, FFN) pruning, increasing use of data-driven and interpretable importance metrics, and focus on minimal or no post-pruning retraining.
- **Relationships:** The submission introduces a novel, topologically-motivated pruning criterion (persistence similarity), complementing metric-based approaches (BI, Shapley, cosine). Several papers (e.g., 4, 6, 11) explore the redundancy of specific components (attention, MLP), while others (2, 3, 7) focus on overall layer/block importance.

#### Cluster 3: Similarity and Importance Metrics for Layers
- **Papers:** Submission, 1, 10, 12, 11
- **Description:** These works develop and survey metrics for quantifying similarity or importance between layers or models, including topological similarity (NNTS, persistence similarity), representational similarity (CCA, CKA), and importance (Shapley value, Block Influence).
- **Trends:** Movement toward unified frameworks (Related 10), and increasing focus on actionable metrics for pruning and interpretability.
- **Relationships:** The submission’s persistence similarity is a new topological metric, extending the family of similarity measures surveyed in Related 10 and developed in Related 1. Shapley-based and influence-based metrics (12, 11, 2) are complementary, focusing on functional impact.

#### Cluster 4: Visualization and Interpretability Tools
- **Papers:** 13, 14, 16
- **Description:** These works provide tools and frameworks for visualizing and interpreting neural network activations and biases, using TDA (TopoAct), topological uncertainty, and information-theoretic probing.
- **Trends:** Emphasis on interactive, generalizable tools and on linking intrinsic properties (e.g., bias, uncertainty) to model behavior.
- **Relationships:** These methods often use or extend the topological analysis frameworks from Cluster 1.

#### Cluster 5: Resource-Efficiency Surveys and Taxonomies
- **Papers:** 9, 10
- **Description:** Comprehensive surveys and taxonomies of methods for resource-efficient LLMs and neural network similarity.
- **Trends:** Standardization of evaluation, holistic view of efficiency, and identification of open challenges.
- **Relationships:** These surveys contextualize the technical developments in other clusters.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

1. **Interpretability and Transparency of LLMs/DNNs**
   - *Papers:* Submission, 1, 8, 13, 14, 16
   - *Approaches:* Topological analysis, visualization, information-theoretic probing, activation space exploration.
   - *Patterns:* Increasingly sophisticated tools (TDA, visualization) for probing internal representations; focus on both global (topology) and local (neighborhood, bias) properties.

2. **Redundancy and Pruning in LLMs**
   - *Papers:* Submission, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 11
   - *Approaches:* Layer/block importance metrics, structured/fine-grained pruning, post-pruning recovery.
   - *Patterns:* Shift from block-level to fine-grained, interpretable, and task-agnostic pruning; use of both topological and functional metrics.

3. **Quantifying and Comparing Internal Representations**
   - *Papers:* Submission, 1, 5, 8, 10, 12
   - *Approaches:* Topological similarity, intrinsic dimension, representational similarity, Shapley value.
   - *Patterns:* Move toward unified, actionable, and interpretable metrics; focus on both static and dynamic aspects of representations.

4. **Resource Efficiency and Model Compression**
   - *Papers:* 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 9, 11
   - *Approaches:* Pruning, quantization, efficient architectures, taxonomy and benchmarking.
   - *Patterns:* Holistic view of efficiency (computation, memory, energy, cost); standardization of evaluation.

5. **Monitoring and Reliability in Deployment**
   - *Papers:* 14, 16
   - *Approaches:* Topological uncertainty, bias metrics.
   - *Patterns:* Post-training, architecture-agnostic methods for OOD detection and bias assessment.

6. **Understanding Evolution of Representations**
   - *Papers:* Submission, 5, 8, 15
   - *Approaches:* Tracking topological/geometric changes across layers or time (zigzag persistence, ID profile, Betti numbers).
   - *Patterns:* Emphasis on dynamic, trajectory-based analysis.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Pruning Redundancy:** Some papers use functional metrics (Block Influence, Shapley), others use topological or similarity-based metrics (persistence similarity, cosine similarity). The submission uniquely leverages topological trajectories for pruning.
- **Interpretability:** Some focus on visualization (TopoAct), others on quantifiable metrics (Betti numbers, persistence similarity, bias extractability).
- **Representation Evolution:** Approaches range from geometric (intrinsic dimension) to topological (Betti numbers, persistence diagrams) to dynamic (zigzag persistence).

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **LLM Benchmarks:** MMLU, GSM8k, BoolQ, C4, HellaSwag, LLaMA, Vicuna, ChatGLM, Llama3
- **Image Benchmarks:** ImageNet, STL-10
- **Protein Data:** ESM-2
- **Text Data:** BERT, unspecified NLP tasks
- **Synthetic/Graph Data:** For TDA and OOD detection

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Topological Metrics:** Persistence similarity (submission), NNTS/NNTP (1), Betti numbers and decay (\Omega) (8), Topological Uncertainty (14)
- **Functional/Performance Metrics:** Accuracy (MMLU, GSM8k, etc.), performance retention, inference speedup, zero-shot ability
- **Importance/Similarity Metrics:** Block Influence (2), Shapley value (12, 11), cosine similarity (11), representational similarity (10)
- **Resource Metrics:** Computation, memory, energy, cost (9)
- **Bias/Uncertainty Metrics:** Bias extractability, contextual WEAT (16), TU score (14)

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Pruning Papers:** Focus on performance retention after pruning (accuracy, zero-shot ability), often reporting percentage of layers pruned and corresponding accuracy drop.
- **Topological Analysis Papers:** Emphasize new topological metrics and their correlation with generalization, transfer learning, or semantic content.
- **Resource-Efficiency Papers:** Use holistic, multi-objective metrics (computation, memory, energy, cost).
- **Interpretability/Bias Papers:** Use both intrinsic (e.g., bias extractability) and extrinsic (task performance) metrics.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Pruning/Compression:** Direct comparison of pruned vs. original model on standard benchmarks; some use post-pruning recovery (LoRA, PEFT).
- **Topological/Similarity Metrics:** Often validated by correlation with downstream performance or by qualitative visualization.
- **Resource-Efficiency:** Emphasis on standardized, multi-dimensional metrics for fair comparison.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Static to Dynamic Topological Analysis:** Early work (8, 1) used static topological metrics (Betti numbers, NNTS) to analyze representations. The submission advances this by introducing zigzag persistence and persistence similarity, enabling dynamic, trajectory-based analysis across layers.
- **From Coarse to Fine-Grained Pruning:** Initial pruning methods focused on blocks or entire layers (2, 3, 7, 11). Recent work (6, 4) moves to finer granularity (attention, FFN), with more interpretable and flexible strategies. The submission’s topological pruning is orthogonal and potentially complementary to these.
- **From Heuristic to Data-Driven and Interpretable Metrics:** Early pruning and similarity metrics were often heuristic (e.g., output agreement, cosine similarity). The field has shifted toward data-driven (Shapley, BI) and interpretable (topological, information-theoretic) metrics.
- **Integration of Topological and Functional Perspectives:** The submission and related work (1, 8, 13, 14) integrate topological analysis with practical tasks (pruning, OOD detection), bridging the gap between theory and application.
- **Standardization and Taxonomy:** Survey papers (9, 10) reflect a maturing field, emphasizing standardization, benchmarking, and holistic evaluation.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Zigzag Persistence:** Mathematical foundation (15) → Applied to neural representations (submission).
- **Topological Metrics:** Betti numbers (8) → NNTS/NNTP (1) → Persistence similarity (submission).
- **Pruning Metrics:** Block Influence (2), Shapley value (12, 11), cosine similarity (11) → Used for increasingly fine-grained and interpretable pruning (6, 4).
- **Visualization:** Mapper construction (13) → Topological uncertainty (14).

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Pruning Criteria:** Topological (submission) vs. functional (BI, Shapley, cosine) metrics; can be used in combination for robust pruning.
- **Interpretability:** Visualization (13), topological metrics (submission, 1, 8), and information-theoretic probing (16) offer complementary insights.
- **Resource Efficiency:** Pruning, quantization, and efficient architectures are complementary; survey (9) highlights the need for multi-objective optimization.

# Conclusion

The research landscape represented by these papers is characterized by a convergence of topological, geometric, and functional approaches to understanding, compressing, and interpreting large neural models, especially LLMs. The submission paper stands at the intersection of topological analysis and practical model compression, introducing a dynamic, trajectory-based topological metric (persistence similarity) that advances both interpretability and efficiency. The field is rapidly evolving toward more interpretable, fine-grained, and standardized methods, with increasing integration of theoretical insights and practical applications.
