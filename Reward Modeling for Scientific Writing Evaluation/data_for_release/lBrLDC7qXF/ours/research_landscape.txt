# Research Landscape Analysis

## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the provided papers, the methodological landscape for Knowledge Graph Completion (KGC) is rich and evolving, with several prominent paradigms:

- **Pre-trained Language Model (PLM)-based Approaches:** Leveraging BERT, LLMs, or other PLMs to encode triples, entity/relation descriptions, or prompts (e.g., KG-BERT, CAB-KGC, SimKGC, MEM-KGC, CSProm-KG, PKGC, KG-LLM, DIFT).
- **Hybrid Structure-Text Models:** Combining graph structure (embeddings, GNNs) with textual encodings (e.g., StAR, CSProm-KG, NNKGC).
- **Contrastive and Negative Sampling Techniques:** Employing advanced negative sampling or contrastive learning to improve discriminative power (e.g., SimKGC, CAB-KGC).
- **Prompt-based and In-context Learning:** Using prompt engineering, soft prompts, or in-context learning to guide PLMs/LLMs (e.g., CSProm-KG, KICGPT, PKGC, MPIKGC).
- **Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Geometric Models:** Utilizing GNNs or geometric embeddings (e.g., NNKGC, Mixed Geometry Message, TransR).
- **Temporal KGC Models:** Incorporating temporal information via sequence models or LLMs (e.g., Chain of History, Learning Sequence Encoders).
- **Multi-task and Multi-perspective Learning:** Jointly optimizing multiple objectives or perspectives (e.g., Multi-task Learning, MPIKGC).
- **Evaluation and Benchmarking Innovations:** Proposing new datasets, evaluation metrics, or settings (e.g., CoDEx, CAB-KGC's EDAS, OWA evaluation in PKGC).

### Methodological Clusters

**Cluster 1: PLM/LLM-based KGC**
- **Papers:** KG-BERT, CAB-KGC, SimKGC, MEM-KGC, CSProm-KG, PKGC, KG-LLM, DIFT, KICGPT, MPIKGC, Pretrain-KGE, Multi-task Learning, Exploring LLMs, Finetuning LLMs
- **Characteristics:** Use of pre-trained language models (BERT, LLMs) to encode triples, often with prompt engineering, masking, or in-context learning. Some (CAB-KGC, CSProm-KG) integrate structural KG information directly into PLM input or prompts.

**Cluster 2: Structure-Text Hybrid Models**
- **Papers:** StAR, CSProm-KG, NNKGC, Representation Learning with Entity Descriptions, Pretrain-KGE
- **Characteristics:** Combine graph structure (embeddings, GNNs) with textual encodings, aiming to leverage both contextual and structural knowledge.

**Cluster 3: Contrastive/Negative Sampling Innovations**
- **Papers:** SimKGC, CAB-KGC
- **Characteristics:** Focus on efficient and effective negative sampling or contrastive learning to improve model discrimination and training efficiency.

**Cluster 4: GNNs and Geometric Models**
- **Papers:** NNKGC, Mixed Geometry Message, TransR
- **Characteristics:** Use GNNs or geometric embeddings to model multi-hop neighborhoods or multiple geometric spaces for richer representations.

**Cluster 5: Temporal KGC**
- **Papers:** Chain of History, Learning Sequence Encoders
- **Characteristics:** Address temporal aspects of KGC, using sequence models or LLMs with temporal augmentation.

**Cluster 6: Evaluation and Benchmarking**
- **Papers:** CoDEx, PKGC (OWA evaluation), CAB-KGC (EDAS)
- **Characteristics:** Focus on creating new datasets, evaluation metrics, or settings to better assess KGC models.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Shift from Pure Embedding to PLM/LLM-based Methods:** Early methods (TransR, graph embeddings) are increasingly complemented or replaced by PLM/LLM-based approaches, which offer better generalization, especially for unseen entities.
- **Integration of Structure and Text:** There is a clear trend toward fusing structural KG information with textual encodings, either via hybrid models (StAR, CSProm-KG) or by encoding context directly into PLMs (CAB-KGC).
- **Prompt Engineering and In-context Learning:** Prompt-based methods (CSProm-KG, KICGPT, PKGC) are gaining traction, leveraging the flexibility of PLMs/LLMs for KGC tasks.
- **Efficiency and Scalability:** Methods like CAB-KGC and SimKGC focus on eliminating negative sampling or improving contrastive learning efficiency, addressing computational bottlenecks.
- **Temporal and Multi-task Extensions:** Recent work extends KGC to temporal settings (Chain of History) and multi-task learning (Multi-task Learning, MPIKGC), reflecting the growing complexity of real-world KGs.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Knowledge Graph Completion (KGC):** The central problem, including link prediction, triple classification, and relation prediction.
- **Generalization to Unseen Entities/Relations:** Addressed by CAB-KGC, MEM-KGC, SimKGC, and others, crucial for real-world, evolving KGs.
- **Long-tail Entity Prediction:** Tackled by KICGPT, MPIKGC, and others, focusing on entities with few connections.
- **Balancing Structure and Text:** Many PLM-based models struggle to utilize KG structure; hybrid and prompt-based models address this.
- **Negative Sampling and Training Efficiency:** CAB-KGC, SimKGC, and others seek to reduce or eliminate negative sampling overhead.
- **Evaluation Limitations:** CAB-KGC, PKGC, and CoDEx highlight issues with current metrics and propose new evaluation frameworks.
- **Temporal KGC:** Chain of History and Learning Sequence Encoders address time-aware link prediction.
- **Zero-shot and Inductive KGC:** Representation Learning with Entity Descriptions, SimKGC, and others focus on handling novel entities.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Generalization to Unseen Entities:** CAB-KGC and MEM-KGC avoid reliance on entity-specific embeddings, using context or masking to predict unseen entities.
- **Long-tail Entities:** KICGPT uses LLMs' internal knowledge and in-context learning; MPIKGC expands entity descriptions and structure via LLMs.
- **Structure-Text Fusion:** CSProm-KG and StAR explicitly combine structural and textual signals, while CAB-KGC encodes context from KG structure into BERT.
- **Negative Sampling:** CAB-KGC eliminates negative sampling via cross-entropy loss; SimKGC innovates with multiple negative types for contrastive learning.
- **Evaluation:** CAB-KGC introduces EDAS; PKGC advocates for OWA evaluation; CoDEx provides harder, more realistic benchmarks.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Static to Dynamic/Temporal KGC:** Recent work (Chain of History) extends KGC to temporal domains.
- **From Closed-World to Open-World Assumptions:** PKGC and others challenge the traditional closed-world evaluation, advocating for more realistic settings.
- **From Entity-centric to Context/Neighborhood-centric Modeling:** NNKGC, CAB-KGC, and others emphasize the importance of multi-hop or contextual information.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **Standard Benchmarks:** FB15k-237, WN18RR, Wikidata5M, ICEWS series, Freebase, YAGO, ConceptNet100K, CoDEx (S/M/L).
- **Temporal Datasets:** ICEWS, YAGO (temporal), others for temporal KGC.
- **Zero-shot/Inductive Datasets:** Some works (SimKGC, StAR) use inductive or zero-shot splits.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Ranking Metrics:** Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR), Mean Rank (MR), Hits@K (Hits@1, @3, @10).
- **Classification Metrics:** Triple classification accuracy, relation prediction accuracy.
- **Novel Metrics:** EDAS (CAB-KGC), OWA-based evaluation (PKGC), inference time (StAR).

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Dominance of Ranking Metrics:** MRR and Hits@K are the most widely used, but their limitations (e.g., insensitivity to hard negatives, lack of robustness) are increasingly recognized.
- **Emergence of New Metrics:** CAB-KGC's EDAS and PKGC's OWA evaluation reflect a push for more nuanced, realistic, and robust evaluation.
- **Efficiency Considerations:** Inference time and training overhead are becoming important, especially for large-scale or real-time applications (StAR, SimKGC, KICGPT).

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Traditional vs. Realistic Evaluation:** CoDEx and PKGC highlight the need for harder, more realistic benchmarks and evaluation settings.
- **Inclusion of Unseen Entities:** Some models (MEM-KGC, SimKGC, CAB-KGC) explicitly evaluate on unseen entities, reflecting real-world KG dynamics.
- **Temporal and Multi-task Evaluation:** Temporal KGC models use time-aware metrics; multi-task models report on multiple objectives.

### Cluster 3: Contrastive/Negative Sampling Innovations

- **Key Papers:** SimKGC, CAB-KGC
- **Characteristics:** Focus on efficient and effective negative sampling or contrastive learning to improve model discrimination and training efficiency.

### Cluster 4: GNNs and Geometric Models

- **Key Papers:** NNKGC, Mixed Geometry Message, TransR
- **Characteristics:** Use GNNs or geometric embeddings to model multi-hop neighborhoods or multiple geometric spaces for richer representations.

### Cluster 5: Temporal KGC

- **Key Papers:** Chain of History, Learning Sequence Encoders

### Cluster 6: Evaluation and Benchmarking

- **Key Papers:** CoDEx, PKGC (OWA evaluation), CAB-KGC (EDAS)

### Relationships Between Clusters

- **PLM/LLM-based and Structure-aware Models:** Increasingly, PLM-based models are integrating structural information (CAB-KGC, CSProm-KG), blurring the line between these clusters.
- **Contrastive/Negative Sampling and PLM-based Models:** Efficient training strategies (SimKGC, CAB-KGC) are critical for scaling PLM-based KGC.
- **Evaluation Cluster as Enabler:** New benchmarks and metrics (CoDEx, EDAS, OWA) are driving methodological innovation and more realistic assessment.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Embedding-based to PLM/LLM-based KGC:** Early work (TransR, graph embeddings) focused on learning entity/relation embeddings. The field has shifted toward leveraging PLMs/LLMs for richer, more generalizable representations.
- **From Text-only to Structure-aware PLM Models:** Initial PLM-based models (KG-BERT) relied heavily on entity descriptions. Newer models (CAB-KGC, CSProm-KG, StAR) integrate structural context, addressing the limitations of text-only approaches.
- **From Negative Sampling to Efficient Training:** Negative sampling, once standard, is being replaced or augmented by more efficient loss functions (cross-entropy in CAB-KGC, InfoNCE in SimKGC) and negative mining strategies.
- **From Closed-world to Open-world and Realistic Evaluation:** There is a growing recognition of the limitations of traditional evaluation settings, leading to new benchmarks (CoDEx), metrics (EDAS), and settings (OWA in PKGC).
- **From Static to Temporal and Multi-task KGC:** The field is expanding to temporal KGs (Chain of History) and multi-task/multi-perspective learning (MPIKGC, Multi-task Learning).
- **From Black-box to Explainable and Efficient Models:** Some recent models (NNKGC) emphasize explainability and efficiency, crucial for real-world deployment.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **KG-BERT as a Foundation:** Many subsequent models (CAB-KGC, SimKGC, MEM-KGC) build on the idea of encoding triples as text for PLMs.
- **Prompt-based and In-context Learning:** Prompt engineering (CSProm-KG, PKGC, KICGPT) is a major extension, leveraging the flexibility of PLMs/LLMs.
- **Hybridization:** Models like StAR and CSProm-KG extend PLM-based approaches by integrating graph structure, leading to better generalization and efficiency.
- **Temporal and Multi-perspective Extensions:** Chain of History and MPIKGC extend KGC to new domains and learning paradigms.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing:** Text-only PLM models vs. structure-aware or hybrid models; negative sampling vs. cross-entropy/contrastive learning; closed-world vs. open-world evaluation.
- **Complementary:** Prompt-based methods can be combined with structure-aware models; efficient training strategies benefit all model types; new evaluation metrics/benchmarks drive progress across the board.

---

**In summary**, the research landscape for KGC is rapidly evolving, with a clear trend toward integrating PLMs/LLMs, structural KG information, and efficient training/evaluation strategies. The submission paper, CAB-KGC, exemplifies this evolution by fusing context-aware structural information into BERT, eliminating negative sampling, and introducing a new evaluation metric, positioning itself at the intersection of several key methodological and problem-space trends.
