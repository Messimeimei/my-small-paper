# METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

**Main Approaches:**
- **Large-Scale Transformer Models for Molecular Data:**  
  - *Encoder-Decoder Transformers:* SMI-TED (submission) introduces a large-scale encoder-decoder transformer for molecular data, pre-trained on massive SMILES datasets.
  - *Encoder-Only Transformers:* MOLFormer, ChemBERTa, and UniMAP use transformer encoders for molecular property prediction and representation learning.
  - *Decoder-Only Transformers:* GP-MoLFormer employs a transformer decoder for autoregressive molecular generation.
  - *Mixture-of-Experts (MoE):* SMI-TED explores scalable MoE architectures for flexible applications.
- **Generative Models for Molecules:**  
  - *Language Model-Based Generators:* GMTransformer, GP-MoLFormer, and earlier RNN-based models (e.g., "Language models can learn complex molecular distributions") generate molecules as SMILES strings.
  - *Graph-Based Generators:* JT-VAE generates molecular graphs directly, ensuring chemical validity.
  - *Multimodal and Cross-Modality Models:* UniMAP and UAM integrate SMILES, graph, and expert features; "Bidirectional generation..." aligns structure and property modalities.
- **Self-Supervised and Contrastive Pretraining:**  
  - *Masked Language Modeling:* ChemBERTa, Egret, and others use masked token prediction for pretraining.
  - *Contrastive Learning:* Egret introduces reaction condition-based contrastive learning; UniMAP uses cross-modality contrastive tasks.
  - *Pair-Tuning and Meta-Learning:* GP-MoLFormer introduces pair-tuning for property optimization; Egret applies meta-learning for low-data regimes.
- **Uncertainty Modeling:**  
  - *Uncertainty-Aware Models:* UAM and Molecular Transformer explicitly model prediction uncertainty.
- **Benchmarking and Evaluation Platforms:**  
  - *MOSES:* Standardizes evaluation for generative models.
  - *Comprehensive LLM Benchmarks:* "What can Large Language Models do in chemistry?" establishes broad benchmarks for LLMs in chemistry.

# TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

**Progression of Ideas:**
- **From Small-Scale to Foundation Models:**  
  - Early models (ChemBERTa, MOLFormer) demonstrated the value of transformer architectures and large-scale pretraining. SMI-TED and GP-MoLFormer scale up both model size and dataset, introducing MoE and more flexible architectures.
- **From Single- to Multi-Modality:**  
  - UniMAP and UAM move beyond SMILES or graph-only models, integrating multiple modalities for richer representations and improved performance.
- **From Discriminative to Generative and Bidirectional Models:**  
  - The field has evolved from property prediction to generative design (GMTransformer, GP-MoLFormer, JT-VAE), and now to models capable of both (e.g., "Bidirectional generation...").
- **From Black-Box to Interpretable and Uncertainty-Aware Models:**  
  - Recent models (GMTransformer, UAM, Molecular Transformer) emphasize interpretability and uncertainty estimation, addressing practical deployment concerns.
- **From Task-Specific to General-Purpose and LLMs:**  
  - The application of LLMs (GPT-3, GPT-4) to chemistry tasks demonstrates the potential for general-purpose models, with competitive performance in low-data regimes.
- **From Ad Hoc Evaluation to Standardized Benchmarks:**  
  - Platforms like MOSES and comprehensive LLM benchmarks have standardized evaluation, enabling more rigorous comparison and progress tracking.

**Building Blocks and Extensions:**
- *Transformer architectures* are the core building block, extended with MoE (SMI-TED), rotary embeddings (MOLFormer), linear attention (GP-MoLFormer), and cross-modality fusion (UniMAP).
- *Self-supervised and contrastive learning* are widely adopted for pretraining, with innovations in task design (e.g., reaction condition-based contrastive learning in Egret).
- *Uncertainty modeling* is increasingly integrated into predictive models for practical reliability.

**Competing and Complementary Approaches:**
- *SMILES-based vs. Graph-based vs. Multimodal:*  
  - SMILES-based models are scalable and leverage NLP advances; graph-based models (JT-VAE) ensure chemical validity; multimodal models (UniMAP, UAM) combine strengths.
- *Domain-Specific vs. General-Purpose LLMs:*  
  - Domain-specific models (SMI-TED, MOLFormer) are tailored for chemistry, while LLMs offer flexibility and strong few-shot performance.
- *Generative vs. Discriminative Models:*  
  - Generative models focus on molecule design, while discriminative models excel at property prediction; recent work aims to unify both.
