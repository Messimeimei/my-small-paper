This paper introduces CADO, a novel framework that integrates Reinforcement Learning (RL) fine-tuning with diffusion models to address Combinatorial Optimization (CO) problems such as the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) and Maximal Independent Set (MIS). The methodological innovations are perceived as incremental, primarily extending existing RL and diffusion model techniques without introducing substantial novel contributions. Comparisons with closely related methods, particularly T2T, are insufficiently addressed, raising concerns about the relative significance of CADO’s improvements. The reliance on multiple hyperparameters and the lack of clarity in explaining key design choices further obscure the paper’s contributions, making it difficult to assess the true impact and scalability of CADO in diverse CO scenarios.
