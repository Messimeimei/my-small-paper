## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Open-Vocabulary Object Detection (OVOD/OVD)**: Detecting and localizing objects from categories not seen during training, specified via text, images, or both.
- **Few-Shot and Zero-Shot Learning**: Generalizing to new categories with few or no labeled examples.
- **Fine-Grained and Attribute-Level Recognition**: Distinguishing visually similar categories and modeling fine-grained attributes.
- **Efficient Knowledge Transfer**: Leveraging large-scale VLMs or unlabeled data to improve detection without extensive annotation.
- **Prompt and Prototype Learning**: Learning effective prompts or prototypes for novel category recognition.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **OVOD/OVD**: Tackled via multimodal fusion (Submission, OVMR, Multi-Modal Classifiers), region-word alignment (VLDet, CoDet), knowledge distillation (ViLD, OADP), and prompt learning (DetPro, PromptDet).
- **Fine-Grained Recognition**: Addressed by hierarchical modeling (Submission, DVDet), attribute-level prototypes (CoAR-ZSL, CPN), and compositional region alignment (BARON).
- **Few-Shot/Zero-Shot**: Approached via prototype completion (Prototype Completion), compositional prototypes (CPN), and pseudo-labeling (Label, Verify, Correct).

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Category-Level to Attribute/Descriptor-Level**: Shift from recognizing categories by name to modeling fine-grained attributes and descriptors.
- **From Supervised to Weakly/Unsupervised**: Increasing use of unlabeled or weakly labeled data (web images, image-text pairs) for scalable learning.
- **Flexible Category Specification**: Allowing users to specify novel categories at inference via text, images, or both.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Image-Level to Region-Level Alignment**: Early works (e.g., ViLD, RegionCLIP) focused on transferring image-level knowledge to detection. Later works (VLDet, CoDet, BARON) developed more sophisticated region-word alignment strategies.
- **From Category Names to Rich Descriptions and Attributes**: The field has evolved from using class names (CLIP, ViLD) to leveraging rich, LLM-generated descriptions (Multi-Modal Classifiers, DVDet) and fine-grained attribute modeling (Submission, CoAR-ZSL).
- **From Supervised to Training-Free and Plug-and-Play**: There is a clear trend toward methods that require less supervision, can be integrated without retraining, and leverage unlabeled or weakly labeled data (Submission, OVMR, PromptDet).
- **From Manual to Learned Prompts**: Prompt learning has evolved from manual engineering to context-aware, region-specific, and dynamically learned prompts (DetPro, PromptDet, CORA).
- **From Single-Modality to Multimodal and Hierarchical Fusion**: The field has moved from text-only or image-only approaches to sophisticated multimodal and hierarchical fusion strategies (Submission, OVMR, DVDet).

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **CLIP and VLMs as Foundations**: Many methods build on CLIP or similar VLMs, extending them for region-level tasks, detection, or multimodal fusion.
- **Prototype and Attribute Modeling**: Prototypes (object, attribute, compositional) are increasingly used as building blocks for generalization.
- **Prompt Learning and Adaptation**: Prompt learning is now a key component for aligning modalities and improving detection.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing**: Some methods compete in how they align region and text (e.g., set matching vs. co-occurrence vs. bag-of-regions).
- **Complementary**: Prototype-based and multimodal fusion methods can be combined for further gains (e.g., Submission’s hierarchical matching could be integrated with prompt learning or knowledge distillation).
