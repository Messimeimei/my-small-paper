## 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission, "Mitigate the Gap: Improving Cross-Modal Alignment in CLIP," addresses the *modality gap* in CLIP's embedding space, proposing (1) parameter sharing between vision and language encoders (AlignCLIP) and (2) a semantically-regularized intra-modality separation objective.
- This places the work squarely in the *Cross-Modal Alignment and Modality Gap Mitigation* cluster, which is currently a central and highly active area in vision-language research.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Liang et al. (2022), Tyshchuk et al. (2023), Schrodi et al. (2024):** Analytical works diagnosing the modality gap and proposing naive translation (shifting) to close it.
- **You et al. (2022):** Explores parameter sharing in CLIP, focusing on downstream performance.
- **Goel et al. (2022) (CyCLIP), Lee et al. (2022), Mu et al. (2022), Zhou et al. (2023), Zhai et al. (2023):** Propose various loss and training modifications to improve alignment and embedding space structure.
- **SoftCLIP, UniCLIP, "It's Not a Modality Gap", Mind the Modality Gap, Two Effects, One Trigger, iCLIP, RA-CLIP:** Recent works (some not cited) that directly address the modality gap, parameter sharing, or embedding space regularization.

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission is most closely aligned with *Cluster A (Parameter Sharing/Unified Encoders)* and *Cluster B (Embedding Space Regularization)*.
- The intra-modality separation objective is conceptually similar to regularization approaches in SoftCLIP, CyCLIP, and others, though the specific formulation may differ.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The modality gap and cross-modal alignment are well-established problems, with multiple recent works proposing both architectural and loss-based solutions.
- The use of CC3M, zero-shot classification, robustness, and retrieval metrics is standard in the field.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission's approach is a natural extension of recent trends: moving from two-tower to shared-parameter architectures and from simple contrastive losses to more nuanced embedding space regularization.
- Several recent works (some not cited) have proposed similar or related ideas, indicating a rapidly maturing and competitive area.

## 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**
- The authors consistently cite Liang et al. (2022), Tyshchuk et al. (2023), and Schrodi et al. (2024) as foundational works diagnosing the modality gap and proposing naive translation (shifting) as a mitigation.
- They position their method as a more principled and effective alternative to naive shifting, emphasizing that prior methods distort embedding structure.
- You et al. (2022) is cited as having explored parameter sharing, but only from a downstream performance perspective, implying the submission is the first to use it for alignment.
- Other works (Goel et al., Lee et al., Mu et al., Zhou et al., Zhai et al., Hu et al.) are grouped as "prior work improving aspects of CLIP," with little detail on their specific contributions to alignment or embedding space structure.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The characterization of Liang et al. (2022) as proposing naive shifting is accurate, but the claim that this approach "fails to maintain the relative distances" is only partially substantiated; some prior works (e.g., Tyshchuk et al., Schrodi et al.) analyze these limitations in more depth.
- The claim that You et al. (2022) only considered parameter sharing for downstream performance is somewhat reductive; their work also discusses effects on alignment, though perhaps not with the same focus.
- The grouping of other works as "improving aspects of CLIP" may understate the direct relevance of methods like SoftCLIP, UniCLIP, and others that also target the modality gap or embedding space structure.

**Discrepancies:**
- The authors do not cite or discuss several recent works (e.g., SoftCLIP, UniCLIP, "It's Not a Modality Gap," Mind the Modality Gap, Two Effects, One Trigger, iCLIP, RA-CLIP) that propose alternative or complementary solutions to the modality gap, some of which use parameter sharing or embedding regularization.
- The rhetoric sometimes overstates the novelty of parameter sharing for alignment, given that related ideas have appeared in the literature (e.g., iCLIP, SoftCLIP, UniCLIP).

## 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

### Contribution 1: **Parameter Sharing Between Vision and Language Encoders (AlignCLIP)**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *You et al. (2022):* Explores transformer sharing in CLIP.
  - *iCLIP, UniCLIP, SoftCLIP (not cited):* Propose unified or partially shared encoders for cross-modal alignment.
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Authors claim their parameter sharing is novel in targeting cross-modal alignment and modality gap reduction, not just downstream performance.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Parameter sharing as a means to improve alignment has been explored in iCLIP, UniCLIP, and SoftCLIP, sometimes with different architectural details (e.g., full vs. partial sharing, shared projection heads).
  - The submission's specific implementation (e.g., which layers are shared, how sharing is regularized) may differ, but the conceptual advance is incremental rather than fundamentally new.
  - The claim that prior work did not target alignment is not fully accurate; several recent works explicitly address this.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The main delta is in the specific design and empirical demonstration, not in the high-level idea.

### Contribution 2: **Semantically-Regularized Intra-Modality Separation Objective**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *SoftCLIP, CyCLIP, "Two Effects, One Trigger", "It's Not a Modality Gap":* Propose loss terms to regularize intra- and inter-modality distances, improve isotropy, or encourage uniformity.
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Authors claim their intra-modality separation is novel and leads to better embedding structure.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The idea of regularizing intra-modality distances is present in SoftCLIP (intra-modal self-similarity), CyCLIP (uniformity/alignment), and others.
  - The specific formulation (e.g., semantic regularization) may be new, but the general approach is well-established.
  - The delta is in the particular loss design and empirical results, not in the overall concept.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The difference is incremental; the novelty lies in the specific regularization term, not the general strategy.

### Contribution 3: **Empirical Demonstration of Reduced Modality Gap and Improved Performance**

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - *SoftCLIP, UniCLIP, CyCLIP, "Two Effects, One Trigger", "Mind the Modality Gap":* All report improved alignment and performance via similar strategies.
- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Authors claim significant improvements over previous methods.
- **Independent Assessment:**
