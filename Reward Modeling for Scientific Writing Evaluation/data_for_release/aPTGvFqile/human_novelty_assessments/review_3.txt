This paper introduces AlignCLIP, a variant of the CLIP model aimed at reducing the modality gap between image and text embeddings in cross-modal learning. Both SharedCLIP and IMSep primarily extend existing contrastive learning techniques; sharing parameters between encoders is a known approach, and the IMSep loss largely repurposes InfoNCE without substantial modifications. The paper’s novelty, therefore, is limited. Empirical comparisons with relevant baselines are lacking, as AlignCLIP is only compared with the original CLIP (and SharedCLIP, also introduced in this paper), omitting several cited "naive" approaches for modality gap reduction. Table 7 also compares AlignCLIP with two other CLIP variations, but from what I understand, these variations were not designed to mitigate the modality gap.
