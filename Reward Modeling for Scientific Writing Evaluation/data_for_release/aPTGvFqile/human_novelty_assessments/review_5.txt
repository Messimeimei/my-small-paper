The paper proposes a method to mitigate the modality gap in the representational space of CLIP by introducing two modifications (termed AlignCLIP): sharing the weights of the vision and text transformers in CLIP (SharedCLIP), and using a new Intra-modality separation loss that encourages separation between images in CLIP space while respecting some of the semantics of the similarities between the images. Understanding and mitigating the modality gap is an important direction in CLIP and its variants, which are ubiquitously used in many applications. The authors suggested two directions (SharedCLIP + IMSep loss) for attempting to reduce the modality gap, and the effects of both SharedCLIP and SharedCLIP + IMSep loss were tested. Further, the impact of enforcing intra-modality separation on images, texts and their combination was tested individually, and the effect of the rescaling mechanism to control separation of similar images in the batch was also tested individually.
