This paper proposes AlignCLIP to reduce the modality gap in CLIP embedding space by leveraging a shared transformer to align image and text embeddings, and introducing an additional loss function to separate semantically distinct unimodal embeddings. The technical contribution appears limited, as prior work has also leveraged the shared transformer framework [1, 2]. The proposed method utilizes a single-encoder framework, which is completely different from CLIP’s two-tower architecture, raising questions about whether the obtained embedding space is relevant to the CLIP embedding space.
