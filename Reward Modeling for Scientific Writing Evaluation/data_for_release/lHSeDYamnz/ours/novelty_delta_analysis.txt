# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission, "Catastrophic Failure of LLM Unlearning via Quantization," is positioned at the intersection of LLM unlearning methodology and model robustness, specifically focusing on the underexplored axis of quantization. The core contribution is the empirical and theoretical demonstration that quantization can reverse the effects of state-of-the-art unlearning methods, leading to the recovery of supposedly forgotten knowledge.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Negative Preference Optimization (NPO)** and its variants (SimNPO, FLAT, ULD, MEOW): These are the main loss-based unlearning methods evaluated in the submission.
- **Gradient Ascent (GA):** A baseline for unlearning.
- **SOUL:** Explores advanced optimization for unlearning.
- **Benchmarks:** TOFU, MUSE, RWKU provide the evaluation context and are referenced for their learning rate and evaluation protocols.

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission builds on the "Loss Function Engineering" cluster (NPO, GA) and the "Optimization and Training Strategies" cluster (SOUL), but introduces a new robustness axis—quantization.
- It is distinct from "Black-Box and Inference-Time" methods (delta-Unlearning, ECO Prompts), as it assumes access to model weights for retraining and quantization.
- The work is also closely tied to the "Evaluation and Benchmarking" cluster, as it critiques the sufficiency of current benchmarks and metrics in the face of quantization.

**Place in Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses the problem of *authentic knowledge removal* and *robustness to recovery*—specifically, whether unlearning is truly effective or merely superficial when models are quantized.
- It extends the evaluation landscape by introducing quantization as a new axis, showing that prior metrics and benchmarks may not capture this vulnerability.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is the first to systematically analyze the interaction between unlearning and quantization, a gap not addressed in prior work.
- Its proposed mitigation strategies (learning rate adjustment, saliency-based targeting) are incremental extensions of existing optimization techniques, but their application to quantization robustness is novel.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors cite NPO, SOUL, and other unlearning methods as foundational, emphasizing their limitations in the context of quantization.
- Benchmarks (TOFU, MUSE, RWKU) are referenced for their learning rate protocols and as standard evaluation tools.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The characterization of NPO and GA as state-of-the-art but vulnerable to quantization is accurate, as these methods do not consider post-unlearning model compression.
- The citation of SOUL and other optimization-based methods is neutral, focusing on their role in improving unlearning stability rather than robustness to quantization.
- The discussion of benchmarks is factual, referencing their learning rate settings and evaluation protocols.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The claim that prior methods "do not address quantization" is accurate; however, the implication that this is a fundamental oversight may overstate the novelty, as quantization has not been a standard consideration in unlearning literature.
- The authors do not mischaracterize the technical content of cited works, but they do not acknowledge recent work on robust evaluation (e.g., adversarial probing) that, while not addressing quantization, shares a similar motivation.

**Substantiation of Claimed Improvements:**
- The empirical results (e.g., knowledge retention jumping from 13% to 89% post-quantization) substantiate the claim of catastrophic failure.
- The mitigation strategies are presented as novel, but their technical basis (learning rate adjustment, saliency targeting) is an adaptation of known optimization techniques.

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** NPO, SimNPO, FLAT, ULD, MEOW—all focus on loss-based unlearning, but none consider quantization.
- **Claimed Difference:** First to show that quantization can reverse unlearning, leading to knowledge recovery.
- **Independent Verification:** No prior work systematically studies the effect of quantization on unlearning efficacy. The empirical evidence (knowledge retention metrics pre- and post-quantization) supports the claim.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The difference is substantive; it exposes a new vulnerability in the unlearning pipeline.
- **Author Characterization vs. Reality:** Accurate; the novelty is in the identification of the problem, not in the unlearning method itself.

- **Most Similar Prior Work:** RWKU, MUSE, TOFU—comprehensive benchmarks for unlearning, but do not consider quantization.
- **Claimed Difference:** Extends evaluation to include quantization as a robustness axis.
- **Independent Verification:** Prior benchmarks focus on adversarial probing, not model compression. The submission's analysis is a genuine extension.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** Substantive in evaluation scope; the theoretical analysis is incremental, applying known robustness concepts to a new context.

- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The technical novelty is limited; the main contribution is the application to a new problem (quantization robustness).

# 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Field Maturity:** LLM unlearning is a rapidly evolving field, with most major advances in the last 1–2 years. The area is active, with frequent new methods and benchmarks.
- **Recent Surveys:** No comprehensive survey yet, but several literature reviews (e.g., in MUSE, RWKU, UWC/ES) summarize the state of the art.
- **Trends:** Shift from naive (GA) to principled (NPO, SimNPO, FLAT) loss functions; increasing focus on adversarial robustness and practical deployment constraints (efficiency, black-box, privacy).
- **Incremental Advances:** Many recent papers offer incremental improvements (e.g., new loss terms, better calibration, parameter efficiency). The submission's identification of quantization as a vulnerability is a more substantial shift in evaluation perspective.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Potential Overstatement of Novelty:** The technical mitigation strategies (learning rate, saliency) are adaptations of known techniques; their novelty lies in application, not in algorithmic innovation.
- **Alignment of Claimed Novelty and Evidence:** The main novelty—exposing quantization-induced unlearning failure—is well supported. The mitigation strategies are less novel than implied.
- **Empirical Improvements:** The dramatic increase in knowledge retention post-quantization is a robust finding, unlikely to be due to implementation details.
- **Terminology Differences:** The submission uses standard terminology; no evidence that terminology is used to mask similarities.
- **Routine Extensions:** The mitigation strategies could be seen as routine adaptations rather than conceptual breakthroughs.

# 6. RELATED WORK CONSIDERATIONS

- **Omitted Relevant Work:** Several recent unlearning methods are not cited (e.g., SimNPO, FLAT, ULD, MEOW, LLM Surgery, delta-Unlearning, ECO Prompts). Some of these address robustness and efficiency, though not quantization.
- **Additional Comparisons Needed:** The submission could compare its mitigation strategies to parameter-efficient or black-box unlearning methods, which may interact differently with quantization.
- **Incomplete Characterizations:** The limitations of prior work are accurately described regarding quantization, but the broader landscape of robustness (e.g., adversarial, black-box) is not fully discussed.
- **Claimed Limitations of Prior Work:** The focus on quantization is justified, but the submission could acknowledge that prior work has addressed other robustness axes.

# 7. KEY OBSERVATION SUMMARY

- **Most Significant Independently Verified Difference:** The submission is the first to systematically demonstrate that quantization can catastrophically reverse LLM unlearning, a vulnerability not previously addressed.
- **Main Relationships to Existing Research:** Builds on loss-based unlearning (NPO, GA), optimization strategies (SOUL), and benchmarking (TOFU, MUSE, RWKU), but introduces quantization as a new robustness axis.
- **Strongest Differentiation:** Identification and empirical demonstration of quantization-induced unlearning failure.
- **Weakest Differentiation:** The proposed mitigation strategies (learning rate, saliency) are adaptations of existing techniques, not fundamentally new.
- **Discrepancies Between Author Characterization and Independent Assessment:** The main novelty is accurately described; however, the technical novelty of mitigation strategies is less than implied.
- **Additional Context:** The field is rapidly evolving, with many incremental advances; the submission's main contribution is a shift in evaluation perspective rather than a new unlearning algorithm.

---

**Overall, the submission makes a substantive and well-supported contribution by exposing a new axis of vulnerability in LLM unlearning—quantization-induced knowledge recovery. The mitigation strategies are incremental adaptations, and the omission of some recent related work is a minor gap. The work is best understood as a critical evaluation and extension of the current unlearning paradigm, rather than a fundamentally new unlearning method.**
