# Research Landscape Analysis: CHASE-SQL and the LLM-based Text-to-SQL Ecosystem

---

## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the provided papers, the following methodological themes emerge:

- **Multi-Agent and Multi-Path Frameworks:**  
  - *CHASE-SQL* (Submission), *CHESS*, and to some extent *DIN-SQL* and *SQL-PaLM* employ multi-agent or multi-path reasoning, where different agents or strategies handle sub-tasks (e.g., candidate generation, schema selection, validation).
- **Prompt Engineering and In-Context Learning (ICL):**  
  - *MCS-SQL*, *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*, *DIN-SQL*, *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL*, and *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention* focus on prompt design, demonstration selection, and in-context learning strategies to maximize LLM performance.
- **Decomposition and Divide-and-Conquer:**  
  - *CHASE-SQL*, *DIN-SQL*, and *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention* decompose complex queries or tasks into sub-tasks or sub-queries, often within a single LLM call or via workflow paradigms.
- **Candidate Generation and Selection:**  
  - *CHASE-SQL*, *MCS-SQL*, *SQL-PaLM*, and *CHESS* generate multiple SQL candidates and employ selection mechanisms (e.g., pairwise ranking, multiple-choice selection, execution-based filtering).
- **Schema Linking and Retrieval:**  
  - *CHESS*, *MCS-SQL*, *SQL-PaLM*, and *TA-SQL* introduce advanced schema linking, retrieval, and selection modules to handle large and complex database schemas.
- **Synthetic Data Generation and Augmentation:**  
  - *SQL-GEN*, *SQL-PaLM*, and *CHASE-SQL* (via instance-aware synthetic examples) generate synthetic data to improve LLM adaptation and generalization.
- **Self-Correction and Error Handling:**  
  - *DIN-SQL*, *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention*, and *CHESS* integrate self-correction, unit testing, or error summarization modules.
- **Model Merging and MoE Architectures:**  
  - *SQL-GEN* introduces Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) and model merging for multi-dialect adaptation.
- **Task Alignment and Hallucination Mitigation:**  
  - *TA-SQL* (Before Generation, Align it!) proposes explicit task alignment modules to reduce hallucinations.

### Method Clusters

- **Cluster 1: Multi-Agent/Path Reasoning & Decomposition**
  - *CHASE-SQL*, *CHESS*, *DIN-SQL*, *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention*
  - These methods break down the Text-to-SQL process into specialized sub-tasks or agents, often combining candidate generation, selection, and validation.
- **Cluster 2: Prompt Engineering & In-Context Learning**
  - *MCS-SQL*, *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*, *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL*, *DIN-SQL*, *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention*
  - Focus on optimizing prompt structure, demonstration selection, and in-context learning strategies.
- **Cluster 3: Schema Linking & Retrieval**
  - *CHESS*, *MCS-SQL*, *SQL-PaLM*, *TA-SQL*
  - Emphasize robust schema linking, retrieval, and selection to handle large/complex databases.
- **Cluster 4: Synthetic Data & Model Adaptation**
  - *SQL-GEN*, *SQL-PaLM*, *CHASE-SQL* (synthetic examples)
  - Use synthetic data generation and model adaptation (including MoE) to improve generalization and cross-dialect performance.
- **Cluster 5: Evaluation, Benchmarking, and Survey**
  - *Benchmarking the Text-to-SQL Capability of LLMs*, *Next-Generation Database Interfaces: A Survey*, *The Dawn of Natural Language to SQL*, *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*
  - Provide systematic evaluation frameworks, taxonomies, and comprehensive benchmarking.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **From Monolithic to Modular:**  
  There is a clear trend from monolithic, single-step LLM prompting to modular, multi-agent, or decomposed frameworks that explicitly handle sub-tasks (e.g., candidate generation, schema selection, validation).
- **Prompt Engineering as a Core Lever:**  
  Prompt design, demonstration selection, and in-context learning are central to maximizing LLM performance, with increasing sophistication in how prompts are constructed and selected.
- **Candidate Diversity and Selection:**  
  Generating diverse SQL candidates and employing robust selection mechanisms (pairwise ranking, execution-based filtering, multiple-choice selection) is a recurring theme for improving accuracy and robustness.
- **Schema Complexity Handling:**  
  As real-world databases grow in complexity, schema linking, retrieval, and selection modules become essential for scalability and accuracy.
- **Synthetic Data and Model Adaptation:**  
  Synthetic data generation and model merging (e.g., MoE) are increasingly used to address data scarcity, dialect diversity, and generalization challenges.
- **Error Mitigation and Self-Correction:**  
  Self-correction, unit testing, and hallucination mitigation modules are being integrated to address LLM limitations and improve reliability.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Accurate Text-to-SQL Translation:**  
  All papers address the core challenge of translating natural language questions into executable SQL queries.
- **Handling Complex and Large Schemas:**  
  *CHASE-SQL*, *CHESS*, *SQL-PaLM*, *MCS-SQL*, and *TA-SQL* focus on scaling to large, complex, or industrial-scale schemas.
- **Candidate Generation and Selection:**  
  *CHASE-SQL*, *MCS-SQL*, *SQL-PaLM*, and *CHESS* address generating multiple SQL candidates and selecting the best one.
- **Prompt Sensitivity and In-Context Learning:**  
  *MCS-SQL*, *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*, *DIN-SQL*, *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL* focus on prompt design and in-context learning strategies.
- **Data Scarcity and Generalization:**  
  *SQL-GEN*, *SQL-PaLM*, and *CHASE-SQL* (synthetic examples) address the lack of annotated data and the need for better generalization, especially across SQL dialects.
- **Hallucination and Error Mitigation:**  
  *TA-SQL*, *CHESS* (unit testing), *DIN-SQL*, and *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention* tackle hallucinations, logical errors, and self-correction.
- **Evaluation and Benchmarking:**  
  *Benchmarking the Text-to-SQL Capability of LLMs*, *The Dawn of Natural Language to SQL*, *Next-Generation Database Interfaces: A Survey* focus on systematic evaluation and benchmarking.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Complex Query Handling:**  
  - *CHASE-SQL* uses divide-and-conquer and execution-plan-based reasoning.
  - *DIN-SQL* and *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention* decompose tasks and use workflow paradigms.
  - *CHESS* employs multi-agent frameworks with schema selection and candidate refinement.
- **Candidate Selection:**  
  - *CHASE-SQL* uses a fine-tuned selection agent for pairwise ranking.
  - *MCS-SQL* uses multiple-choice selection.
  - *SQL-PaLM* and *CHESS* use execution-based filtering and unit testing.
- **Schema Linking:**  
  - *CHESS* and *SQL-PaLM* use retrieval and selection agents.
  - *MCS-SQL* and *TA-SQL* use prompt-based and task-aligned schema linking.
- **Data Scarcity:**  
  - *SQL-GEN* generates dialect-specific synthetic data.
  - *SQL-PaLM* and *CHASE-SQL* use LLM-generated synthetic examples for few-shot learning.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **Decomposition and Modularity:**  
  Problems are increasingly formulated as modular or decomposable, with explicit sub-tasks (e.g., schema selection, candidate generation, validation).
- **Candidate Diversity and Robustness:**  
  Emphasis on generating diverse candidates and robust selection to handle ambiguity and complexity.
- **Scalability and Real-World Applicability:**  
  Focus on handling large, real-world databases and industrial-scale schemas.
- **Reliability and Error Mitigation:**  
  Addressing hallucinations, logical errors, and ensuring functional correctness is a growing concern.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **BIRD:**  
  Used extensively for large, complex, and realistic schema evaluation (e.g., *CHASE-SQL*, *CHESS*, *MCS-SQL*, *SQL-PaLM*, *TA-SQL*, *SQL-GEN*, *DIN-SQL*).
- **Spider:**  
  The most widely used academic benchmark for Text-to-SQL (e.g., *SQL-PaLM*, *CHESS*, *MCS-SQL*, *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*, *DIN-SQL*, *SQL-GEN*, *TA-SQL*, *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention*, *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL*).
- **Other Datasets:**  
  Some papers introduce new datasets (e.g., *Benchmarking the Text-to-SQL Capability of LLMs*), or use dialect-specific real-world datasets (*SQL-GEN*).

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Execution Accuracy:**  
  The primary metric across all papers, measuring whether the generated SQL produces the correct result.
- **Exact Set Match Accuracy:**  
  Used in *DIN-SQL* for stricter evaluation.
- **Valid Efficiency Score (VES):**  
  Used in *MCS-SQL* and *DIN-SQL* to combine accuracy and efficiency.
- **Column Selection Accuracy:**  
  Used in *SQL-PaLM* for schema selection evaluation.
- **Token Usage and LLM Calls:**  
  Used in *CHESS* and *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs* to measure efficiency and cost.
- **Task-Specific Accuracies:**  
  Used in *Benchmarking the Text-to-SQL Capability of LLMs* for sub-tasks (e.g., SQL Debugging, Schema Linking).

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Execution accuracy is the gold standard**, but efficiency (token usage, LLM calls) is increasingly important for practical deployment.
- **Relative improvements over baselines** (e.g., GPT-4, previous SOTA) are commonly reported.
- **Multi-metric evaluation** (accuracy, efficiency, robustness) is becoming standard, especially for industrial applicability.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Comprehensive Benchmarking:**  
  Papers like *Benchmarking the Text-to-SQL Capability of LLMs* and *The Dawn of Natural Language to SQL* provide multi-angle, multi-task evaluation frameworks.
- **Efficiency Metrics:**  
  *CHESS* and *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs* emphasize token and call efficiency, reflecting real-world deployment concerns.
- **Dataset Diversity:**  
  *SQL-GEN* and *SQL-PaLM* evaluate across multiple dialects and real-world datasets, pushing beyond academic benchmarks.

## 4. RESEARCH CLUSTERS

### Cluster 1: Multi-Agent/Decomposition Frameworks
- **Papers:** *CHASE-SQL*, *CHESS*, *DIN-SQL*, *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention*
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Modular, multi-agent or workflow-based architectures
  - Task decomposition (e.g., sub-query generation, schema selection, validation)
  - Emphasis on robustness, scalability, and error correction
- **Relationships:**  
  - *CHASE-SQL* and *CHESS* both use multi-agent frameworks, but *CHASE-SQL* focuses more on candidate diversity and selection, while *CHESS* emphasizes schema selection and unit testing.
  - *DIN-SQL* and *Decomposition for Enhancing Attention* share decomposition and self-correction strategies.

### Cluster 2: Prompt Engineering & In-Context Learning
- **Papers:** *MCS-SQL*, *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*, *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL*, *DIN-SQL*
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Systematic exploration of prompt design, demonstration selection, and in-context learning
  - Focus on maximizing LLM performance with minimal fine-tuning
- **Relationships:**  
  - *MCS-SQL* and *Enhancing Few-shot Text-to-SQL* both explore prompt diversity and selection.
  - *DIN-SQL* bridges this cluster with decomposition strategies.

### Cluster 3: Schema Linking & Retrieval
- **Papers:** *CHESS*, *SQL-PaLM*, *MCS-SQL*, *TA-SQL*
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Advanced schema linking, retrieval, and selection modules
  - Handling large and complex database schemas
- **Relationships:**  
  - *CHESS* and *SQL-PaLM* both introduce efficient schema selection for large databases.
  - *TA-SQL* focuses on schema alignment to mitigate hallucinations.

### Cluster 4: Synthetic Data & Model Adaptation
- **Papers:** *SQL-GEN*, *SQL-PaLM*, *CHASE-SQL* (synthetic examples)
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Synthetic data generation for improved generalization and cross-dialect adaptation
  - Model merging and adaptation (e.g., MoE)
- **Relationships:**  
  - *SQL-GEN* is unique in its focus on dialect adaptation and model merging.

### Cluster 5: Evaluation, Benchmarking, and Survey
- **Papers:** *Benchmarking the Text-to-SQL Capability of LLMs*, *Next-Generation Database Interfaces: A Survey*, *The Dawn of Natural Language to SQL*, *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*
- **Characteristics:**  
  - Systematic evaluation frameworks, taxonomies, and benchmarking
  - Analysis of LLM capabilities, limitations, and future directions

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Prompt Engineering to Modular Frameworks:**  
  Early work focused on prompt engineering and in-context learning. Recent advances (e.g., *CHASE-SQL*, *CHESS*, *DIN-SQL*) move towards modular, multi-agent, and decomposed frameworks for greater robustness and scalability.
- **From Single Candidate to Candidate Diversity and Selection:**  
  The field has shifted from generating a single SQL candidate to producing diverse candidates and employing sophisticated selection mechanisms (pairwise ranking, execution-based filtering, multiple-choice selection).
- **From Academic Benchmarks to Real-World Applicability:**  
  There is a growing emphasis on handling large, complex, and industrial-scale schemas (e.g., *BIRD* dataset, schema selection modules).
- **From Data Scarcity to Synthetic Data and Model Adaptation:**  
  Synthetic data generation (e.g., *SQL-GEN*, *SQL-PaLM*) and model merging (MoE) address data scarcity and cross-dialect challenges.
- **From Accuracy to Efficiency and Reliability:**  
  Efficiency (token usage, LLM calls) and reliability (hallucination mitigation, self-correction) are increasingly prioritized for real-world deployment.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Prompt Engineering → Decomposition/Modularization:**  
  Prompt engineering insights inform the design of decomposed and modular frameworks.
- **Candidate Generation → Selection Agents:**  
  Early candidate generation methods are extended with robust selection agents (e.g., *CHASE-SQL*'s pairwise ranking, *MCS-SQL*'s multiple-choice selection).
- **Schema Linking → Retrieval and Alignment:**  
  Schema linking evolves into advanced retrieval and alignment modules, often integrated with task alignment for error mitigation.
- **Synthetic Data → Model Merging:**  
  Synthetic data generation is extended with model merging and MoE architectures for multi-dialect adaptation.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing:**  
  - *Prompt-based* vs. *Fine-tuned* LLMs: Some works (e.g., *Text-to-SQL Empowered by LLMs*) compare prompt-based and fine-tuned approaches.
  - *Single-step* vs. *Decomposed/Workflow* prompting: Decomposition-based methods (e.g., *CHASE-SQL*, *DIN-SQL*) show advantages over single-step prompting.
- **Complementary:**  
  - Synthetic data generation and modular frameworks can be combined for further gains.
  - Schema linking, candidate selection, and error mitigation modules are often integrated within larger frameworks.

# Conclusion

The LLM-based Text-to-SQL research landscape is rapidly evolving from prompt-centric approaches to sophisticated, modular, and multi-agent frameworks. The field is converging on several key strategies:

- **Decomposition and modularization** for handling complexity and improving robustness.
- **Diverse candidate generation and robust selection** to address ambiguity and improve accuracy.
- **Advanced schema linking and retrieval** for scalability to real-world databases.
- **Synthetic data and model adaptation** to overcome data scarcity and dialect diversity.
- **Efficiency and error mitigation** as essential considerations for practical deployment.

**CHASE-SQL** exemplifies the state-of-the-art by integrating multi-path reasoning, divide-and-conquer decomposition, instance-aware synthetic examples, and a robust selection agent, achieving leading performance on challenging benchmarks. The broader landscape reflects a dynamic interplay of complementary and competing approaches, with ongoing innovation in modular architectures, prompt engineering, and evaluation methodologies.
