## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the dominant methodological axis is **explicit 3D scene representation using Gaussian Splatting (GS)**, especially for dynamic and monocular video scenarios. This is complemented by:
- **Deformation fields** for modeling scene dynamics (e.g., MoDGS, GauFRe, Deformable 3D Gaussians, Gaussian-Flow, MoSca, SC-GS).
- **Integration of 2D/3D priors** (depth, optical flow, tracking) from pretrained models (e.g., MoDGS, MoSca, Dynamic Gaussian Marbles, DepthSplat, Shape of Motion).
- **Regularization and initialization strategies** to address underconstrained monocular settings (e.g., ordinal depth loss, ARAP, flow-based loss, inductive bias-aware initialization).
- **Hybrid or alternative representations**: voxel grids (MixVoxels), neural fields (NeRF, DpDy), diffusion models (MultiDiff, DpDy), and MPI (MINE).

### Method Clusters

**Cluster 1: Dynamic Gaussian Splatting for Monocular/Dynamic Scenes**
- **Papers**: MoDGS (submission), MoSca, Dynamic Gaussian Marbles, GauFRe, Deformable 3D Gaussians, SC-GS, Shape of Motion, GFlow, Gaussian-Flow, PVG, Real-time Photorealistic 4DGS
- **Characteristics**: Use of explicit 3D Gaussian primitives, deformation fields for motion, monocular video as input, often with depth/flow priors, and focus on real-time or efficient rendering.

**Cluster 2: Integration of 2D/3D Priors and Regularization**
- **Papers**: MoDGS, MoSca, Dynamic Gaussian Marbles, DepthSplat, Shape of Motion, GFlow, Consistent Video Depth Estimation
- **Characteristics**: Leverage pretrained depth/flow/segmentation/tracking models, introduce regularization (ordinal depth loss, ARAP, flow-based, surface/patch consistency) to stabilize learning from monocular or sparse data.

**Cluster 3: Hybrid/Alternative Representations**
- **Papers**: MixVoxels, DpDy, MultiDiff, MINE, FSGS, HDR-GS, CoherentGS
- **Characteristics**: Use of voxel grids, neural fields, diffusion models, or hybrid approaches for view synthesis, often focusing on efficiency, few-shot, or HDR scenarios.

**Cluster 4: Scene Editing, Control, and Efficiency**
- **Papers**: SC-GS, MixVoxels, PVG, Gaussian-Flow
- **Characteristics**: Explicit control over motion (sparse control points, motion editing), efficient training/rendering, and support for large-scale or urban scenes.

### Methodological Trends and Relationships

- **Explicit, point-based 3D representations (GS) are rapidly supplanting implicit neural fields (NeRF) for dynamic and monocular scenarios** due to their efficiency, compositionality, and editability.
- **Deformation fields** are the standard for modeling scene dynamics, with innovations in initialization, regularization, and parameterization (e.g., SE(3) motion bases, ARAP, DDDM).
- **Integration of 2D/3D priors** (depth, flow, tracking) is now routine, with increasing sophistication in how these are fused (e.g., robust ordinal loss, multi-view cost volumes, fusion of monocular and multi-view depth).
- **Regularization and initialization** are critical for monocular settings, with new losses (ordinal, flow-based, ARAP) and initialization schemes (depth-based, inductive bias-aware) being proposed.
- **Efficiency and scalability** are major concerns, with methods like MixVoxels, Gaussian-Flow, and HDR-GS achieving orders-of-magnitude speedups over NeRF-based baselines.
- **Scene editing and control** (e.g., SC-GS, PVG) are emerging as important features, enabled by explicit, sparse motion representations.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

1. **Novel View Synthesis of Dynamic Scenes from Monocular Video**
   - Central to MoDGS, MoSca, Dynamic Gaussian Marbles, Shape of Motion, SC-GS, GFlow, Deformable 3D Gaussians, and others.
   - Focus: Overcoming weak multiview constraints, underconstrained geometry, and motion ambiguity in monocular settings.

2. **Efficient and Real-time Dynamic Scene Reconstruction**
   - Addressed by GauFRe, Gaussian-Flow, Real-time Photorealistic 4DGS, MixVoxels, HDR-GS, PVG.
   - Focus: Achieving high-quality rendering at real-time speeds, reducing training/inference time.

3. **Integration and Consistency of Depth/Geometry in Monocular Settings**
   - MoDGS, DepthSplat, Consistent Video Depth Estimation, Shape of Motion, CoherentGS.
   - Focus: Achieving scale-consistent, temporally stable, and geometrically accurate depth/geometry from monocular input.

4. **Few-shot/Sparse-view Novel View Synthesis**
   - FSGS, CoherentGS, MINE.
   - Focus: High-quality synthesis from very limited input views.

5. **Scene Editing, Control, and Large-scale/Urban Scene Modeling**
   - SC-GS, PVG, MixVoxels.
   - Focus: Editable motion, handling large-scale or highly dynamic urban scenes.

6. **HDR and Photorealistic Rendering**
   - HDR-GS, Real-time Photorealistic 4DGS.
   - Focus: High dynamic range, photorealism, and exposure control.

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Monocular Dynamic View Synthesis**: Most methods use explicit 3D GS with deformation fields, but differ in how they initialize/regularize (e.g., MoDGS uses ordinal depth loss, MoSca uses ARAP and bundle adjustment, Dynamic Gaussian Marbles uses isotropic marbles and hierarchical learning).
- **Depth Consistency**: MoDGS introduces ordinal loss, DepthSplat fuses monocular and multi-view depth, Consistent Video Depth Estimation uses test-time fine-tuning with geometric constraints.
- **Efficiency**: MixVoxels and Gaussian-Flow use hybrid/static-dynamic representations and explicit deformation modeling for speed; HDR-GS and FSGS focus on fast rendering and training.
- **Scene Editing**: SC-GS introduces sparse control points and MLPs for motion editing; PVG uses periodic vibration for temporal dynamics and control.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **Monocular video as the primary input** is a unifying theme, with the challenge being to extract 3D/4D structure and motion from limited cues.
- **Dynamic scene modeling** is increasingly formulated as a 4D (3D+time) problem, with explicit separation of static and dynamic components.
- **Depth/geometry consistency** is often treated as a weakly supervised or self-supervised problem, leveraging priors and regularization.
- **Efficiency and scalability** are now core requirements, not just afterthoughts.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **Static to Dynamic GS**: Early GS methods focused on static scenes; recent work (Deformable 3D Gaussians, GauFRe, MoDGS, MoSca, etc.) extends this to dynamic scenes via deformation fields.
- **Monocular to Multi-view to Monocular Dynamic**: The field has moved from multi-view static (classic NeRF/GS) to monocular dynamic, with increasing sophistication in handling weak constraints.
- **Integration of Priors**: There is a clear trend toward leveraging pretrained 2D/3D priors (depth, flow, tracking) and fusing them with explicit 3D representations.
- **Regularization and Initialization**: New losses (ordinal, ARAP, flow-based) and initialization schemes (depth-based, inductive bias-aware) have been developed to address underconstrained monocular settings.
- **Efficiency and Scalability**: Methods like MixVoxels, Gaussian-Flow, HDR-GS, and FSGS have dramatically improved training and inference speed, making real-time and large-scale applications feasible.
- **Scene Editing and Control**: Recent methods (SC-GS, PVG) enable explicit motion editing and control, a step toward interactive and editable scene representations.
- **Generative Priors and Diffusion Models**: The latest works (MultiDiff, DpDy) incorporate diffusion models for geometry/appearance priors, enabling hallucination and improved consistency.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **3D Gaussian Splatting** is the foundational building block, extended with:
  - **Deformation fields** (for dynamics)
  - **Regularization/priors** (for stability and consistency)
  - **Hybrid representations** (for efficiency)
  - **Control/editing mechanisms** (for interactivity)
  - **Generative models** (for hallucination and consistency)

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **GS-based methods** compete with NeRF-based and voxel-based methods, but are increasingly dominant due to efficiency and flexibility.
- **Diffusion-based methods** (MultiDiff, DpDy) are complementary, providing strong priors that can be integrated into GS pipelines.
- **Hybrid methods** (MixVoxels, MINE) offer alternative trade-offs between efficiency, quality, and flexibility.
