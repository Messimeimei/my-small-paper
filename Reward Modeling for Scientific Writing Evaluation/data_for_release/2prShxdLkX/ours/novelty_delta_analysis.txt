# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Landscape:**

- **MoDGS** is situated within the most active and competitive cluster: *Dynamic Gaussian Splatting (GS) for Monocular/Dynamic Scenes*. This cluster includes MoSca, Dynamic Gaussian Marbles, GauFRe, Deformable 3D Gaussians, SC-GS, Shape of Motion, and others.
- The *core problem* addressed—novel view synthesis of dynamic scenes from casually captured monocular videos with static or slow-moving cameras—is a central challenge in this cluster.
- **Most closely related prior works** (by technical approach and problem):  
  - **MoSca (Lei et al., 2024):** Uses explicit motion representation, depth estimation, and video tracking priors for dynamic GS from monocular video.
  - **Dynamic Gaussian Marbles (Stearns et al., 2024):** Hierarchical learning of dynamic GS from monocular video.
  - **Deformable 3D Gaussians (Yang et al., 2023):** Introduces deformation MLP for dynamic GS, but primarily for multi-view or more constrained monocular settings.
  - **SC-GS (Huang et al., 2024):** Sparse control for dynamic GS, with a focus on editability.
  - **Shape of Motion (Wang et al., 2024b):** Explicit motion representation, depth/flow priors, and initialization for monocular dynamic scenes.
- **Methodological relationship:** MoDGS, like MoSca and Shape of Motion, leverages depth priors for initialization and regularization, but claims a novel 3D-aware initialization and a robust ordinal depth loss.
- **Problem space:** The submission targets the *most challenging monocular dynamic scenario* (casual, static/slow camera, weak multiview cues), which is a current frontier in the field.
- **Evaluation:** Standard datasets (Nvidia, DyNeRF, DAVIS, in-the-wild) and metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) are used, consistent with the field.

**Verification:**  
The submission is not the first to attempt monocular dynamic GS with depth priors (see MoSca, Shape of Motion, Dynamic Gaussian Marbles), but claims specific technical advances in initialization and depth loss.
