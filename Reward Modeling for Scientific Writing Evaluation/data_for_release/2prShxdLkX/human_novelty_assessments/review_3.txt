This paper introduces MoDGS (Monocular Dynamic Gaussian Splatting), a novel approach for rendering dynamic 3D scenes from casually captured monocular videos by incorporating depth supervision into the domain of dynamic Gaussian Splatting (DGS) for monocular dynamic input. This approach is novel yet intuitive, filling a key gap in the field for cases where the input consists of casually captured videos with minimal camera movement. Compared to other papers in the field that mechanically combine various complex input feature streams or loss functions, the proposed solution is conceptually straightforward but impactful, pushing forward the capabilities of monocular dynamic scene reconstruction. The method introduces a novel ordinal depth loss to address depth inconsistency in single-view depth maps, enhancing the robustness and continuity of 3D scene reconstruction, and the results clearly show MoDGS’s robustness and superiority over baseline methods, adding confidence in its effectiveness.
