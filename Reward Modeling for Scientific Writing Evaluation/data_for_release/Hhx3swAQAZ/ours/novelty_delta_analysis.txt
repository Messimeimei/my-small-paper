- **Methodological Cluster:** ExVideo is most closely aligned with the "Latent Video Diffusion and Compression" cluster, which includes works like Stable Video Diffusion (Blattmann et al., 2023), Latent Video Diffusion Models (LVDM), MagicVideo, and LAVIE. These models use latent spaces for efficient video generation.
- **Temporal Modeling:** ExVideo also intersects with the "Temporal Modeling and Attention Mechanisms" cluster, sharing ground with AnimateDiff (Guo et al., 2023), CogVideoX, and Open-Sora, which focus on temporal attention, 3D convolutions, and transformer-based modules.

- **Problem Space:** The submission addresses the challenge of extending the temporal length of video diffusion models without retraining or significant computational overhead—a recognized bottleneck in the field.
- **Evaluation:** ExVideo’s evaluation (max video length, training cost, video quality) is consistent with current standards, though the dataset size (40k videos) is smaller than some recent large-scale works.

**Independent Assessment:**
- ExVideo is not the first to address long video generation (see LVDM, MagicVideo, CogVideoX), but is the first to propose a parameter-efficient, post-tuning method specifically for temporal extension of existing video diffusion models.
- The approach is an incremental but practical advance, leveraging adapters for temporal module extension, which is a natural evolution of modular adaptation trends in the field.
- The method is positioned as a practical solution for extending existing models, rather than a fundamentally new generative architecture.
