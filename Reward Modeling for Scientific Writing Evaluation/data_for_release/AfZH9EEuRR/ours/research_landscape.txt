## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From General to Egocentric**:  
  - Early QR code detection focused on general scenes and robustness to noise/distortion. Recent works (EGOQR, BioVL-QR) specialize for egocentric data, addressing new challenges (wide FOV, motion blur, resource constraints).
- **From Isolated Tasks to Multimodal, Integrated Systems**:  
  - The field has evolved from single-modality detection to multimodal perception (vision, audio, language, text) and integrated application systems (assistive tech, supply chain, AR).
- **From Manual Annotation to Automated, Large-Scale Datasets**:  
  - Datasets like Ego4D and BioVL-QR enable automated annotation and large-scale benchmarking, supporting more complex and realistic tasks.
- **From Security as an Afterthought to a Core Concern**:  
  - Security and authentication have become central, with novel attacks (Li-Man), physical/digital authentication, and cryptographic protocols.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **ZXing Library**:  
  - Used as a foundation for decoding in EGOQR, extended for egocentric challenges.
- **Deep Learning and Object Detection**:  
  - Extended from general object detection to QR code-specific and egocentric applications.
- **Contrastive Learning and Pretraining**:  
  - Adapted for egocentric video-language tasks (EgoNCE, MAE pretraining).

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing**:  
  - Traditional image processing vs. deep learning for QR code detection.
  - Physical vs. digital security/authentication methods.
- **Complementary**:  
  - Multimodal integration (vision, audio, language) complements robust detection/decoding.
  - Application systems benefit from advances in both detection robustness and security.
