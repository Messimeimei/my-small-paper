## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the following methodological themes emerge:

- **Weight-Input Alignment & Linear Decomposition**: Methods that enforce or exploit alignment between model weights and input features to enable faithful, interpretable explanations (e.g., WASUP, B-cos, CoDA-Nets, WeightAlign, WFA).
- **Prototype/Case-Based Reasoning**: Models that classify by comparing input features to learned prototypes or support vectors, often providing explanations in terms of similarity to these cases (e.g., WASUP, ProtoPNet, Deformable ProtoPNet, XProtoNet, PANIC, IAIA-BL, PIP-Net).
- **Dynamic/Adaptive Alignment**: Approaches that dynamically align model parameters or representations with input patterns for improved interpretability (e.g., CoDA-Nets, DAUs, Deformable ProtoPNet).
- **Nonparametric & Support-Based Heads**: Use of nonparametric prediction heads (e.g., Nadaraya-Watson) or explicit support sets for classification and explanation (e.g., WASUP, NW Head).
- **Concept Bottlenecking**: Models with explicit intermediate concept layers, supporting human intervention and concept-level explanations (e.g., Concept Bottleneck Models).
- **Black-box Explanation Methods**: Post-hoc methods that generate explanations for any model, regardless of internal structure (e.g., RISE).
- **Bag-of-Features/Local Feature Models**: Architectures that base decisions on local features, facilitating part-based explanations (e.g., BagNet).

### Method Clusters

- **Cluster 1: Weight-Input Alignment for Interpretability**
  - **Papers**: WASUP, B-cos (both papers), CoDA-Nets (both papers), WeightAlign, WFA
  - **Description**: These methods enforce or leverage alignment between weights and input features, enabling faithful, model-inherent explanations. B-cos and CoDA-Nets introduce new layers or units (B-cos transform, DAUs) to achieve this, while WASUP combines alignment with support vector reasoning.
  - **Trends**: Increasing focus on making explanations both faithful (reflecting true model computation) and human-interpretable, often with minimal accuracy loss.

- **Cluster 2: Prototype/Case-Based Reasoning**
  - **Papers**: WASUP, ProtoPNet/ProtoPFaith, Deformable ProtoPNet, XProtoNet, PANIC, IAIA-BL, PIP-Net
  - **Description**: These models learn class-representative prototypes or support vectors and classify by comparing input features to these. Explanations are provided in terms of similarity to prototypes, often visualized as image patches or parts.
  - **Trends**: Movement from rigid, fixed-size prototypes (ProtoPNet) to more flexible, context-aware, and semantically meaningful prototypes (Deformable ProtoPNet, XProtoNet, PIP-Net).

- **Cluster 3: Nonparametric/Support-Based Heads**
  - **Papers**: WASUP, NW Head, SVM-based methods (animal sound, anomaly detection)
  - **Description**: Use of nonparametric or support-based classification heads (e.g., Nadaraya-Watson, SVM) to provide interpretable, support-based explanations and improved calibration.

- **Cluster 4: Concept Bottlenecking**
  - **Papers**: Concept Bottleneck Models
  - **Description**: Models with explicit concept layers, supporting direct human intervention and concept-level explanations.

- **Cluster 5: Black-box/Post-hoc Explanation**
  - **Papers**: RISE
  - **Description**: Methods that generate explanations for any model, without requiring access to model internals.

- **Cluster 6: Bag-of-Features/Local Feature Models**
  - **Papers**: BagNet
  - **Description**: Models that base decisions on local features, facilitating part-based and spatially localized explanations.

### Methodological Trends & Relationships

- **Faithfulness as a Core Goal**: There is a clear trend toward explanations that are not just visually plausible but are provably faithful to the model’s actual computations (WASUP, B-cos, ProtoPFaith, CoDA-Nets).
- **From Post-hoc to Inherent Interpretability**: The field is moving from post-hoc explanation methods (e.g., RISE) to models that are interpretable by design (WASUP, B-cos, CoDA-Nets, prototype-based models).
- **Hybridization**: Some models combine multiple approaches, e.g., WASUP integrates weight-input alignment (from B-cos) with support vector/prototype reasoning.
- **Flexibility and Generality**: Newer methods (WASUP, B-cos) are designed to be architecture-agnostic, compatible with CNNs, Transformers, and other backbones.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

- **Faithful, Inherently Interpretable Explanations for Image Classification**
  - **Papers**: WASUP, B-cos, CoDA-Nets, ProtoPFaith, Deformable ProtoPNet, PIP-Net, XProtoNet, PANIC, IAIA-BL
  - **Approaches**: Weight-input alignment, prototype-based reasoning, dynamic alignment, concept bottlenecking.

- **Limitations of Post-hoc Explanations**
  - **Papers**: WASUP, ProtoPFaith, B-cos, CoDA-Nets, RISE
  - **Approaches**: Critique of post-hoc methods for lack of faithfulness; development of inherently interpretable models.

- **Complexity and Faithfulness of Prototype-Based Explanations**
  - **Papers**: WASUP, ProtoPFaith, Deformable ProtoPNet, PIP-Net, XProtoNet
  - **Approaches**: Reducing explanation complexity, improving semantic alignment, handling spatial misalignment, and providing richer, more context-aware explanations.

- **Interpretability in High-Stakes Domains (Medical, Fine-Grained)**
  - **Papers**: WASUP, XProtoNet, PANIC, IAIA-BL, ProtoPFaith
  - **Approaches**: Ensuring explanations are clinically meaningful, faithful, and actionable.

- **Calibration and Debuggability**
  - **Papers**: NW Head, WASUP
  - **Approaches**: Nonparametric heads, support influence functions, and support-based explanations for better calibration and debugging.

- **Handling Data Limitations and Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Data**
  - **Papers**: PIP-Net, NW Head
  - **Approaches**: Abstention mechanisms, support set distillation, and improved calibration.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Visual Plausibility to Faithfulness**: Early works focused on visually plausible explanations; recent works emphasize faithfulness to model computation and theoretical guarantees.
- **From Local to Global Explanations**: There is a push to provide both local (instance-level) and global (class-level or model-level) explanations (WASUP, XProtoNet, PANIC, PIP-Net).
- **From Rigid to Flexible Prototypes**: Prototype-based models are evolving from fixed, spatially rigid prototypes to flexible, context-aware, and semantically meaningful representations.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From Post-hoc to Inherent Interpretability**: Early reliance on post-hoc explanations (e.g., RISE) is giving way to models that are interpretable by design (B-cos, CoDA-Nets, WASUP).
- **From Rigid to Flexible Prototypes**: Prototype-based models have evolved from fixed, spatially rigid prototypes (ProtoPNet) to deformable (Deformable ProtoPNet), context-aware (XProtoNet), and semantically aligned (PIP-Net) prototypes.
- **From Visual Plausibility to Faithfulness**: There is a shift from visually plausible explanations to those that are provably faithful to the model’s computation (WASUP, B-cos, ProtoPFaith, CoDA-Nets).
- **Integration of Alignment and Prototype Reasoning**: WASUP represents a synthesis, combining weight-input alignment (from B-cos) with support vector/prototype-based classification, and providing both local and global, faithful explanations.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **B-cos as a Foundation**: B-cos transformation is used as a building block in WASUP, enabling weight-input alignment and faithful explanations.
- **Dynamic Alignment Units (DAUs)**: CoDA-Nets introduce DAUs, which are conceptually related to B-cos and further the idea of dynamic, input-dependent alignment.
- **Prototype Models**: ProtoPNet and its variants (ProtoPFaith, Deformable ProtoPNet, XProtoNet, PIP-Net) build upon the idea of case-based reasoning, each addressing limitations of the previous.
- **Support-Based Reasoning**: WASUP and NW Head both leverage support-based classification for interpretability and calibration.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing**: Post-hoc methods (RISE) vs. inherently interpretable models (WASUP, B-cos, CoDA-Nets, prototype-based models).
- **Complementary**: Concept bottlenecking (CBMs) can be combined with alignment or prototype-based methods for richer explanations.
- **Calibration and Debuggability**: Nonparametric heads (NW Head) complement alignment/prototype-based models by improving calibration and providing explicit influence measures.
