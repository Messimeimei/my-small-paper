# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
- The submission is positioned within the *adapter/parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT)* cluster, specifically targeting *incremental/continual learning* in vision (CIFAR-100, ImageNet).
- It proposes using *task-specific adapters* as feature modifiers, co-trained with a backbone, and integrates with regularization-based methods (LwF, EWC).
- The most closely related prior works are:  
  - **Adapter-based continual learning**: InfLoRA (Bhat et al., 2023), Liang & Li (2024), Zhang et al. (2020a), Pham et al. (2021), Wang et al. (2022), Rajasegaran et al. (2020).
  - **Regularization-based methods**: EWC (Kirkpatrick et al., 2017), LwF (Li & Hoiem, 2017), LwM (Dhar et al., 2019), MAS (Aljundi et al., 2018), PathInt (Zenke et al., 2017).
- The submission’s approach is most similar to *adapter-based continual learning* methods that freeze the backbone and only train adapters (e.g., Liang & Li, 2024; Zhang et al., 2020a; InfLoRA), but differs by co-training the backbone and adapters with a regularization term.
- Within the problem space, it addresses *catastrophic forgetting* and the *stability-plasticity dilemma*, aiming for efficient memory usage and compatibility with existing regularization-based approaches.
- Evaluation is standard: classification accuracy on CIFAR-100 and ImageNet, consistent with the field.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is a *modular, parameter-efficient adaptation* method, extending the adapter paradigm by allowing backbone co-training (with regularization) rather than freezing.
- It is not the first to use adapters in continual learning, but claims a novel training regime and integration with regularization-based methods.
- The approach is compatible with both *adapter/PEFT* and *regularization/distillation* clusters, aiming for plug-and-play extensibility.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**
- Authors consistently group prior adapter-based continual learning works (Rajasegaran et al., 2020; Bhat et al., 2023; Pham et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2022; Liang & Li, 2024; Zhang et al., 2020a) as “freezing the backbone” and “relying on custom, complex loss functions.”
- They claim their method differs by *co-training* the backbone and adapters, using a *regularization term* for shared features, and being more compatible with regularization/prediction-based approaches (LwF, EWC).

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The characterization that prior works “freeze the backbone” is accurate for some (Liang & Li, 2024; Zhang et al., 2020a; InfLoRA), but not all. For example, some works (e.g., Rajasegaran et al., 2020; Wang et al., 2022) use prompt-based or meta-learning approaches that may involve more flexible backbone adaptation.
- The claim that prior works “do not incorporate regularization- or prediction-based approaches” is somewhat overstated. Some prior works (e.g., InfLoRA, AdapterFusion) do explore compatibility with regularization or distillation, though perhaps not in the exact form proposed here.
- The authors’ rhetoric sometimes overstates the novelty of “co-training” and “regularization” as unique, when these are common strategies in the field (see EWC, LwF, AdapterFusion).

**Discrepancies:**
- The claim that “none incorporates regularization- or prediction-based approaches” is not fully accurate; some prior works do combine adapters with regularization/distillation, though perhaps not with the same regularization term or in the same architecture.
- The authors understate the flexibility of some prior adapter-based methods, which may allow partial backbone adaptation or more nuanced integration with regularization.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Main Claimed Contributions and Closest Prior Work:**

### (a) **Adapters as Feature Modifiers for Incremental Learning**
- **Closest prior work:** InfLoRA (Bhat et al., 2023), Liang & Li (2024), Zhang et al. (2020a), AdapterFusion.
- **Claimed difference:** Repurposing adapters as feature modifiers, not just for parameter efficiency but for capturing task-specific details in incremental learning.
- **Independent assessment:** Prior works (InfLoRA, AdapterFusion) already use adapters for task-specific adaptation in continual learning. The “feature modifier” framing is a matter of emphasis; the technical novelty is limited unless the adapter design or integration is substantially different (not clear from the summary).
- **Delta:** The main difference is the *co-training* of backbone and adapters, not the use of adapters per se.

### (b) **Co-training Backbone and Adapters (vs. Freezing Backbone)**
- **Closest prior work:** Liang & Li (2024), Zhang et al. (2020a), InfLoRA.
- **Claimed difference:** Prior works freeze the backbone; this work co-trains both, with a regularization term.
- **Independent assessment:** It is accurate that many adapter-based continual learning methods freeze the backbone. Co-training with regularization is a plausible extension, but not a fundamentally new concept—regularization-based co-training is common in the field (e.g., EWC, LwF, AdapterFusion).
- **Delta:** The specific combination of co-training backbone/adapters with a regularization term is a modest extension, not a conceptual leap.

### (c) **Regularization Term for Shared Feature Learning**
- **Closest prior work:** EWC (Kirkpatrick et al., 2017), LwF (Li & Hoiem, 2017), AdapterFusion, InfLoRA.
- **Claimed difference:** Introduces a regularization term to encourage the backbone to learn shared features.
- **Independent assessment:** Regularization to encourage shared features is a standard approach (EWC, LwF, AdapterFusion). The novelty depends on the specific form of the regularization term, which is not detailed here. If it is a standard weight or output regularization, the contribution is incremental.
- **Delta:** Likely a routine adaptation of regularization to the adapter setting.

### (d) **Integration with LwF/EWC and Outperforming Non-adapter Baselines**
- **Closest prior work:** AdapterFusion, InfLoRA, Unified PETL.
- **Claimed difference:** Seamless integration with established regularization-based methods, outperforming non-adapter baselines.
- **Independent assessment:** Many prior works (AdapterFusion, InfLoRA) also demonstrate compatibility with regularization/distillation methods. Outperforming non-adapter baselines is expected for adapter-based methods; the key comparison is with other adapter-based or PEFT methods.
- **Delta:** The integration is not unique; the main difference is the specific co-training/regularization regime.

**Summary:**  
- The *substantive difference* is the co-training of backbone and adapters with a regularization term, rather than freezing the backbone.  
- The *novelty* is incremental, as co-training and regularization are well-established; the specific combination in this context is new but not a conceptual leap.  
- Some author claims about the uniqueness of their approach are overstated; prior works have explored similar directions.

- **Recent surveys:**

- **Trends:**  
  - Increasing use of adapters, prompts, and PEFT for scalable continual learning.
  - Emphasis on modular, plug-and-play architectures.
  - Growing interest in task-agnostic and data-free approaches.
- **Incremental advances:**  
  - Many recent papers propose new combinations or minor extensions of existing techniques (e.g., new regularization terms, adapter variants, integration with existing frameworks).
  - Substantial conceptual advances are rare; most progress is incremental and empirical.
