This paper proposes using different peak and final learning rates for different types of layers in GPT training, across both dense and mixture-of-expert configurations, with the aim of improving training efficiency and transferability of hyperparameter tuning to larger models. However, the overall novelty of the work appears limited, as the idea of tuning learning rates for each component of a neural network is already well established in the literature, particularly in recent muP works [1][2]. While the authors demonstrate practical speedups and provide some analysis of the tuned values, the approach does not significantly advance beyond existing methods. The main contribution seems to be an empirical application of per-layer learning rate schedules, but without a large-scale validation or a deeper theoretical analysis, the work does not clearly differentiate itself from prior art. As such, the paper’s novelty is insufficient to warrant publication in its current form, and it would benefit from a more explicit comparison to existing approaches and stronger evidence justifying the necessity of the proposed final learning rate values.
