This paper introduces ProX, a framework that leverages small language models to refine large-scale pre-training data through programmatic, fine-grained operations on individual examples. The core contribution is a novel chunk-level rewriting technique that moves beyond traditional binary keep/dismiss judgments by enabling deterministic edits to specific chunks of text within documents. This approach represents a significant advance in data curation, as it allows for more nuanced and flexible refinement of pre-training corpora. However, the novelty of ProX is primarily concentrated in this chunk-level refinement; the document-level component largely adopts existing filtering techniques from prior work, such as those used in FineWeb-edu, and thus offers limited innovation. The distinction between the novel chunk-level method and the more conventional document-level filtering could be more clearly articulated. Overall, while the chunk-level rewriting presents a meaningful and original contribution, the paper’s novelty is somewhat diminished by its reliance on established document-level approaches, and would benefit from a more explicit differentiation between these components.
