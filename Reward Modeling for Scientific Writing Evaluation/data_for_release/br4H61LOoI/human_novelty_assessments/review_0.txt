This paper introduces a new evaluation paradigm for large language models (LLMs) that emphasizes fine-grained, solution-driven assessment over traditional answer correctness, focusing on mathematical reasoning tasks using an extended GSM8k dataset. While the authors present three types of question transformations in their MR-GSM8k benchmark—sampling from GSM8k, modifying problems to require code in solutions, and applying “reversed reasoning”—each of these approaches has been previously suggested and studied in the literature. As such, the novelty or added value of the proposed benchmark is unclear, particularly regarding the data generation process. Furthermore, the benchmarking-transformation techniques employed, such as POT and reverse reasoning, have already appeared in prior work, making the overall contribution appear only marginally novel. If the authors intend to claim a new, generalizable method for transforming any benchmark, this is not convincingly demonstrated, as the current work is limited to a math benchmark and does not clearly articulate what distinguishes their approach from existing methods. The paper would benefit from providing more explicit information about the unique aspects of their data generation and transformation process to better establish its novelty.
