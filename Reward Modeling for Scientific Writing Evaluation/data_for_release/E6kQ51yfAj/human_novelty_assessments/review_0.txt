This paper introduces a framework that formulates the alignment problem as a two-player zero-sum game, involving an adversarial agent that generates challenging prompts and a defensive agent that adapts its responses to improve the performance of large language models. While the approach is well-executed and demonstrates effectiveness, particularly in safety-related tasks, its novelty is limited. The two-player game framework, though effective in this context, represents more of an incremental improvement rather than a significant innovation, as similar adversarial setups have been explored in related domains. Additionally, the inclusion of an extra diversity reward is a common technique in multi-agent settings and appears to be a practical trick rather than a substantive methodological contribution. Overall, the paper’s contributions are solid in terms of implementation and empirical results, but the methodological advances are relatively straightforward and do not substantially push the boundaries of existing work.
