## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the dominant methodological theme is **parameter-efficient adaptation and editing of large-scale models** (both language and vision), with a focus on *delta parameter editing*, *pruning*, *quantization*, *model merging*, and *representation editing*. The methods can be grouped as follows:

#### **Cluster 1: Delta Parameter Editing and Pruning**
- **DARE** and its variants (DAREx-q, DAREx-L2): Randomly drop and rescale delta parameters to sparsify fine-tuned models, with improvements for high pruning rates and large deltas.
- **BitDelta**: Quantizes delta parameters to 1 bit plus a scale, enabling extreme compression.
- **Importance-based DPP (e.g., MAGPRUNE, WANDA)**: Prune deltas based on magnitude or other importance criteria.
- **Unified Framework (Submission Paper)**: Theoretically unifies and extends delta parameter editing methods using Riemann sum approximations, categorizing and generalizing existing approaches.

#### **Cluster 2: Model Merging and Interference Mitigation**
- **DARE-based Merging**: Uses sparsified deltas to merge multiple SFT models.
- **DELLA-Merging**: Magnitude-based sampling and rescaling to reduce interference in merged models.
- **TIES-Merging**: Trimming, sign election, and selective merging to resolve parameter interference.
- **Task Arithmetic, Parameter Averaging**: Baseline merging techniques.

#### **Cluster 3: Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)**
- **LoRA**: Low-rank adaptation by injecting trainable matrices.
- **RED (Representation Editing)**: Edits internal representations via scaling/biasing, not weights.
- **Adapter-based/Partial/Unified PEFT**: Surveyed and benchmarked in vision and language domains.
- **KAdaptation, AdaptFormer**: Vision-specific PEFT modules (Kronecker decomposition, lightweight adapters).

#### **Cluster 4: Delta Tuning and Theoretical Frameworks**
- **Delta Tuning**: Categorizes and analyzes parameter-efficient adaptation methods (addition, specification, reparameterization).
- **Unified Theoretical Frameworks**: Optimization and control perspectives (Delta Tuning paper), Riemann sum approximation (Submission Paper).

#### **Cluster 5: Extrapolation/Interpolation**
- **ExPO**: Model extrapolation using first-order approximations to improve alignment.
- **Submission Paper**: Generalizes extrapolation/interpolation in delta editing via theoretical analysis.

#### **Cluster 6: Test-Time Adaptation**
- **DELTA**: Test-time adaptation using batch renormalization and dynamic reweighting.

### Methodological Trends and Patterns

- **Unification and Theoretical Analysis**: Recent works (Submission, Delta Tuning) seek to provide unified frameworks, moving from empirical heuristics to principled understanding.
- **Extreme Compression**: Methods like BitDelta and DARE variants push the limits of how much delta information can be compressed or pruned while retaining performance.
- **Interference Mitigation in Merging**: As model merging becomes more common, methods increasingly address parameter interference (DELLA, TIES).
- **Cross-Domain Generalization**: Techniques originally for NLP (e.g., LoRA) are adapted and benchmarked for vision models.
- **Shift from Weight to Representation Editing**: RED and similar methods focus on editing internal activations rather than weights.

### Relationships Between Approaches

- **DARE, BitDelta, and ExPO** are all delta parameter editing methods, now unified and extended in the Submission Paper.
- **Model merging methods** (DARE, DELLA, TIES) build on delta editing/pruning to enable multi-task or multi-domain models.
- **PEFT methods** (LoRA, RED, adapters) are often the basis for delta parameter editing and merging.
- **Theoretical frameworks** (Submission, Delta Tuning) provide the foundation for understanding and improving practical methods.

## 2. PROBLEM SPACE MAPPING

### Key Problems Addressed

1. **Parameter-Efficient Adaptation**: How to adapt large pre-trained models to new tasks with minimal trainable parameters (LoRA, RED, KAdaptation, AdaptFormer, PEFT survey).
2. **Delta Parameter Editing**: How to efficiently store, compress, or prune the changes (deltas) induced by fine-tuning (DARE, BitDelta, DPP, Submission).
3. **Model Merging**: How to combine multiple fine-tuned models into one, minimizing interference and maximizing multitask performance (DARE merging, DELLA, TIES).
4. **Theoretical Understanding**: How to systematically explain and categorize delta parameter editing/adaptation methods (Submission, Delta Tuning).
5. **Test-Time Adaptation**: How to adapt models to distribution shifts at inference time without retraining (DELTA).
6. **Alignment and Extrapolation**: How to improve model alignment with human preferences efficiently (ExPO).

### Approaches to Similar Problems

- **Parameter Efficiency**: LoRA, RED, and KAdaptation all reduce trainable parameters but differ in mechanism (low-rank, representation, Kronecker).
- **Delta Compression/Pruning**: DARE and BitDelta both target storage/memory efficiency, but DARE uses sparsification while BitDelta uses quantization.
- **Model Merging**: DARE, DELLA, and TIES all address merging, but DELLA and TIES introduce more sophisticated interference mitigation.
- **Theoretical Unification**: Submission and Delta Tuning both provide frameworks, but Submission uses Riemann sum approximation, while Delta Tuning uses optimization/control theory.

### Patterns in Problem Formulation

- **From Empirical to Theoretical**: Early works focus on empirical gains; recent works seek principled explanations and generalizations.
- **From Single-Task to Multi-Task**: Increasing focus on merging and multi-task adaptation.
- **From Weight to Representation**: Some methods shift the focus from weight editing to representation editing for efficiency and simplicity.

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **Language**: GLUE, SST2, COLA, AlpacaEval, GSM8K, MBPP, Open LLM Leaderboard, 100+ NLP tasks (Delta Tuning).
- **Vision**: ViT, CLIP, Something-Something v2, HMDB51, CIFAR100-C, various image classification datasets.
- **Multi-Modal**: Some merging and adaptation methods are evaluated on both language and vision tasks.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Performance**: Accuracy, F1, win rate, task-specific metrics.
- **Parameter Efficiency**: Number of trainable parameters, storage size, memory usage.
- **Computation/Serving**: GPU memory, serving latency, throughput.
- **Generalization**: In-domain and out-of-domain performance, multitask/merged model performance.

### Patterns in Performance Measurement

- **Relative to Full Fine-Tuning**: Most methods are compared to full fine-tuning as a baseline.
- **Parameter/Storage Reduction**: Quantitative reporting of parameter or storage reduction (e.g., 10,000x, 25,700x).
- **Merged Model Performance**: For merging methods, performance is measured both on individual tasks and aggregate benchmarks.
- **Compression/Pruning Impact**: Methods like DARE and BitDelta report performance drop (or lack thereof) after extreme compression/pruning.

### Comparison of Evaluation Approaches

- **Comprehensive Benchmarks**: Delta Tuning and KAdaptation papers use large-scale, multi-task benchmarks.
- **Task-Specific**: Some works focus on a few representative datasets (e.g., COLA, SST2 for DARE variants).
- **Efficiency-Performance Tradeoff**: Many works introduce new metrics (e.g., performance-efficiency) to capture tradeoffs.

## 4. RESEARCH CLUSTERS

### Cluster 1: **Delta Parameter Editing, Compression, and Pruning**
- **Papers**: Submission, DARE the Extreme, BitDelta, DARE (Super Mario), DELLA-Merging
- **Characteristics**: Focus on editing, compressing, or pruning delta parameters for storage, efficiency, and merging. Methods are often evaluated on both language and vision models.
- **Relationships**: Submission unifies and extends DARE, BitDelta, and EXPO; DELLA and DARE are compared and sometimes combined.

### Cluster 2: **Model Merging and Interference Mitigation**
- **Papers**: DELLA-Merging, TIES-Merging, DARE (Super Mario), Submission (via extensions to merging methods)
- **Characteristics**: Address merging of multiple fine-tuned models, with a focus on reducing parameter interference and maximizing multitask performance.
- **Relationships**: DELLA and TIES build on DARE, introducing more sophisticated selection and conflict resolution.

### Cluster 3: **Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)**
- **Papers**: LoRA, RED, AdaptFormer, KAdaptation, PEFT Survey, Delta Tuning, Parameter-Efficient Model Adaptation for ViTs
- **Characteristics**: Develop and benchmark methods for adapting large models with minimal trainable parameters, across both NLP and vision.
- **Relationships**: LoRA is foundational; RED and KAdaptation offer alternative mechanisms; PEFT survey and Delta Tuning provide taxonomy and theoretical analysis.

### Cluster 4: **Theoretical Frameworks and Unification**
- **Papers**: Submission, Delta Tuning, PEFT Survey
- **Characteristics**: Provide formal frameworks, taxonomies, and theoretical explanations for the empirical success of delta editing and PEFT methods.
- **Relationships**: Submission and Delta Tuning both seek to unify and explain the landscape, with different mathematical tools.

### Cluster 5: **Alignment and Extrapolation**
- **Papers**: ExPO, Submission (via generalization of EXPO)
- **Characteristics**: Use extrapolation/interpolation of model weights to improve alignment or compensate for insufficient training.
- **Relationships**: Submission generalizes EXPO's approach within its unified framework.

### Cluster 6: **Test-Time Adaptation**
- **Papers**: DELTA
- **Characteristics**: Focus on adapting models at inference time to distribution shifts, using normalization and reweighting.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Empirical PEFT to Theoretical Unification**: Early works (LoRA, DARE) introduced practical methods for parameter efficiency and delta editing. Recent works (Submission, Delta Tuning) provide theoretical frameworks that explain, categorize, and generalize these methods.
- **From Simple Pruning to Sophisticated Selection**: DARE started with random drop and rescale; later works (DAREx-q, DELLA, TIES) introduce importance-based selection, sign resolution, and adaptive rescaling.
- **From Weight Editing to Representation Editing**: RED and similar methods shift the focus from weight to representation, simplifying adaptation and hyperparameter selection.
- **From Single-Task to Multi-Task and Merging**: As the number of fine-tuned models grows, research shifts to merging and multi-task adaptation, with increasing attention to parameter interference.
- **From Compression to Extreme Quantization**: BitDelta demonstrates that even 1-bit quantization of deltas can suffice, pushing the limits of model compression.
- **From Vision to Language and Back**: Techniques are increasingly cross-pollinated between NLP and vision (e.g., LoRA, adapters, KAdaptation).

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **DARE** is extended by DAREx-q, DAREx-L2, and forms the basis for DELLA and TIES.
- **LoRA** is adapted for vision (KAdaptation, AdaptFormer) and forms a baseline for many PEFT studies.
- **BitDelta** generalizes delta quantization and is extended in the Submission Paper.
- **EXPO** is generalized in the Submission Paper to a broader class of extrapolation/interpolation methods.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Random vs. Importance-Based Pruning**: DARE (random) vs. DELLA/MAGPRUNE (magnitude-based) vs. TIES (sign-based).
- **Weight vs. Representation Editing**: LoRA/DARE (weight) vs. RED (representation).
- **Compression vs. Merging**: BitDelta/DARE (compression) vs. DELLA/TIES (merging).
- **Theoretical vs. Empirical**: Submission and Delta Tuning (theoretical) complement empirical advances.

# **Conclusion**

The research landscape is rapidly evolving from practical, empirical methods for parameter-efficient adaptation and delta editing toward unified, theoretically grounded frameworks. The Submission Paper is at the forefront of this trend, providing a principled, generalizable view that connects and extends a wide array of delta parameter editing, pruning, quantization, and merging methods. The field is also seeing increasing sophistication in model merging and interference mitigation, as well as cross-domain generalization of techniques between NLP and vision. Theoretical unification is enabling more systematic design and analysis of new methods, setting the stage for further innovation in efficient, scalable model adaptation and deployment.
