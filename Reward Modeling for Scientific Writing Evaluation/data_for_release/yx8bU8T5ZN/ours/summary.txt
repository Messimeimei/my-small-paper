This assessment reviews a submission that proposes a Riemann sum-based theoretical framework to unify and extend delta parameter editing methods for post-trained large-scale models in both vision and language domains. The main novelty lies in using Riemann sum approximations to categorize and predict the effects of delta editing techniques (e.g., DARE, BitDelta, EXPO), which is a new analytical lens compared to prior frameworks like Delta Tuning that use optimization/control theory. While the submission’s theoretical unification and categorization are substantive contributions, its claims of being the first to unify or categorize delta editing methods are somewhat overstated, as recent works (notably Delta Tuning) have also provided unifying frameworks, albeit with different mathematical tools. Extensions to existing methods (DARE, BitDelta) may be routine unless accompanied by strong theoretical or empirical justification, and the submission does not fully acknowledge the sophistication or theoretical advances of some prior work. Reviewers should recognize the novelty of the Riemann sum approach and its predictive categorization, but also critically assess the incremental nature of some extensions and the incomplete engagement with related literature.
