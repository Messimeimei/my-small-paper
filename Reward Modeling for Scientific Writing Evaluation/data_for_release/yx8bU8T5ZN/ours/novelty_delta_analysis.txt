# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission, "A Unified View of Delta Parameter Editing in Post-Trained Large-Scale Models," positions itself as a theoretical unification and extension of delta parameter editing methods, specifically targeting post-trained large-scale models in both vision (ViT) and language (LLaMA 3, Mistral) domains. The core novelty claim is a Riemann sum-based theoretical framework that categorizes and generalizes existing delta parameter editing techniques (e.g., DARE, BitDelta, EXPO).

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **DARE** and its variants (random drop and rescale of delta parameters)
- **BitDelta** (quantization of delta parameters)
- **EXPO** (extrapolation/interpolation of delta parameters)
- **DELLA-Merging, TIES-Merging** (extensions for model merging)
- **Delta Tuning** (theoretical taxonomy of parameter-efficient methods)
- **Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) Survey** (taxonomy and benchmarking)

**Independent Assessment of Methodological Clusters:**
The submission is most closely aligned with the "Delta Parameter Editing, Compression, and Pruning" cluster, but also touches on "Theoretical Frameworks and Unification" and, to a lesser extent, "Model Merging." Its main contribution is theoretical: providing a unified lens (Riemann sum approximation) to explain, categorize, and extend delta parameter editing methods.

**Place in Problem Space and Evaluation:**
The submission addresses the lack of a unified theoretical understanding of delta parameter editing, aiming to explain and predict the effects of various editing operations on model performance. It evaluates its framework on both vision and language models, using standard post-training adaptation metrics (accuracy, loss).

**Verification of Positioning:**
While the authors claim a unique unification, the "Delta Tuning" paper and recent surveys also attempt to provide theoretical frameworks, though with different mathematical tools (optimization/control theory vs. Riemann sum). The submission's focus on the Riemann sum as a unifying principle appears novel within the surveyed literature, but the general goal of unification is shared with other recent works.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Characterization:**
- Authors consistently cite prior delta editing methods (DARE, BitDelta, EXPO) as isolated or heuristic, positioning their work as providing the first unified theoretical explanation.
- Merging methods (DELLA, TIES) are described as inspired by or extending DARE, with the submission claiming to generalize these within its framework.
- The PEFT and adaptation literature (LoRA, RED, KAdaptation, PEFT Survey) is cited to establish the importance of parameter-efficient adaptation, but not as direct competitors.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The characterization of DARE as random drop and rescale is accurate, as is BitDelta's focus on 1-bit quantization.
- The description of DELLA-Merging as "magnitude-aware drop" is correct, but the submission does not deeply engage with the technical details of DELLA's interference mitigation, which may understate its sophistication.
- EXPO is described as using extrapolation to enhance performance, which matches the original work.
- The PEFT survey and Delta Tuning are cited for context, but the submission does not fully acknowledge that Delta Tuning also provides a unifying theoretical framework (albeit with different mathematics).

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The submission sometimes frames prior work as lacking theoretical explanation, which is only partially accurate: Delta Tuning and some surveys do provide theoretical perspectives, though not via Riemann sums.
- The claim that the submission is the "first" to systematically categorize delta editing methods may be overstated, given the existence of Delta Tuning's taxonomy.
- The extensions to DARE and BitDelta are described as generalizations, but the technical novelty of these extensions (beyond parameterization tweaks) is not always clear from the citation context.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

## Claimed Contribution 1: **Unified Theoretical Framework (Riemann Sum Approximation)**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Delta Tuning (theoretical taxonomy), PEFT Survey (categorization)
- **Claimed Difference:** Submission uses Riemann sum approximation to unify and explain delta parameter editing methods, categorizing them by their effect on model performance.
- **Independent Assessment:** Delta Tuning provides a theoretical framework (optimization/control theory) but does not use Riemann sums or focus on the specific categorization (competitive, decreased, improved) based on approximation terms. The Riemann sum approach appears novel in this context.
- **Substantive vs. Superficial:** The mathematical tool (Riemann sum) is new, but the general goal of unification is shared with Delta Tuning. The substantive difference is the specific analytical lens, not the overall aim.

## Claimed Contribution 2: **Systematic Categorization of Delta Editing Methods**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Delta Tuning, PEFT Survey
- **Claimed Difference:** Submission categorizes methods based on the sign and magnitude of the Riemann sum term, predicting performance impact.
- **Independent Assessment:** Delta Tuning categorizes methods by parameterization and adaptation mechanism, not by theoretical performance impact. The submission's categorization is more tightly linked to theoretical predictions, which is a substantive difference.

## Claimed Contribution 3: **Extensions to DARE (Dropped Parameter Factor)**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** DARE, DELLA-Merging
- **Claimed Difference:** Adds a factor for dropped parameters to DARE, generalizing its applicability.
- **Independent Assessment:** DELLA-Merging already introduces magnitude-based dropout, which is a more sophisticated selection than random drop. The submission's extension appears to be a parameterization tweak rather than a conceptual advance, unless the new factor is theoretically justified and empirically validated as superior.

## Claimed Contribution 4: **Extensions to BitDelta (Generalized Quantification)**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** BitDelta
- **Claimed Difference:** Generalizes quantification beyond average sum.
- **Independent Assessment:** BitDelta focuses on 1-bit quantization with a global scale. If the submission introduces a theoretically motivated, more flexible quantification scheme, this could be substantive, but the technical details are needed to assess whether this is a routine extension or a genuine advance.

## Claimed Contribution 5: **Generalized Extrapolation/Interpolation (EXPO-like Methods)**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** EXPO
- **Claimed Difference:** Provides a theoretical basis for when to use extrapolation or interpolation, based on the direction of the approximation term.
- **Independent Assessment:** EXPO empirically explores extrapolation; the submission's theoretical guidance is a new angle. This appears to be a substantive difference, provided the theory is sound and predictive.

# 4. FIELD CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

**Field Maturity and Activity:**
- The field of parameter-efficient adaptation and delta parameter editing is highly active, with rapid evolution from empirical methods (LoRA, DARE) to theoretical frameworks (Delta Tuning, Submission).
- Recent survey papers: "Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning for Pre-Trained Vision Models: A Survey" (Han et al., 2024), "Delta Tuning: A Comprehensive Study..." (2024).
- The trend is toward unification, theoretical explanation, and cross-domain generalization (NLP ↔ vision).

**Typical Advances:**
- Many recent papers offer incremental improvements (e.g., new pruning criteria, quantization schemes, merging heuristics).
- Theoretical unification is less common and more valued, but multiple groups are now pursuing this (e.g., Delta Tuning, Submission).

**Reviewer Calibration:**
- Reviewers should expect both incremental and conceptual advances; theoretical frameworks are increasingly common, so claims of "first unification" should be scrutinized.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Overstated Novelty:** The claim of being the first to unify/categorize delta parameter editing is somewhat overstated, as Delta Tuning and recent surveys also provide unification, though with different tools.
- **Empirical Improvements:** For extensions to DARE and BitDelta, improvements may result from implementation details (e.g., new scaling factors) rather than conceptual breakthroughs.
- **Terminology Differences:** The submission's use of Riemann sum terminology may obscure similarities to prior theoretical frameworks (e.g., optimization/control in Delta Tuning).
- **Routine Extensions:** Adding a dropped parameter factor to DARE or generalizing quantification in BitDelta may be routine adaptations unless accompanied by strong theoretical or empirical justification.
- **Understated Prior Work:** The submission does not fully acknowledge the theoretical contributions of Delta Tuning, which may mislead readers about the state of the art.
