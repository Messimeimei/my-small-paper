# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Landscape Placement:**  
The submission is situated within the "Reasoning Type Isolation and Cognitive Diagnosis" cluster, which focuses on disentangling and evaluating specific reasoning abilities (inductive vs. deductive) in LLMs. This cluster is characterized by the use of controlled, often synthetic, datasets and experimental setups designed to minimize confounding factors such as world knowledge or memorization.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**  
- **Language Models as Inductive Reasoners** (Yang et al., 2022; Gendron et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2023b): These works probe LLMs' inductive reasoning, often via Input-Output (IO) prompting.
- **Phenomenal Yet Puzzling: Testing Inductive Reasoning Capabilities of Language Models with Hypothesis Refinement**: Focuses on hypothesis refinement as a means to test inductive reasoning.
- **Logic-LM: Empowering Large Language Models with Symbolic Solvers for Faithful Logical Reasoning** (Pan et al., 2023): Integrates LLMs with symbolic solvers, focusing on deductive reasoning.
- **Deductive Verification of Chain-of-Thought Reasoning** and **Deductive Beam Search**: Emphasize stepwise verification and decomposition of deductive reasoning.

**Methodological Clusters:**  
The submission's SolverLearner framework is most closely aligned with Cluster 1 (Reasoning Type Isolation), but also draws on prompt engineering strategies from Cluster 3. Unlike neuro-symbolic or verification-focused works (Clusters 2 and 4), the submission does not integrate symbolic solvers or stepwise verification, but rather aims to *isolate* reasoning types through prompt and task design.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**  
The submission addresses the challenge of *disentangling* inductive from deductive reasoning in LLMs, a problem also tackled by recent works using synthetic datasets and controlled prompts. Its evaluation approach—custom arithmetic tasks with accuracy as the main metric—is consistent with the field's trend toward content-neutral, controlled evaluation, but is narrower than some related works that include explanation quality or error analysis.

**Independent Assessment:**  
- The submission's main novelty is the explicit *isolation* of inductive reasoning via the SolverLearner framework, rather than the introduction of new reasoning capabilities or integration with symbolic systems.
- The approach is an incremental methodological advance within a rapidly evolving area, building on and refining prior prompt-based and dataset-driven evaluation strategies.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Citation Patterns and Characterizations:**

- **Large Language Models are In-Context Semantic Reasoners rather than Symbolic Reasoners**  
  - *Author Characterization:* Cited as evidence that direct comparisons of reasoning types are challenging.
  - *Assessment:* Accurate; these works highlight the difficulty of mapping LLM behavior to classical reasoning categories.

- **Language Models as Inductive Reasoners**  
  - *Author Characterization:* Prior studies "primarily used Input-Output (IO) prompting," which "does not provide the underlying mapping function."
  - *Assessment:* Largely accurate—these works do focus on IO prompting. However, some (e.g., Gendron et al., 2023) also discuss limitations and possible extensions, which the submission does not fully acknowledge.

- **Logic-LM: Empowering Large Language Models with Symbolic Solvers for Faithful Logical Reasoning**  
  - *Author Characterization:* Focuses "exclusively on deductive reasoning," without "incorporating the capacity for inductive reasoning."
  - *Assessment:* This is a fair summary; Logic-LM and similar works are indeed focused on deductive tasks.

**Patterns:**  
- The authors consistently position their work as filling a gap left by prior studies: previous works either do not isolate inductive reasoning or focus only on deductive reasoning.
- There is a tendency to frame prior work as limited in scope (e.g., "primarily used IO prompting," "focus exclusively on deductive reasoning"), which may understate the breadth of some related studies.

**Discrepancies:**  
- The submission does not cite several recent works that also attempt to isolate or probe inductive reasoning (see Section 6), which may lead to an incomplete picture of the state of the art.
- The characterization of IO prompting as insufficient for isolating inductive reasoning is reasonable, but the submission does not fully engage with recent advances in prompt engineering or hypothesis refinement that address similar goals.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claimed Contribution 1:**  
*Proposes SolverLearner, a novel framework that isolates and evaluates the pure inductive reasoning capabilities of LLMs, separating them from deductive reasoning.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - "Language Models as Inductive Reasoners" (Yang et al., 2022; Gendron et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2023b)
  - "Phenomenal Yet Puzzling: Testing Inductive Reasoning Capabilities of Language Models with Hypothesis Refinement"
  - "Hypothesis Search: Inductive Reasoning with Language Models"

- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Prior works use IO prompting, which does not isolate inductive reasoning; SolverLearner provides a framework for "pure" inductive reasoning.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - IO prompting is indeed limited in isolating inductive reasoning, as it may conflate pattern recognition with true induction.
  - However, recent works (e.g., "Phenomenal Yet Puzzling," "Hypothesis Search") have introduced more sophisticated setups (e.g., hypothesis refinement, search) to better probe inductive reasoning. The submission does not compare against these, nor does it clarify how SolverLearner's isolation is more rigorous.
  - The main technical delta appears to be the *explicit experimental separation* of reasoning types, rather than a fundamentally new method for eliciting induction.

- **Substantive vs. Superficial:**  
  - The contribution is substantive in terms of experimental design, but may be incremental relative to recent advances in prompt engineering and task design.

**Claimed Contribution 2:**  
*Systematically disentangles inductive from deductive reasoning in LLMs using a consistent experimental setup.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - "Phenomenal Yet Puzzling," "Deductive Verification of Chain-of-Thought Reasoning," "Deductive Beam Search," "Hypothesis Search"

- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Prior works do not provide a systematic, side-by-side comparison of inductive and deductive reasoning.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Some prior works do attempt to disentangle reasoning types, but often focus on one or the other, or use different datasets for each. The submission's use of a unified experimental setup is a genuine, if incremental, methodological advance.
  - However, the submission does not benchmark against the most recent or sophisticated approaches, nor does it address whether its framework could be adapted from or to those methods.

**Claimed Contribution 3:**  
*Finds that LLMs have strong inductive reasoning abilities (near-perfect accuracy) but relatively weak deductive reasoning, especially in counterfactual tasks.*

- **Most Similar Prior Work:**  
  - "Language Models as Inductive Reasoners," "Phenomenal Yet Puzzling," "Deductive Verification of Chain-of-Thought Reasoning"

- **Claimed Difference:**  
  - Previous underperformance in inductive tasks may be due to poor deductive execution, not lack of inductive ability.

- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - This is a plausible and interesting empirical finding, but its novelty depends on the rigor of the experimental isolation and the breadth of tasks considered.
  - Prior works have noted similar patterns (e.g., LLMs struggle with counterfactuals and logical deduction), though perhaps not with the same experimental clarity.

- **Implementation vs. Conceptual Advance:**  
  - The improvement in measured inductive ability may be due to better experimental control rather than a new reasoning capability in LLMs.

- Many recent papers introduce new frameworks or datasets that provide incremental improvements in isolating or evaluating reasoning types.

# 5. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT CONSIDERATIONS

- **Overstated Novelty:**  
  - The claim of being the first to "isolate pure inductive reasoning" may be overstated, as recent works have made similar attempts using different methodologies (e.g., hypothesis refinement, search).
  - The submission does not engage with the full breadth of recent related work, particularly those that also attempt to disentangle reasoning types.

- **Empirical Improvements:**  
  - The observed improvement in inductive reasoning accuracy may be due to the experimental setup (e.g., task design, prompt format) rather than a new insight into LLM capabilities.

- **Terminology Differences:**  
  - The distinction between "IO prompting" and "SolverLearner" may be partly terminological; both are prompt-based frameworks, though SolverLearner claims more rigorous isolation.

- **Routine Adaptations:**  
  - The systematic comparison of reasoning types, while valuable, may be a routine extension of existing prompt engineering and evaluation strategies.

**In summary:**  
The submission provides a useful, incremental methodological advance in the experimental isolation of inductive and deductive reasoning in LLMs. Its main novelty lies in the systematic, side-by-side comparison enabled by the SolverLearner framework. However, the degree of novelty is somewhat overstated, as several recent works have addressed similar goals using alternative methods. The submission would benefit from a more comprehensive engagement with the latest literature and a more nuanced characterization of prior work. The observed empirical findings are interesting, but may be attributable to improved experimental control rather than a fundamentally new insight into LLM reasoning.
