This paper introduces JOSH, a self-training framework designed to enable agentic models to achieve self-alignment through a data rollout pipeline that leverages agent interactions with a GPT-based simulator to generate multi-turn, tool-calling conversations. A key feature of the approach is the use of beam search to construct a tree-structured trajectory from which supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and preference data are extracted for further model training. However, the novelty of the proposed framework is limited, as it primarily builds upon concepts and techniques that have been extensively studied within the community. Specifically, data rollouts, beam search, and supervised or preference-based fine-tuning are all well-established methods in prior work. As such, while the integration of these components is competently executed, the framework does not introduce fundamentally new ideas or approaches, and its contribution is incremental rather than groundbreaking. The paper would benefit from a clearer articulation of how JOSH differentiates itself from existing methods, particularly in light of the substantial overlap with previously explored techniques.
