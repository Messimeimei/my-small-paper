## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression of Ideas

- **From Supervised to Self-Supervised NCO:** Early works (e.g., JSP-DNN, pointer networks) required labeled data; later works (SLIM, SIL, Self-Improvement, submission) eliminate this need via self-labeling or iterative improvement.
- **From Generic to Structured Architectures:** There is a clear trend toward architectures that encode problem structure (e.g., job/machine layers, GNNs for disjunctive graphs, bisimulation quotienting).
- **From Deterministic to Fuzzy/Uncertain Scheduling:** The field is moving toward handling real-world uncertainty, as seen in the submission and related fuzzy scheduling works.
- **From Small to Large-Scale Problems:** Scalability is a major focus, with methods like SIL, LEHD, and BQ-NCO enabling NCO on problems with thousands to hundreds of thousands of nodes.
- **From Solution Construction to Improvement:** There is a growing interest in learning to improve existing solutions (local search, improvement heuristics) rather than only constructing solutions from scratch.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Pseudo-Labeling and Iterative Refinement:** The submission extends self-labeling (SLIM) and self-improvement (SIL, Self-Improvement for NCO) to the fuzzy scheduling domain, introducing iterative pseudo-label refinement for FJSSP.
- **Hybridization:** Fuzzy scheduling works combine learning-based and evolutionary/fuzzy methods, showing complementarity.
- **Symmetry and Constraint Exploitation:** Methods like BQ-NCO and Sym-NCO exploit problem symmetries for efficiency and generalization.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Supervised vs. Self-Supervised:** Self-supervised/self-improvement methods compete with supervised approaches by removing the need for expert solutions, often matching or exceeding their performance.
- **Learning-Based vs. Traditional Heuristics:** Learning-based methods increasingly outperform traditional heuristics in both quality and speed, especially as architectures and training strategies become more sophisticated.
- **Domain-Specific vs. General NCO:** Some methods are highly specialized (e.g., for FJSSP, picker routing), while others (e.g., BQ-NCO, LEHD) aim for generality across COPs.
