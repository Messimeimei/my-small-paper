# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating the Submission:**
The submission introduces "Temporally Augmented Patient Contrastive Learning" (TA-PCLR) for ECG foundation models, combining patient-based contrastive learning with temporal augmentations (zero-masking, random cropping) and pretraining on a new, very large, multi-center ECG dataset. This places the work at the intersection of three major methodological clusters:
- **Patient-based contrastive learning** (e.g., PCLR, Diamant et al. 2022)
- **Physiologically-inspired/temporal augmentations** (e.g., 3KG, Gopal et al. 2021; Soltanieh et al. 2022)
- **Large-scale foundation model pretraining** (e.g., Song et al. 2024; Vaid et al. 2023; Strodthoff et al. 2021)

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- **Patient-based contrastive learning:** Diamant et al. (2022, PCLR)
- **Temporal augmentations in contrastive learning:** Gopal et al. (2021, 3KG); Soltanieh et al. (2022)
- **Foundation models for ECG:** Song et al. (2024, ExChanGeAI); Vaid et al. (2023, HeartBeiT); Strodthoff et al. (2021)
- **Augmentation analysis:** Soltanieh et al. (2022); Raghu et al. (2022)
- **Supervised deep learning benchmarks:** Strodthoff et al. (2021); Ribeiro et al. (2020)

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- The submission is a direct extension of patient-based contrastive learning, adding temporal augmentations (already explored in other contexts, but not always combined with patient identity).
- It fits within the trend of scaling up foundation models with large, diverse datasets.
- The approach is not the first to use temporal augmentations or patient-based contrastive learning, but claims novelty in their specific combination and scale.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses label scarcity, generalization, and robust representation learning—core problems in the field.
- Evaluation follows standard practice: downstream task performance, external validation, and comparison to both self-supervised and supervised baselines.

**Independent Assessment:**
- The submission is an incremental but potentially meaningful extension of established methods, combining two previously explored ideas (patient-based contrastive learning and temporal augmentations) at a new scale.
- The field is mature and competitive, with rapid progress in both methodological sophistication and dataset/model scale.

# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors consistently position their work as an improvement over prior patient-based contrastive learning (Diamant et al. 2022) by adding temporal augmentations.
- They cite augmentation analysis works (Gopal et al. 2021; Soltanieh et al. 2022) to justify their choice of augmentations.
- Foundation model works (Song et al. 2024; Vaid et al. 2023; Strodthoff et al. 2021) are cited as benchmarks for scale and generalization.
- Supervised benchmarks (Strodthoff et al. 2021; Ribeiro et al. 2020) are used to contextualize performance.

**Accuracy and Balance:**
- The authors generally describe prior work accurately, but sometimes understate the sophistication of augmentations or the scale of previous foundation models.
- For example, they claim to "improve on existing patient contrastive methods, by adding temporal augmentation like zero-masking and random cropping," but Gopal et al. (2021) and Soltanieh et al. (2022) already explored similar augmentations, albeit not always in combination with patient identity.
- The claim that their model "outperforms other, much larger, self-supervised foundation models" (Song et al. 2024) is supported by reported metrics, but the margin and generality of this improvement are not always clear.

**Discrepancies:**
- The distinction between "patient-based contrastive learning" and "temporal augmentations" is sometimes presented as more novel than it is; prior works have explored both, though perhaps not in the exact combination or at this scale.
- The authors refrain from certain augmentations (scaling, rotating, frequency-warping) for generalization, citing Raghu et al. (2022), but do not fully acknowledge that other works have also considered the impact of augmentation choice on generalization.

**Potential Overstatement:**
- The rhetoric sometimes implies a larger conceptual leap than is independently evident; the main novelty is in the combination and scale, not in fundamentally new techniques.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Contribution 1: TA-PCLR (Patient-based contrastive learning + temporal augmentations)**
- **Most similar prior work:** Diamant et al. (2022, PCLR); Gopal et al. (2021, 3KG); Soltanieh et al. (2022)
- **Claimed difference:** First to combine patient-based contrastive learning with temporal augmentations for ECGs.
- **Independent assessment:** 
    - Diamant et al. (2022) used patient identity for contrastive learning but did not systematically explore temporal augmentations.
    - Gopal et al. (2021) and Soltanieh et al. (2022) explored temporal augmentations (zero-masking, cropping) in contrastive learning, but not always with patient identity as the anchor.
    - The combination is a logical extension, but not a conceptual leap; it is an incremental advance.
    - The novelty is primarily in the integration and empirical demonstration at scale.

**Contribution 2: Large-scale, multi-center ECG dataset**
- **Most similar prior work:** Song et al. (2024, ExChanGeAI); Strodthoff et al. (2021); Vaid et al. (2023)
- **Claimed difference:** New dataset of over six million ECGs from three continents.
- **Independent assessment:** 
    - Song et al. (2024) used over a million ECGs; Strodthoff et al. (2021) and Vaid et al. (2023) also used large, multi-center datasets.
    - The scale is impressive, but not unprecedented; the diversity (three continents) may be a differentiator, but this is not deeply analyzed.
    - The dataset construction is a valuable resource, but the novelty is incremental.

**Contribution 3: Empirical demonstration of improved representation quality**
- **Most similar prior work:** Song et al. (2024); Strodthoff et al. (2021); Gopal et al. (2021)
- **Claimed difference:** Outperforms other self-supervised and some fully supervised models on external validation.
- **Independent assessment:** 
    - The reported improvements are credible, but the field is highly competitive and improvements may be due to scale, implementation, or data curation as much as to methodological innovation.
    - The margin of improvement is not always large, and the generality across tasks is not fully established.

**Contribution 4: Demonstration that temporal augmentations improve ECG representation**
- **Most similar prior work:** Soltanieh et al. (2022); Gopal et al. (2021)
- **Claimed difference:** Temporal augmentations specifically improve patient-based contrastive learning.
- **Independent assessment:** 
    - Prior works have shown that augmentations can improve contrastive learning for ECGs, but the specific effect in the context of patient-based contrastive learning is less well established.
    - The contribution is a targeted empirical validation rather than a conceptual advance.

**Summary:** The main deltas are in the combination and scale of existing ideas, with empirical validation. The conceptual novelty is modest; the primary value is in integration, scale, and thorough evaluation.
