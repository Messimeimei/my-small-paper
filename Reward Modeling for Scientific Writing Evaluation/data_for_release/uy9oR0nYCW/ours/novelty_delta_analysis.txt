# 2. AUTHOR CITATION ANALYSIS

**Patterns in Author Positioning:**
- Authors consistently position their work as addressing gaps in explainability and real-world generalization, often by highlighting limitations in prior works’ datasets, evaluation scope, or interpretability.
- There is a tendency to emphasize the novelty of their attention roll-out mechanism and conceptual framework, sometimes by downplaying the scope or sophistication of related works.

**Specific Citation Analyses:**

- **FakeSound (Xie et al., 2024):**
  - Authors claim FakeSound focuses on environmental/background audio, uses a custom dataset, and does not evaluate on standard benchmarks.
  - *Independent check*: FakeSound indeed targets general audio, but its architecture (ResNet + transformer + BiLSTM) is similar in spirit to the submission’s transformer-based approach. The lack of standard benchmark evaluation is accurate, but the technical overlap is understated.

- **FakeAVCeleb (Khalid et al., 2021):**
  - Authors describe it as a standard but "slightly out-of-date" dataset, emphasizing its demographic diversity.
  - *Independent check*: The dataset remains widely used and is a reasonable choice for cross-dataset evaluation. The "out-of-date" characterization may be overstated, as it is still a benchmark in recent works.

- **Continuous Learning (Le et al., 2024):**
  - Authors note the use of AST features with GBDT for continuous learning.
  - *Independent check*: This is accurate, but the submission does not discuss whether similar explainability or generalization strategies are used, missing an opportunity for deeper comparison.

- **Waveform Boundary Detection (Cai et al., 2022):**
  - Authors describe the use of Wav2Vec/MFCC features, CNNs, transformer encoder, BiLSTM, and linear classifier for in-painted deepfake audio.
  - *Independent check*: The technical pipeline is similar to the submission’s, but the focus on localization (boundary detection) is a key difference. The submission does not address localization, so the comparison is fair.

- **Light CNN with Feature Genuinization (Wu et al., 2020):**
  - Authors highlight the popularity of CNN-based classifiers and the use of MFM activation for compact features.
  - *Independent check*: The technical description is accurate, but the submission does not discuss whether attention-based explainability has been explored in these CNN-based models.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The authors sometimes understate the technical sophistication or scope of prior works (e.g., FakeSound, FakeAVCeleb) to emphasize their own novelty.
- There is little discussion of recent explainability-focused works (e.g., neurosymbolic, XAI-based adversarial detection), which are highly relevant.
- Claimed improvements (e.g., in explainability or generalization) are not always substantiated with direct, head-to-head comparisons.
