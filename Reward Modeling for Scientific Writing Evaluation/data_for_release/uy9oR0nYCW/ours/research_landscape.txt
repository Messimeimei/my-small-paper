## 1. METHODOLOGICAL LANDSCAPE

### Main Methodological Approaches

Across the collection, the following methodological themes emerge:

- **Transformer-based and Deep Neural Models**: Many papers leverage transformer architectures (e.g., Audio Spectrogram Transformer, WavLM, RawBMamba, attention roll-out in transformers) for feature extraction and classification.
- **Self-Supervised and Pre-trained Models**: Use of large-scale self-supervised models (WavLM, Wav2Vec2, Whisper, multilingual PTMs) as feature extractors, often combined with lightweight or classical classifiers.
- **Contrastive and Representation Learning**: Contrastive learning (CLAD) and feature genuinization (LCNN with transformer) are used to improve robustness and generalization.
- **Explainability and XAI**: Several works (submission, neurosymbolic, XAI-based adversarial detection) focus on explainability, using attention mechanisms, neurosymbolic reasoning, or XAI interpretability maps.
- **Multimodal and Neurosymbolic Reasoning**: Some methods combine audio and visual modalities (neurosymbolic, FakeAVCeleb) or use symbolic reasoning for explainability.
- **Classical Machine Learning**: Green AI approaches use classical ML (logistic regression, shallow NNs) on top of SSL embeddings for efficiency.
- **Entropy and Statistical Features**: Multi-Scale Permutation Entropy (MPE) and LFCC are used for lightweight, interpretable detection.
- **Boundary and Region Localization**: Frame-level and region localization (Waveform Boundary Detection, ADD 2023) go beyond binary classification to localize manipulated segments.
- **Continuous and Few-Shot Learning**: Continuous learning plugins and few-shot adaptation (Continuous Learning, Cross-Domain ADD) address the need for rapid adaptation to new attacks.
- **Dataset Creation and Benchmarking**: Several works focus on dataset creation (FakeAVCeleb, FakeSound, CD-ADD, white-box datasets) and systematic benchmarking (SoK, survey papers).

## 3. EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

### Common Datasets

- **ASVspoof Series**: ASVspoof 2019, 2021, and 5 are widely used for speech deepfake detection.
- **FakeAVCeleb**: Used for multimodal (audio-video) detection and cross-dataset generalization.
- **FakeSound**: For general audio (beyond speech) deepfake detection.
- **CD-ADD**: For cross-domain audio deepfake detection.
- **ADD 2023**: For advanced tasks (localization, algorithm recognition).
- **Other Benchmarks**: In-the-Wild, DECRO, Celeb-DF(-B), FaceForensics++, DeepFakeDetection, World Leaders, Presidential Deepfakes.

### Evaluation Metrics

- **Accuracy**: Used in many works, especially for binary classification.
- **Equal Error Rate (EER)**: Standard for ASV and audio deepfake detection.
- **AUC (Area Under Curve)**: Used for overall detection performance.
- **False Acceptance Rate (FAR)**: Used for robustness to manipulations.
- **t-DCF**: For ASV system integration.
- **Generalization Performance**: Cross-dataset or cross-domain accuracy/EER.
- **Explainability**: Qualitative and conceptual evaluation (submission, XAI-based).
- **Localization Metrics**: For region/boundary detection (ADD 2023, Waveform Boundary Detection).
- **Model Efficiency**: Number of parameters, computational cost (Green AI).

### Patterns in Evaluation

- **Cross-Dataset/Domain Testing**: Increasingly common to test models trained on one dataset against another (submission, Cross-Domain ADD, Continuous Learning).
- **Robustness to Manipulations**: Systematic evaluation under various attacks (CLAD, Beautification Filters).
- **Human vs. Model Performance**: Some works compare human and model accuracy (FakeSound, Beautification Filters).
- **Explainability Assessment**: Both qualitative (visualizations, textual explanations) and conceptual frameworks are used.

### Comparison Across Papers

- **Standardization**: EER and accuracy remain the most common, but new tasks (localization, attribution) require new metrics.
- **Benchmarking**: SoK and survey papers push for unified evaluation frameworks.
- **Efficiency**: Green AI introduces model size and computational cost as key metrics.

## 5. TECHNICAL EVOLUTION

### Progression and Evolution of Ideas

- **From Feature Engineering to Deep Learning**: Early works relied on hand-crafted features (LFCC, entropy); recent works use deep, self-supervised, and transformer-based models.
- **From Black-Box to Explainable AI**: There is a clear evolution toward explainability, with attention mechanisms, symbolic reasoning, and XAI methods.
- **From Binary to Granular Tasks**: The field is moving from simple real/fake classification to localization, attribution, and manipulation-type recognition.
- **From Single-Modal to Multimodal and Multilingual**: Datasets and models now support audio, video, and multiple languages, reflecting real-world diversity.
- **From Static to Adaptive Models**: Continuous learning, few-shot adaptation, and robust training strategies address the need for models that adapt to new attacks and domains.
- **From High-Resource to Green AI**: There is a growing concern for computational efficiency, leading to lightweight and eco-friendly approaches.

### Building Blocks and Extensions

- **Transformer and SSL Models**: Serve as the backbone for many recent advances; extended with attention roll-out (submission), multi-fusion (WavLM+MFA), and bidirectional state space (RawBMamba).
- **Contrastive and Representation Learning**: CLAD and Light CNN with Genuinization extend basic deep models for robustness.
- **Explainability Frameworks**: Submission and neurosymbolic works extend detection with conceptual and technical explainability.
- **Dataset Creation**: New datasets (FakeAVCeleb, FakeSound, CD-ADD) enable new research directions and more realistic evaluation.

### Competing and Complementary Approaches

- **Competing**: Deep transformer-based models vs. lightweight/Green AI approaches; end-to-end vs. feature-based pipelines.
- **Complementary**: Explainability methods can be layered on top of robust or efficient detectors; multimodal and multilingual approaches can be combined with robust and explainable models.
