# 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Situating DMQR-RAG in the Landscape:**

- **Cluster Placement:** DMQR-RAG is most closely aligned with the "Query Rewriting and Refinement" cluster, sharing space with RQ-RAG, Query Rewriting for Retrieval-Augmented LLMs, Enhancing Conversational Search, CAR, and Agent4Ranking. It also incorporates elements from the "Dynamic/Active Retrieval" cluster (via adaptive strategy selection) and "Document Reranking" (via cross-attention reranker).
- **Most Related Prior Works:** The most directly comparable works are RQ-RAG (Ma et al., 2023), Query Rewriting for Retrieval-Augmented LLMs (Chan et al., 2024), and Agent4Ranking. These all address multi-query rewriting, diversity, and/or adaptivity in RAG.
- **Methodological Relationship:** DMQR-RAG extends the multi-query rewriting paradigm by introducing explicit information-level-based diversity in rewrites and an adaptive mechanism to minimize unnecessary rewrites. The use of a cross-attention reranker is also present in some prior works (e.g., RichRAG, Agent4Ranking).
- **Problem Space:** The submission addresses noisy/intent-deviated queries, low recall due to lack of diversity, and inefficiency from excessive rewrites—problems also tackled by RQ-RAG, Query Rewriting for RAG LLMs, and Agent4Ranking, though with different emphases (e.g., demographic diversity, informativeness, robustness).
- **Evaluation Approach:** The claim of a standardized evaluation setup is in line with recent trends (RGB Benchmark, RAG Survey), but the field already has several such efforts.

**Independent Assessment:**  
DMQR-RAG is an incremental but potentially meaningful extension within a highly active area. Its main novelty is the explicit use of information-level-based diversity in query rewriting and adaptive selection, but similar ideas (diversity, adaptivity, reranking) have been explored in various forms in recent literature.

# 3. CONTRIBUTION DELTA ANALYSIS

**Claim 1: General-purpose multi-query rewriting framework with information-level-based diversity**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** RQ-RAG, Query Rewriting for Retrieval-Augmented LLMs, Agent4Ranking.
- **Claimed Difference:** DMQR-RAG uses "information-level-based" diversity in rewrites.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - RQ-RAG and Query Rewriting for RAG LLMs both generate multiple rewrites per query, often using LLMs to increase diversity and informativeness. Agent4Ranking generates rewrites from multiple demographic perspectives.
  - The explicit categorization of rewrites by "information level" (if well-defined and operationalized) may be a more systematic approach to diversity than prior work, which often relies on LLM prompting or demographic simulation.
  - However, the distinction may be superficial if "information level" is not clearly differentiated from "informativeness" or "aspect coverage" in prior work.
  - The submission does not provide direct empirical or technical comparison to these diversity mechanisms.

**Claim 2: Adaptive strategy selection to minimize rewrites while optimizing performance**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** Query Rewriting for RAG LLMs (Chan et al., 2024), DRAGIN, FLARE.
- **Claimed Difference:** Adaptive selection of rewriting strategies to reduce unnecessary rewrites.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Query Rewriting for RAG LLMs and DRAGIN both include adaptive or dynamic mechanisms (e.g., deciding when to rewrite or retrieve based on model confidence or predicted need).
  - The novelty of DMQR-RAG’s adaptive selection depends on the specific mechanism and whether it is more efficient or effective than prior adaptive approaches.
  - Without detailed comparison, it is unclear if the adaptivity is a substantive advance or a routine extension.

**Claim 3: Multi-query rewriting with information diversity outperforms single-query rewriting and vanilla RAG-Fusion**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** RQ-RAG, Query Rewriting for RAG LLMs, RAG-Fusion.
- **Claimed Difference:** Information-diverse multi-query rewriting is superior.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - Prior work (RQ-RAG, Query Rewriting for RAG LLMs) has already shown that multi-query rewriting can outperform single-query and vanilla RAG.
  - The incremental improvement may be due to the specific diversity mechanism or implementation details, rather than a conceptual leap.

**Claim 4: Standardized evaluation setup for fair comparison**
- **Most Similar Prior Work:** RGB Benchmark, RAG Survey, Benchmarking LLMs in RAG.
- **Claimed Difference:** Provides a standardized setup for evaluating rewriting modules.
- **Independent Assessment:**  
  - The field already has several standardized benchmarks and evaluation frameworks.
  - Unless the submission’s setup is widely adopted or addresses a previously unmet need, this is likely an incremental contribution.
