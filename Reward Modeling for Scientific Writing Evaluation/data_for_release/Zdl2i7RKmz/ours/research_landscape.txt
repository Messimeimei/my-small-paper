# EVALUATION LANDSCAPE

**Common Datasets and Environments:**
- **StarCraft II/SMAC/SMACv2**: The most widely used benchmark for cooperative MARL.
- **Multi-Agent MuJoCo, Google Research Football, Dexterous Hands**: Used for generalization and robustness testing.
- **Aerial Wildfire Suppression (AWS)**: Used in the submission paper for real-world complexity.
- **Ultimatum Game, Matrix Games, DealOrNoDeal**: Used for LLM-based reward design.
- **PettingZoo**: Provides a suite of environments for MARL.

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- **Performance/Win Rate**: Most common, especially in StarCraft/SMAC environments.
- **Training/Data Efficiency**: Time or samples to reach expert-level or optimal performance.
- **Alignment with User Objectives**: Human ratings or objective alignment accuracy (for LLM-based methods).
- **Generalization/Few-Shot Learning**: Ability to perform on unseen tasks or scenarios.
- **Attribute Consistency/Fluency**: For text generation and controllable language models.
- **Learning Speed/Convergence**: Time to optimal policy, especially in curriculum/intervention studies.

**Patterns and Comparisons:**
- There is a strong reliance on win rate and performance metrics in simulated environments.
- Newer works (including the submission) emphasize training efficiency and alignment with user intent, reflecting a shift toward practical deployment and human-in-the-loop systems.
- Benchmarks are evolving (e.g., SMACv2) to better test generalization and robustness, not just raw performance.
