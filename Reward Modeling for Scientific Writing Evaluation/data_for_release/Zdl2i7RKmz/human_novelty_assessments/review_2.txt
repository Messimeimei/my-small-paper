This paper presents an empirical study investigating how LLMs can be used as a controller to intervene in the learning process of MARL, with the main contributions being the evaluation of two representations of prompts: a rule-based controller and a natural language controller. From the perspective of an empirical and engineering work, this paper seems like an original work, as I cannot find duplicated counterparts online. Although the paradigm of intervening in the MARL learning process is not a completely new idea, the leverage of LLMs to produce the interventions should be novel. The trial made in this paper well shedded light on the possibility of this combination, which is thrilled to MARL researchers such as myself. In this sense, I believe this paper studied significant work.
