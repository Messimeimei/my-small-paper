## 1. RESEARCH CONTEXT POSITIONING

**Independent Positioning within the Landscape:**
- The submission (MMARP) is situated within the **LLM-based multi-agent simulation and market modeling cluster** (Cluster 1), alongside works like *Simulating Financial Market via LLM Agents*, *StockAgent*, *EconAgent*, and *FinCon*.
- Its core methodological innovation is the use of **theory-driven prompt designs** and **role-based LLM agents** (buyers/sellers) whose aggregate response curves are intersected to simulate price discovery.
- The submission’s focus is on **accurately simulating market participant behavior** and **forecasting prices** by leveraging LLMs as agents, with explicit attention to both **irrational behaviors** and **numerical/financial literacy limitations** of LLMs.

**Most Closely Related Prior Works:**
- *Simulating Financial Market via LLM Agents* (Zhang et al., 2024a; Gao et al., 2024): Uses LLM agents to model investor actions, focusing on qualitative evaluation of emergent market behaviors.
- *EconAgent* (Li et al., 2024): Simulates macroeconomic activities with LLM-empowered agents, focusing on large-scale, system-level behaviors.
- *StockAgent*, *FinCon*: Employ LLM agents in market simulation, with FinCon introducing hierarchical agent structures and risk control.

**Relation to Methodological Clusters:**
- MMARP is most closely aligned with **Cluster 1** (LLM-based multi-agent simulation), but distinguishes itself by emphasizing **theory-driven prompt design** and **quantitative evaluation of simulation accuracy** (forecasting/profitability).
- It is less focused on **memory/reasoning augmentation** (Cluster 2) or **multimodal data integration** (Cluster 4), though it shares the general goal of improving realism and predictive power.

**Problem Space and Evaluation:**
- The submission addresses the **simulation of realistic market participant behavior** and **price forecasting**, with explicit attention to LLM limitations (irrationality, numerical errors).
- Evaluation is conducted on **forecasting and profitability metrics**, directly comparing MMARP to both deep learning and LLM-based agent methods.

**Verification of Author Positioning:**
- The claim of being the “first to rigorously investigate” LLM agent validity in financial market simulation is **partially overstated**: prior works (*Simulating Financial Market via LLM Agents*, *StockAgent*) have explored similar agent-based LLM simulations, though often with more qualitative or system-level focus.
- The **intersection of buyer/seller response curves** for price discovery appears to be a distinguishing technical detail, but the general approach of using LLM agents for market simulation is established.

- For *PIXIU*: Cited as providing benchmarks, which is accurate.

**Discrepancies and Rhetoric:**
- The characterization of prior LLM-agent works as “qualitative” may **understate the quantitative aspects** present in some of these papers.
- The claim that prior LLM agents “do not give consistent judgments” is a **generalization**; some prior works do attempt to address consistency and realism, though perhaps not with the same methodology.
- There is a tendency to **emphasize limitations** of prior work to highlight MMARP’s contributions, sometimes without full acknowledgment of overlapping aims or partial solutions in earlier papers.
