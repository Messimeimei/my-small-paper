This paper introduces YOLO-MARL, a framework that integrates large language models (LLMs) with multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) to enhance policy learning in cooperative game environments. The key contribution lies in the one-time interaction strategy with LLMs per environment, which aims to minimize computational overhead while still leveraging the capabilities of LLMs to generate strategy plans, interpret states, and create planning functions for MARL agents. While the use of LLMs in MARL to reduce the frequency of model calls is relatively novel, and the one-time interaction approach is an innovative means of improving efficiency, the overall claim of innovation within the MARL domain appears somewhat overstated. The paper does not convincingly differentiate its approach from potential single-agent applications, raising questions about whether the novelty is specific to MARL or more broadly applicable. Furthermore, the manuscript omits discussion of several relevant recent works that also integrate LLMs with multi-agent systems, such as Hu et al. (2024), Gong et al. (2023), Wu et al. (2024), and Zhang et al. (2023), which undermines the depth of its literature review and may call into question the distinctiveness of its contribution. In summary, while the proposed reduction in LLM invocation frequency is a noteworthy idea, the novelty is limited by insufficient differentiation from related approaches and a lack of engagement with pertinent prior work.
