This submission, "Progressive LLM Alignments Using Two-Player Games," builds on established game-theoretic and adversarial prompt generation approaches for LLM alignment, closely paralleling recent works such as COMAL, INPO, PromptAttack, SelfPrompt, and CRT. Its primary technical novelty is the explicit use of BLEU and embedding similarity as diversity constraints in adversarial prompt generation within a two-player game framework, which is a minor but practical extension of existing methods. The core elements—two-player game setup, adaptive adversarial agent, and Nash equilibrium convergence—are already standard in the literature, and the submission does not provide direct comparisons to the most similar recent works, potentially overstating its originality. While the implementation appears sound and may yield empirical improvements, the conceptual advances are incremental, with the main differentiation being the specific diversity metrics employed. Reviewers should note that the submission’s contributions are routine extensions of current trends, and its novelty is limited to technical details rather than fundamental methodological innovation.
