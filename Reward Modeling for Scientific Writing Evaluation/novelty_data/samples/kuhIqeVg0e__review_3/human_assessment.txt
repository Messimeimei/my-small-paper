This paper presents ChemAgent, a framework designed to enhance the chemical reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs) by introducing a dynamic memory updating mechanism and a self-updating library system that decomposes complex chemical problems into subtasks and stores solutions for future retrieval. While ChemAgent’s approach to iterative problem solving and self-improvement through dynamic memory is innovative, its core methodology relies heavily on established techniques such as prompt engineering and the integration of pre-trained models—areas that have been extensively explored in the literature. The novelty of ChemAgent primarily lies in its integration of prompt design with memory modules to manage LLM interactions and context retrieval; however, the dependence on prompt-based strategies and memory retrieval mechanisms limits the distinctiveness of the contribution. To better establish its novelty, the paper would benefit from a clearer articulation of how ChemAgent’s dynamic memory updating and prompt engineering differ from existing approaches, and whether there are specific design choices in memory retrieval or self-improvement that have not been widely explored in prior work. Overall, while the self-improvement capabilities of the dynamic library system are noteworthy, the incremental nature of the integration suggests that the contribution is somewhat limited in novelty unless further distinctions from prior art are clarified.
