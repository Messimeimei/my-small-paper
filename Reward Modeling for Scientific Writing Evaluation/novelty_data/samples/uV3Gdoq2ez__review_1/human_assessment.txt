This paper introduces the ReviewMT dataset, a large-scale collection of academic peer review records from ICLR and NeurIPS conferences, intended to facilitate research on language model-assisted peer review. While the dataset is positioned as a novel resource for studying the iterative and dialogic nature of real-world peer review, the technical novelty of the work is limited. The dataset itself is primarily assembled by downloading and processing publicly available review documents from the OpenReview platform, with its structure directly mirroring the existing formats used by ICLR and NeurIPS, including the standard multi-reviewer and area chair (AC) framework. The subsequent experiments involve fine-tuning several sub-10B parameter language models using the LLAMA-FACTOR library, but do not introduce new modeling techniques or analytical approaches. The claim of offering a "novel perspective on the complete peer-review process" is overstated, as the dataset’s structure and content are largely inherited from the original conference review processes rather than representing a fundamentally new approach. Overall, the contribution is primarily in dataset aggregation and empirical benchmarking, without significant technical innovation or advancement beyond existing resources and methodologies.
