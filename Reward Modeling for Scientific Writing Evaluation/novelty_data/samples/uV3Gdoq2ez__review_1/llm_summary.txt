This submission presents an integrated framework for LLM-driven peer review, combining multi-turn, role-based dialogue modeling, a large-scale annotated dataset (ReviewMT), and a comprehensive benchmark with role-specific evaluation metrics. While the integration and scale are notable, the core concepts—multi-turn, role-based simulation, and fine-grained benchmarking—have been explored in prior works such as AgentReview, PeerSum, SEA, and MT-Bench-101. The authors tend to overstate their novelty, particularly in claiming to be the first to model peer review as multi-turn, role-based dialogue and to construct such a dataset, while omitting relevant related work (e.g., PeerSum, SciAssess, TELeR) that addresses similar problems. The main contribution is the unification and extension of existing approaches rather than a fundamental conceptual breakthrough. Reviewers should be aware that empirical improvements may stem from dataset scale or annotation detail, and that the submission’s novelty is primarily incremental and integrative.
