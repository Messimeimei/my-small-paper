This assessment finds that the submission "Do LLMs have Consistent Values?" is a well-executed, incremental extension of established research on probing value and personality consistency in large language models (LLMs). The main novelty lies in its systematic focus on within-session, single-persona value consistency and the introduction of a highly explicit 'Value Anchor' prompt, but these are variants of existing persona induction and prompting strategies rather than fundamentally new methods. The authors’ claims of being the "first" to study within-session consistency and to introduce a novel prompting strategy are somewhat overstated, as prior work has addressed closely related questions with similar methodologies. The empirical improvements reported may be attributable to prompt explicitness or implementation details, and the submission does not fully compare against the most advanced persona induction or LLM-native measurement paradigms. Overall, the work is a solid incremental contribution to a rapidly evolving field, but its differentiation from prior research is primarily in experimental framing and prompt specificity rather than conceptual or methodological innovation.
