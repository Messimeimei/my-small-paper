This paper proposes a multi-level optimization framework for deploying BERT models on hardware-constrained devices, combining pruning, quantization, and knowledge distillation to produce a more compact model while maintaining high accuracy. However, the novelty of the approach is limited, as it primarily applies established techniques—quantization, pruning, and knowledge distillation—without introducing significant methodological innovations or improvements over existing work. The integration of these three techniques is not sufficiently justified or tailored to the unique characteristics of the BERT architecture, and the paper does not provide a detailed discussion of the individual or combined benefits of each method. Furthermore, the approach closely resembles prior state-of-the-art works such as Q-BERT (Shen et al., 2020) for quantization, EBERT (Liu et al., 2021) for structured pruning, and CTR-BERT (Muhamed et al., 2021) for knowledge distillation, but lacks a comprehensive comparison or clear differentiation from these methods. As a result, the contribution appears to be a direct application of existing strategies rather than a novel advancement. To strengthen its originality, the paper would benefit from either a more in-depth analysis of how the combined techniques interact specifically within BERT, the development of a new integration algorithm, or a thorough comparison with recent state-of-the-art approaches to clearly establish its unique contributions. Without such enhancements or comparative analysis, it is difficult to draw a tangible conclusion regarding the effectiveness and novelty of the proposed method.
