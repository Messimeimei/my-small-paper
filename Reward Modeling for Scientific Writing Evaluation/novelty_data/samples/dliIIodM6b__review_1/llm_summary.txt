This assessment finds that the submission "Bootstrapping Language Models with DPO Implicit Rewards" is an incremental extension of existing work, combining iterative DPO, implicit reward bootstrapping, length-regularized reward shaping at dataset construction, and experience replay. While each component is well-established in the literature, the explicit combination—particularly the use of experience replay to address knowledge corruption in iterative bootstrapping—represents a modest operational innovation. The authors' claims of being "first" to use implicit DPO rewards for bootstrapping are overstated, as similar approaches (e.g., SeRA, SimPO) exist, and several highly relevant recent works are omitted from the related work discussion. Empirical improvements are competitive but not unprecedented, and may be influenced by implementation details rather than conceptual advances. Reviewers should recognize the thoughtful integration of techniques but weigh the incremental nature of the contribution and the incomplete citation of closely related prior work.
