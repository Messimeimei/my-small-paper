This paper proposes a self-supervised iterative finetuning procedure for language models trained with Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), in which, after an initial round of DPO using human data, the model’s implicit reward function is used to label new self-supervised preference data for further rounds of training. The approach is augmented with two techniques—adjusting implicit rewards with a length penalty and incorporating experience replay with human-labeled data—which are shown to be critical for achieving strong empirical results. In terms of novelty, while the implementation and the specific use of implicit rewards for iterative finetuning are new as far as the reviewer is aware, the underlying concepts of iterative finetuning and implicit reward models themselves are not novel. The idea of using any reward model, including the implicit DPO reward, for iterative finetuning is considered somewhat "obvious," and similar directions have been explored in prior work, such as Anthropic's Constitutional AI and RLAIF, as well as He et al. (2024). Thus, the main novelty is limited to the empirical demonstration of this approach and the introduction of the length-normalization penalty (though similar variations have been investigated elsewhere) and experience replay (which, while not entirely new, is usefully demonstrated in this context). Overall, the contribution is incremental, with the most distinctive aspects being the empirical results and the specific implementation details, rather than a fundamentally new conceptual advance.
