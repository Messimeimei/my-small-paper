This paper introduces SubgraphRAG, a method that extends the knowledge graph-based retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) framework by integrating a lightweight multilayer perceptron (MLP) with a parallel triple-scoring mechanism for efficient subgraph retrieval, and by encoding directional structural distances to enhance retrieval accuracy. While the approach aims to balance model complexity and reasoning power, its novelty appears limited. The core technique—embedding-based retrieval for triplets—closely resembles existing methods in the literature, such as those described by Li et al. (2023) [1], which also leverage embeddings of questions and triplets to retrieve relevant triples. The main distinction in the proposed method is the concatenation of $z_\tau$ with previous embeddings, but this modification may not constitute a significant innovation over prior work. As such, the contribution may be incremental, and the paper would benefit from more clearly articulating how its approach substantively differs from established embedding-based retrieval methods to better establish its novelty.
