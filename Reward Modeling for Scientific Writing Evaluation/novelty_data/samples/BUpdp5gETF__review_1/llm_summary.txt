The submission introduces Relative Learning Rate Schedules (RLRS), a method that generalizes component-specific learning rates from adapters (as in LoRA+) to all Transformer components, particularly within large Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models. This approach is positioned as a practical, incremental advance over prior work, offering finer-grained control than global or adapter-only learning rate schedules, and is empirically shown to accelerate training and reduce resource usage. The authors also demonstrate that RLRS hyperparameters tuned on small models can be effectively transferred to much larger models, reducing hyperparameter search costs—a useful application of the "proxy" principle from prior literature. While the conceptual novelty is limited (the main innovation is in scope and application rather than underlying principle), the work is well-situated within current trends toward scalable, efficient, and heterogeneous model optimization. Reviewers should note that the claimed improvements are meaningful but incremental, and that careful ablation is needed to isolate the effects of RLRS from other factors.
