This paper proposes a novel approach for studying the alignment between large language models (LLMs) and brain activity as measured by fMRI, by extracting representative patterns from all artificial neurons (ANs) across the entire LLM, rather than focusing on layer-specific representations as in prior work. The key contribution is that this method enables LLM representations to span multiple layers, potentially offering a more holistic view of model organization in relation to neural data. While this cross-layer representation is a new methodological angle, the paper’s claims of novelty are somewhat overstated. Although the authors frame their work as addressing a gap—namely, that prior studies operated at a "population-level" and had not linked individual ANs to neural responses—the proposed method does not actually establish direct links between individual ANs and brain activity. Instead, it aggregates all ANs to extract representative patterns (a dictionary), which are then related to neural responses. This distinction is important, as the framing suggests a finer granularity than is actually achieved. Furthermore, while the abstract asserts that this is the first exploration of brain-like functional organization within LLMs, several previous studies have also investigated the alignment between LLMs and neural responses with the goal of understanding brain-like organization. Thus, although the specific approach of spanning representations across layers is new, the broader research question and motivation are shared with earlier work. It remains unclear from the results whether this methodological novelty leads to new or complementary scientific insights compared to prior approaches that align LLM layer representations to brain activity. To more convincingly establish its contribution, the paper would benefit from clarifying how its findings differ from or extend those of existing studies, and from tempering claims regarding the granularity and uniqueness of its analysis.
