This submission introduces a unified framework (HorizonAI) and benchmark (ResBench) for jointly synthesizing historical scientific research and predicting future trends, positioning itself at the intersection of LLM-driven research automation, temporal knowledge graph (TKG) reasoning, and trend forecasting. Its main novelty is the explicit integration of retrospective synthesis and predictive analysis within a single dual-system pipeline, inspired by cognitive science, though the technical distinction from existing modular or multi-stage LLM+KG systems is limited. The new benchmark, ResBench, addresses a gap by evaluating both historical coverage and predictive reliability, but is a routine extension of existing evaluation practices. While the empirical results show improvements over baselines, it is unclear if these gains stem from the unified framework or from better prompting and implementation details. Overall, the work’s primary contribution is in task unification and evaluation protocol, with conceptual framing that is interesting but not a major technical advance, and some claims about prior work’s limitations are overstated or insufficiently nuanced.
