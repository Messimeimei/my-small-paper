Q-Sparse is presented as a method for enforcing explicit, hard top-K activation sparsity in large language models (LLMs), distinguishing itself from prior threshold-based or regularized approaches (e.g., ProSparse, Turbo Sparse, ReLU Strikes Back) by guaranteeing a fixed number of nonzero activations per layer. The main technical contributions are the use of top-K selection with a straight-through estimator, a block-wise implementation for hardware efficiency, and demonstrated compatibility with extremely quantized models (down to 1-bit precision). While the authors claim that prior methods only achieve "partial" sparsity, this is somewhat overstated, as previous work has reported high sparsity ratios, albeit with less explicit control. The block-wise adaptation and application to various training regimes are routine extensions, and the empirical "scaling law" for sparsity is an incremental rather than conceptual advance. Notably, the submission omits direct comparison to the most relevant recent works and sometimes mischaracterizes the capabilities of prior methods, so reviewers should weigh the explicitness and practical impact of Q-Sparse’s approach against the incremental nature of its contributions.
