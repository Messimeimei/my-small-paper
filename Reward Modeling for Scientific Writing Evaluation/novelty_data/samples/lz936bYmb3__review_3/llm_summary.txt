DMQR-RAG is an incremental extension to the multi-query rewriting paradigm for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), introducing explicit "information-level-based" diversity in query rewrites and an adaptive mechanism to minimize unnecessary rewrites. While the authors position their approach as novel, similar ideas—such as multi-query rewriting, diversity (e.g., informativeness, aspect coverage, demographic simulation), and adaptivity—have been explored in recent works like RQ-RAG, Query Rewriting for RAG LLMs, and Agent4Ranking. The main technical distinction claimed is the systematic categorization of rewrites by "information level," but this may overlap with existing diversity mechanisms unless more clearly defined and empirically differentiated. The submission tends to understate the capabilities of prior work and omits several highly relevant recent papers, limiting the strength of its novelty claims. Overall, DMQR-RAG is a well-situated but modest advance, and reviewers should carefully assess whether its contributions represent substantive innovation or routine refinement within a rapidly evolving field.
