This paper introduces SymTex, a benchmark for evaluating non-monotonic reasoning abilities of large language models, and proposes the MG-SymTex framework for generating non-monotonic reasoning samples in both symbolic and textual forms. While the work aims to address an underexplored area by providing new tasks—tri-state boolean querying and answer set computation—the novelty of the approach is limited. Several recent works, such as $\delta$-NLI and LogicNMR, have already introduced benchmarks for logical reasoning, including non-monotonic reasoning, and the present paper does not clearly differentiate itself from these prior efforts. The main distinction appears to be the inclusion of symbolic samples, but this addition lacks a strong justification and does not convincingly establish a significant advance over existing benchmarks. Furthermore, the experimental results primarily confirm known limitations of LLMs in handling complex logical tasks, rather than uncovering new insights. As such, the contribution is incremental, and the paper would benefit from more clearly articulating how SymTex advances the state of the art beyond what has already been established in the literature.
