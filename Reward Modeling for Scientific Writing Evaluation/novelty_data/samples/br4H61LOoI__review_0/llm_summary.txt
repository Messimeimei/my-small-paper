MR-GSM8K introduces a GSM8K-derived benchmark and a new MR-Score metric for evaluating LLMs' meta-reasoning abilities, specifically their capacity to judge, identify errors, and explain reasoning in math solutions. While the submission claims to pioneer the meta-reasoning evaluation paradigm, similar benchmarks (notably MR-Ben) and process supervision approaches already exist, making the overall paradigm less novel than suggested. The main substantive contributions are the adaptation of the widely used GSM8K dataset for meta-reasoning and the introduction of a process-level metric, which may facilitate broader adoption and comparison. However, the omission of direct comparisons to highly relevant prior work (e.g., MR-Ben) and somewhat overstated novelty claims are notable weaknesses. Reviewers should recognize the value of the new dataset and metric, but also weigh the incremental nature of the contribution within a rapidly evolving field.
