The submission introduces the Evaluation-Oriented Problem-Solving (EOP) framework, an inference-time, evaluation-driven prompting method specifically adapted for small language models (SLMs), building on existing reasoning frameworks like Tree of Thoughts and Buffer of Thoughts. Its main contribution is the systematic adaptation and optimization of evaluation-centric prompting for SLMs, with empirical results showing that SLMs can, in some cases, outperform larger models like GPT-4 on select tasks. While the core mechanisms (structured evaluation, multiple trials, adaptive schemes) are not fundamentally new, the work provides a useful, incremental extension by tailoring these techniques to SLMs and analyzing their failure modes. However, the submission sometimes overstates its novelty and the limitations of prior work, as some recent studies have begun to adapt advanced prompting for SLMs, and the conceptual advances are largely routine extensions rather than breakthroughs. Overall, the work is best viewed as a practical, empirically validated adaptation of known methods for SLMs, with its strongest differentiation in systematic application and analysis rather than in introducing fundamentally new paradigms.
