This assessment finds that the submission is an incremental extension of established methods for adapting LLMs to specialized domains using synthetic data generated from structured sources, specifically applying these techniques to industrial asset management with ISO standards. While the authors claim novelty in their two-step iterative KG-LLM process and entity expansion for knowledge document generation, similar approaches (KG-to-text, synthetic QA generation) are well-documented in prior work across other domains such as medical and chip design. The main substantive difference lies in the application domain and the use of ISO tables, rather than in methodological innovation. Reported empirical improvements (~4% accuracy) are modest and consistent with typical gains from domain adaptation, and the analysis of diminishing returns with more data is routine. The submission overstates its novelty, omits citation of several directly relevant works, and does not fully acknowledge the breadth of similar synthetic data pipelines in the literature, which reviewers should consider when evaluating the true contribution delta.
