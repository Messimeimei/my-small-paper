This paper presents WARD, a method for detecting unauthorized dataset usage in Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems, and introduces FARAD, a specialized benchmark dataset designed to evaluate this task under realistic conditions. The primary contribution lies in the identification and formalization of the RAG Dataset Inference (RAG-DI) problem, which addresses a significant and previously underexplored need for data owners to detect if their data is being used without permission in RAG systems via black-box queries. This formalization fills a notable gap in the literature and establishes a foundation for future research on secure data usage in RAG contexts. Additionally, the creation of the FARAD dataset provides a valuable resource tailored specifically for RAG-DI research, incorporating fact redundancy to better reflect practical scenarios. The novelty of the work is substantial, as both the problem definition and the benchmark dataset represent new directions for the field. However, while the paper’s contributions are clearly distinct from prior work, the review notes that the practical prevalence and application range of RAG-DI scenarios could be further discussed to contextualize the impact of this novelty. Overall, the paper makes a strong and original contribution by formalizing a new problem and providing the first dedicated benchmark for its study.
