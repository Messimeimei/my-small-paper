This submission provides the first systematic analysis showing that quantization can catastrophically reverse the effects of state-of-the-art LLM unlearning methods, revealing a critical vulnerability not previously addressed in the literature. The authors empirically and theoretically demonstrate that knowledge believed to be "forgotten" by unlearning can be largely recovered after quantization, challenging the sufficiency of current benchmarks and evaluation protocols. While the proposed mitigation strategies—such as learning rate adjustment and saliency-based targeting—are technically incremental adaptations of existing optimization techniques, their application to quantization robustness is novel. The work is well-positioned within the context of loss-based unlearning and benchmarking research, but could benefit from a broader discussion of related robustness axes and more comprehensive citation of recent methods. Overall, the main contribution is a substantive shift in evaluation perspective, highlighting the need for new benchmarks and objectives that account for quantization, rather than the introduction of fundamentally new unlearning algorithms.
