**Summary for Reviewer:**

The submission, *Spark Transformer*, proposes a unified, statistical top-k approach to induce sparsity in both the FFN and Attention modules of Transformer models, emphasizing practical, hardware-efficient, and drop-in applicability. Its main novelty lies in the single-stage, end-to-end training of both modules using a unified statistical top-k mechanism, framed as a "key-value lookup," which distinguishes it from prior works that typically focus on one module, require post-training steps, or introduce extra parameters. While the use of statistical or approximate top-k is not fundamentally new—closely related works like HiRE and Q-Sparse have explored similar ideas—the unified, practical implementation and training simplicity are the primary contributions. The authors tend to overstate the conceptual novelty and underplay the adaptability of prior methods, and some relevant works (e.g., Q-Sparse) are not fully discussed. Overall, the submission offers an incremental but practical advance in the field, with its strongest differentiation being unified, single-stage training and implementation, rather than a fundamentally new sparsity mechanism.
