This assessment situates SaMer within the rapidly evolving field of multi-dimensional, interpretable, and scenario-aware LLM evaluation, noting its closest methodological ties to works like FLASK, Prometheus, and ArmoRM. SaMer’s main claimed novelty is its *automatic, scenario-aware selection and weighting* of evaluation dimensions per query, implemented via a multi-head model, rather than relying on fixed or user-specified rubrics. However, the assessment finds that this advance is incremental, as prior works have explored context-dependent and fine-grained evaluation, with the distinction lying mainly in the automation of dimension selection rather than a fundamentally new conceptual framework. The architectural and dataset contributions are also incremental, and the claim of being "first" in this space is somewhat overstated, especially given the lack of direct, head-to-head comparison with the most similar prior systems. Overall, reviewers are advised to recognize SaMer’s unified, model-driven approach as a meaningful but incremental step, and to encourage more explicit comparative analysis with closely related scenario-aware evaluators to clarify its true contribution.
