This paper presents an analysis of the impact of sparsity on various components and experimental settings of large language models (LLMs), and introduces Q-Sparse, a method for training sparsely activated LLMs. However, the methodological novelty of the work is limited. The proposed sparsity method is relatively simple and is applied as a straightforward modification across different components of LLMs, which the reviewer characterizes as more of a "trick novelty" rather than a substantive methodological advance. The core principle behind the sparsity approach is not clearly articulated beyond formulaic descriptions, lacking a deeper theoretical explanation or justification. As such, the contribution appears incremental, with the novelty residing primarily in the application of a simple sparsity technique rather than in the development of a fundamentally new method. The paper would benefit from a clearer exposition of how its approach differs from existing sparsity methods and from a stronger theoretical grounding to better establish its contribution to the field.
