This paper introduces CALF, a benchmark for evaluating long-form question answering (LFQA) that is constructed from real, expert-written Chinese examination questions and translated for broader evaluation. The benchmark aims to provide high-quality, knowledge-rich content and addresses the need for a standardized, effective evaluation framework in LFQA by leveraging authoritative question-answer data across diverse subjects. While CALF’s use of authentic examination material is a notable strength, the novelty of the paper is somewhat limited. The evaluation settings explored—analyzing the consistency and reliability of various metrics—have already been examined in previous works, which diminishes the originality of this aspect. Furthermore, although the paper highlights the ongoing challenge of developing automatic, reliable, and human-aligned evaluation metrics for LFQA, it primarily investigates existing methods and demonstrates that even advanced LLMs struggle with the subtlety required for LFQA tasks. The paper does not propose a clear path forward for overcoming these evaluation challenges, further restricting its novelty. Overall, while the CALF benchmark offers a valuable resource by curating high-quality data, the methodological contributions are incremental, and the work would benefit from a more distinct advancement beyond current approaches to LFQA evaluation.
