This submission presents the first comprehensive, population-normed benchmarking of generative AI models using the full WAIS-IV, enabling direct, percentile-based comparison to human cognitive norms across multiple domains. Its main methodological novelty is the application of a standardized, gold-standard human intelligence test to AI, allowing for multi-domain assessment and quantification of model strengths (verbal, working memory) and weaknesses (perceptual reasoning) in human terms. While prior work has explored visual reasoning gaps and adaptive psychometric testing, none have used the full WAIS-IV for such direct, population-normed comparison, making this a substantive—though incremental—advance. The submission accurately situates itself within the human-centric cognitive benchmarking literature but could more thoroughly engage with related adaptive testing and custom benchmark approaches. Overall, the work offers a meaningful extension of psychometric evaluation to AI, with its primary contribution being the breadth and standardization of its human-AI comparisons rather than fundamentally new methodology or findings.
