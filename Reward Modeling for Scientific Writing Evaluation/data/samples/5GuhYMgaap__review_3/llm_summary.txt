This assessment finds that the submission offers an incremental methodological advance by systematically isolating and comparing inductive and deductive reasoning in LLMs via the SolverLearner framework. The main contribution is a unified experimental setup that enables side-by-side evaluation of reasoning types, which is a useful but not unprecedented improvement over prior prompt-based approaches. However, the submission overstates its novelty, as several recent works—some not cited—have also sought to disentangle reasoning types using alternative methods such as hypothesis refinement and search. The empirical findings (strong inductive but weak deductive performance) are interesting, but may result from improved experimental control rather than a fundamentally new insight into LLM capabilities. Overall, the work is a solid, incremental step in a rapidly evolving area, but would benefit from broader engagement with the latest literature and a more nuanced positioning relative to existing research.
