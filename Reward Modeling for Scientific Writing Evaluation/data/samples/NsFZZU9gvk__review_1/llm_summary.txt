This assessment finds that the submission offers a substantive and timely contribution by introducing BrowserART, a comprehensive, publicly released toolkit for red teaming browser-based LLM agents, and by empirically demonstrating a significant alignment gap between chat LLMs and their browser agent counterparts. The work is well-positioned within the rapidly evolving field of LLM safety, extending systematic evaluation to the relatively underexplored domain of browser agents. However, the authors’ claims of being the "first" comprehensive study in this area are overstated, as they omit citation and comparison to recent, directly relevant agentic jailbreak literature (e.g., "Refusal-Trained LLMs Are Easily Jailbroken As Browser Agents"). The technical advances—while incremental—are valuable, particularly in the breadth and agent-specific focus of the toolkit and the robust empirical results. Reviewers should weigh the contribution’s novelty in the context of recent agentic studies and recognize that the main differentiation lies in the scale and public release of the toolkit, rather than in conceptual breakthroughs.
