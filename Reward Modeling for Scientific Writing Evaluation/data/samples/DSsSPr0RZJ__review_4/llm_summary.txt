DSBench is a new benchmark for data science agents that distinguishes itself primarily by sourcing tasks from real-world competitions (ModelOff and Kaggle), offering a large and diverse set of data analysis and modeling challenges. While the authors claim comprehensive realism and multimodality, these aspects are already addressed by recent benchmarks such as Spider2-V, OSWorld, and InfiAgent-DABench, making DSBench’s main novelty its specific task sourcing and explicit coverage of both analysis and modeling. The introduction of the Relative Performance Gap (RPG) metric for normalized evaluation across heterogeneous tasks is a useful, though incremental, methodological contribution. Some claims of uniqueness—particularly regarding multimodality, end-to-end workflows, and revealing agent-expert performance gaps—are overstated, as similar features and findings exist in prior work. Overall, DSBench is a timely and relevant addition to the field, but reviewers should recognize that its advances are incremental rather than fundamentally transformative within the current landscape of data science agent benchmarks.
