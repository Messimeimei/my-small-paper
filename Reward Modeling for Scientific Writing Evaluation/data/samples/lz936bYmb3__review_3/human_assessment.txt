This paper introduces DMQR-RAG, a multi-query rewriting approach designed to enhance document retrieval diversity and recall within the Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) framework by leveraging multiple rewriting strategies. While the method aims to improve the transmission of information from queries to documents and demonstrates promising results, its technical novelty appears limited. Specifically, if the approach for equality, expansion, and reduction relies solely on established baselines, the main contribution is restricted to employing a large language model (LLM) to select among different types of "known" rewriting techniques. In this case, the work does not introduce fundamentally new rewriting strategies but rather orchestrates existing methods through an LLM-based selection mechanism. As such, the novelty is incremental, centering on the application of LLMs for choosing between pre-existing query rewriting approaches, rather than advancing the underlying techniques themselves. The paper would benefit from clarifying any novel additions to the baselines and more explicitly distinguishing its contributions from prior work in both classical and modern query modeling.
