SymTex introduces a benchmark for non-monotonic reasoning (NMR) in large language models, distinguished by its systematic, automated pairing of symbolic and textual samples and explicit predicate diversity. While these features represent a useful extension over prior benchmarks like LogicBench and LogicNMR, the advances are primarily incremental, focusing on dataset construction and granularity rather than fundamentally new tasks or evaluation protocols. The authors’ claims of being the "first" to provide paired modality evaluation are somewhat overstated, as similar capabilities exist in prior work, though perhaps less systematically. The tri-state querying and answer set computation tasks are routine adaptations of existing evaluation protocols, and the empirical findings largely confirm known limitations of LLMs in NMR. Reviewers should recognize SymTex as a valuable but incremental contribution and may wish to request more detailed, direct comparisons with closely related benchmarks to clarify its true novelty.
