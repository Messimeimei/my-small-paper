This submission extends recent work on aligning artificial neural network (ANN) activations with human brain activity by mapping sub-groups of large language model (LLM) neurons directly to functional brain networks (FBNs), rather than at the population or layer level. The main technical novelty lies in applying fine-grained neuron-to-network mapping—previously used in audio and vision domains—to LLMs in the language domain, and explicitly analyzing the internal organization of LLM neurons in relation to FBNs. While the authors claim to be the first to achieve this sub-group-to-FBN mapping, this is an incremental advance, as prior work has explored similar fine-grained correspondences in other modalities and, to some extent, in language models. The methodology (encoding models, fixed regressors) and evaluation metrics are standard, with the primary difference being the granularity and explicit FBN focus. Reviewers should note that the submission’s novelty is mainly in the combination and application of existing methods, and that the “first” claims somewhat overstate the technical delta relative to prior work.
