This paper proposes a method for measuring large language model (LLM) similarity using topological data analysis (TDA), specifically introducing zigzag persistence to account for the properties of the LLM graph structure, with a particular focus on the layer structure of neural networks. While the idea of applying zigzag persistence in this context is new in the sense that it considers the layer structure, the overall novelty is limited. Prior work has already explored representing neural networks as graphs and applying TDA, as in Lacombe et al. (IJCAI2021), and the use of zigzag persistence for graph structures has been verified in studies such as Myers et al. (EPJ Data Science, 2023) and Chen et al. (NeurIPS2022). The main contribution of this paper appears to be the specific combination of zigzag persistence with a focus on the layer structure, but this is viewed as a straightforward extension of existing methods rather than a fundamentally new approach. As such, the degree of novelty is weak, and the paper would benefit from more clearly articulating how its approach differs from or advances beyond these prior works.
