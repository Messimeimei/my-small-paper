This submission presents a training-free, plug-and-play constrained decoding method for improving LLM tool use, with a particular focus on order consistency via structured sampling and majority voting. While the approach is practical and empirically strong, its core techniques—constrained decoding and plug-and-play enhancement—are already well-established in recent literature, and the main novelty lies in the specific engineering of order consistency mechanisms. The authors overstate the uniqueness of their method, omitting direct comparison to highly relevant, recent works that offer similar training-free, documentation-driven constrained decoding for tool use. The claimed performance improvements are notable but may be attributable to implementation details rather than conceptual advances. Overall, the submission represents an incremental extension of current methods, and reviewers should weigh its engineering contributions against the rapidly evolving state of the field and the need for more thorough related work comparison.
