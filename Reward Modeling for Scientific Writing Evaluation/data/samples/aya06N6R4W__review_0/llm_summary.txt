This assessment finds that the submission proposes an end-to-end, explainable LLM-based pipeline for causal structure discovery and counterfactual inference from unstructured news text, but similar pipelines have recently been published in the field. While the explicit use of a second LLM for multi-source graph merging and a detailed stepwise analysis of LLM bottlenecks in counterfactual reasoning are highlighted as distinguishing features, these contributions are incremental, as comparable methods and analyses exist in recent literature. The submission’s related work section is incomplete, omitting several highly relevant and recent works that perform both extraction and inference, which may overstate the novelty of the approach. Empirical improvements may stem from implementation details rather than conceptual advances, and terminology differences (e.g., "second LLM") do not reflect substantive methodological innovation. Overall, the work is a useful addition to a rapidly evolving area, but its claims of novelty should be calibrated against a crowded field with many similar pipelines and recent advances.
