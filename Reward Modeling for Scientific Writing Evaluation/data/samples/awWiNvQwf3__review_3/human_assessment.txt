This paper introduces MOLLEO, a framework that integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) such as GPT-4, BioT5, and MoleculeSTM into evolutionary algorithms for molecular discovery by substituting traditional genetic operators (mutation and crossover) with LLM-driven operations. While the approach is straightforward and offers flexibility in incorporating different LLMs, the novelty of the methodology is limited. The core contribution is essentially a direct replacement of genetic operators in the existing Graph-GA framework with LLMs, which constitutes a relatively simple extension of established techniques. Leveraging LLMs for mutation and crossover does not fundamentally depart from prior work that uses deep learning models to enhance genetic operations, and the substitution can be viewed as an incremental adaptation rather than a conceptual breakthrough. Additionally, the paper’s approach to multi-objective optimization—retaining only the Pareto frontier of the current population—is a standard practice in evolutionary algorithms, as exemplified by well-known methods like NSGA [1] and MOEA/D [2]. Although the authors do not claim novelty in their use of Pareto set selection, it is important to note that this concept has already been widely applied in molecular optimization problems in previous studies [3-5]. As such, the paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of its approach to multi-objective optimization and a clearer positioning within the broader landscape of Pareto-based optimization methods. Overall, while MOLLEO demonstrates practical applicability and ease of implementation, its methodological advances are incremental and do not significantly push the boundaries of the field.
