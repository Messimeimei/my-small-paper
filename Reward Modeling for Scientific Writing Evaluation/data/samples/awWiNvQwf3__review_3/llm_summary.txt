This assessment finds that the submission extends the use of large language models (LLMs) in molecular optimization by explicitly integrating them as mutation and crossover operators within evolutionary algorithms (EAs), with a focus on chemistry-aware proposal generation and empirical validation. While the authors claim to be the first to use LLMs as genetic operators in EAs for molecular optimization, this novelty is somewhat overstated, as closely related works (e.g., LICO, OPRO) have explored similar ideas, albeit with different terminology or framing. The main technical contribution is the explicit mapping of LLMs to EA operators and the demonstration of improved performance on standard benchmarks, but the conceptual advance over prior LLM-guided proposal generation is modest and largely incremental. The assessment notes that some relevant prior work is not cited or directly compared, and that distinctions between "prompt engineering" and "LLM as EA operator" may be more terminological than substantive. Overall, the submission is a timely and empirically strong extension of LLM-driven optimization in this domain, but reviewers should weigh its incremental conceptual novelty against the rapidly evolving field and the need for comprehensive comparisons.
