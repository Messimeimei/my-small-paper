This assessment finds that the submission "Adversarial Search Engine Optimization for Large Language Models" primarily contributes an empirical demonstration of black-box, adversarial content manipulation attacks on production LLM-powered search engines and plugin APIs. While the authors introduce the term "Preference Manipulation Attacks" and frame the ecosystem effects as a "prisoner's dilemma," these conceptual contributions are largely extensions or rebrandings of established ideas from prompt injection, SEO manipulation, and adversarial ML literature. The main technical advance is the real-world, black-box evaluation, which distinguishes the work from prior studies that often used white-box or simulated settings. However, the novelty of the attack mechanism itself is incremental, and the submission overstates its conceptual uniqueness while omitting discussion of several closely related recent works. Overall, the work offers a valuable empirical advance but is best viewed as a synthesis and extension of existing adversarial attack paradigms applied to LLM-driven selection and ranking.
